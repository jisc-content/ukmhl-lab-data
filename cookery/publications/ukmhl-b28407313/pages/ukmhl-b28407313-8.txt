Among the means suggested to obviate the pressure of present distress, it is much however to be regretted, that they should have been induced to sanction the practice of grinding mixed grain, and afterwards making it into bread of undetermined quality. The foie criterion which the public of the capital at present possess, to judge of the goodness of bread, is its colour ; when a certain de- gree of whiteness ceases to be required by law, bread will become an aggregate of all the impurities which can be forced through the mill. The-use of whole grain as here recommended renders the adoption of such a meafure wholly unnecessary. London, Dec. 24, 1800.

This character of veal is illustrated in a passage occurring in the first number of the famous Almanack des Gourmands, which appeared in Paris at the commencement of the present century:—“Veal lends itself to so many metamorphoses that we may fairly term it ‘the chameleon of cookery.’ ” * Precisely the same view is held by Brillat-Savarin, but in relation to poultry, when he says, “ Poultry is for cookery what canvas is to the painter.” f In such conditions, therefore, it is natural that the French cuisine should be essentially distinguished for its sauces, by which it adorns and transforms material in itself somewhat uninteresting or uninviting.

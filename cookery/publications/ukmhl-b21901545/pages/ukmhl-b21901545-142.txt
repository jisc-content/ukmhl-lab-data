Soups neglected by many; I THINK it may be said that soups, whether clear (that is, prepared from the juices of meat and vegetables only) or thick (that is, purges of animal or vegetable matters), are far too lightly esteemed by most classes in England, while they are almost unknown to the working man. For the latter they might furnish an important cheap and savoury dish ; by the former they are too often regarded as the mere prelude to a meal, to be swallowed hastily, or disregarded altogether as mostly unworthy of atten- an important 0 J form of diet tion.

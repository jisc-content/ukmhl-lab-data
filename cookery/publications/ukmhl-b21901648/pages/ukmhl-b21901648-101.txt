The best parts of chicken for a devil are the wings and legs. Remove the skin, score the flesh deeply in several places, and rub in a mixture made of salt, pepper, cayenne, mustard, anchovy and butter. Broil over a clear fire, and serve the fowl hot on a napkin. Chicken, Fried. 274. Take the remains of a cold chicken, cut it into neat pieces, brush a little oil over each piece, and strew over it rather thickly salt and curry powder. Melt a little butter in a frying-pan, and fry some onions, cut into thin strips about half an inch long and the eighth of an inch wide ; fry them slowly, and keep them in the pan until they are a dark brown colour and quite dry. They will require a little care, as they must on no account be burnt. Fry the chicken, strew the onions over it, and serve with slices of lemon. Time to fry the chicken, ten minutes.

THE APSLEY COOKERY BOOK 72 134. Semolina and Cheese Pudding. Ingredients. Two tablespoonfuls of Semolina. Two ounces of grated Cheese. One ounce of Butter. Half a pint of Milk. A little grated Onion. Put the milk into a double saucepan and bring it to boiling point, then stir in the semolina; boil for fifteen minutes, stirring constantly. Put into a well-greased baking-dish, sprinkle rusk-crumbs on the top, and bake a light brown. Equals 480 grains proteid. 135. Semolina and Cheese Rissoles. Any of the above mixture when cold will make these. Mix in first the white of one egg (for about half the above quantity), form into balls or rissoles, roll in rusk- crumbs or raspings, and fry in boiling oil, or Albene, till brown. 136. Sea-kale (Scalloped). Ingredients. Cold boiled Sea Kale. Bread-crumbs. Butter. Pepper. When any sea-kale is left, it can be cut into inch lengths and put into buttered scallop shells, with any remaining sauce and pepper. Cover with bread-crumbs; put bits of butter over the top, and bake in a hot oven for ten minutes.

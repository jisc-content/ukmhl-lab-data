Peel the turnips very thinly and throw them into cold water ; if they are young, they can be boiled whole for twenty minutes and served plain ; if old, they are better cut up and boiled for half an hour. Drain and press them well; then mash them with a wooden spoon, add one ounce of butter, make very hot, and serve. A tablespoonful of cream can be added if liked. 196. Turnip Tops. Take as many turnip tops as required; pick out all the decayed leaves, wash well in plenty of water. Put into boiling'water for half an hour, drain them well, and cut or chop them like spinach; return to the saucepan with one ounce of butter. Make very hot, and serve. 197. Turnips (Stewed).

Boil a fine fowl in poele (No. 9), and when done send it up with oyster sauce as follows:—^Take three dozen of oysters, let them boil up in their own liquor, drain them in a small cullender, let the liquor settle, put two ounces of fresh butter into a stew- pan, with a spoonful of flour, pass it over the fire for a few minutes, then pour in the liquor of the oysters, and half a pint of cream; stir it till the sauce boils, add two or three spoonsful of bechamel (No. 7), and reduce the sauce, as the oysters are sure to relax it; pass the sauce through a tammy; then take out the hard part of the oysters, wipe them in a cloth, and put them into the sauce; give the sauce a boil, and pour it over the fowl. 324—Fowl, d la Monglas.

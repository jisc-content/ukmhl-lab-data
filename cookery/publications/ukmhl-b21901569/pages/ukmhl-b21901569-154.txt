It should be added that Dumas admits mutton and veal as ingredients, to be adopted only in the south of France, where beef is often inferior or not procurable. " The basis of a good pot-au-fcu is beef .... " Choose the freshest and juiciest meat you can find; let it be thick; for if thin, it will be exhausted in the cooking; do not wash it, or you will rob it of a portion of its juice. When the meat has been sepa- rated from the bones, tie it up, so that it may keep its shape, and put it in the pot with a pint of water to every pound of meat " We have advised you to separate the bones from the meat, not indeed because we banish them from pot-au-feu; on the contrary, we reserve for them a separate place, only we break them up well with a mallet, because the more they are broken, the more effectually is the gelatine extracted from them. Then we place them in a horsehair-bag with any scraps of fowl, rabbit, partridge, or roast pigeon, which may be found in the larder; in fact, the remains of yesterday's dinner.

There is not now much clanger in that direction, as the fashion of naming dishes after people has nearly gone out of date, but there is a difficulty in providing dishes to suit the tastes of individual guests, and to avoid this it is an advantage to have some of a plain character included in an elaborate menu. Eight or wrong, it has become the fashion to make out the menu in French, though there are words enough in English to provide a name for every dish. The neglect of the English language has been shown to a still greater degree, for at the Etonian club’s annual ball, given at Oxford, the menu of the supper was printed in Latin.

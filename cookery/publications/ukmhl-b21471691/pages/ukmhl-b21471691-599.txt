In the following Removes still greater innovations wiU be found than in any other department, for I have always aimed at producing the greatest effect upon the last dishes sent to table, particularly as they are the ones over which each convive has time to pass remarks, and admire, if pro- perly executed. The transformation of the second course from the first has never failed to give the greatest satisfac- tion, and has often caused the greatest hilarity at table; some parties, unacquainted with them, have ordered their removal, thinldng they belonged to the first com-se, whilst others have actually carved them before discovering their mistake. And, again, those Removes being only demi- glace, and not too sweet, refresh the palate, disposes it more favorably for the dessert, and does not prevent the freely partaking of the ices which there cannot be avoided.

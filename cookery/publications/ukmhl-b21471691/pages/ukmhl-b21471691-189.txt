No. 348. Pike with caper sauce. Boil the fish as before, and have ready caper sauce made as follows : put fifteen tablespoonfuls of melted butter in a stewpan, and when it boils add a quarter of a pound of fresh butter; when it melts, add two tablespoonfuls of Haison (No. 119); let it remain on the fire to thicken, but do not let it boil; moisten with a Httle milk if required, then add two tablespoonfuls of capers, and poinr over the fish. No. 349. Pike a la Maitre d'Hotel. Boil the fish as usual, and dish it vdthout a napkin; then put twelve tablespoonfuls of melted butter in a stew- pan; and when it is upon the point of boiling, add a quarter of a pound of maitre d'hotel butter, and when it melts pour over and round the fish; serve very hot.

THE GUINEA HEN. This bird, called by the ancients the " Hen of Numidia,"comes origi- nally from many burning regions of Africa. In Greece, and especially in Rome, vanity alone gave it a price which was willingly granted, more on account of its scarcity than for its taste.98 The Guinea hen appeared at great banquets, when the Amphytrion was more anxious to show his opulence than to demonstrate the delicacy of his dishes. Martial,99 and Pliny,100 the naturalist, raised great objections against this ostentatious and useless rarity.

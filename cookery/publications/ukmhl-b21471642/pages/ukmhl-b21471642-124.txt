They distinguished two kinds, the wild and the cultivated.76 This much esteemed root received the honour of being prepared in many ways. Sometimes it was eaten as a salad, with salt, oil, and vinegar." It was also stewed, and mixed afterwards with cenogarum.78 Again, they boiled it in a stewpan, over a slow fire, with some cummin and a little oil, and just before serving it was sprinkled with ground cummin seeds.79 BLIT 5 (a sort of beet).

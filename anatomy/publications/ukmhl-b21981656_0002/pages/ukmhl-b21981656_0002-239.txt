The thickened or polypous membrane may be altogether normal in its apparent structure. But as the hyperplastic process is very frequently associated with some form of inflammation, the overgrown portions usually exhibit certain morbid alterations. The epithelium is often atrophied, the glands obliterated or transformed into cysts (Art. 422), while the fibrous elements are increased, and infiltrated with cells. In other instances the glands may be hypertrophied.

THE ALIMENTARY SYSTEM. XI.] 453 15. In that man possesses a liver, he agrees, as has been mentioned in the First Lesson, with all the other members of his sub-kingdom ; and in that he possesses a solid liver he agrees also with all, excepting only the Lancelet, in which animal this organ is a mere caecum-like, saccular diverticulum (or offshoot) of the intestine, with its apex turned forwards. The liver may attain a much greater complexity, as regards the number of its lobes, than we find in man, but in the majority of Vertebrates (in all, that is, below his own class)

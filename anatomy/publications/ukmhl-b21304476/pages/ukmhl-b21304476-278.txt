N T N the thigh we saw a strong fascia, arising from the neighbouring bones and ligaments, firmly in- vesting the muscles, and giving origin to some of their fibres; but, on removing the integuments of the shoulder and arm, we do not meet with any regu- lar fascia. The muscles, like those on the outside of the thorax, are covered by condensed cellular sub- stance; and this substance might be taken for a thin aponeurosis, but it is widely different in its nature, and ought to be regularly dissected off with the inte- guments.

In the different Mammalia, as in Man, they are two in number; but there is considerable variety in their situation and struc- ture. In the Ape tribe, in most of the Carnivora, in Soli- peds, and in most Ruminants, they are placed in a Scrotum, with a Cellular Partition between them. They are pendulous in such Animals as have Vesiculae Semi- nales, in which the Semen is readily conducted to the Pe- nis. In the Kangaroo' and Opossum, they are placed at the fore part of the Pelvis, in a Scrotum without any partition. In the Belluce and Civet, they are situated under the Skin of the Perineum. In the Otter and Camel, they lie under the Integuments of the Groin. In the Bat, Squirrel, Rat, Guinea Pig, Beaver, Por- Opine, Hedge-hog, Shrew, Mole, &c. they pass from the Abdomen into the Scrotum, upon certain occa- sions, particularly at their seasons of sexual intercourse.

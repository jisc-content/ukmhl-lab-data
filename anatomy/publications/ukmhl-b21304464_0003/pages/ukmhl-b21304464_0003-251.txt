The eye-ball, being made up of parts of differ- ent structures, is liable to a great variety of orga- nic derangements. The sclerotic coat, the choroid coat, and optic nerves, are subject to particular organic derange- ments; and the eye lids and lacrymal passages, to all the diseases of mucous membranes. Besides, diseases which were primarily limited to one part of the eye, are frequently communicated to the ad- jacent parts. Sect. L OF ORGANIC DERANGEMENTS OF THE CORNEA.

The posterior ulnar recurrent makes for the interval between the internal condyle and the olecranon, to join with the inferior profunda and anastomotica magna. The posterior interosseous recurrent ascends, between the supinator brevis and the anconeus, to anastomose on the back of the external condyle with the superior profunda. It further joins, by a large anastomotic arch across the back of the joint, with the anastomotica magna and posterior ulnar recurrent. Fig. 722.—Diagram of the Anastomoses of the Brachial Artery.

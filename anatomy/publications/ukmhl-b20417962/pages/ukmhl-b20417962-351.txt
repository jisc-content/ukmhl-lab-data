297 SUBCLAVIUS Action.—To draw the outer end of the clavicle, and with it the point of the shoulder, downwards and slightly forwards; but chiefly by pulling the bone inwards to supplement the ligaments which prevent dislocation of the sterno-clavieular joint. Relations.—In front, the clavi-pectoral fascia and the pectoralis major; behind, Fig. 266.—The Pectoralis Minor, Obliquus Internus, Pyramidalis, and Rectus Abdominis.

THE EDINBURGH STEREOSCOPIC ATLAS OF ANATOMY, CENTRAL NERVOUS SYSTEM. BRAIN—No. 12. PATHOLOGICAL SPECIMEN—HYDROCEPHALUS. Ventricles of the brain greatly distended by fluid. Foramina of Monro much expanded and fornix stretched. The septum lucidum has in great part disappeared, so that the fifth ventricle is thrown into the ventricular system, and the lateral ventricles are brought into direct communication with each other.

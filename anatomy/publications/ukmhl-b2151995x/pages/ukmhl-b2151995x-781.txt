XIV. If it is a tertian or quartan, which intermits completely, on the intermediate days, it is proper to have recourse to both walking and other exercises and anointing. One of the more ancient physicians, named Cleophantus, in this kind of disesise, some time before the paroxysm, bathed the patient from over the head, with a great quantity of warm water, and then gave him wine, which Asclepiades, however, has properly omitted, although he followed most of the precepts of that man: for it is doubtful (as to its good effect.)

At the opening of some of the Veins in the Heart, or where Branches end in some of the principal Trunks, there is a sort of Valves, which prevent the retrograde motion of the Blood. From the different parts of the Body, the Blood is returned to the Heart by one or two Posterior Cavse, situated at the sides of the Aorta, by the Hepatic Vein, and by two Anterior Cavse, one at each side of the Breast.

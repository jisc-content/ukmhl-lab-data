It is less intimately connected to the Stomach in this than in the former classes. In some, it is fixed to the left side of that Organ, as in a Salamander ; in others, to the Anterior Arch, as in a Chameleon ; or to the beginning of the Intestines, as in a Tortoise and Ser- pents. In a Frog, it is above the Stomach, between the plies of the Mesentery : In a Crocodile, at the left portion of the Intestines. OF THE KIDNEYS.

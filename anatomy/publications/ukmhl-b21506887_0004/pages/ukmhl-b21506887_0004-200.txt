The Umbilical Cord is observed to be proportionally shorter in Quadrupeds than in the Human Species. The peculiarities of a Foetal Quadruped, with re- spect to structure, are not much different from those in a Child at the end of pregnancy. The number of young produced, at a time, varies according to the kind, and is observed to be greater in tame than in wild Animals, as in a Sow and Cat. A Whale produces only a single Foetus, and once in two years. In suckling, she turns almost on her baclt, on the rim of the water. The nourishment of a Foetus, in Mammalia, is de- rived from the Mother's milk, which is secreted from the Mamma, and is peculiar to the Animals of this class. In Multiparous Mammalia, especially in a Sow^ the Animal has the power of retaining her Milk till the young have arranged themselves properly about her, and have each selected the Teat that is appropriat- ed to it.

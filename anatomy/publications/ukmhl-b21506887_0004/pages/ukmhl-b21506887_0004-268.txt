The two principal divisions of the Animals of this class, viz. those with, and those without Feet, have a remarkable difference in the form and number of their Muscles. In the former, viz. a Turtle^ Frog^ &c. where the Trunk of the Body possesses proportionally a small degree of mobility, a number of Muscles are wanting, though the others are strong. In the Animals of this class without Feet, namely, the Serpents, the Muscles are slender, but extremely numerous, on ac- count of the number of the Vertebrae^ and the want of Anterior and Posterior Extremities, In a Turtle, the Spine possesses no motion but in the parts belonging to the Neck and Tail. The Back and Loins, being ossified together, have no Muscles. The Diaphragm, and also the Muscles of the Abdo- men, are wanting; but the other Muscles are remark-^ ably strong.

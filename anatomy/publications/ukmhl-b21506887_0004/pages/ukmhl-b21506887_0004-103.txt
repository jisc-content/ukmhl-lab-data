99 Part I.] OF MAMMALIA. OF THE EAR. The Organ of Hearing exists in all the Animals of the class, and the External Ear in most of them; but there are several individuals in which the latter is defi- cient, as those which live much under ground, as a Mole, or in water, as an Ornithorrynchus, most Seals, a Walrus, Manati, and Cetacea, The essential parts are in general as in Man, varying chiefly in form, and in all adapted to their manner of life. The Lobe is wanting in all Quadrupeds. An Auricle, resembling that in Man, is found only in some of the Ape kind, though a Concha, or External Ear, exists in Quadrupeds in general; but even in an Ourang- outang, as well as in ocher Apes, it is more pointed than in Man.

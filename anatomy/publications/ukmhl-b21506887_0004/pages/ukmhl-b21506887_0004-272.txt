OF THE INTEGUMENTS, The Integuments of Reptiles consist of simple Skin, Scales, Rings, or Shields, and all of these covered with Cuticle. The greater number are covered with Scales. The surface of the Body differs from that in the former classes, in having neither Hairs nor Feathers. In a Turtle^ the Epidej^mis is only distinct upon,the Neck and Limbs; in other parts of the Body, it is incor- porated with the Shell.

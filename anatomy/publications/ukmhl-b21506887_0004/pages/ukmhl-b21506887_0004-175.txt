The structure in the greater part of the Animals of this class, is somewhat similar to that in Man ; but in an Ox and Sheep, it is peculiar for the appearance of its Cells. Omentum. The Omentum exists in all Mammalia, and is pe- culiar to this class, though something similar to it is observed in several other Animals. It varies less in its extent and general appearance in the same Species, than in Man. It commonly goes as far in one direc- tion as the Kidneys, and in another, extends to the Pubes. In an Ourang-outang, the Omentum is as in Man ; but in the rest of the Ape tribe, it is similar to what is found in other Brutes. In Carnivora and Ruminants, its extent is very great.

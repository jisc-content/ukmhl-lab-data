In Snakes^ it comes off at moulting seasons, forming what is called the Snake's Shirty but leaving the Scales below adhering to the Skin. In a Tortoise, the Shell never changes. The Scales vary according to the kind. In a Tor- toise, they are of a Horny nature, differing in hardness and figure. In a Crocodile, they are osseous. In a great part of Lizards and Serpents, they are small plates of Skin, with the Cuticle between them. In Reptiles with Scales, as Lizards, the Skin is dry. In those with naked Skins, as a Frog and Salamander, it is moist, being covered with a viscid fluid. In Serpents, there is a strong Skin under the Scales, intimately connected with the Muscles. The Cellidar Substance^ more or less loose in Rep* tiles, connects the Skin to the parts underneath. In a Frog and Toad, the Integuments are remark- able in adhering to the Body at certain points only, and there, chiefly by Vessels and Nerves, viz. about the Mouth, Linea Mediana of the Body, Arm-pits, and Groins. In other parts, the Animal lies loose in the Skin, as in a Bag. Between the Epidermis and Skin, and under the Scales, the Corpus Mucosum is situated, which varies in colour, having all the different tints in the diff'erent individuals of the class. In a Chameleon, some Frogs, &c. the colour changes occasionally, in consequence of light, heat, eff*ects of passion, &c.

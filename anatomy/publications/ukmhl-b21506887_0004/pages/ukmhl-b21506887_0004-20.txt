16 ARRANGEMENT OF ANIMALS. CLASS L A. Vertebral warm-blooded Animals. Mammalia. In the Mammalia, the Females are viviparous, and suckle their young. The greater number of them are Quadrupeds, and these, for the most part, are covered with Hair, and walk on the Earth ; the rest have a sleek Skin, and inhabit the Ocean. They consist of the following Orders : 1. BiMANUM.—Two-handed, Thumbs separate on the Superior Extremities only. Homo, Man. 2.

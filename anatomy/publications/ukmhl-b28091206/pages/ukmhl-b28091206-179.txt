But as love accompanies beauty, and as beauty disap- pears with youth, it is not astonishing that a woman should sometimes become morose as she advances in life. Having lost the power of charming, she is no longer follow- ed ; and this neglect she is too often unable to support. Love, we may conclude, does not al- ways accompany affection. Violent love without affection is finely exemplified in the following story. When Constantinople was taken by the Turks, Irene, a young Greek of an illus- trious family fell into the hands of Maho- met II., who was at that time in the prime of youth and glory.

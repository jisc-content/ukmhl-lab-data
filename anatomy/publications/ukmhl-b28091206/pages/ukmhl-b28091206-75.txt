The only sense which all animals pos- sess, and which resides in almost the whole surface of their bodies, is that of feeling. It makes them sensible of the forms of bodies, and of their motions and tempera- ture, by means of the forms which these present. The other senses are affected only by different modifications of touch, but are capable of receiving from it more delicate impressions.

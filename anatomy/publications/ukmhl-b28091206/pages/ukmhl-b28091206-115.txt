Female beauty differs among the various races of mankind. There is, however, a standard of beauty independent of all idea of that partiality which is wounded by pride, and which self-love with such obstinacy maintains. The negro, who wisely in a hot clime, prefers for his mate a woman of colour, always awards the superiority in beauty to the white. The Calmuck knows well the immense difference there is be- tween his own beauties and those of Tcher- cassia whom he impassionately seeks for, and obtains by the weight of gold or by the force of arms.

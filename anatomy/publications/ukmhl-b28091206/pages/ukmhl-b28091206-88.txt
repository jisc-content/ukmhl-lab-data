Under the vital organs, are classed, first, the organs of digestion, the absorbent sur- faces, and the vessels which absorb from these surfaces, or the organs of absorption ; second, the heart, lungs and blood vessels, which derive their contents (the blood) from the absorbed lymph, or the organs of cir- culation ; and third, the secreting cavities, glands, &c. which separate various matters from the blood, or the organs of secretion, and of which generation is the sequel. Under the intellectual organs, are class- ed, first, the organs of sense, where impres- sions take place; second, the brain or or- gan of thought, where these excite ideas ; and third, the cerebellum, where volition results from the last.

We observe a woman possessing one species of beauty :—Her neck is tapering; her shoulders, without being angular, are sufficiently broad and definite ; her waist remarkable for fine proportion, is almost an inverted cone ; her haunches are mode- rately expanded ; her thighs proportional; her arms, as well as her limbs, are taper- ing ; and her hands and feet are small.— The whole figure is precise, striking and brilliant. From its proportions, it seems almost aerial; and you would imagine that if your hands were placed under the lateral parts of her tapering waist, the slightest pressure would suffice to throw her into the air.

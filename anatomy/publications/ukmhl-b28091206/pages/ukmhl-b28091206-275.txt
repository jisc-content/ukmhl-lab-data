221 LAWS OF FEMALE BEAUTY. SECTION III. Of Circumstances which ensure or prevent Impregnation. Fecundity may be prevented by many causes : such as a connection physically or morally unsuitable, too early marriage, peculiar character or manner of thinking, strong passions, caprices, regrets, the no- tion of preserving beauty, intemperance, an abuse of pleasure, the embarrassment of a family, &c. In general, the most fecund women are of a sanguine temperament.

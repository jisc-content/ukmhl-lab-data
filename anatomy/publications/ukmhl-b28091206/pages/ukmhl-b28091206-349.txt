LAWS OF FEMALE BEAUTY. 295 Domestic infelicity, resulting from sex- ual infidelity, happens chiefly to young people, whose want of experience, igno- rance of the world, and extravagant expec- tations, are very often a source of great misery. The wants of physical love, which actuate them powerfully, though unseen and undefined, and the attractions of beauty, which may be more or less par- tial, completely blind them to almost every circumstance in the character of the person with whom they accidentally asso- ciate. The imagination, rendered active by the excitement of love, associates the peculiar form of the person beloved, with the gratification of the passion itself;—the former is felt to be a necessary condition of the latter;—and so complete does the unity of the passion and its object become.

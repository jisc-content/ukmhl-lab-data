The following Old English anec- dote is well known : — “A Gentlewo- man comming to one that stood at a window reading a booke, Sir (sayd she), I would I were your booke (because she loued the Gentleman). So would I (quoth he), I wish you were. But what booke would you have me to bee (sayd the other) if I were to be so ? Marry, an Almanacke (quoth the Gen- tleman) because I would change every yeare.” If, then, the laws of nature are thus op- posed to the laws of society, in what, let us enquire, is founded the justice of the latter ?

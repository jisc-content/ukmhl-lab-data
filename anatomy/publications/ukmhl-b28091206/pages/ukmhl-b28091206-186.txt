KALOGYNOMIA, OR THE 132 Shame, it has been said, was invented by pleasure. It is the refinement of volup- tuousness. It confers an inestimable value on things which have none in themselves. A glance detected through the Paphian armour of a transparent veil causes a pleasure always new, and which would be soon exhausted if it experienced no obstacle. All these little thefts of love would have no value, were it not for modesty. Shame, like all our other factitious sen- timents sprung up in society, presents ex- ceptions which are very worthy of philo- sophic observation.

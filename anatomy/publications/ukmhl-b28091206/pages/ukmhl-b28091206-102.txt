At that early age, and soon after it, similar moral dispositions chiefly prevail. Girls have the petulance of boys, and boys have the volatility of girls. Boys, however, become less attentive to little circumstances, and seem more determin- ed in their actions. Girls also become evidently interested about the impressions which they make on those around them ; and, when at play, they prefer always that sort of it which has a relation to their future destiny in life.

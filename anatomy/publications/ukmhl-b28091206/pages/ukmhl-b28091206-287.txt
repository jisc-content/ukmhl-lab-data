Women ought to be depend- ent : for reason cannot procure them in old age a power that beauty did not give them even in youth.” In the East, women are much more nu- merous than men ; and “ from this,” says an ingenious writer, “ it would appear as if polygamy, so common among the Orien- tals, had been pointed out by nature it- self : for were they obliged, like the Euro- peans, to confine themselves to one wo- man, all the rest would be useless; and this superabundance would be an excep- tion to a very true axiom, that nature has made nothing in vain.”

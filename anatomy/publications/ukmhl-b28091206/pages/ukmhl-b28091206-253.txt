199 LAWS OF FEMALE BEAUTY. Women who are too early married, are speedily enervated ; and, if this take place before their full growth, they remain always of diminished stature, weak and pale. Men also in southern countries, among whom puberty is early, and who marry at twelve or fourteen, are small, delicate, effeminate and destitute of vigour. It may not be irrelevant to observe, that a flatter thorax, a broader back, and thick- er cartilages of the bones of the pubis, dis- tinguish the skeleton of a weak woman who has too often had children when very young, from the skeleton of a virgin.

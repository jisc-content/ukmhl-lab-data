At the period, then, of puberty, which we have thus described in both sexes, the sexual instinct, as if by a spontaneous internal voice of nature, at first excites, and then renders more prone to, the venereal embrace, man who is now in the flower of his life. At that age, the agitation and the disorder of the senses give birth to a new sense, in which man alone, seems to receive his existence, in which every thing becomes animated and embellished, and in which all around him appears to burn with the same flame by which he is deliciously consumed.

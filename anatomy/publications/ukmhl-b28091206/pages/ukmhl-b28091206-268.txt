The admirable sources of comparative anatomy show us, that any one function being given, all the rest have a correspond- ence to it The quick and irritable genus felis has muscles capable of sudden exer- tion, pointed teeth, claws, &c., the duller pecora have slow moving muscles, molar teeth, hoofs, &c. Indeed, it is evident that any attempt at the performance of a subordinate function must be completely modified by, and accommodated to, the function on which it is dependent.

Most frequently the defects exist on the part of the woman ; and though our space here does not permit our enumerating and describing the whole of these, yet, may we point out one of the most obvious. The uterus is often placed nearer to one side of the pelvis, than to the other ; and hence the os tincae presents itself very differently in the vagina. (See Plate 24.)

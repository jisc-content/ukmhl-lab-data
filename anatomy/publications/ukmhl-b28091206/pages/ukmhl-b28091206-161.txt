LAWS OF FEMALE BEAUTY. 107 The symptoms by which puberty is distinguished in the female, demand here particular notice. The swelling of the genital parts renders their apertures and canals narrower ; the breasts become round and elevated, forming considerable pro- jections beyond the thorax ; and she is subject to a sanguineous discharge which takes place every month from the vessels of the uterus, and is known by the name of menses.

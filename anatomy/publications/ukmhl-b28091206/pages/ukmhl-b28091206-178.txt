Men being formed for accom- plishing great things, seem to abandon their nature when they suffer themselves to be overcome by love. When the fire of youth engages them in this passion, they fall into a state of languor, by which all their faculties are absorbed; but this im- petuosity is cooled by years; they soon return to themselves; and reason gaining a lasting power over them, permits them to entertain only mild and regular affec- tions, which are displayed in their exterior conduct.

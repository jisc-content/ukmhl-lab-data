In Gramenivorous Animals, and in those which go in quest of prey in the night, the Pigmentum is of a light and shining colour in the bottom of the Eye, and is called Tapetum. In some entirely white Animals, as the white Rabbit, the Paint is wanting, or transparent, and the Eye has a red colour, from the Vessels of the Choroid Coat being seen in its bottom; but the redness disappears when the Animal is dead. In the Albinos also, and white Persons born of Negro Parents, the Pigmentum Ni- grum is entirely or nearly deficient, and a red colour appears in the bottom of the Eye. The fore part of the Choroid Coat, opposite to the Ciliary Circle, forms a black radiated Ring, called Cor- pus Ciliare, which is about the sixth part of an inch in breadth towards the Temple, but somewhat narrower towards the Nose. In the posterior portion of the Corpus Ciliare, there are numerous pale radiated Ciliary Strict, but so cover- ed by the Pigmentum Nigrum, as not to be distinctly seen till the Paint is removed. Near the connexion of the Corpus Ciliare with the root of the Iris, these Striae become gradually broader and more elevated, and form white Plicoe or Foldsy a- bout seventy in number, termed Processus Ciliares, the intervals of which are also covered by the Pigmentum Nigrum.

About the Eighth Month of Pregnancy, the Testis, by means not yet completely ascertained, passes gra- dually along to the Scrotum, a Process of the Perito- neum preceding it, which afterwards forms its Va- ginal Coat. Sometimes one or both Testes remain several Weeks after Birth in the Groins ; and cases have been found where they have continued during life in the Abdomen. The Testis, through the whole of its course, conti- nues to be covered by the Peritoneum, is connected to the parts on which it rests, and has its Vessels passing to it from behind forwards, the same as when situated in the Abdomen. While the Testicle is advancing through the Rings of the Abdominal Muscles, the Ligamentum Testis is found to be in some measure inverted, and to form the under and fore part of the Vaginal Coat, on which the Cremaster is expanded. After the descent of the Testicle, the Peritoneal Process, which accompanies it, begins to contract at the under Abdominal Ring; and a firm adhesion of its sides, to within a little distance of the Testicle, is commonly found to be produced by the time of Birth, though in some cases it remains open during life. The Prepuce of the Penis is so long in a Foetus, as not only to cover the Glans, but to extend some way beyond it.

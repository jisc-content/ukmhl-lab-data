But these affections then assume the very same character as those of the whole system; se- cretion is slowly performed, the disease constantly assumes a chronic type, and most frequently ter- minates in death. Mucous absorption at that stage of life, is like every other, slow, and attended with difficulty : the different infections are not so readily taken, either by the surfaces of the organs for respira- tion, or by the contact of morbid emanations with the surfaces adjoining the skin.

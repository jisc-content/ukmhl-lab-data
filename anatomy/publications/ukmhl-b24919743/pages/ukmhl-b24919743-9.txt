PREFACE. In the composition of the subjoined work the author has kept the following objects constantly in view :— To give a condensed and methodical description of the different structures and organs which enter into the composition of the human body ;— To point out the most convenient methods of conducting their anatomical examination ;—• To indicate some of the more important prac- tical applications that may be made of the facts disclosed to the student during the progress of his inquiries ;— And, finally, to present abridged summaries of the most instructive principles of general Ana- tomy.

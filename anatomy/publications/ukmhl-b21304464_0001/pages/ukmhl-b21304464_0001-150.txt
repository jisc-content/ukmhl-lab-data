In the last place, each of them may act with different degrees of force, and hence move the place of their insertions in all the diagonal lines we may suppose to be drawn between them ; or we may compare the pair of oblique muscles to the outer sticks of a spread fan, and the variety of the directions of the motions they are capable of producing, to the co-operations of intermediate sticks of the fan. In consequence of this motion in diagonal lines, the four recti muscles of the eye- ball enable us to follow an object moving before us in a circle.

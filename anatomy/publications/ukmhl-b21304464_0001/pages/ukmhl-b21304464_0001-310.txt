The upper middle part of the head of a child, in a natural birth, being what presents itself first at the os uteri *, an accoucheur may reach the bregma with his finger, when the os uteri is a little opened. If the bregma is- stretched, and the pulsation of the brain is felt through it, the child is certainly alive : But if it is shrivelled and flaccid, without any observable pulsation in it, there is some reason to suspect the child to be very weak, or dead. Those who practise midwifery, should therefore examine the state of the bregma accurately. All the bregma is generally ossified before seven years of age. Ossa Temporum.

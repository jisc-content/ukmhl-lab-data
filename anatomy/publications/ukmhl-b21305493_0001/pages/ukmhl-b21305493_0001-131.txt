GENERAL ANATOMY. SYSTEMS GENERAL TO ALL THE APPENDAGES. GENERAL CONSIDERATIONS. The organic systems of the living economy may be divided into two grand classes. The first, generally distributed, and every where met with, not only concur to the formation of all structures, but moreover to that of the other systems, and offer to every organized part a common and uniform basis; they consist of the cellular, arterial, venous, exhaling, absorbent, and nervous systems. The last, on the contrary, situated in some peculiar structures, and foreign to other parts of the (Economy, have a smaller share of the living power, and exist almost distinctly; such as the bony, cartilaginous, tendinous, muscular, mucous, the serous systems, &c. &c.

NERVOUS SYSTEM OF ANIMAL LIFE. Anatomists, till now, have considered the nervous system in an uniform manner; but how- ever little we reflect on the forms, the distribution, the texture, or the properties and uses of the di- vers ramifications of which it is composed, it is easily perceived that they should be referred to two general systems, essentially distinct from each other, and having for principal centres, the one the brain and its dependencies, the other the ganglions ; the first appertains especially to ani- mal life ; on one hand it is the agent which trans- mits to the brain the external impressions calcu- lated to produce sensations ; on the other hand it serves to convey the volitions of that organ which are performed by the voluntary muscles to which it resorts.

Tubercular Synovitis, involving the Lower Ends of the Tibia and Fibula.—Lower ends of a left tibia and fibula —macerated, illustrating the above. The lower end of the fibula is enlarged by new periosteal bone, and the tip of the malleolus is rough. The lower end of the tibia is also enlarged, especially at the back and inner side. The articular surface is rarefied and eroded. This probably has been a tubercular case turned septic. F. P. C. 801. 7.114.

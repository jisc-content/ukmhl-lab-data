Badly united Fracture of both Bones.-Lower half of a left tibia and fibula—macerated, showing the above. The patient was an old man, who died of malignant disease of the intestine. His leg had been injured by a reaping machine many years before his death, and he had walked without discomfort, though probably with a limp. The fracture has been at the same level in both bones. The fragments have over-ridden to a great extent.

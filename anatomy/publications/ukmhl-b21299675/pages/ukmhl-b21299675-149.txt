The most common appearance of dis- ease in the oesophagus, is that of an ulcer in its cavity. Ulcers of the oesophagus are sometimes of a common nature, but most frequently they arc attended with a scir- rhous affection. When they arise from common inflammation, the structure of the oesophagus immediately surrounding the ulcer is but little thickened, and there is the appearance of the usual erosion in ul- cers. When the ulcer is of a scirrhous nature the oesophagus in the neighbour- hood is very much thickened, and is very hard in its texture.

[ 464 ] When tumours have deen found adher- ing to the dura mater, or the other mem- branes of the brain, a long continued pain in the head has commonly been remarked, somelimes delirium, sometimes convul- sions, and sometimes, it has been said, the ordinary symptoms of apoplexy. Where bony matter has been formed in the dura mater, with sharp processes grow- ing from it, convulsive motions have very commonly occurred in various parts of the body, often a continued pain in the head, and sometimes delirium. In cases where the veins of the pia mater have been found turgid with blood, stupor has very frequently occurred, sometimes delirium, and sometimes, it has been said, even apoplexy in its perfect form.

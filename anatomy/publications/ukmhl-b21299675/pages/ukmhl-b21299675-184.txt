This has probably given rise to the opinion, that the air is often contained in the cavity of the belly. I can believe, how- ever, that air may on some occasions be accumulated in the cavity of the belly, but this is very uncommon. A part of the in- testine when very much distended with air may burst, and a portion of the air may escape into the cavity of the belly ; or the small arteries of the peritonaeum may se- crete air in the same manner as it appears to be formed by the inner membrane of the stomach and the intestines.

The inner membrane of the urethra is very liable to be inflamed, particularly at its anterior extremity, and the inflamma- tion occasionally spreads over the whole extent of the canal. This exhibits no ap- pearance different from the inflammation of membranes lining secretory canals which open exteraally. The membrane is much more crowded with small blood vessels than in a natural state, and there is an in- creased secretion of the glands which open upon it. The inflammation is often not con- fined to the inner membrane of the urethra, but spreads into the substance of the cor- pus spongiosum, affecting both its cellular structure and its glands.

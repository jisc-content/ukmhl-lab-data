The glands being increased in size from the inflammation, become sensible to the touch, like very small rounded tubercles. Ulcers in the Urethra. Ulcers are also seen occasionally in lay- ing open the urethra, but these are not fre- quent. This canal when inflamed has little disposition to ulcerate, as happens also to some other canals in the body, as for ex- ample the trachea. Stricture. The most ordinary diseased appearance of the urethra is stricture. This consists in a part of the canal being narrowed, or per- haps altogether obliterated.

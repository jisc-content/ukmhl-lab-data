It may continue for a great many years, and the person seems to be in good health, except for the difficulty of swallowing. The diffi- culty is constant, which will distinguish it from a mere spasmodic contraction of the muscular fibres of the oesophagus. The symptoms which belong to a stric- ture of the oesophagus depending on a scir- rhous thickening and ulcer, distinguish it sufficiently from the other two diseases. The difficulty of swallowing is small at first, and gradually becomes worse, but is con- stant.

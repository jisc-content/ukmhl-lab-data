[6^] Empyema may be distinguished with a good deal of certainty, after inflammation of the pleura or of the lungs, by rigors having taken place, by a remission of the pain, by the cough and difficulty of breath- ing continuing, and by the person being able to lie more easily upon the diseased side than the other. There is sometimes a very evident enlargement of the side where the matter is accumulated. When water is accumulated in the chest, it can generally be sufficiently distinguished in practice by the following symptoms. There is great difficulty of breathing, and commonly the patient cannot rest in bed unless the head and the upper part of the trunk be more or less elevated from the horizontal posture.

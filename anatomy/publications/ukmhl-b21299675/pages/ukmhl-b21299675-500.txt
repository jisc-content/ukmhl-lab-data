Blood effused or extravasaled. Blood is frequently found eflPused within the cavity of the cranium in various situa- tions. It may cither be poured out by the rupture of some vessel in the substance of the brain itself, or into some of the ven- tricles. It is frequently effused upon the surface of the brain, or upon some of its membranes. This is most apt to happen where the effusion is in consequence of ex- ternal violence. The quantity of blood which is effused from the rupture of vessels in the brain is frequently very considerable. It is com- monly found in a coagulated state, and the texture of the brain in the neighbourhood is often very much hurt from the pressure. Blood is not equally liable to be effused into every part of the substance of the brain..

All of these are to be considered as varieties in the na- tural formation, and not as disease. SYMPTOMS. In inflammation of the uterus, there is a sense of pain and tension in the hypogastric region, and the pain is increased upon pres- sure there, or upon touching the os uteri. The stomach is affected with vomiting, and the bowels are sometimes costive, and some- times disposed to looseness.

I have also seen, in some instances, small spots of cartilage over the whole sur- face of the spleen. It would appear that ossifications* are sometimes to be found in this cartilage, but in the cases which have come under my own examination, bony matter was not to be observed. The car- tilage into which the coats of the spleen are changed, is in general a good deal softer than that which covers the extremities of bones. This diseased process, it is natural to think, is slow in its progress, can hardly produce any impediment to the functions of the spleen, and is probably not marked by any peculiar feelings to the patient.

The Jiverhas sometimes been said to have been in a state of mortification * This how- ever occurs very rarely, and has never fallen under my own observation. Common Tubercle of the Liver. One of the most common diseases in the liver (and perhaps the most common, except the adhesions, which we have lately de- scribed), is the formation of tubercles in its substance. This disease is hardly ever met with in very young persons, but frequently takes place in persons of middle or advanced age: it is likewise more common in men than women.

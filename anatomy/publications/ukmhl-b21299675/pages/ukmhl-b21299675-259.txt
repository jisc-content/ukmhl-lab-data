There is often difficulty of breathing, which is produced by the accu- mulation of the air pushing up the dia- phragm, and impeding its free motion. There are severe colicky pains, and quanti- ties of air are frequently expelled both upwards and downwards with a loud noise. The bowels are costive, and there is a dif- ficulty in making water, which is probably occasioned by the accumulation of air in the rectum. The symptoms of an inflamed mesentery cannot be separated from those which be- long to inflammation of the peritonaeum generally; and these symptoms have been already described.

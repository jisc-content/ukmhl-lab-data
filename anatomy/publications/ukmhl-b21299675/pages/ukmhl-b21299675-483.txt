Diseased Appearances of the Tunica Arach^ noides. Diseased appearances of structure are very rare in the turiica arachnoides. and have almost been entirely overlooked by writers. The only diseased appearance of this coat which I have observed, is that of its becoming a good deal thicker than it is naturally, so as to be a tolerably firm mem- brane. In this, as well as in its natural state, no blood vessels are to be seen rami- fying upon it; or at least they are ex- tremely few. It is also separated at some distance from the pia mater, by a serous fluid being interposed between the one and the other..

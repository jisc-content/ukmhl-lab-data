I have to add, that the womb is not unfrequently ruptured, which is rather to be considered as an accident than a disease. This, perhaps, never takes place but in the pregnant uterus, and at the time of delivery. It may arise either from too violent an action of the muscular fibres of the uterus upon the child, or upon the hand of an accoucheur, who may for some reason or other have introduced it into its cavity.

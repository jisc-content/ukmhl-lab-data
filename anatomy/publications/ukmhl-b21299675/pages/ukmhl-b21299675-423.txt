It is remarkable, that such masses within the cavity of the uterus commonly do not adhere in any part closely to it, but are connected with it loosely, by the intervention of cellular mem- brane, and small blood vessels, so that they can be very easily peeled off, without in- juring the structure of the uterus. The uterus itself is more or less enlarged ac- cording to the bulk of the mass it contains, but it appears to be perfectly healthy in its structure. Polypus, Polypus forms a very common disease of the uterus, and may take place almost at any period of life; it is more frequent, however, at middle or advanced age, and rarely happens in persons who are young.

[ 76] Air Cells of the Lungs enlargtd. The lungs are sometimes, although I be- lieve very rarely, formed into pretty large cells, so as to resemble somewhat the lungs of an amphibious animal. Of this I have now seen three instances. The enlarge- ment of the cells cannot well be supposed to arise from any other cause, than the air being not allowed the common free egress From the lungs, and therefore accumulating in them. It is not improbable also, that this accumulation may sometimes break down two or three contiguous cells into one, and thereby form a cell of a very large size.

[ 556 ] SYMPTOMS. Inflammation of the coats of the gall- bladder is not known to be distinguished by any peculiar symptoms. They are pro- bably much the same with the symptoms which attend inflammation of the mem- branous covering of the liver. An obliteration either of the hepatic duct or of the ductus communis choledo- chus, must produce a jaundice which is permanent, because it depends upon a cause not liable to change. This -svill be ex- tremely difficult to distinguish, in practice, from jaundice produced by a hardened, or tuberculated state of the liver, for this cause of jaundice may likewise be said to be generally permanent.

The symptoms which attend inflamma- tion of the prostate gland have been little taken notice of by authors. It is reasonable to think, that there will be a sense of pain more or less acute at the neck of the blad- der, with much difficulty in making water, and probably tenesmus. This disease may be distinguished from a scirrhous enlarge- ment of the prostate gland, by its quick progress, and by the pain which is felt in it.

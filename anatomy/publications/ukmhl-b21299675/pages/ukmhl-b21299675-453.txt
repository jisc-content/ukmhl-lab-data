An example of this kind is preserved in Dr. Hunter's collection. Some instances have been recorded, in which no vestige* of an ovarium could be observed on either side. SYMPTOMS. As the ovaria are very seldom inflamed, unless when inflamm-ation has at the same time attacked the uterus; it is not known whether there be any particular symptoms which characterize inflammation of the ovaria. Inflammation of the ovaria cannot at present be distinguished, by its symp- toms, from inflammation of the uterus.

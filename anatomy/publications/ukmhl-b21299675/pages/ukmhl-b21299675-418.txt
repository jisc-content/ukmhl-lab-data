[ 370 ] CHAP. XIX. DISEASED APPEARANCES IN THE FEMALE ORGANS. Inflamm&lion of the Uterus. W HEN the uterus becomes inflamed, it takes place almost always under the same circumstances, viz. very soon after partu- rition. The inflammation is sometimes confined to the uterus itself, or its appen- dages, but the peritonaeum in the neigh- bourhood is most commonly affected, and frequently over its whole extent. The uterus when inflamed, exhibits the same appearances as the inflammation of the substance of other parts, and these are principally observable in its body or fun- dus.

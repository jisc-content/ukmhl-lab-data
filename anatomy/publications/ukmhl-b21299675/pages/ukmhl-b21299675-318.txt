This state of the spleen is often at length attended with dropsy. There are no peculiar symptoms which characlbrize the formation of hydatids in the spleen. A pain has been remarked to be felt, in such cases, in the left hypochon- drium ; but this also belongs to many other complaints. When a swelling begins in the situation of the spleen, and spreads very slowly into the cavity of the abdomen, be- ing somewhat soft to the feeling, and per- haps giving some obscure sense of fluctua- tion, the disease may then be reasonably supposed to depend on the formation of hydatids in this organ.

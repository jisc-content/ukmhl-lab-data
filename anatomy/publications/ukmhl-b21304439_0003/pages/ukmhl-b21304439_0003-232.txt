They send many Branches to the Muscles and other parts about the Anus and Perinccum, and then pass for- wards to supply the different parts of the Penis. On the Penis, the Nerves follow the course of the Arteries ; the Superior Fasciculus constituting the Ner- vus Dorsalis, and the Inferior giving Branches to the under part of that Organ. The Nervus Dorsalis, which is the most considerable Nerve of the Penis, runs forwards between the corre- sponding Artery and Vena Magna, expanding into many Branches, which, after supplying the Corpus Caverno- sum and Teguments of the corresponding side, termi- nate in the Substance of the Gians.

The form* of the distended bladder is oval, and its long axis, if produced, would pass superiorly through the umbilicus, and inferiorly through the end of the coccyx. The axis of a child's bladder is more vertical than in the adult; for in children, the bladder is not a pelvic viscus. It is this which makes lithotomy so much more difficult in their case.

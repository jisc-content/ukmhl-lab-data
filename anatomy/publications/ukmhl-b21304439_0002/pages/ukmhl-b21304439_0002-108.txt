The Porlio Mollis is the Primary Part of the Organ of Hearing, to which all the other parts are subservient, and may be regarded as being of the same service to the Ear, as the Iiclina is to the Eye. Sound is conveyed to the Portio Mollis, by the Ex- ternal Ear, by the small chain of Bones in the Tympa- num, by the Membrana Tympani Secundarii, by the walls of the Tympanum and Labyrinth, by the Bones of* the Head in general, and by the Aqua Labyrinthi, which communicates the tremor directly to the pulpy Substance of the Nerve.

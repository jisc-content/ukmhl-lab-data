It is larger in the Ape and other Quadrupeds than in the Human Species, and still larger in Birds, in which, as well as in Quadrupeds, it is called Membrana Nicti- tans. It is in form of a Crescent, the Horns of which are turned towai'ds the Puncta Lacrymalia, and assists the Caruncula in conducting the Tears to the Puncta. BALL OF THE EYE. The Ball, Globe, or Bulb of the Eye, is of a sphe- rical form, to collect the rays of light into a proper Focus, and is surrounded behind by a quantity of soft Fat, to allow the Eye and its Muscles to move with fa- cility. It is composed of Coats, Humours, Vessels, and Nerves, to be next described. Coats. Tunica Adnata. The Tunica Adnata, or Conjunctiva, named from its connecting the Eye to the Orbit, is a reflection of the Skin continued from the Eye-lids over the whole fore part of the Ball of the Eye.

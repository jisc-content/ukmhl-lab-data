The organs of generation are more per- fect, and the modes of copulation exceed- ingly various. The males and females are in some instances so unlike, that they could scarcely be supposed to be of the same spe- cies. Several only copulate once, and the act is soon followed by death. The in- crease of the pregnant female is in many instances extraordinary; for instance, the abdomen of the white ant, when ready for laying, is calculated to be two thousand times longer than it was before impregna- tion.

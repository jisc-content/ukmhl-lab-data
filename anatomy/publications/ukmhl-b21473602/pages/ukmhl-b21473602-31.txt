Dissection, or cutting up, is indeed a principal method in the pursuit of these investigations; but maceration, de- siccation, injection, and other means are also frequently employed. It has been justly observed, that a minute history of Anatomy, by which anatomical courses have been ordinarily commenced, presupposes a knowledge of the science in its perfect state, and is therefore misplaced in an address , to those who come to learn its rudiments.

Such, Gentlemen, is the general outline of the history of anatomy, which I have thought it necessary here to offer you; and I trust, hereafter, that the names of many who now hear me, will be classed by future lecturers with those I have mentioned. In examining the whole of the animal kingdom, we find a gradation from the most simple structure of the common polypus, to the extremely intricate formation of the human body.

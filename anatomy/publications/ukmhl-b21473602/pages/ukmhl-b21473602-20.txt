Another great evil existing in the present schools is that of allowing each pupil to dissect what part he pleases, and of not keeping him to any regular plan; on which account much time is often spent in minute examinations of parts really of little practi- cal utility, but of which a neat and pretty exhibition can be made. It is perfectly right that dissections should be performed with neatness and attention; but as ex- treme minuteness is not necessary for prac- tical purposes, the student may employ his time better than upon dissection for such exhibitions.

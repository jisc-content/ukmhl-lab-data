For in- stance, the brain of the Canary bird is equal in size to one fifth or one fourth of its whole body; while in man, it is not more than one twenty-fifth: but in point of struc- ture there is still a great difference. The organs of sight have an apparatus connected to the choroid coat, and project- ing into the interior of the eyes, a structure which is peculiar to them. It has been supposed to assist in altering the position of the crystalline lens, for the purpose of adapting the organ to distinguish either near or distant objects.

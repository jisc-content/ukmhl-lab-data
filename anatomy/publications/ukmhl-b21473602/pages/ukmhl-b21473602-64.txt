The organs of smell are contained in the head, and consist of cavities lined with membrane, but not communicating with the fauces, or other cavities of the part; the ex- ternal opening of these organs can be closed at pleasure by a kind of sphincter muscle. Organs of taste cannot be very perfect in fish, as their tongue does not exhibit the usual character and appearances which are found in the tongues of those animals which we know to possess this sense.

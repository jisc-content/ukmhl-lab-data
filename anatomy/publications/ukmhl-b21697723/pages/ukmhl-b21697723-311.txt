Tuberculosis of Ovaries. Gen.-U. Q. e. Gen.-U. Q. e. 1 (1825a). Tubercular Disease of the Ovaries, together with peritoneo-rectal fistula. The uterus is enlarged and filled with cheesy material, apparently tubercular in nature. The lumina of the Fallopian tubes are obliterated. Both ovaries are tubercular, caseous, and partially calcified, a large quill being introduced into the fistulous communication passing from the peritoneal cavity near the left ovary to the rectum, into which it opened about 2J inches above the anus. Described by Dr John Cleland in the Edin. Med. Jour, for July i860.

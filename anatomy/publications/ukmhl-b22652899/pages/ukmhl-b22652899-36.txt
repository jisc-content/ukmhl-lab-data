If we describe the hand as that extremity which has the thumb and fingers opposed to each other, so as to form an instrument of pre- hension, we extend it to the quadrumana or monkeys. But the possession of four hands by animals of that class implies that we include the posterior as well as the anterior extremities. Now the anterior extremity of the monkey is as much a foot as the posterior extremity is a hand ; both are calculated for their mode of progression, climbing, and leaping from the branches of trees,

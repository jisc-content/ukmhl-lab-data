Changes in the lens with age. The form of the lens is nearly spherical in the foetus; but its convexity decreases with age, particularly on the anterior surface, until it becomes flattened in the adult. In the foetus it is soft, rather reddish in colour, and not quite transparent; in mature age it is firm and clear ; and in old age it becomes flatter on both sur- faces, denser, and of a yellowish colour. * A representation of the laminae in a hardened lens. a.

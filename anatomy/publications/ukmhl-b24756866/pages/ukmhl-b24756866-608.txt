602 Organs of the Senses. The Organs of the Senses are tlie instruments by which the mind is brought into relation with external objects. These organs are five in number, viz., the organs of touch, of smell, of taste, of hearing, and of sight. The Skin. The skin is the principal seat of the sense of touch, and may be regarded as a covering for the protection of the deeper tissues; it is also an important excretory and absorbing organ. It consists of two layers, the derma or cutis vera, and the epidermis or cuticle.

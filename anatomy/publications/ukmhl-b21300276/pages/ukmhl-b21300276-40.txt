At the fourteenth or fif- teenth year, the signs of puberty appear; and in both sexes, gi-eat physical and mental alterations take place; the body changes its form, new functions begin, and the mind evinces more energy and power. About the twenty-first year, the body usually attains its full stature: the bones are fully ossified, the joints are strong, and the muscles firm and powerful. In fact, at this period of life, all the organs perform their offices with the greatest vigour. At the twenty-eighth year, the denies sapientiae appear, and the body, after that period, is complete in all its parts.

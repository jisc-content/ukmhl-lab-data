They seem to shew, that the temperature of the body is a little higher in the warm weather of summer than in the moderate degree of cold of our winter ; the heart perhaps being an exception. The subject demands further inquiry, especially as regards the effects of extreme cold.—I have brought forward such observations as I have made, with the hope that they may lead to others ; and I have given them, miscellaneous as they are, believing that they may have some little value in connexion with the theory of animal heat generally, which, to be true and per- manent, must rest on a very extensive series of facts —or what is identical, be an induction from them.

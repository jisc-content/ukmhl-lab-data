The effect of cooling influences on the brain is shewn in a very decided manner in the instance of the brain of man after death. Excepting the hands and feet, the brain appears to lose its heat more rapidly than any other part of the body. Some precise observations on the temperature of the brain after death, compared with that of other parts, warrant this remark: they will be given in detail in the sequel.

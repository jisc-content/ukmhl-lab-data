SERIES LIV. » CONCRETIONS FROM THE CIRCULATORY, AND OTHER ORGANS. —♦— 300. A Collection of Phebolithes, or Calculi from Veins. Most of them are spherical; some are oval; they vary from half a line to half an inch in diameter: and some are laminated. They consist chiefly of Phosphate of Lime. 301. Twelve small round Calculi from a Spleen; formed probably in its Veins. 302. Two small earthy Concretions discharged by coughing. Vide No. 3382, Series L.

Divide each spinal nerve trunk midway between the point of fusion of its two roots and the point of division into an anterior and a posterior ramus. Cut through the cord at the level of the first cervical nerve. Lift the cord and its membranes gently from the vertebral canal and transfer to formalin for subsequent study. THE SPINAL CORD. Blood-vessels of the Spinal Cord. As a rule, special injections are necessary to demonstrate the blood-vessels of the cord well, but a good deal can be made out in ordinary cadavers.

The muscles of the face. (After Toldt, Anat. Atlas, Wien, 1900, 2 Aufl., p. 296, Fig. 537.) Constrictors. (a) Procerus (“prolonged”) muscle {M. procerus) (0. T. pyramid- alis nasi). This muscle is wrongly classed as a part of the M. frontalis. Comparative anatomy has shown it to have been derived from the muscle which lifts the lip. Note that the M. procerus is an antagonist of the M. frontalis. (b) Orbicular muscle of eye {M. orbicularis oculi) (0. T. orbicularis palpebrarum). (ba) Palpebral part {pars palpebralis).

POSTERIOR REGION OF LEG AND HEEL {REGIO CRURIS POSTERIOR ET REGIO CALCANEA) (0. T. POSTERIOR TIBIOFIBULAR REGION). Note how the leg tapers from above downward. The large, convex, fleshy upper half is known as the calf {sura). The lower half rapidly diminishes in size, so that the tibia and fibula may be felt as they pass into their respective malleoli. Behind each malleolus will be found a fossa. Flex the foot and extend it and note the play of tendons in each fossa. These fossae become obliterated in pathological processes involving the synovial mem- brane of the ankle-joint.

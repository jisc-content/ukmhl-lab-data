In an eyeball no longer fresh, one that has been kept for from one to four days, divide the tunicae of the eye at the equator, reflect the cut edges backward and forward, and, as Anderson Stuart has suggested, allow the eye-kerneP^ (corpus vitreum + lens crystallina) to slip out into a vessel filled with clean water. Transfer it for a few minutes to a strong solution of picrocarmin, then wash in water. The membrana hyaloidea, capsula lentis, and zonula ciliaris [Zinni] will be stained red, and their connections are easily visible.

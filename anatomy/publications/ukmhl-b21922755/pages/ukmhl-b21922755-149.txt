There is also a great variety of sensations produced, not by external objects, but by the general affections of our nature, and our power of distinguishing between right and wrong. We receive a sensation of pleasure from the ob- servation of virtuous conduct, and a sensation of pain from the observation of the contrary. We are conscious of the various operations of our own minds, which may be said to pro- duce another class of sensations. Both of these form very interesting and extended subjects of investigation, which it is impos- sible at present to consider.

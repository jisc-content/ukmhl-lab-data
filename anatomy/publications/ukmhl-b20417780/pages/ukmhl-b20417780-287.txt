VEINS AT THE ELBOW. 273 The external cutaneous branches of the musculo-spiral nerve are two in number : the upper and smaller accompanies the cephalic vein in the lower half of the arm; the lower may be traced down the outer and back part of the forearm nearly as far as the wrist, where it joins the musculo-cutaneous nerve. On the outer side of the tendon of the biceps, the cutaneous branch of the musculo-cutaneous nerve perforates the fascia, and divides into many filaments, which supply the skin of the outer part of the fore- arm.

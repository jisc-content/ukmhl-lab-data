It is the largest nerve in the body. From its origin it passes outward through the great sacrosciatic foramen below the pyriformis muscle into the gluteal region ; for further course, see page 610. The sacral g-angliated cord is the continuation of the lumbar cord from behind the iliac vessels to the central ganglion (impar) over the front of the coccyx ; there are four small ganglia usually in the course of the cord, but may be fewer.

326 A MANUAL OF ANATOMY. The Innominate Veins. Figs. 68, 69, 70. Each is formed by the internal jugular and subclavian veins behind the sternoclavicular articulation. The right is only an inch long, and vertical in its course : the left, three inches long, and very oblique in its course ; both veins unite in the first intercostal space of the right side close to the sternum and form the superior vena cava.

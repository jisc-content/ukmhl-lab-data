Its average length in the people of the British Islands is a little more than 7 inches; its greatest breadth about 5| inches; and its height, from the plane of the foramen magnum to the vertex, about inches. Its greatest circumference is about 21 inches. The breadth of the face across the zygomatic arches is about 5 inches.^ The average capacity of the brain cavity is 92 cubic inches. The British skull is dolico-cephalic and orthognathic. The lateral regions of the skull are called the temporal fossa, and give origin to the temporal muscles. Under cover of each zygomatic arch is the zygomatic fossa.

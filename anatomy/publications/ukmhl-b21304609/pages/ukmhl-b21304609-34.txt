Into the dissecting-room he must bring his imagina- tion and judgment ever awake;—he must in the dead structure presented to him by their means, read its appropriate function; —he must call to mind the diseases and accidents to which the parts are liable which lie before him;—he must consider and examine their appropriate treatment. —Thus he shall learn to know the value and meaning of the human structure.

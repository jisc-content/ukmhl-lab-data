C 275 3 State of the Kidneys in Diabetes. Opportunities do not frequently occur of examining the state of the kidneys in dia- betes. I have once, however, been able to make this examination in a satisfactory manner, where a person had been long affected with diabetes, and had been a pa- tient under my care at St. George's Hos- pital. In both kidneys the superficial veins were much fuller of blood than usual, form- ing upon their surface a most beautiful network of vessels. The whole substance of the kidneys was much more vascular than in a healthy state, approaching a good deal in appearance to what takes place in inflammation. In both of them there was a very small quantity of a whitish fluid, somewhat resembling pus ; but there was no appearance of ulceration whatever.

Its blood vessels are enlarged in their size, and the number of branches which are capable of containing the red globules of blood is increased. Hence it appears much more vascular than in a natural state. It is increased in its bulk, and feels considerably firmer to the touch than when healthy; and these effects are produced partly by the increased quantity of blood which is circulating through it, and partly by the extravasation of coagulable lymph, and per- haps ot blood, into its substance.

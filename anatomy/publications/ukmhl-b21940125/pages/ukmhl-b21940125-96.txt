There are also interspersed through it a great number of very small vessels containing florid blood, and a layer of coagulable lymph, is at the same time thrown out upon its surface. This layer is sometimes very thin, and at other times of considerable thickness. It is either smooth upon its surface, or it throws out many fine small flocculi, which exhibit the appearance of a rich lace-work. There is also poured into the cavity of the thorax a se- rous fluid, in which are floating many small broken laminae of the coagulable lymph ; and there is occasionally some mixture of pus.

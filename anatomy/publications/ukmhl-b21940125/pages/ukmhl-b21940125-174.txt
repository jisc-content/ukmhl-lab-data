This membrane con- sists of a cellular substance, similar to the general cellular membrane of the body, and has a moderate share of vascularity. It does not naturally shew many vessels large enough to admit the red globules of the blood, but it shews its vascularity upon slight degrees of inflammation, or when its vessels have been filled with the fine in- jection. This membrane is capable of elongating gradually by the motion of the viscera upon themselves, so as ultimately to be attended with very little inconve- nience. I have very often had an oppor- tunity of observing these adhesions, either joining all the viscera of the abdomen more or less together, or joining some particular viscera to each other.

It very seldom in this state shews, in any part, the real schirrous structure. But I have seen this to be the case, which renders it very probable, that the one is the beginning of a change into the other. When the pan- creas in any part assumes the schirrous structure, that part loses entirely its natural appearance, and is converted into a hard, uniform, white mass, somewhat intersected by membrane, like schirrus in other parts of the body. In some cases it has been observed, in this state, to be considerably enlarged beyond its natural size. Calculi of the Pancreas. Calculi are occasionally formed in the ducts of the pancreas.

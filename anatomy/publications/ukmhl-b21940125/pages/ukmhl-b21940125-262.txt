C *16 3 Large white Tubercle of the Liver. Hard white masses are sometimes formed in the liver, of a considerable size. They are often as large as a chesnut; but I have seen them both a good deal larger and smaller than this size. They are to be found near the surface of the liver in greater number, than near the middle of its substance: two r or three frequently lie contiguous to each other, with a considerable portion of the liver, in a healthy state, interposed between them and a cluster of similar tubercles. They consist of a very firm, opaque, white substance, and are generally somewhat de- pressed, or hollow, upon their outer sur- face. The liver in this disease is frequently a good deal enlarged beyond its natural size.

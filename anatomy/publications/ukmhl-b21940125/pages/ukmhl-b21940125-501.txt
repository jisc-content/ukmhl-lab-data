The symptoms are, pain in the head, delirium, symptomatic fever, and sometimes convulsive motions. When tumours have been found adher- ing to the dura mater, or the other mem- branes of the brain, a long continued pain in the head has commonly been remarked, sometimes delirium, sometimes convulsions, and sometimes, it has been said, the ordi- nary symptoms of apoplexy. Where bony matter has been formed in the dura mater, with sharp processes grow- ing from it, convulsive motions have very commonly occurred in various parts of the body, often a continued pain in the head, and sometimes delirium.

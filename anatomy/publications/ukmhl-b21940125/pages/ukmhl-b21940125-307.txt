The human spleen has even been removed in a few instances, and the persons have not only recovered, but have enjoyed afterwards good health. It does not appear, therefore, very wonder- ful that a body should be formed without a spleen, and carry on its vital functions without any obvious imperfection. SYMPTOMS. When inflammation attacks the coats of the spleen, it is attended with the same symptoms as an inflammation of that por- tion of the peritonaeum, which lies in the left hypochondrium.

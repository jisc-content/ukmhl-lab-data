The rectum is affected from its connection with the bladder, and is excited to tenesmus. The stomach like- wise takes a part in the disease, being af- fected with sickness and vomiting. In some cases there is delirium. When pus has been formed in consequence of the inflammation, it is known by being mixed with the urine which is evacuated. When the bladder has become affected by an ulcer spreading to it from the neigh- bouring parts, it may be suspected by the pain and difficulty which occur in making water.

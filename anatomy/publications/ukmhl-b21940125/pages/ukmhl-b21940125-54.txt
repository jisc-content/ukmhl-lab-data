C 8 3 Adhesions of the Pericardium to the Heart. In opening dead bodies, adhesions of the pericardium to the heart, are not uncom- monly found. The adhesion is sometimes at different spots ; at other times is extend- ed over the whole surface. It either con- sists of a thin membrane, or of a more solid matter. When it is a thin membrane, it re- sembles exactly the common cellular mem- brane of the body; and when the matter is solid, it differs little from the coagulable lymph of the blood. Whether the adhesion be in the one way or the other, the matter of the adhesion is in both cases capable of being Tendered vascular by injection. The adhesion too, is in both cases formed from the pulpy matter formerly explained, for I have oftener than once had an oppor- tunity of tracing its gradual changes into each.

When cut into, it shews precisely the same structure with the tubercle of the uterus just describ- ed ; so that a person looking upon a sec- tion of the one and the other, out of the body, could not at all distinguish between them. This sort of polypus varies very much in its size, some being not larger than a walnut, and others being larger than a child's head. It adheres by a nar- rower portion or neck, which varies a great deal in its size, and in its proportion to the body of the polypus. The largest polypus I ever saw was suspended by a neck hardly thicker than the thumb; and I have seen a polypus, much less than the fist, adher- ing by a neck fully as thick as the wrist. The place of adhesion also differs consi- derably.

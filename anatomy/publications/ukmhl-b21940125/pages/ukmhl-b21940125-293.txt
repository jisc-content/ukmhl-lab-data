Such an effect may be supposed to have depended upon a very imperfect action of the secretory structure of the liver. The liver was not sound, being studded with scrofulous tubercles, and the absorbent glands of the mesentery were affected with the same disease. In opening dead bodies, the bile is almost always found to have transuded in small quantity through the coats of the gall- bladder, so as to tinge the neighbouring parts, especially the small end of the sto- mach, and the beginning of the duodenum. This is to be considered as a natural effect, which has taken place after death, and not as a diseased appearance.

The uterus varies a good deal in its size in different persons, in some being fully twice as large as it is in others. It differs also somewhat in the thickness of its sub- stance. There is some difference too in its situation, being often placed much nearer one side of the pelvis than the other. All of these are to be considered as varieties in the natural formation, and not as disease. SYMPTOMS.

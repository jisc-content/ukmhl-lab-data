When the scalp is removed, so as to give an opportunity of looking immediately upon the cranium, the bones are found to be very thin, often not thicker than a shilling, and there are frequently broad spots of mem- brane in the bone. The reason of this last appearance is, that ossification takes place in many points of the membrane in such cases in order to make a quicker progress, but the water accumulates too rapidly for it, so that spots of membrane are left not converted into bone. When such appear- ances take place in hydrocephalus, the dis- ease has been of long continuance, occa- sionally for some years. Water upon the Surface of the Brain, and between its Membranes.

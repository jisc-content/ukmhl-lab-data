C 400 3 CHAP. XXI. DISEASED APPEARANCES OF THE FALLOPIAN TUBES. I Inflammation of the Fallopian Tubes. WHEN the uterus is inflamed to a con- siderable degree, the inflammation often spreads along the Fallopian tubes: they become, in this case, highly vascular, and when cut open, sometimes contain blood in their cavities. The inflammation may even advance to suppuration, and their ca- vities will be found loaded with pus. Adhesions.

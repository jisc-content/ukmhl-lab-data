Intus-susceptio is attended with no pecu- liar feeling, as far as I know, till inflamma- tion begins to take place, and then the symptoms correspond exactly with those of inflammation of the intestines. The parts where the intus-susceptio has been formed, are glued together by the coagulable lymph which is extravasated during the inflamma- tion, and cannot be reduced again to their natural situation. This, therefore, is to be considered as a much more fatal disease, than simple inflammation of the intestines. Simple inflammation may be removed, and the patient perfectly recover, but intus-sus- ceptio attended with violent inflammation, must prove certainly fatal.

Abscesses of the Kidneys. When the substance of the kidneys is in- flamed, it frequently advances to suppura- tion, and perhaps there is no considerable gland in the body so liable to form abscesses as the kidneys. In some cases which I have seen, the abscesses have appeared to be of a common nature ; but in the greater number of cases they have been scrofulous. When a kidney is attacked with scrofula, and the disease has advanced to suppuration, it exhibits different appearances, according to the degree of the advancement. Some- times there are only one or two circum- scribed abscesses, containing a curdly pus, without any thing being particularly ob- servable in the inner surface of the absces- ses.

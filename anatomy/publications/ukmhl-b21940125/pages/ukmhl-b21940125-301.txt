The spleen is sometimes much harder than natural, and at the same time is ge- nerally a good deal enlarged. It will oc- casionally be enlarged to five or six times its natural size, and it then forms a tumour, very capable of being distinguished by an examination in the living body. When cut into, the natural structure seems to be preserved, except that it is much more compact, the parts being much more close- ly applied together.

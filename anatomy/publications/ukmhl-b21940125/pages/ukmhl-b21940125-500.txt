There is also frequently, instead of this excrescence, a bag growing from the skin of the scalp, and covering more or less the back of the trunk. This bag sometimes consists of a fine membrane, with little strength ; and sometimes it is pretty thick, and has very considerable firmness. It sometimes communicates with the cavity of the cranium by a considerable opening ; and sometimes the communication is Very small. It is filled with an aqueous fluid, and in some instances there is also in it a portion of the brain. SYMPTOMS. Inflammation of the dura mater is not distinguished by any peculiar symptoms.

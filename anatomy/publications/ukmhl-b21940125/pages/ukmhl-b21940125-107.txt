This change of colour is pro- duced by the veinal blood not passing rea- dily through the lungs, which are prevented from expanding themselves sufficiently for this purpose, by the accumulation of the water. When ossification of the pleura is of small extent, respiration cannot be affect- ed by it ; but when it is large it must pro- duce difficulty of breathing, either by pre- venting the full expansion of the lungs, or the free motion of the ribs, according to its situation.

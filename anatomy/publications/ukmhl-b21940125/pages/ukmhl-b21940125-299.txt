It is more common, however, to find ad- hesions formed between the spleen and the neighbouring parts, than to find its coats in an actual state of inflammation. These adhesions consist of a white transparent membrane of more or less firmness, and generally connect the broad surface of the spleen, more or less closely to the dia- phragm. They often connect too the spleen to the great end of the stomach, and a part of the transverse arch of the colon. Inflammation of the Substance of the Spleen.

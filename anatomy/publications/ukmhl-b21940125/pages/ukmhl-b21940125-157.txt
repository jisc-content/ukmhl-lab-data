Inflammation of the thyroid gland may be known by an increase of its size and firmness, by a pain felt in it, which is in- creased upon pressure, and which is pro- bably also increased during the act of swal- lowing. It may he distinguished from some other swellings of this gland, as for instance from bronchocele, by its rapid progress, by the feeling of pain, and by its not increasing to a very large size. It is worth while to remark, that practi- tioners ought to be particularly cautious to prevent inflammation of the thyroid gland from advancing to suppuration. If it should suppurate, and the pus be evacuated exter- nally, there will be a scar in the neck ; and if it should point internally, it will probably make its way into the cavity of the larynx or the trachea, and suffocate the patient.

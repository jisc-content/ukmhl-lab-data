The brain has sometimes been found # more firm and elastic than is natural in cases of mania. I have been informed, however, lately, from the best authority, that this state of brain is not common in maniacs; and that in them it is generally not more firm, nor more elastic, than in people whose minds have always been sound. The symptoms which have been obser- ved to attend the formation of solid, or encysted tumours in the brain, are a per- manent uneasiness or pain in the head, sometimes delirium, sometimes convul- sions, and sometimes, it has been said, the common symptoms of apoplexy.

Polypus. 1 his has been considered by the older anatomists, as a very common and a very fatal disease. By many of the moderns it has been rejected as a disease altogether. It consists in a mass of the coagulable lymph filling up some of the large cavities of the heart, particularly the ventricles, and ex- tending into the neighbouring large vessels. The coagulable lymph is of a yellowish white colour, sometimes of a veryyellowr co- lour, and has considerable firmness. It fills up the cavity completely, or nearly so, in which it is found ; and in the ventricles it shoots out processes between the fasciculi of the muscular fibres.

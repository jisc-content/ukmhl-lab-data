Inflammation of the substance of the lungs, I believe, seldom takes place without some similar affection of the pleura ; at least in the instances which I have seen, this has been most frequently the case. When a portion of the lungs is inflamed, its spungy structure appears much redder than usual, the colour being partly florid and partly of a darker hue. This arises from a much greater number of small vessels than usual, being distributed upon the cells of the lungs, which are capable of admitting the red globules of the blood.

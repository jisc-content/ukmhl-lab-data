LECTURE. The duty I am honoured with, in this Institution, is that of making known the construction and admirable modes of action of the parts com- posing the human body. The importance of our subject can scarcely be over-rated, however it may be misunderstood: it is in itself so excel- lent, so universal in its application to philoso- phical and rational medicine, so beneficial to humanity, and admirably suited for displaying the infinitely beneficent wisdom of the Creator, as to be justly entitled to our most profound attention, and worthy of our noblest intellectual energies.

14 Frozen Sections of a Child. The serratus magnus is well shown beneath the scapula, which it holds firmly in place. It is supplied by the long thoracic nerve. Its paralysis allows the posterior border of the scapula to fall outward -—“angel palsy.” A somewhat similar result follows paralysis of the trapezius, but the eversion of the shoulder is less marked and its fall- ing greater. The supra-scapular artery is seen on the left, passing over the upper border of the scapula.

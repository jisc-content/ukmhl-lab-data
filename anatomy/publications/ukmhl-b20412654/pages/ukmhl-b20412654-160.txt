148 INTERNAL ILIAC ARTERY. Short thick trunk which extends from bifurcation of common iliao artery opposite sacro- vertebral articulation (some Authors say opposite sacro-iliac synchondrosis) to tipper border of great sacro-sciatic foramen, near which it divides into anterior & posterior divisions. Usually inches long, but its length may vary from | an inch to 3 inches. Rather smaller in adult than the external iliac. Twice as large in foetus: - Under the name of hypogastric artery, it then continues the common iliac along side of bladder, and ascends to umbilicus where it becomes one of the umbilical arteries. RELATIONS: In Fbont - Peritoneum, ureter. Behind - Pyriformis, internal iliac vein, lumbo-sacral cord. On Outer Side neae Oeigin - Psoas & external iliac vein. BRANCHES — Are given off as follows, from: - ANT. DIVISION — Sup. Middle & Inf. Vesical, Middle Hemor- rhoidal, and in female, uterine & Vaginal; Obturator, Sciatic, Pudie. POST. DIVISION - Ilio-Lumbar, Lateral Sacral, Gluteal.

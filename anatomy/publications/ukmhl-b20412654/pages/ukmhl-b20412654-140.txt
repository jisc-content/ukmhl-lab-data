128 COMMON ILIAC ARTERY. Downwards & outwards from bifurcation of aorta a little to the left of 4th lumbar vertebra to opposite sacro-vertebral articulation (some Authors say opposite sacro-iliao synchondrosis), where it divides into external & internal iliacs. Is about two inches long, the right artery being slightly the longer. RELATIONS Right Side: In Front - Small intestine, peritoneum, sympathetic nerve, ureter near bifurcation Behind - Both common iliao veins. Outer Side - Bight common iliao vein, inferior vena cava, psoas muscle. Left Side: In Front - Same, plus rectum & superior hemorrhoidal artery. Outer Side - Psoas. Left Common Iliac Vein - Lies at first on inner side of its artery, and then passes beneath artery of right side.

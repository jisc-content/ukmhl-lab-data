This he has accomplished by patient per- severance, by unexampled labour, in dissect- ing, I may say, thousands of animals, in order, by comparison, to throw light on the organi- zation and functions of the human body; and after deep reflection and meditation, he has presented us with the result of his labours. No metaphysical opinions are to be found in his works; they belong to a different class of philosophers—his object was facts.

1 PREFACE TO PART THE THIRD. The difficulty often experienced by students in gaining a know- ledge of the position and relations of the viscera to each other, and to the skeleton, has suggested the plan of introducing these in outline. A knowledge, moreover, of the landmarks and guides to the organs within, rightly shares an important place in medical examinations at the present day. In this endeavour it- must be obvious that perfect accuracy cannot be attained ; allowance must be made for the varying size and shape of the viscera. The bony framework, too, not only presents special individual differences, but is subject to alterations impressed upon it by habits of life, dress, and occupation, as well as to the changes resulting from the respiratory process.

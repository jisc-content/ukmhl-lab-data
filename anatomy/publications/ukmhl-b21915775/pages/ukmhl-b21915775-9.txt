\MWt This Introduction to Human Anatomy was prepared as the Article “Anatomy” for the Ninth Edition of the “Encyclopaedia Britannica,” now in course of publication. It has no pretence to he an exhaustive treatise on the subject, and in writing it, my object has been to give an exposition of the principles on which the human body is constructed, rather than to put before the reader a detailed description of the several organs. At the suggestion of the publishers it has been reproduced in a separate volume. The illustrations, except when otherwise stated in the text, are original. The figures of the dissected parts are from drawings by Dr W. M. Banks and Dr J. Halliday Scott. The great majority of the illustrations of the microscopic objects have been drawn on the wood, from original material and specimens, by Drs J. C. Ewart and J. Halliday Scott, and by Mr C.

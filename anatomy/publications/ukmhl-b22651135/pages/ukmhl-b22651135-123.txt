The blood is thus more disposed to move from the circumference to the centre than in the opposite direction, were there even no valves to oppose its motion ; whence that it may leave its source and enter more confined and colder channels, and flow against the direction to which it spontaneously inclines, the blood requires both force and an impelling power. Now such is the heart and the heart alone, and that in the way and manner already explained.

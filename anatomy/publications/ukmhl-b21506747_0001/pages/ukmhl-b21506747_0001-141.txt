57 POSTERIOR ANNULAR LIGAMENT. The Posterior carpal branch (Fig. 22, 17) of the ulnar artery will he found appearing beneath the extensor carpi ulnaris, and hav- ing completed the arch formed by the posterior carpal of the radial, it ends in a branch to the ulnar side of the fifth metacarpal bone. The Posterior Annular Li- gament (Fig. 19, 15) is attached to the radius externally, and to the cuneiform and pisiform bones internally. It has six subdivi- sions, which should be carefully examined and compared with the grooves on the bones.

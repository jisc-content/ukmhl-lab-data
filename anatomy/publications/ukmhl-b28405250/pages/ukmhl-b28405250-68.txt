28 The arterial system consists- of the aorta, its branches, and their subdivisions. 1. The aorta, before it emerges from the pericardium, gives oft’ a. Arteria cordis coronaria dextra. b. Arteria cordis coronaria sinistra. 2. The aorta gives off. at the upper part of its arch a. Arteria innominata—which is the trunk of the common carotid and subclavian of the right side. b. Carotis sinistra communis. c. Subclavia sinistra. 3. The common carotid artery divides into a. Carotis externa. b. Carotis interna. 4. The external carotid gives off’ eight branches. a. Thyroidea superior. 1. Ramus ascendens. 2. Ramus descendens. 3. Ramus laryngeus. 4. Ramus thyroideus. b. Lingualis. 1. Rami inusculares. 2. Dorsalis linguaj. • 3. Sublingualis. 4. Ranina. c. Maxillaris externa. Between external carotid and inferior edge of lower jaw.

OBSERVATIONS ON SECTION II. OF PART II. Amongst the Preparations of diseased Joints, are several good specimens of the soft and highly-vascular Membrane, which constitutes the agent by which the Articular Carti- lage is absorbed. 1329* is, perhaps, the most characteristic specimen of this kind. 1369 is a specimen of Muscle, in Avhich complete fatty degeneration took place during life. Many other Muscles were similarly affected, in the same subject.

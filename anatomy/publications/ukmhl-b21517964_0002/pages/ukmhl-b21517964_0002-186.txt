He adds, " multo floridior sanguis in pulmonibus, et exinde exprimitur, quam in arteriis reperitur." The merit of Lower consists in having associated the colour with its cause, and that not hypothetically; but experimentally, as described by Mayow, and as detailed by himself in his excellent little treatise, De Corde et de Motu et Colore Sanguinis, published in 1669.

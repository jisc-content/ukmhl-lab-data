HEAD AND NECK. 398 Lips.—The structure of the hps has in a great measure been aheady examined in the dissection of the face (p. 277). Each hp may be regarded as being composed of four layers. From before backwards these are—(i) cutaneous; (2) muscular; (3) glandular; and (4) mucous. The skin and mucous membrane become continuous with each other at Fig. 253.—Mesial section through the nose, mouth, pharynx, and larynx. (From Gegenbaur.) the free margin of the lip. From the deep aspect of each Hp the mucous membrane is reflected on to the alveolar margin of the corresponding jaw, and in the mesial plane it is raised in the form of a free fold.

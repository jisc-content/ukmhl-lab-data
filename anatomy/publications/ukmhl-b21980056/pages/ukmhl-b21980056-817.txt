ORGANIZATION OF THE BLADDER. 803 They vary much in number and size. The largest are situated on the lateral parts of the fundus, and in the vicinity of the neck. They have all a very flexuous course. Its veins, which are much more numerous than its arteries, join the hypogastric venous plexus (2734). Its nerves emanate from the sciatic (1831) and hypogastric (1930) plexuses. Its lymphatic vessels ramify in the hypogastric ganglia (2774).

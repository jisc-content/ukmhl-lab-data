The action of this muscle preserves the equilibrium of the body in rope- dancers, by the aid of a pole. Many of the speculations connected with muscu- lar motion are exceedingly interesting, but it would occupy too much space to dwell longer on this sub- ject ; sufficient has been said, to shew the great in- fluence the muscles must have, not only in the pro- duction and maintenance of deformities, but also the necessity of taking them into consideration in ap- plying our remedial means. A careful study of the various actions of muscles will prevent the useless application of machinery in many cases, and in others will enable us to make use of it to the most advantage.

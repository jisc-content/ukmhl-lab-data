S86 COMPENDIUM OF ANATOMY. [Pakt II. Triceps Adductor Femoris. Under this appellation are comprehended these dis- tinct Muscles, viz. Adductor Longus^ Adductor Brevis, and Adductor Magnus. Adductor Longus, vel Pubio-femoralis, Origin : By a strong roundish Tendon, from the up- per and fore part of the Os Pubis, and Ligament of the Synchondrosis, at the inner side of the Pectinalis: It runs downwards and outwards. Insertion : By a broad flat Tendon, into the middle of the Linea Aspera. Adductor Brevis, vel Suh-pubio-fimoralis. Origin : Tendinous from the Os Pubis, at the side of its Symphysis, below and behind the former Muscle; it runs obliquely outwards. Insertion : By a short flat Tendon, into the ilmer and upper part of the Linea Aspera, from a little below the Trochanter Minor, to the beginning of the Inser- tion of the Adductor Longus. Adductor Magnus^ vel Ischio-^emoralis. Origin : From the side of the Symphysis Pubis, a little lower than the former.

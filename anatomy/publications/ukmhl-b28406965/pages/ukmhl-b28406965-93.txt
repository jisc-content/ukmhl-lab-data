Concerning the first of these suppo- sitions, that muscles may have a per- ceptibility of injury, distinct from that which we understand to be feeling, I have to observe, that we can have no idea of sensation but what results from our own experience, which may be de- fined to be perception attended with con- sciousness; which kind of sensation is confined to the brain alone. Of any other kind of perception, it is evident we can never form any idea. If a man's leg be amputated, and by voltaic electricity I excite contraction in its muscles for some hours, how can I know whether they feel or not?

When also in the prosecution of our anatomical enquiries, we as it were ana- lyze the body, or reduce it to its ele- mentary parts; when we find that every organ, and every portion of it, is com- posed of a few and simple vessels, a few and simple fibres; that by these it is originally formed, kept in constant repair, endowed with animation, sensation, and motion; we become lost in astonishment that such important ends can be effected by apparently such simple means.

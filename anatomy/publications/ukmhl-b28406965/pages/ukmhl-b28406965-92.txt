This subject has been the cause of much controversy. Haller maintained that irritability was a distinct property in- herent in muscles ; to use his own words, that they had a vis insita, independent of the vis nervea; which opinion has of late received additional corroboration from some experiments of Mr. Brodie. Those who object to this opinion, can, I think, only oppose it on the following grounds.

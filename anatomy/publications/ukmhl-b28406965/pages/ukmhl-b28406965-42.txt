The foregoing facts appear to me to shew the impropriety of the phrase, exhausted irritability, which is in common use to express our inability by the effort of our will to continue the actions of our voluntary muscles : it seems manifest that the irritability is not ex- hausted but fatigued. The rigid contraction of' the muscles after death, is the effect of irritability: it is its last act.

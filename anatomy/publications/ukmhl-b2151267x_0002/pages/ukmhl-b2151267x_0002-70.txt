A length of intestine with mesentery, of a child, injected black, distended with plaster of Paris, coiled, and mounted in turpentine. "A portion of the jejunum from the adult human subject; the arteries injected most minutely with quicksilver. After filling them from the mesentery, a general ligature was made on the mesentery, and every set of arteries and veins tied separately just at the end (insertion into the intestine) of the mesentery, otherwise the mercury escaped by one set, as fast as it was injected by the other." 35. 32. The Arteries and Veins of the Intestine. Hunterian. P. 36. A portion of ileum, the veins as well as the arteries injected with mercury, showing the accompanying vein single, but much larger than the artery.

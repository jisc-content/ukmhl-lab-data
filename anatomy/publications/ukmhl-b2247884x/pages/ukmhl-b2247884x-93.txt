The poorer class are destitute of al- most every comfort, inhabit miserable huts, sleep on the hard floor, and live poorly: they are dependent on good seasons even for the sparing food on which they live. If the an- nual rains do not fall, they are ruined. Sixty years since, no fewer than three millions of the people of Bengal died of famine, and the diseases produced by famine. The rice-crops failed, and rice became exceedingly dear : the people fled to the woods, and eagerly devoured the bark of trees*, to escape death from star- vation. * Kennedy on Cholera.

As a general rule, each artery is surrounded by a sheath, formed of connective tissue, which is attached to the outer wall of the artery by slender fibres, and the attachment is so loose, that when the artery is cut across it retracts within its sheath. The wall of the artery consists of several coats, wbich are distinguished from each otber not only by differences in the direction of the fibres of which they are composed, but by structural differences.

THE BONES. 162 THE PHALANGES. The phalanges (Figs. 181-184) resemble so closely those of the fingers that only the differences need be noticed. Those of the great toe are larger than those of the thumb, while those of the other toes are much smaller than those of the corresponding fingers. The shafts of the phalanges of the first row, in the four smaller toes, are narrowed in the middle, being compressed laterally.

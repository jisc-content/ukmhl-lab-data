294 THE MUSCLES. Fig. 335.—Adductores magnus and brevis of right side. (Testut.) Adductor Magnus (Figs. 335,336, 353).—" The great adductor." Situation, in the inner side of the thigh. Origin, the ramus of the os pubis, the ramus and tuberosity of the ischium. Direc- tion, from the ischial tuberosity downward ; from the pubic body outward, from the intermediate parts obliquely downward and outward. Inser- tion, the gluteal ridge, the inner lip of the linea aspera, the internal supracondylar ridge and the adductor tubercle. The femoral attachment is broken by several arches. Action, adduction and external rotation of thigh. The part from the ischial tuberosity is extensor. Nerves, the obtu- rator and the great sciatic. Adductor Longus (Figs. 337, 338).—"The long adductor." Situation, in the inner side of the thigh. Origin, the body of the os pubis near the angle. Direction, down-, out-, and backward.

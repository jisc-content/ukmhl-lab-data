It arises from the Tuberosity at the under and back part of the Os Calcis, and, is divided into three Por- tions, which run forwards, to be connected to the Heads of the Metatarsal Bones of all the Toes. The middle Portion is subdivided into five Slips, which split at the roots of the Toes, and embrace the Tendons of the Flexor Muscles. Besides serving the general purpose of Aponeuroses, it performs the office of a Ligament, by binding the two ends of the arch of the Foot together.

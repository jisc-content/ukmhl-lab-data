On the Structure and Functions of the Kidney. The structure of the kidney, as regards its secret- ing apparatus, forms one of the most simple arrange- ments that can be conceived. The renal artery on reaching the central part of the kidney throws off its branches in a radiated direction towards the surface of the gland; and these again, are subdivided into innumerable ramifi- cations on entering the cortical substance.

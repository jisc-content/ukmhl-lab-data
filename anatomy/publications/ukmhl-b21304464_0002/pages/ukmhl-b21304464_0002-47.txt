The wisdom teeth begin to be formed about the eighth or ninth year, but do not appear until the twentieth or twenty-first year ; or sometimes until a later period of life. The teeth fill the jaws, by which the principal grinders are near to the condyles of the jaw, both for the sake of strength, and also that every part of the food may be manducated. Two sets are given, not merely as they last longer, but also to fill the jaw in its enlarged state j for the teeth could not grow like the soft bone.

The opinion that cartilaginous and osseous induration of the semilimar and mitral valves depends on chronic inflam- mation, derives great probability from some circumstances observed in the origin and progress of these changes. One of the strongest arguments in its favour is the fact, that in the serous membranes the formation of cartilaginous and osseous patches is often preceded by distinct marks of in- flammation.

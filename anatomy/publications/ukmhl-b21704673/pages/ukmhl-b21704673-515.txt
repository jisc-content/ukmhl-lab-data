The Arteriae Sigmoideae, two or three in number, are distributed to the lower part of the descending colon, the iliac colon, and the pelvic colon. The highest branch enters the left iliac fossa, behind the parietal peritoneum. It sends a branch upwards to form an arch with the descending branch of the left colic, and another down- wards, which ultimately enters the pelvic meso-colon and joins the other sigmoid branches.

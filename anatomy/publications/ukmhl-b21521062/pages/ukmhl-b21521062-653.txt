The Lacteal Glands are two large, symmetrical organs, -whicli are only fully developed in the female. In the male, however^ they exist in a very rudimentary state. During the later half of pregnancy, the lacteal glands increase very much in size; and about the period of parturition, they begin to secrete milk. They are racemose glands, and are ultimately composed of numerous roundish follicles, arranged round the terminal extremities of the ducts. The structure of the lacteal glands formed the subject of a very important investigation by Sir Astley Cooper.

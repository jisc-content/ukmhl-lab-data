ORGANS OF VOICE AND RESPIRATION. 678 The apex forms a tapering cone, which extends into the root of the neck, about an inch to an inch and a half above the level of the first rib. The base is broad, concave, and rests upon the convex surface of the Diaphragm ; its circumference is thin, and fits into the space between the lower ribs and the costal attachment of the Diaphragm, extending lower down externally and behind than in front. The external or thoracic surface is smooth, convex, of considerably extent, and corresponds to the form of the cavity of the chest, being deeper behind than in front. The inner surface is concave.

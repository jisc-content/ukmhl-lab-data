The posterior intermediate sulcus is also accentuated by Burdach's column, likewise enlarged into the funiculus cuneatus of the medulla. The lateral funiculus of the medulla, of course, does not contain the lateral or crossed pyramidal tract present in the spinal cord. At the border of the calamus scriptorius the funiculus gracilis terminates in a slight elevation, the clava, which is the superficial indication of the nucleus funiculi gracilis.

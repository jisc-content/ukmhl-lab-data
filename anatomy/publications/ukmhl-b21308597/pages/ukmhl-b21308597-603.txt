These vessels open into the posterior part of the cava, either singly, or conjointly with those of the opposite side. The veins of the left side are longer than those of the right, and pass beneath the aorta. Beneath the psoas muscle the lumbar veins communicate freely around the transverse processes with one another, with the ilio-lumbar, and sometimes with the common iliac, so as to form a plexus of veins. Issuing from the plexus on each side is a venous trunk, the ascending lumbar vein, which joins the azygos vein of the corresponding half of the body. and open into the cava; left longest.

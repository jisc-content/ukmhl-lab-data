Left vena innominata. Externally (2 structures). Left pneumogastric nerve. Left subclavian artery. Behind. Trachea. CEsophagus. Thoracic duct. Within the neck, the two common carotid arteries resemble each other so closely that one description will answer for both. They extend from behind the sterno-calvicular articulation of either side, in a direction upwards and outwards, to the upper border of the thyroid cartilage, where each artery bifurcates into the external and internal carotid arteries.

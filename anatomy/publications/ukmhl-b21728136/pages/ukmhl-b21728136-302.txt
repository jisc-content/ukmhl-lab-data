In fracture of the coronoid process of the ulna, the brachialis anticus muscle elevates the fragment and the power of flexion of the elbow joint is partially destroyed. In fracture of the neck of the radius, the lower fragment is drawn upwards by the biceps muscle ; and the action of the supin- ator brevis and the pronator radii teres muscles tend also to disturb the relation of the two fragments to each other. The inner border of the coraco-brachialis and biceps muscles serves as a guide to the brachial artery, in its course down the inner aspect of the arm.

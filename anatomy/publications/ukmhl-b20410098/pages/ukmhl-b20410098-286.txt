THE MUSCLES. 260 narrow muscular belly extends outwards along the groove on the under surface of the clavicle, and, becoming pointed at its end, is inserted at the extremity of the groove between the conoid and trapezoid ligaments. The great vessels and nerves passing between the neck and the axilla lie behind the muscle. It is supplied by a special nerve from the front of the union of the fifth and sixth cervical nerves in the brachial plexus. MUSCLES OF THE SHOULDER.

In the inner, or mucous coating, are the gastric vessels, that secrete the gastric^ juice. The liver is the large gland which secretes the bile ; it is situated in the upper part the abdomen, mostly on the right side; the bile is contained in a little bag called the gall bladder. pancreas, or sweet bread, is a gland situated behind the stomach; it is the organ which secretes the pancreatic juice. The duodenum is the first twelve inches of the small in- testines, joined to the small end of the stomach; it is the organ in which the chyle^ is formed. The small intestines are membraneous tubes, in which the nutritious portion of the food is absorbed by small vessels, having their mouths in the interior, called the lac- teals ; the innutritions portion passing onwards to the re- ceptacle called the large intestines. The mesenteric glands are small glands between the folds of the mesentery;^ they serve to purify the chyle and pass it onwards into a small tube called the thoracic duct, by which it is carried upwards and emptied into the venous blood, at the junction of the subclavian and jugular veins. 6 Gadric juice, the fluid secreted in the stomach for dissolving the food.

In the human pancreas the follicles are so closely packed that their individual shape cannot well be seen ; but in the rodents the arborescent arrangement of the gland exhibits them well ; and they are seen, when isolated, to be ovoidal or nearly spherical *, although, in the central part of the lobule, they become variously polygonal from mutual compression. The outline, however, even where they are not compressed, is not that of smooth sphe- rules, but presents slight convexities cor- responding to the epithelium within them ; but the endosmosis of water, by detaching the epithelium from the basement membrane, and at the same time distending the follicle, causes these convexities to disappear.

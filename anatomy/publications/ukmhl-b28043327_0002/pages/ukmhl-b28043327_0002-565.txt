In the rabbit, the ovary exhibits some trace of the racemose structure, and, by the different modifications of the uterus, dependent on the proportionate size of its body and cornua, we are conducted from the marsupialia, in which the two uteri are entirely dis- tinct, to the human female, in which the single uterus exists in its greatest degree of concentration.

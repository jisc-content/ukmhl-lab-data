There are : 1 anterior f o n t a n e 11 e, Fontanella frontalis, 1 posterior f o n t a u e 11 e, Fontanella occipitalis, 2 sphenoi- dal fontanelles, Fontanellae sphenoidale^, and 2 mastoid fonta- nelles, Fontanellae mastoides. The anterior fontanelle is of rhombic form and remains till up to the second year of life; the smaller trian- gular posterior fontanelle is almost closed at Ihe time of birth; the 2 sphenoidal fontanelles at the Angulus spiltcnoidalis and the 2 mastoid fontanelles at the Angidus mastoidcus of the parietal bone have also almost disappeard at birth. The place of the fontanelles is taken later on by the sutures, the coronal, sagittal, lambdoid, mastoid and abnormal frontal sutures. If the brain is fully developed, the sutures blend by synostoses.

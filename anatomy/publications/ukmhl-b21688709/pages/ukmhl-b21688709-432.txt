The posterior tibial nerve, JV. tibialis posticus, lies at first to the inner side of the posterior tibial artery, which it soon crosses and then lies to its outer side as far as the ankle. In the interval between the inner malleolus and the heel, it divides into the external and internal plantar nerves. Its muscular branches supply the MM. tibialis posticus, flexor longus digitorum and flexor longus pollicis. a) The internal plantar, JV. plantaris interims, lies between M. ab- ductor pollicis and M. flexor digitorum brcvis; it divides into seven plantar digital nerves, Nervi digitales plantares, distributed to both sides of the three inner toes and to the inner side of the fourth toe.

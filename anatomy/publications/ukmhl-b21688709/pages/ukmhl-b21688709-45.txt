Bones of the Cranium. IT 17. Left Temporal Bone at birth, from the outside. 18. Left Temporal Bone at birth, from the inner side. In the first year of life the temporal bone consists of three pieces: a) Squamous portion, Pars squamosa, b) Auditory process or Annulus tym- panicus, and c) Petroso-mastoid portion, Pars petroso-mastoidea. The Pars petrosa-inastoidea is separated from the Pars squamosa by the Sutura petroso- squamosa and the Mssura mastoideo-squamosa (Jos. G-ruber). The Annulus tympanicus contains the Membrana tympani; in the tympanic cavity, Cavum tympani (behind the Membrana tympani) the Foramen ovale and Foramen rotundum can be seen. The temporal bone articulates with 5 bones: with the occipital, the parietal, the sphenoid, the malar and the inferior maxillary bones. Heitzmann, Atlas. I.

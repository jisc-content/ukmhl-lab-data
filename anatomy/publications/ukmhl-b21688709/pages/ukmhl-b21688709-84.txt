56 Ligaments of the Spine. 72. The Anterior 73. The Posterior Common or Common or Longitudinal Ligament, Longitudinal Ligament, Ligamentum longitudinale Ligamentum longitudinale anterms. posterius. The Ligamentum longitudinale anterius is attached above to the basilar portion of the occipital bone, is at first narrow (Fig. 77), but soon becomes broader, runs along the anterior surface of the vertebral column, and is lost below in the periosteum of the sacrum. The weaker Ligamentum longitudinale posterius runs along the posterior surface of the vertebral bodies, within the spinal canal from the axis to the sacrum; it becomes somewhat broader at each intervertebral disc.

197 Topography. 265. Horizontal Section in the Region of the Condyles of the Femur. M. biceps femoris arises by two heads: The long head, Caput longum, from the Tuber isclui; this runs obliquely downwards and outwards to the knee-joint; the short head, Caput breve, from the external lip of the Linea aspera femoris; it is inserted by a single tendon into the head of the fibula. (Flexor of the leg.) M. semitendinosus arises from the Tuber isclui in common with the long head of the biceps, and is inserted by a long round tendon into the inner surface of the tibia near the Spina tibiae. (Flexor of the leg.)

The posterior tibial nerVe passing be- neath the abductor pollicis muscle near its origin, divides into the external and internal plantar nerves, which will be described with the dissection of the sole of the foot. The posterior tibial nerve gives off many branches to the muscles in its course down the leg, and just before its division into the plantar nerves, it distributes many branches to the muscles of the foot.

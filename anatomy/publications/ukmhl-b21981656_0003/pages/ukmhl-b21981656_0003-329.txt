The same thing sometimes happens in the case of the cranial nerves. Some nodes of the brain and cord which have been described as gummatous appear beyond a doubt to have been tuberculous. As the periphery and the neighbourhood of these nodes do not always contain tubercles, before the discovery of the tubercle-bacillus it was not always easy to determine the nature of a given caseous mass.

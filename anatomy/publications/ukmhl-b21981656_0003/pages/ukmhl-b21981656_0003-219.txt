Beneath the first-temporal convolution lies the first-temporal or parallel furrow (/), and beneath that the second- temporal convolution (H2). Springing from the upper part of the latter convolution the angular gyrus or convolution (F) curves round the end of the first-temporal furrow (/): it also is assigned to the parietal lobe.

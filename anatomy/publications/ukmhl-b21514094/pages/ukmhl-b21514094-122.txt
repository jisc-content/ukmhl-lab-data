Paints mixed with oil and varnish were formerly used for colouring the different parts of preparations, but latterly water-colours only have been employed, except for the nerves, which require white paint mixed with a little drying oil and spirit of turpentine. The white paint should be procured in a small bladder; a puncture may be made in this and a little squeezed out as it is wanted, and the aperture may be stopped up with a small nail.

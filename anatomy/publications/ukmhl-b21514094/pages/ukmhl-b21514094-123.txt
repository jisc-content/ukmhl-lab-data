107 ON INJECTIONS. CHAPTER XXIII. ON INJECTIONS. OF INJECTIONS WHICH REQUIRE THE SUBJECT TO BE HEATED. As in warm weather the coarse injection generally used is apt to escape from wounds that may have been made in the vessels, the following has, for some purposes, been substituted. Red Injection. Wax, four ounces, Common copal varnish, half an ounce, Red lead, half an ounce, Vermilion, two drachms; these are to be melted together in the ordinary way. I Green Injection. Wax, four ounces, Blue verditer, half an ounce, Common copal varnish half an ounce. Blue Injection. The same as for the green, with the addition of half a drachm of powdered Prussian blue.

In those cases in which life is not immediately destroyed by haemorrhage, the injury may prove fatal by the secondary inflammation and tumefaction of the mucous membrane, or by coagula obstructing the passage of air. The latter is particularly likely to happen as a consequence of hasty surgical interference, in prematurely sewing up the wound, and thus pre- venting the necessary escape of the blood.

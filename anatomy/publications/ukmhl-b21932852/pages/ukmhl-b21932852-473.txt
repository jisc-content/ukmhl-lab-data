Blood, derived from haemorrhage in any part of the respiratory tract, in the nose, mouth, or stomach, or from the bursting of an aneurism into any of these parts, may also be found; as well as pus from pulmonary abscesses or vomicae, from empyema, or even from hepatic abscess (the latter stained with bile) and other products of inflammation. Parasites, if they are seen at all, are derived from elsewhere, since they are not in man,'as in some of the lower animals, found in the bronchial tubes.

Mix with cream ; beat well and bake on hot tins. 1 ZWIEBACK GERMAN RUSKS mWO pounds of Vienna flour, three-quarters of an ounce of German yeast, half a pint of milk, half a pint of water, three ounces of castor sugar and a pinch of salt. Sift the flour into a pan and add the salt and rub in a little lard ; make a well in the centre, in it mix the yeast with a little tepid water and very little flour ; melt the butter and work it into the dough. Divide the dough into half-pounds and shape into large rolls. Place them close together on a floured baking sheet when proved ; bake them from forty to forty-five minutes and glaze with sweetened milk. This is called “ milk-bread.” When it is one or twro days old, it should be cut up into even-sized slices, placed on wire trays and dried in the oven till quite crisp. They are then called rusks or zwieback.

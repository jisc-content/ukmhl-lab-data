THE DUDLEY RECIPES 248 A BECHAMEL SAUCE ONE and a half ounces of flour, two ounces of butter, one and a half pints of milk, one and a half pints of white meat stock, one onion or shallot, one small bouquet garni, ten peppercorns, half a bay leaf, one small blade of mace and seasoning. Put the milk on to boil with the onion (or shallot), peeled, the bouquet, peppercorns, mace and bay leaf. Melt the butter ; stir in the flour and cook a little, without browning; stir in the milk and the stock, which have both been made hot; whisk this over the fire until it boils and let simmer from fifteen to twenty minutes. Take out the bouquet; rub through a sieve or tammy; return to the stewpan ; season lightly with a pinch of nutmeg, half a pinch of cayenne and half a teaspoonful of salt.

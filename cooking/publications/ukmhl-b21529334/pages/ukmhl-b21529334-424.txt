THE DUDLEY RECIPES 376 FRENCH ROLLS 111VKE a quarter of a peck of flour and rub into it a quarter of a pound of butter. Put in a little salt. Then take a pint of milk and half a pint of good ale yeast, and mix in the same way as other bread. Lay it to rise before the fire for half an hour, covered with a cloth. Make it into rolls, then prick and salt them, and place before the fire again for half an hour. Put in a quick oven for half an hour more. Before serving, rasp them.

Pare thin two Seville or China oranges; boil the peel tender, and shred it line; pare and core twenty apples, put them in a stew-pan, and as little water as possible; when half done add half a pound of sugar, the orange-peel and juice; boil till pretty thick. When cold, put in a shallow dish, or pattypans lined with paste, to turn out, and be eaten cold. Prune Tart. Give prunes a scald, take out the stones and break them; put the kernels into a little cranberry juice, with the prunes and sugar; simmer; and when cold, make a tart of the sweetmeat. Orange Tart. Squeeze, pulp, and boil, two Seville oranges tender; weigh them, and double of sugar; beat both together to a paste, and then add the juice and pulp of the fruit, and the size of a walnut of fresh butter, and beat all together. Choose a very shallow dish, liue it with a light pulf crust, and lay the paste of orange in it. You may ice it. Codlin Tart.

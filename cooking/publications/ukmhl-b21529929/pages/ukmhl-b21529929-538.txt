No. 1146. Vol-au-vent de Pommes. Procure twenty small golden pippins, peel them neatly, and take out the cores with a long round vegetable cutter; rub them over with lemon, and stew till tender in syrup made from three quarters of a pound of sugar as for the peaches (No. 1141); when cold dress them as high as pos- sible in a vol-au-vent, and when ready to serve, pour the syrup over. No. 1147. Vol-au-vent Oranges. Take ten fine oranges, cut them in halves, peel them, but not to lose their shapes, have a rather thicker syrup than usual, simmer the oranges five minutes, ten at a time, lay them upon a dish, reduce the syrup, and when cold dress in pyramid in a vol-au-vent, and pom the syrup over. No. 1148. Gateau de Millefeuille a la Moderne.

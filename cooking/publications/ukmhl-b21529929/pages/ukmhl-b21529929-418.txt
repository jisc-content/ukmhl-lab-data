Babbit Currie. Cut up two rabbits as before, and cook them as for saute aux truffes (No. 861); when done and nicely brown- ed pour off as much butter as possible and pom a quart of currie sauce (No. 46) over, add twenty button onions, pre- viously stewed in a little broth, and twenty pieces of cook- ed streaky bacon cut in small diamonds, stand them over a slow fire twenty minutes, keeping it stirred occasionally; then build the pieces up in your dish, sauce over and serve with rice (No. 129) in a separate dish; should the sauce be too thick moisten it with a drop of broth, but it requires to be thick enough to adhere to the rabbit.

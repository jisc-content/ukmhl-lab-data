When the vegetables have fried for ten minutes take half a pint of the liquor (in which the meat is soaking), pour it into the saucepan, and let it boil to a quarter of a pint. 12. Stir the vegetables occasionally. 13. Put two tablespoonsful of flour and one tablespoonful of curry powder into a basin, and mix them into a smooth paste with one gill (-£ pint) of the liquor. 14. Stir this into the saucepan with the vegetables. 15. Put the meat and the liquor into the saucepan, and let it come to the boil. 16. When it boils add a little salt and moist sugar. 17. Move the saucepan to the side of the fire and let it simmer for two hours and a half. 18. Watch it, and skim it. 19. Strain off the soup into a basin. 20. Put it back into the saucepan, and add a two lb. tin of calf’s head. GIBLET SOUP. TO MAKE TWO QUARTS. Ingredients. 2 sets of Giblets. Bouquet garni. 1. Scald and pick very clean two sets of goose, or four of duck giblets (the fresher the better). 2. Wash them well in two or three warm waters. 3. Cut off the beaks and split the heads. 4. Divide the gizzards and necks into one-inch pieces. If the gizzards are not cut into small pieces, the rest of the meat will be done too much. 5. Crack the bones of the legs, and put all into a stewpan. 6. Add four pints of cold water. 7. Let it come to boil and take off the scum. 8. Add a bouquet garni. 9.

Stir in one ounce of flour over the fire until it becomes of a. light brown colour. 3. Cover the stewpan, and let it re- main for half an hour on the stove, then add half a pint of boiling water. 4. Season with pepper and salt. 5. Stir gently till well mixed.. 6. Continue the stirring five minutes after it comes to the boil. Stock, as I have before explained, is better than water. Roux is used for thickening sauces and gravies ; when wanted white it must not remain on the fire long enough to brown. The difference between brown roux and white roux is simply in the browning of the butter. It can be kept for some days in a clean earthenware jar in a cool place.

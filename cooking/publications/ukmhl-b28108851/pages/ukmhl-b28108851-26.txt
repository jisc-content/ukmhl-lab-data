The hot-plate top is a great advantage, but a closed range makes the kitchen very hot, and if anything boils over there is not sufficient ventilation to carry off the smell which finds its way all over the house. Kitcheners are rather complicated in their construction, and there- fore require more care in their management. Some are of opinion that roasting can be done quite as well in the roaster of a kitchener as before an open fire, but I believe in constantly basting roast meat, which can- not be done conveniently in an oven, and what is not easily done is easily neglected.

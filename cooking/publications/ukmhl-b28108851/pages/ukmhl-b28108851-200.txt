Parsley. 2 oz. of Butter. Flour. 1. Take some of the remnants of boiled turbot, soles, or any other kind of fish. 2. Pull them into small pieces with a fork, season with pepper and salt, and put them aside on a plate. 3. Put two ounces of butter in a saucepan, let it come to a light brown. 4. Add a tablespoonful of flour, stir well over a gentle fire. 5. Then add half a pint of hot milk, and keep stirring till the mixture thickens. 6. Add a teaspoon- ful of anchovy sauce, or a little Worcester sauce, a very small quantity of grated nutmeg, and just a dust of cayenne; put the fish into the sauce, shake it gently until it nearly boils, and turn the whole on to a plate.

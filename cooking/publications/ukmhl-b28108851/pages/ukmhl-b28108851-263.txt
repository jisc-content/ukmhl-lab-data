Prepare a sauce. 1. Melt in a stewpan half an ounce of butter and stir in half an ounce of flour. 2. Place the stewpan over the fire and well mix with a wooden spoon ; cut off the ends of the stalks of a dozen button mush, rooms and wash them in cold water. 3. Remove them from the water, and peel them carefully with a sharp knife. 4. Add one pint of white stock to the mixture in the stewpan and the peelings of the mush- rooms. 5. Stir with a wooden spoon till the mixture thickens and boils. 6. Stand the stewpan aside with the lid partly off, and let it gently boil for fifteen minutes. 7. Skim off all the fat. 8. Stir in a good tablespoonful of cream, and stand the stewpan aside to keep hot. 9. Put into another stewpan a piece of butter the size of a marble and the twelve mushrooms. 10. Add a teaspoonful of lemon juice and same quantity of cold water. 11. Let the contents come to the boil. 12.

Truss a partridge as for roasting-, it should not be high ; put it into a stewpan with half a pint of good stock or beef-tea, let it simmer gently for three quarters of an hour. Take it up, brush the breast over with yolk of egg, sprinkle it with pepper, salt, and sifted bread crumbs, and brown in the Dutch oven. Thicken the gravy, and serve with the bird. Stewed Pigeon. Take care that the bird is perfectly fresh, young, and plump. Before trussing, as for roasting, put in the body a minced shalot mixed with an ounce of cooked fat bacon, fasten it securely, and put the pigeon into sufficient beef-tea or good stock to half cover it.

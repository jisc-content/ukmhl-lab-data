Tomato Sauce. Choose ripe, perfectly sound tomatoes, break them into small pieces, put them in a stewpan with a very small bit of butter or a spoonful of gravy, salt, and, if allowed, pepper. Let them simmer gently for three-quarters of an hour, then rub through a sieve, taking care that none of the seeds get into the puree, which put back into the stewpan and stir over the fire until it is as thick as good apple sauce.

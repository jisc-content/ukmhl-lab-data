io8 COOKERY FOR INVALIDS. Stewed Endive. Choose a fine white head, wash, pick, and pour boiling water over, let it lie for ten minutes, then squeeze perfectly dry, chop, and put it into a stewpan with a teaspoonful of salt and a cupful of white broth or milk. Having stewed the endive until tender, add enough potato flour to make the sauce thick, and when ready to serve stir in a little lemon juice. Stewed Water-Cress. Well wash and pick the cress, put them into boiling water with a little salt, and boil them for ten minutes. Drain as dry as possible and mince them, then put them back into the stewpan with a spoonful of gravy, and stew until tender. When ready to serve, add a few drops of lemon juice. Vegetable Puree.

COOKERY FOR INVALIDS. 74 White Sauce. This sauce may be made very good without butter, by using good veal or chicken broth and a little cream or milk. Boil a quarter of a pint of white broth with an onion for half an hour, remove the onion, and stir in a dessertspoonful of flour mixed smoothly with two tablespoonfuls of cold milk; stir the sauce over the fire until thick, then add two tablespoonfuls of good cream ; keep stirr- ing for two or three minutes, but do not let it boil; add salt, and, if allowed, a little pepper or cayenne. The sauce can also be made with milk instead of broth, and, if too rich, the cream can be omitted. Anchovy Relish. A small quantity of this relish may, in many cases, be given with advantage, and is especially valuable when it will induce the invalid to eat bread plain or toasted.

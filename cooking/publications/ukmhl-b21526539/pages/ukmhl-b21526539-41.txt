In some articles a discount of five per cent, is allowed for ready money, in London, and other large cities; and those who thus pay are usually best served. Under the idea of buying cheap, many go to new shops ; but it is safest to deal with people of established credit, wlio do not dispose of goods by underselling. To make tradesmen wait for their money injures them greatly, besides that a higher price must be paid; and in long bills, articles never bought are often charged. Perhaps the irregularity and failure of payment may have much evil influence on the jirice of various articles, and may contribute to the destruction of many families, from the highest to the lowest.

The same proportions of flour and suet, and half the quantity of fruit, with spice, lemon, a glass of wine, or not, and one egg, and milk, will make an excellent pud- ding, if long boiled. Another. Lay a pound of beef suet in lumps, the size of nut- megs, in a basin, half a pound of jar-raisins, a large spoonful of fine sugar, three eggs, a spoonful and a half of flour, and a glass of brandy. Tie a wet cloth, doubled and well floured, over the basin. Put it into a pot of water that boils very fast, and move it about for some minutes. Boil five or six hours.

To twelve pounds of Kentish or duke cherries, when ripe, weigh one pound of sugar ; break the stones of part, and blanch them; then put them to the fruit and sugar, and boil all gently till the jam comes clear from the pan. Pour it into china plates to come up dry to table. Keep in boxes with white paper between. Currant Jam, black, red, or lohite. Let the fruit be very ripe, pick it clean from the stalks, bruise it, and to every pound put three-quarters of a pound of loaf-sugar ; stir it well, and boil half an hour.

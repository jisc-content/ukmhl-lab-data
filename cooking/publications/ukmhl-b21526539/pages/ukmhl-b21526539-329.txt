Bone and skin a fine young fowl: take the odd bits of it, four ounces of green truffles, a small carrot, (both in thin slices,) with mace, pepper, and salt, and stuff the inside of it: tie it lightly, and lay it on slices of fat bacon; and lay over it a whole lemon sliced, after cutting off the rind. Put into a casserole a bay-leaf, two cloves, a small carrot, an onion, and as much water as will cover the fowl. Simmer, as slowly as possible, with embers over the pan, two hours, by which time the liquor will be much wasted; strain, and add to it half a pint of very strong gravy, and two ounces of sliced truffles : continue to stew gently three-quarters of an hour, and then serve altogether. To some tastes, mushrooms, substituted for truffles, might be more agreeable, with the addition of a little butter. Salade de Perdreaux.—Partridges in Salad. Bone two partridges, and put into them a few sliced truffles. Put them into a small casserole, with slices of lean ham under and over, some slices of lemon, and a small quantity of veal gravy, with which occasionally wet them. Serve them next day with clear jelly of veal, properly seasoned; and put round them, alternately, hard eggs, split, but left in the whites, lettuces, cut short and in quarters, and nasturtium flowers.

Turn the neck end of the goose towards you, and cut the whole breast in long slices from one wing to another; but only remove them as you help each person, unless the company is so large as to recpiire the legs likewise. This way gives more prime hits than by making w’ings. Take off the leg by putting the fork into the small end of the bone, pressing it to the body, and, having passed the knife at d, turn the leg back, and, if a young bird, it will easily separate. To take off the wing, put your fork into the small end of the pinion, and press it close to the body ; then put in the knife at d, and divide the joint, taking it down in the direction c/, e. Nothing but practice will enable people to hit the joint exactly at the first trial. When the leg and wing of one side are done, go on to the other ; but it is not often necessary to cut up the whole goose, unless the company be very large. There are two side-bones, by the wing, which may be cut off; as like- wise the back and lower side-bones ; but the best pieces are the breast and the thighs, after being divided from the drum-sticks.

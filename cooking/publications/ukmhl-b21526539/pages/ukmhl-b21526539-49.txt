Bread is so heavy an article of expense that all waste should he guarded against; and having it cut in the room will tend much to prevent it. Since the scarcity in 1795 and 1800, that custom has been much adopted. It should not he cut until a day old. Earthen pans and covers keep it best. Straw to lay apples on should he quite dry, to prevent a musty taste. Large pears should he tied up by the stalk. Basil, savory, or knotted marjoram, or London thyme, to he used when herbs are ordered ; hut with discretion, as they are very pungent. The best means to preserve blankets from moths is to fold and lay them under the feather-beds that are in use; and they should he shaken occasionally. When soiled, they ^should he washed, not scoured. Soda, by softening the water, saves a gi’eat deal of soap. It should he melted in a large jug of water, some of which pour into the tubs and boiler; and when the latter becomes weak, add more. The new improvement in soft soap is, if jiroperly used, a saving of nearly half in quantity; and though sometliing dearer than the hard, reduces the price of washing considerably. Many good laundresses advise soaping linen in warm water the night previous to washing, as facilitating the operation, with less friction.

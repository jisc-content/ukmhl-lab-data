Somersetshire Frumenty. To a quart of ready-boiled wheat^put by degrees two quarts of new milk, breaking the jelly, and then four ounces of currants, picked clean, and washed ; stir them and boil till they are done. Beat the yolks of three eggs, and a little nutmeg, with two or three spoonsful of milk; add this to the wheat; stir them together while over the fire; then sweeten, and serve cold in a deep dish.

Season with pepper and salt, and a good-sized knob of white sugar. Carrot Soup. Put some beef-bones, with four quarts of the liquor in which a leg of mutton or beef has been boiled, tvvo large onions, a turnip, pepper, and salt, into a saucepan, and stew for three hours. Ilave ready six large carrots scraped and cut thin; strain the soup on them, and stew them till soft enough to pulp through a hair-sieve or coarse cloth; then boil the pulp with the soup, which is to be as thick as peas soup. If too thick, add some broth; if the contrary, add a little flour rubbed down ! with two spoonsful of the soup. Use two wooden spoons to rub the carrots through. Make the soup the day before it is to be used. Pulp only the red part of the carrot, and not the yellow. Season with salt, cayenne, and three knobs of white sugar. Onion Soup. Into the water that has boiled a leg or neck of mutton, put carrots, turnips, and (if you have one) a shank-bone, and simmer two hours. Strain it on six onions, first sliced and fried of a light brown ; simmer three hours, skim it carefully, and serve. Put into it a little roll, small dumplings, or fried bread. Spinach Soup.

Season this to taste, and pour it upon dice of fried bread laid in the tureen. Roast Mutton, stuffed. Have the shoulder - bone extracted by the butcher, leaving a cavity for the stuffing. Make a dressing of crumbs and chopped pork, well sea- soned with pepper, salt, and sweet herbs. Take a few stitches in the edges of the opening to keep the forcemeat from escaping. Lay the meat in the baking pan, dash a cup of boiling water over it, and bake about fifteen minutes to the pound. Baste frequently. When done, transfer to a plat- ter and set in a hot place while you cool, skim, and strain the gravy, and thicken it with brown flour. Pour a little of the gravy over the meat and serve the remainder separately. Canned Succotash.

126 DAINTY DINNER TABLES. A lable-cloth of either ordinary white damask or yellow art linen—the latter for preference—silver bowl or basket in the middle filled with yellow roses and maidenhair-fern, silver vases and speci- men glasses similarly filled should be placed here and there and everywhere, wherever room can be found for them. Quaint bits of silver and old silver spoons of odd shapes should also be arranged in between; and if this festivity is a supper party, the candles should be yellow wax, in silver candle- sticks. Scheme 89. A “ lily ” table could be charmingly carried out on similar lines, but all these latter schemes are really more suitable for nearly grown-up girls, as I am afraid very little people would hardly appreciate them.

20 RECEIPT I. Two pounds of beef, four onions, ten turnips, half a pound of rice, a large handful of parsley, thyme and savory; some pepper and saltj eight quarts of water. Cut the beef in slices, and after it has boiled some time, cut it smaller. The whole should boil gently about two hours on a slow fire. If fuel be scarce, it may be stewed all night in an oven, and warmed up next day. You may add oatmeal and potatoes. RECEIPT II. Take half a pound of beef, mutton, or pork, cut into small slices; half a pint of pease, four sliced turnips, six potatoes cut very small, two onions; put to them seven pints of water. Let the whole boil gently over a very slow fire two hours and a halt. Then thicken it with a quar- ter of a pound of oatmeal. After the thickening is put in, boil it a quarter of an hour, stirring it all the time, then season it with salt and pepper. RECEIPT III.

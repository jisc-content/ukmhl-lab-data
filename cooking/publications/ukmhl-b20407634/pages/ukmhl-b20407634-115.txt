107 SICKROOM COOKERY. GERMAN SAUCE. 2 yolks of egg. 1 glass of skerry. 1 dessertspoonful of castor sugar. Put the yolks of egg, skerry and sugar, into a sauce- pan. Whisk over the fire until it becomes a thick froth. It is ready. If it becomes too thick, add a little milk. If desired, the sherry may be omitted, and the juice of half a lemon, put into a wine glass and made up to the quantity with cold water, may be used instead. Serve for puddings. HOLLANDAISE SAUCE. oz. of butter. ^ oz. of flour. 1 gill of water. 2 yolks of egg. 1 teaspoonful of lemon juice. Cayenne. Melt the butter, stir in the flour, add the water and bring to the boil. Stir in the yolks of egg, cook for a moment, add the lemon juice and cayenne. Used for fish, or for serving with vegetables. MELTED BUTTER SAUCE. 1 oz. of butter. \ oz. of flour. 1| gill of water or milk. Pepper and salt. Melt the butter in a saucepan, stir in the flour, add the liquid, and stir until it boils. Boil for two minutes. Season and serve. For anchovy sauce a few drops of anchovy essence may be added when cooked.

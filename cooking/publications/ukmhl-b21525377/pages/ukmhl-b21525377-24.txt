THE EARLY ENGLISHMAN AND HIS FOOD. ILLIAM OF MALMESBURY par- ticularly dwells on the broad line of distinction still existing between the southern English and the folk of the more northerly dis- tricts in his day, twelve hundred years after the visit of Caesar. He says that they were then (about a.d. 1150) as different as if they had been different races; and so in fact they were—different in their origin, in their lan- guage, and their diet.

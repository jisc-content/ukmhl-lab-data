174 Food and Feeding. ECONOMICAL DISH, OR FISH STEW FOR WORKING man’s FAMILY. Take three or four pounds of hake, ling, skate, or haddock, and a pound of “ cuttings or trimmings,” which are the best part of the fish for stock making. Remove all the fish from the bones, break up or pound the latter, and set aside with any portion of head there may he, and the cuttings. Put into a saucepan, over the fire, 2 ozs. of lard and two or three onions sliced, and let them fry until brown; then add two quarts of water and all the pounded hones and trim- mings, some parsley or other green herbs, pepper and salt. Let the whole simmer for three hours. Strain out the bones, bits of skin, &c., add the fish in pieces, and boil gently ten or fifteen minutes. Thicken wuth sufficient flour mixed smoothly with a small portion of stock, and added before finishing. In order to make the dish complete and substantial, a few small suet dumplings should be well boiled, and put into the tureen. THE END. BRADBURY, AGNEW, 129313487313 & CO., PRINTERS, WHJTEFRIARS.

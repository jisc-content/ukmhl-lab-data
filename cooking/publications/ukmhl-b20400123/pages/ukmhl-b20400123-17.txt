Take the flesh of a lobster, pound it well in the mortar with cream, rub it through a sieve ; add an eggspoonful of anchovy sauce and beat it up well in a pound of Devonshire clotted cream ; put this into the little French china ramequin moulds, strew coral over the top, and serve. Gratin of Lobster. Gratin de Homard. Cut a good-sized lobster in half and pick out all the meat, which must be cut into thin slices. Put a piece of butter the size of an egg into a saucepan with three shalots finely minced. Brown them lightly, then mix in a tablespoonful of flour, and, when quite smooth, add half a pint of milk. Simmer for five minutes. Put in the sliced lobster, a table- spoonful of chopped parsley, a little salt and cay- enne, and half a teaspoonful of anchovy sauce. Stir over the fire, and, when boiling, draw the saucepan back, and add the well-beaten yolk of an egg. Fill the shell of the lobster with the mix- ture, sprinkle bread-crumbs over it, and pour on a little clarified butter. Put the shell in a hot oven for twenty minutes, dish on a napkin, and serve.

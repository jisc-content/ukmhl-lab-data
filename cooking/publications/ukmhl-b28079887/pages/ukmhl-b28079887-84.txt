PUT two quarts of warm water, and a tablespoonful of salt into a saucepan, and put it on the fire to boil. Take some turnips, wash them in cold water, and peel them thickly with a sharp knife. If the turnips are very large, they should be cut in quarters. When the water in the saucepan is quite boiling, put in the turnips, and let them boil gently until they are quite tender. Feel them with a fork to see if they are tender all through. Turn them into a colander, and drain them very dry.

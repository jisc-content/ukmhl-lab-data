CUT one pound of cooked cold meat very fine, and put it into stewpan with two ounces of butter, and pepper, and salt, to taste, pledge- over it an even tablespoonful of flour; then add a quart or more of boiling water, cover it close, and set it over a moderate fire for half an hour. Strain it through a loose cloth ; toast some thin slices of bread delicately brown, cut them in small squares, put them in a tureen, and pour the soup over them. Macaroni or vermicelli boiled tender may be put to the soup ten or twelve minutes before serving.

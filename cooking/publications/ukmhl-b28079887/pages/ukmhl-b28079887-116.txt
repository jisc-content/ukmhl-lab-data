LINE a dish throughout with paste. The pudding can be made without paste if preferred; in which case the dish must be buttered inside. Break four eggs, put the yolks into a basin, with the whites of two eggs, and beat them well together (the others put aside). Add a tablespoonful of castor sugar. Stir one pint of cold milk in with the eggs, and a quarter of a spoonful of salt. Pour this custard into the dish lined with paste. Grate half a teaspoonful of nutmeg over the top.

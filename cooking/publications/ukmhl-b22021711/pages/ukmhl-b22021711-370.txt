In a very short time the gum with which the insect had covered the co- coon is dissolved, and the loose threads will begin to float on the water ; and, five or six being col- lected, the reeling of the silk begins. If well fed and kept in a proper temperature, the caterpillars will finish their labours twenty-four days after they are hatched. An ounce of eggs will produce about forty thousand caterpillars, which will con- sume nearly eleven hundred pounds of leaves, which will produce about eighty pounds of co- coons, or eight pounds of raw silk.

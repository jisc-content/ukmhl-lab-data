6 TO BOIL SHOULDER OP MUTTON WITH OYSTERS. Hang it some days, then salt it well for two days: bone it; and sprinkle it with pepper and a bit of mace pounded : lay some oysters over it, and roll the meat up tight and tie it. Stew it in a small quantity of water, with an onion and a few pepper- corns till quite tender. Have ready a little good gravy, and some oysters stewed in it: thicken this with flour and butter, and pour over the mutton when the tape is taken off. The stew-pan should be kept close covered. MUTTON SAUSAGES. Take a pound of the rawest part of the leg of mutton that has been either roasted or boiled ; chop it extremely small, and season it with pepper, salt, mace, and nutmeg; add to it six ounces of beef- suet, some sweet herbs, two anchovies, and a pint of oysters, all choped very small: a quarter of a pound of grated bread, some of the anchovy liquor, and the yolks and whites of two eggs well beaten. Put it all, when well mixed, into a little pot: and use it by rolling it into balls or sausage-shape and frying. If approved, a little shalot may be added, or garlick, which is a great improvement. TO DRESS MUTTON RUMPS AND KIDNEYS. Stew six rumps in some good mutton-gravy half an hour; then take them up, and let them stand to cool.

65 BAUCES. 124.—Maitre d’Hotel Butter. Knead some very finely chopped parsley in with some fresh butter; add pepper, salt, and a little lemon- juice. If required hot, melt it over the fire, but do not let it boil. 125. —Ravigote Butter. Scald some parsley, tarragon, chervil, chives, and shallot; press out all the water, and chop them up very fine. Now pound them up with two anchovies and some butter. Pass through a sieve. 126. —Tarragon Buttei’. Chop some tarragon very fine; beat it up with a lump of butter, pepper, salt, and lemon-juice. Serve over mackerel, soles, etc. 127.—Devil Mixture. Mix well on a plate a teaspoonful of French or English mustard, half a teaspoonful of anchovy paste, 2 of salad oil.

In this way 3 sheep’s heads will give you 4 different dishes. 508. —Chicken and Chestnuts. Have a plump chicken; 4 ounces of fresh breast of pork; cut in small squares. Put 8 ounces of butter in a saucepan; toss the chicken and pork in the butter. When they are coloured light brown, remove them, and thicken the butter with 2 tablespoonfuLs of flour ; put the chicken and pork back in the sauce- pan ; pepper and salt to taste ; add 4 ounces of force- meat balls, and simmer for 1 hour; then add 20 to 30 roasted and peeled chestnuts, and simmer for another half-hour. The quantity of butter is for a small chicken. 509. —Stewed Chicken Livers. Melt 1 ounce of dripping or butter in a stewpan; add a little chopped onion, chopped parsley; stew for a few minutes, stirring all the time; put in 6 livers; dredge over 1 teaspoonful of flour, 1 wine-glassful of gravy or stock, 1 wine-glassful of mushroom ketchup, 1 wine-glassful of red wine; stew very gently for an hour with the lid on. Serve with fried sippets.

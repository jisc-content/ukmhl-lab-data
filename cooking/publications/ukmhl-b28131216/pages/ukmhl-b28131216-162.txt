The flavour of broiled hare is particularly fine: the legs or wings must be seasoned first, rub with cold butter, and served very hot.—The other parts, warmed with gravy and a little stuffing, may be served sepa- rately. To pot Hare, For which an old one does well, as likewise for soup and pie.—After seasoning, bake it with butter. When cold take the meat from the bones, and beat it in a mortar If not high enough add salt, mace, pepper, and a piece of the finest fresh butter melted in a spoon- • ful or two of the gravy that came from the hare. When well mixed put it into small pots, and cover with butter. The legs and back should be baked at the bottom of the jar, to keep them moist, and the bones be put over them.

PASTRY. 173 Almond Custard. Blanch and beat four ounces of almonds fine with a spoonful of water ; then beat a pint of cream with two spoonfuls of rose-water, and put them to the yolks of four eggs, and as much sugar as will make it pretty sweet; then add the almonds ; stir it all over a slow lire till it is of a proper thickness; but do not boil. Pour it into cups. Cheesecakes. A plainer sort. Turn three quarts of milk to curd, break it and drain the whey ; when dry, break it up in a pan, with two ounces of butter, till perfectly smooth ; put to it a pint and a half of thin cream, or good milk, and add sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and three ounces of currants. Cheesecakes, another may.

Pheasants and Partridges, Roast them as turkey ; and serve with a fine gravy (into which put a small bit of garlick), and bread- sauce. When cold, they may be made into excellent patties, but their flavour should not be overpowered by lemon. For the manner of trussing a pheasant or partridge, see plate IX. To pot Partridge. Clean them nicely ; and season with mace, allspice, white pepper, and salt, in fine powder. Rub every part well ; then lay the breasts downwards in a pan, and pack the birds as close as ymu possibly can. Put a good deal of butter on them; then cover the pan Avith a coarse flour-paste and a paper over, tie it close, and bake. When cold,, put the birds into pots, and cover them with butter. A very cheap way of potting Birds. Prepare them as directed in the last receipt; and when baked and grown cold, cut them into proper pieces for helping, pack them close in a large potting- pot, and (if possible) leave no spaces to receive the butter.

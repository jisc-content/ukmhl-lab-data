To each pound put three quarters of a pound ot fine sugar, and half a pint of water. Boil and clarify the sugar in the water as directed in page 219, then add the fruit; simmer gently till clear, then break it, and in a few minutes put the jam into small pots. Gooseberry Hops. Of the largest green walnut kind, take and cut the >jud end in four quarters, leaving the stalk end whole; pick out the seeds, and %vith a strong needle and thread fasten five or six together, by running the thread through the bottoms, till they are of the size of a hop. Lay vine leaves at the bottom of a tin pre- serving pan, cover them with the hops, then a layer of leaves, and so on; lay a good many on the top, then fill the pan with water. Stop it so close down that no steam can get out; set it by a slow fire till scalding hot; then take it off till cold, and so do till on open- ing while cold, the gooseberries are of a good green. Then drain them on sieves, and make a thin syrup of a pound of sugar to a pint of water, boil, and skim it well; when half cold, put in the fruit; next day give it one boil, and do this thrice.

To clean the back of the Grate, the inner Hearth, anu the fronts of Cast Iron Stoves. Boil about a quarter of a pound of the best black lead, with a pint of small beer, and a bit of soap the size of a walnut. When that is melted, dip a painter’s brush, and wet the grate, having first brushed off all the soot and dust; then take a hard brush, and rub it till of a beautiful brightness. Another may to clean Cast Iron, and black. Hearths. Mix black lead and rvhites of eggs well beaten toge- ther ; dip a painter’s brush, and wet all over, then rub it bright with a hard brush. To take the Black off the bright Bars of polished Stoves in a fern Minutes. Rub them well with some of the following mixture on a bit of broad cloth ; when the dirt is removed, wipe them clean, and polish with glass, not sand-paper. The mixture.—Boil slowly one pound of soft soap in two quarts of water, to one. Of this jelly take three or four spoonfuls, and mix to a consistence with emery, No. 3. To clean. Tin Covers, and Patent Pewter Porter Pots. Get the finest whiting, which is only sold in large cakes, the small being mixed with sand : mix a little of it powdered, with the least drop of sweet oil, and rub well, and wipe clean ; then dust some dry whiting in a muslin bag over, and rub bright with dry leather.

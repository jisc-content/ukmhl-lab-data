Let them soak six days ; then change the brine, let them stand six more; then drain them, and pour over them in the jar a pickle of the best white wine vinegar, with a good quantity of pep- per, pimento, ginger, mace, cloves, mustard-seed and horse-radish ; all boiled together, but cold. To every hundred of walnuts put six spoonfuls of mustard-seed, and two or three heads of garlick or shalot; but the latter is least strong. Thus done, they will be good for several years, if kept close covered. The air will soften them. They will not be fit to eat under six months. The pickle will serve as good ketchup, when all the walnuts are used.

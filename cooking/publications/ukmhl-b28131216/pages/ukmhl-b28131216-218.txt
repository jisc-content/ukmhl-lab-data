Baked Gooseberry Pudding. Stew gooseberries in a jar over a hot hearth, or in a sauce-pan of water till they will pulp. Take a pint of the juice pressed through a coarse sieve, the yolks and whites of three. eggs beaten and strained, and one ounce and a half of butter; sweeten it well, and put a , crust round the dish. A few crumbs of rolls should I be mixed with the above to give a little consistence, or four ounces of Naples biscuits. A Green-bean Pudding. Boil and blanch old beans, beat them in a mortar, with very little pepper and salt, some cream, and the yolk of an egg. A little spinach-juice will give a finer colour, but it is as good without. Boil it in a bason that will just hold it, an hour; and pour par- sley and butter over. Serve bacon to eat with it. Shelford Pudding.

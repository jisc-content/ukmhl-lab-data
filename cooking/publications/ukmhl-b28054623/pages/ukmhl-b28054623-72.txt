STEWED BEEF. (Second dressing.) A piece of cold roast beef without bone, about three pounds weight or less. Cut it even and trim off the gristle. Put it into a stewpan, the cut side downwards. Peel, cut up, and fry in dripping, one onion, one turnip, one carrot, all medium size, and a small head of celery. While frying, season them with a tea- spoonful of moist sugar, the same of salt, a salt- spoonful of flour of mustard, and the same of pepper. When nicely browned and nearly tender, turn them into a sieve to drain off the fat before the fire. Then put them with the beef, add a small teacupful of mixed pickles and half a pint of gravy or water. Simmer very gently for an hour and a quarter, then serve. 9â€™. BEEF EOLLOYEE. !

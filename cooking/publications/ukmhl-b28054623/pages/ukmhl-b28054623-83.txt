BEEF SAUSAGES. Put the sausages into a folding gridiron, and broil them either over or before a bright fire till they are nicely browned all over. If they are large (six to the pound), they will require sixteen minutes; if small (eight to the pound), twelve minutes will be sufficient. Turn them twice while broiling, but be careful not to prick them. Serve them the moment they are done. 125. TOAD IN A HOLE OF BEEF SAUSAGE MEAT. The shoulder, or rather the bladebone, of veal is the cheapest part for roasting. Make a stuffing with the following ingredients. Two ounces of fine crumbs of bread, a small dessertspoonful of chopped parsley, a saltspoonful of mixed sweet herbs in powder, a small saltspoonful of salt, the same of white pepper, two ounces of dripping or chopped suet, and one egg beaten ; rub the dripping or suet with the other in- gredients till well mixed, then add the egg.

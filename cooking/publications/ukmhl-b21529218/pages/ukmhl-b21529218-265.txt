RED CURRANT JELLY TT^ULL all the stems off the currants and stew them gently so as to draw out all the juice. Strain it, and to each pint of juice allow one pound of sugar. Then boil from three-quarters of an hour to an hour until it jellies. CHERRY JAM STONE twelve pounds of cherries — Morellas are the best — and make a good syrup, allowing three-quarters of a pound of sugar to one pound of cherries. Put the cherries into the syrup and boil up quickly for about three-quarters of an hour. Crack the stones and add the kernels to the jam a few minutes before it is done. 837 ^ ^ ^

Sauces. 4* Cream Bechamel Sauce. Put a pint of white sauce into a stew-pan with a few sliced mushrooms; reduce this by stirring it over the fire for ten minutes. Then add half a pint of cream; and, as soon as the sauce comes to the boil, strain it into a basin or stew-pan for use. White Sauce. Mix well in a stew-pan two ounces of flour, one ounce of butter, a little nutmeg, pepper, and salt; to these add a pint of milk; stir over the fire for ten minutes, and strain off for use. Brown Butter Sauce. Put four ounces of fresh butter in a stew-pan on the fire, and keep stirring it until it becomes browned by frying. Then add a small glassful of tarragon vinegar, ditto of Harvey, a table-spoonful of chopped capers, a little anchovy, and either a gill of brown sauce or gravey. Boil this all together for five minutes, and serve with boiled skate or grilled mackerel. Cardinal Sauce. Put a gill of good white sauce into a stew-pan; add some lobster-spawn, a little anchovy, and Chili vinegar. Boil it all together, and strain. Genoese Sauce.

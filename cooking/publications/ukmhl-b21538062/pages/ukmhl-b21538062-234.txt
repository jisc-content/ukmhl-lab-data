Mix in lightly four ounces of flour; put the mixture into small pans, previously prepared, and bake in a slow oven for about ten minutes. Gingerbread Cake. Ingredients.—One pound and a-half of flour, half a pound of butter, half a pound of sugar, the same of treacle, orange peel, three ounces of almonds, one ounce of ground ginger, teaspoonful of carbonate of soda, the juice of one lemon, and six eggs. Beat the butter and sugar to a cream; add the eggsr two at a time—beat five minutes between each two. Next add the treacle, then the flour, orange peel, almonds (minced small), lemon juice, and carbonate of soda. Put the mixture into a prepared tin, and bake in rather a slow oven for about three hours. Note.—It is impossible to determine the exact length of time that this, or indeed any other, cake should remain in the oven before it is done. This will mainly depend upon the construction of the oven, and partly on the necessity there may be for occasionally opening it during the process of baking the cake. The best way to ascertain whether the cake is done is to run a skewer down the centre, and if, when withdrawn, the skewer is dry and free from any portion of the cake in an unbaked state, it will be safe to turn it out of the mould.

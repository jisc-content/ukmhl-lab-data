Hog’s lard is the inner fat of the bacon-hog. Pickled pork is made of the flesh of the hog, as well as bacon. Porkers are not so old as hogs ; their flesh is whiter and less rich, but it is not so tender. It is divided into four quarters. The fore-quarter has the spring or fore-leg, the fore-loin or neck, the sparerib and griskin. The hind lias the leg and the loin. The feet of pork make various good dishes, and should be cut off before the legs are cured. Observe the same of the ears.

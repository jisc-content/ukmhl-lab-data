261 CAKES, kc. Tea Cakes. Rub fine four ounces of butter into eight ounces of flour ; mix eight ounces of currants, and six of fine Lisbon sugar, two yolks and one white of eggs, and a spoonful of brandy. Roll the paste the thickness of an Oliver biscuit, and cut with a wine-glass. You may beat the other white, and wash over them ; and either dust sugar, or not, as yon like. Benton Tea Cakes. Mix a paste of flour, a little bit of butter and milk ; roll as thin as possible, and bake on a back-stone over the fire, or on a hot hearth. Another sort, as Biscuits.—Rub into a pound of flour six ounces of butter, and three large spoonfuls of yeast, and make into a paste, with a sufficient quan- tity of new milk ; make into biscuits, and prick them with a clean fork. Another sort.—Melt six or seven ounces of butter with a sufficiency of new milk warmed to make seven Dounds of flour into a stiff paste ; roll thin, and make into biscuits. A Biscuit Cake. One pound of flour, five eggs well beaten and strain- ed, eight ounces of sugar, a little rose, or orange-flower water ; beat the whole thoroughly, and bake one hour. Macaroons.

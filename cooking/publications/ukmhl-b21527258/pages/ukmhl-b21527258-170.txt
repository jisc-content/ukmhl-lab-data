Boil a pound of the best macaroni in a quart of good stock till quite tender ; then take out half, and put it into another stew-pot. To the remainder add some more stock, and boil it till you can pulp all the maca- roni through a fine sieve. Tlien add together that, the two liquors, a pint or more of cream boiling hot, the macaroni that was first taken out, and half a pound of grated Parmesan cheese ; make it hot, but do not let it boil. Serve it with the crust of a French roll cut into the size of a shilling. A Pepper-pot, to he served in a Tureen.

In this manner you may do any firm fish. To dry Salmon. Cut the fish down, take out the inside and roe. Eub the whole with common salt after scaling it ; let it hang 24 hours to drain. Pound three or four ounces of salt-petre, according to the size of the fish, two ounces of bay-salt, and two ounces of coarse su- gar ; rub these, when mixed well, into the salmon, and lay it on a large dish or tray two days, then rub it well with common salt, and in 24 hours more it will be fit to dry ; wipe it well after draining. Hang it either in a wood chimney or in a dry place ; keep- ing it open with two small sticks.

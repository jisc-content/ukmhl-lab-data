Set a quart of new milk upon the fire with two or three blades of mace ; and when ready to boil, put to it the yolks and whites of nine eggs well beaten, and as much salt as wrill lie upon a small knife’s point. Let it boil till the whey is clear ; then drain it in a thin cloth, or hair-sieve ; season it with sugar or a lit- tle cinnamon, rose-water, orange-flower water, or white wine, to your taste ; and put it into a star form, or any other. Let it stand some hours before you turn it into a dish ; then pat round it thick cream or custard. Blanc-inange, or Blamange.

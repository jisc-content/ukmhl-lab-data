137 BEVERAGES. BEVERAGES. CHICKEN PANADA. Boil a chicken till about three parts done; take off the skin when cold, cut off the meat and pound it to a paste in a mortar, with a little of the water it was boiled in; season with a little salt, and make it thick by adding milk to it so that it can easily be drunk. RICE CAUDLE. Soak some Carolina rice in water an hour, strain it, and put two spoonfuls of it into a pint and half of milk; simmer till it will pulp through a sieve, then add a bit of sugar; if too thick add more milk, and serve with toast. WHITE CAUDLE. Mix two spoonfuls of finely ground oatmeal in a pint of water; two hours previous to using it pour off the water and boil it; then sweeten it. BARLEY WATER. Put two ounces of pearl barley with half an ounce of sifted sugar in a jug, pour on it a quart of boiling water, and let it stand two hours; then strain off the liquor; it may be flavoured with orange juice or currant jelly. ISINGLASS WATER. Dissolve a quarter of an ounce of isinglass in a little milk ; boil it with a pint of new milk until it is reduced to half a pint; season with sugar and orange juice.

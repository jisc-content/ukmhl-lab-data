100 SWEET DISHES, ETC. ORANGE FOOL. Mix the juice of three oranges, three eggs well beaten, a pint of cream, and some sifted sugar; set it in a pan on the fire, but do not let it boil, allow it only to become thick; put it into a dish, and eat it cold. APPLE FOOL. Pare and core some apples, put them into a stone jar with some brown sugar, let it stew slowly; when done, serve with milk. If liked, some dried currants may be stewed with the apples. MILK PANADA. Boil a quart of milk, mix with it four ounces of either grated biscuits or bread, sweeten with sugar ; a few currants or raisins may be added. EGG PANADA. Melt quarter of a pound of fresh butter in a quart of water, and add to it half a pound of finely grated bread crumbs; beat up the yolks of four eggs with a little salt, mix them with the bread, put all into a pan, and warm. BISCUIT PANADA. Grate a quarter of a pound of water biscuits ; mix with them one ounce of sugar, and the juice of a sweet orange ; warm it in a pint of water.

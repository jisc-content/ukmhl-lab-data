18 INVALID COOKERY. Time required, about fifteen minutes. CHICKEN BROTH. Three pounds of tender chicken. Two quarts of cold water. Two scant teaspoonfuls of salt. Skin the chicken (if it is very fat), cut it up, and pound the pieces with a mallet until the bones are broken. Put it into a stew-pan with the water and salt, and boil slowly two hours; strain, and set aside to cool. When cold, remove all fat and dregs, and heat as required for use. Time required, two hours. OYSTER BROTH. One pint of oysters. Half a pint of cold water. Half a teaspoonful of salt. A shake of black pepper. Cut the oysters into small pieces, put them into a stew-pan with the water and salt, and simmer slowly ten minutes. Skim, strain, and add the pepper. Serve hot, with toasted crackers.

22 INVALID COOKERY. Time required, two hours. A piece of celery two inches long (if desired). Two tablespoonfuls of cooked rice. One tablespoonful of toast crumbs. A shake of black pepper. Mince the meat, put it into a stew-pan with the water, salt and celery, and boil slowly thirty minutes. Add the rice and toast crumbs, and boil fifteen minutes longer. Take the meat and celery out, pour the soup into a bowl, and shake a very little pepper over the top. Serve hot, with crackers or toast. Time required, about three-quarters of an hour. MUTTON SOUP. One pound of lean, juicy mutton. One quart of cold water. One teaspoonful of salt. One tablespoonful of uncooked rice. One teaspoonful of chopped parsley. A shake of black pepper. Remove every particle of fat and skin from the meat, put it into a stew-pan with the water and salt, boil thirty minutes, and skim. Add the rice, parsley and pepper, and boil slowly one hour and a half. Remove the meat and serve the soup very hot, with toast or crackers.

Put one pound of mutton, one of veal, and two of beef, cut and beaten, into a covered vessel with four or five quarts of water, with sweet herbs, seasoning, and roots, and let it stew till nearly half the quantity is consumed. CALF'S FOOT BROTH. Boil three calf's feet in a gallon of water to half; when cold, take off the fat, and keep the jelly for use. When you wish to make the broth, take a cup-full, and add a small quan- tity of wine, nutmeg, and salt, and, if approved, sugar ; stirring it quickly, but do not let it boil.

22 THE invalid's OWN BOOK. LINSEED EMULSION. Take an ounce of linseeds, two drachms of liquorice root sliced and bruised; pour on them nearly a pint of boiling water. Place the jug containing the mixture on the hob by the fire for four hours, when strain it off. ♦ COMPOUND LINSEED EMULSION. Boil half a teacupful of linseeds in a pint of water for about ten minutes, skimming it as the scum rises; strain it through a sieve. Beat in with a fork half a teacupful of new milk; sweeten with honey or sugar, and flavour it with lemon- peel or cinnamon, or a few sliced bitter almonds boiled in it. —♦— WHITE OF EGG EMULSION. Beat the whites of two fresh eggs Avith a few table-spoonfuls of milk or cream, half a tea- spoonful of powdered gum-arabic, and a small quantity of honey or sugar. This is very efficacious to swallow gently in sore throat.

For roasting a wild duck, you must have a clear brisk fire ; for it must be browned upon the outside, without being sodden within. To have it well froth- ed and full of gravy is the nicety. Prepare the fire by stirring and raking just before it is laid down, and fifteen or twenty minutes will do it. This is the fashionable way ; but if it is required a little more done, allow it a few minutes more : if it is too much, it will lose all its fine high flavour. For the sauce, see INo. 338.

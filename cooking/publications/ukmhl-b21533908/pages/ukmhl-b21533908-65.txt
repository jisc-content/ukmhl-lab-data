THE COOK’S ORACLE. BOILING. • * * 4 GENERAL RULES. This most simple of culinary processes is not often performed in perfection. Boiling requires less nicety and attendance than roasting; and to skim your pot ’well, and keep it really boiling (the slower the better) all the while, and to know how long is required for doing the joint, &c. com- prehends almost the whole art and mystery. This, however, requires a patient and perpetual vigi- lance, of which few persons are capable. — The cook must take care that the water really boils all the while she is cooking, or she will be deceived in the time; and she should make up a sufficient fire at first, to last all the time, without much mending or stirring.

Partridge Soup. (No. 242.) If you have two or three birds that are too old to send up as roasts, pick and draw them, cut them into three or four pieces, and put them into a large stew pan with a quarter of a pound of lean ham, a large carrot, a turnip, two onions, two heads of celery, a dozen corns of allspice, and the same of black pepper, two blades of mace, and two or three cloves finely pounded : pour in a pint of cold water, set the stewpan over a quick fire, and watch it till the water has all boiled away ; take care it does not burn : when the par- ti tridges and ham have taken a good browning, add two quarts of boiling water, or beef or veal broth is better, if you have it; let it stew slowly for two hours, then strain it through a sieve into a clean stewpan.

VEGETABLES. Red Beet Roots (No. 127.) Are not so much used as they deserve ; they are boiled exactly in (he same way as parsnips : a large root will take an hour and a half boiling ; to be sent to table with salt fish, boiled beef, &c. Parsnips (No. 12S.) Are to be done just in the same manner as carrots, only give them as much water as you can ; they require more or less time according to their size, and you must try them by thrusting a fork into them as they are in the water; when that goes easily through, they are done enough : an hour and a quarter will boil a large parsnip.

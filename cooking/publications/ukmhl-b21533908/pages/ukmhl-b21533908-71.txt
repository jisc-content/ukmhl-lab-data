In plain English, is understood to mean boiled beef; but its culinary acceptation, is fresh beef dressed without boiling, but kept gently simmer- ing over a slow7 fire. English cooks seem to have no notion that good soup can be made without destroying a great deal of meat: however, by a judicious regulation of the fire, and a vigilant attendance on the soup kettle, this may be accom- plished without much difficulty, and you shall have a tureen of such soup as the finest palate will be pleased with, and the meat make its appearance at table possessing its full portion of nutritious succulence. This requires nothing more than to boil or rather stew the meat slowly, instead of fast, and to take it up when it is done enough.

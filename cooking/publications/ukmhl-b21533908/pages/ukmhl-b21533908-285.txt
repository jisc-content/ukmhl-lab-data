SAUCES AND GRAVIES. Bread Sauce. (No. 321.) Take half a pint of milk, put it on to boil, with an onion in it, and two blades of mace, and a dozen corns of black pepper tied up in a muslin bag; when it has boiled a quarter of an hour, strain the milk, put it into a saucepan with two tablespoonsful of fine bread crumbs, (stale bread is best,) that have been rubbed through a cullen- der, or wire sieve, boil ten minutes in au open saucepan, taking care they do not burn. Obs. — You may rub the bread in a clean cloth, till the crumbs are as fine as if they had been grated, or rubbed through a cullender. Plain Browning (No.. 322.)

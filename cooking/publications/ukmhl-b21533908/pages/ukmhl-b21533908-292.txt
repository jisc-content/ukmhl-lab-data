Fish Gravy. (No. 333.) Skin and clean a pound of good eels or four floun- ders, cut them to pieces, and put them into a saucepan with a pint and a half of water; let them boil a little ; put in two blades of mace, ten corns of whole pepper, a bundle of sweet herbs, a piece of bread toasted brown, and a roll of lemon-peel; boil all together till it is very rich; then put into another saucepan a piece of butter about as big as an egg; melt it, dredge in some flour, toss it about till it is brown, strain the gravy to it, and mix all well together.

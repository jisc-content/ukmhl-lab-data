When you have kept two or three ribs of beef till quite tender, take out the bones, and skewer it as round as possible, (like a fillet of veal,) and bind it w ith tape to prevent the skewers breaking out: as the meat is more in a solid mass, it will require more time at the fire than in the preced- ing receipt; and a piece of ten or twelve pounds weight will not be well and thoroughly roasted in less than three hours and three quarters. For the first half hour it should not be less than 12 inches from the fire, that it may get gradually warm to the centre : the last half hour before it will be finished, take oft'the tape, sprinkle a little salt over it, and flour and froth it.

Turkey. (No. 57 ) A fowl and a turkey require the same manage- ment at the fire, only the latter will take more time. Let them be carefully picked, &c. and twist up a sheet of large clean writing paper, light it, and tho- roughly singe the turkey all over, turning it about over the flame. Be careful when you draw them to preserve the liver, and not to break the gall-bag, as no washing will take off the bitter taste it gives, where it once touches. Prepare a nice clear brisk fire, for if the fire be poor and dead, your poultry will be vapid and ill-tasted. A very brisk and clear fire will only answer the purpose, and this will give them their true taste, and make them look beautiful.

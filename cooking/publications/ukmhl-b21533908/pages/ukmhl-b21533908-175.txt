Scolloped Oysters. (No. 182.) Parboil twenty-four oysters in as much water as will just cover them, strain the liquor, and add to it about an ounce of fresh butter, a tablespoonful of flour, a teaspoonfut of salt, and half a teaspoon- ful of ground black pepper, a roll of the rind of a lemon, and a little grated nutmeg. Beard and wash the oysters, and let them stew slowly for a few minutes in the above liquor, put them into scallop shells, with a little of the liquor in each, and cover them with some fine bread crumbs, put little bits of butter on the top, and bake and brown them in a Dutch oven.

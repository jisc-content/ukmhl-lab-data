Cumberland Pudding, (No. 556.) Beat four eggs well, mix them with a pint of milk and four ounces of lump sugar pounded and sifted ; raisins stoned, currants washed and picked, ap- ples chopped fine, of each four ounces; a little grated nutmeg, and a very little salt. Mix all well together: dip a pudding cloth in boiling water, wring it, and flour it; put your pudding in, and boil it three hours and a half; send it up with pudding sauce in a boat; see re- ceipt, No. 268.

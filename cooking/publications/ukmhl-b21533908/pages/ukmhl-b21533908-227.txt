To make your soup, you must have one pound of good lean ham, a knuckle of veal, and an old fowl; cut your ham in slices, and lay them at the bottom of a large stewpan, cut your veal to pieces and your fowl, and put them to the ham, with a ladleful of broth, or water ; set your stew- pan on the fire, and let it reduce till it begins,to stick to the stewpan, then cover your meat with broth, or water, skim it well, put in some salt, with two carrots, one onion, two cloves, a head of celery, two turnips, and one parsnip ; let it sim- mer four or five hours, then strain the liquor through a hair sieve ; three quarts is all you must expect, if you like to have good soup ; then have three quarters of a pound of parmesan cheese grated ; when ready to dish, take away the onion and bayleaf from the maccaroni, put at the bottom of jrour tureen a bed of maccaroni, then a bed of parmesan cheese, and so on, till all is in ; pour your soup over, and send it up as hot as possible.

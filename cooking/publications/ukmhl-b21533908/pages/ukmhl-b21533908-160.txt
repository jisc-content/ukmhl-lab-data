Clean and wash your fish, and rub a little salt in the inside of it; if the weather is very cold, a large cod is always the better for being kept a day : put plenty of water in your fish-kettle, so ' that the fish may be well covered ; put in a hand- ful of salt: when the water boils, stop its boiling by pouring in a pint of cold water, and then put in your fish ; a very small fish will require from twenty to thirty minutes, a very large one near an hour; drain it on the fish-plate; dish it with garnish of the roe, liver, chitterling, See. Salt Fish Boiled. (No. 150.) Salted fish requires soaking, according to the time it has been in salt; that which is hard and dry requires two nights soaking ; the intermediate day lay it on a stone floor ; for the barrelled cod, less time will do ; and for the best Dogger Bank split fish, which has not been more than a fort- night or three weeks in salt, less time will be needful.

See No. 398. Apple Sauce. (No. 304.) Pare, core, and slice three good sized baking apples, put them into a pint saucepan, (cover it close, because the steam helps to do them,) with one clove, a bit of cinnamon about as big as a clove, a roll of lemon-peel carefully pared thin, without any of the white, one tablespoonful of cold water, and a teaspoonful of moist sugar; set the saucepan near (but not on) a slow lire ; be sure to put them on a couple of hours before dinner, for some apples will take an hour’s stewing, arid ofliers will be ready to press in a quarter of an hour : when the apples are done enough, take out the spice and lemon-peel, and mash the apples with a wooden spoon* Mushroom Sauce.

Beat a quarter of a pound of fresh butter until it creams, add to it two ounces of powdered and sifted sugar, two tablespoonsful of flour, a dessert-spoonful of brandy, and four eggs, first beaten and then strained. Mix all well together, butter six small cups, put the pudding into them, and bake them for twenty minutes. CHERRY-AND-CURRANT TART. Stem and stone your cherries : take an equal weight of very ripe red currants, press them through a sieve, add the juice to your cherries with the crumb of two penny sponge-cakes, a quarter of a pound of sugar, and a wineglassful of brandy. Put it into a tart-dish lined with a rim of paste, cover it with a top crust, and bake it for an hour. QUICK-MADE MARROW PUDDING.

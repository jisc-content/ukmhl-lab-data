When eaten cold, some rich cream may be poured over the top before sending to table, which will greatly increase its richness. CUED PUDDINGS. Take a good pint of curds well pressed from the whey; rub them through a sieve ; add the crumb of two penny rolls, a grated nutmeg, six ounces of butter first melted, a teacupful of cream, and enough sugar to sweeten it: work it well together with a spoon; butter the insides of small pudding-cups, three parts fill them with the mixture, and bake them for ten minutes or a quarter of an hour. Serve them in the cups, or turn them into a dish, as you fancy. RASPBERRY DUMPLINGS. Make a good suet crust as for a pudding, roll it out, but not too thin, spread it over with raspberry- jam, roll it up, tie it in a cloth, and boil it for an hour. When done, cut it into five pieces, pour melted butter into the dish, and strew the dumplings thickly over with sifted loaf-sugar. If preferred, you can make the crust with lard or butter, add your jam, and simply bake in a mode- rately-heated oven.

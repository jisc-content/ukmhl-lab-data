Boil a good-sized stick of vanilla in a pint of new milk, then remove the vanilla, stir in a large spoonful of arrowroot, first mixed smooth with a little cold water; stir it^overthe fire until it is thick enough; sweeten it; add the yolks of four eggs, and the whites of two. Line the edge of a buttered tart-dish with a rim of paste; put in the pudding; pour a little cream upon the top, and bake it for half an hour. OXFOED PUDDINGS. Wash four ounces of currants, add four ounces of finely-grated biscuits, four ounces of shred suet, a dessert-spoonful of powdered sugar, and some nut- meg; mix all well together with the yolks of three eggs; shape them into balls of the size of turkey- eggs, and fry them nicely brown.

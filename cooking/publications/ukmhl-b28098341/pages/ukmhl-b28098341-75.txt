Grate the rinds of two lemons, rubbing the grater well with a quarter of a pound of sifted loaf-sugar, add a quarter of a pound of finely-shred beef-suet, four eggs well whisked, the juice of a lemon, and a tablespoonful of white wine; work the ingredients well together, put them into a buttered basin, tie it well down and boil for forty minutes. Serve with plain melted butter and sugar. FRENCH FLUMMERY. Put half an ounce of the best isinglass into a quart of cream or good new milk. Let it simmer gently over a slow fire for twenty minutes ; then take it off, sweeten it to your taste, and put in a spoonful each of rose and orange-flower water: strain it into a mould, and when quite cold, turn it out upon a dish.

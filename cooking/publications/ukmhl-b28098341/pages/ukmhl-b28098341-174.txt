Brandy is often substituted for the wine. JAM SAUCE. Take two good dessert-spoonsful of jam; stir it over the fire in a glazed saucepan until pretty hot, then add two ounces of fresh butter and a table- spoonful of brandy, wine, cider, or grape-juice : when the butter is melted, serve. CUKEANT SAUCE. Put a dessert-spoonful of cleaned and dried cur- rants, a dessert-spoonful of brandy, and a dessert- spoonful of sugar into half a pint of thick melted butter ; stir it over the fire until quite hot. CIDEE SAUCE. Simmer down a pint of cider and a quarter of a pound of loaf-sugar until it is quite a syrup; then put in two ounces of fresh butter, and when this is melted, serve.

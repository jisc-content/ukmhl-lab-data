CREAM PUDDING, BOILED. Take a pint of sweet cream, boil it with a blade of mace and a little nutmeg, then strain it, and let it stand to cool. Beat the yolks of four eggs and the whites of two, mix with them a dessert-spoonful of flour, two ounces of blanched sweet-almonds, pounded to a paste, and a spoonful of orange-flower water. Gradually mix these ingredients with the cream, heat it up well, take a doubled cloth, wet and well flour it, pour in the pudding, tie it tight, and boil it for twenty minutes in a good deal of water. When done, turn it into a dish, and pour over it melted butter and sugar. WHORTLEBERRY PIE.

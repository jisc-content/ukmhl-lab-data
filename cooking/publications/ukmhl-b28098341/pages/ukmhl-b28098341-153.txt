Take a sufficient quantity of the pith of an ox; soak it in water for a night; then free it from the skin, and beat it with a little orange-flower water until quite smooth: then scald half a pint each of new milk and cream; pour them upon a quarter of a pound of blanched and beaten almonds, a table- spoonful of grated bread ; add this to the pith; sugar it to taste, and stir in the yolks of three eggs and the whites of two. Line a dish with a puff-paste; lay in your pudding, put some pieces of butter on the top and bake it for an hour in a moderate oven. HUNTERSâ€™ PUDDING.

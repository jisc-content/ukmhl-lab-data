AY ell wash some sprigs of thyme; pick off, singly, a quarter of an ounce of the tender leaves; mix them with the crumb of a penny-roll grated, three-quarters of a pound of finely-shred beef-suet, a dessert-spoon- ful of sugar, four eggs well beaten, and enough brandy to make it the consistency of paste. Form it into dumplings ; flour them well outside ; put them into cloths, and boil them for three-quarters of an hour. Serve with wine sauce poured over them. BLACK-CURRANT TART. Lightly stem and top the currants, being careful not to bruise them; put them into a tart-dish with a rim of paste, and, as they are considered to be too rich by themselves, it is advisable to add a little white-currant juice or cider to dilute their flavour : throw in a good deal of sugar, cover them with a top crust, and bake rather more than an hour.

FRESH RASPBERRY OR STRAWBERRY CREAM. A pint and a half of fresh fruit beaten with half a pound of loaf-sugar, and the juice of a lemon; stir to it a pint and a half of cream, or half that quantity of cream and half of new milk, putting the cream first. Beat it long till it bears a fine froth, and put it in glasses or in a glass dish. FIG PUDDING. Half a pound of figs finely chopped, half a pound of bread-crumbs, half a pound of suet, quarter of a pound of sugar, some lemon-peel, and three eggs; mix all together, the same as for plum-pudding, and boil it for two hours.

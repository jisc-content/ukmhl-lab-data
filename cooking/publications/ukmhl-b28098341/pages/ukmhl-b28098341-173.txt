PUDDING SAUCES. 171 add half an ounce of powdered and sifted ginger, the juice of half a lemon, and two ounces of white sugar. Note—all sauces should he sent to the table as hot as possible. SWEET EGG SAUCE. Put the yolks of four hard-boiled eggs into a mortar with an equal weight of fresh butter and sugar; beat it smooth, then dilute it with a suffi- ciency of either milk or white wine as agreeable: add the grated rind of half a lemon; give it a boil, and serve. EGG SAUCE A L’OEDINAIEE. Lightly chop two hard-boiled eggs, and mix them with half a pint of melted butter ; sweetened to the taste. SAUCE AU YIN. Pour half a pint of wine upon the yolks of three eggs; beat it together for ten minutes; add sugar, grated lemon-peel, and cinnamon to your taste. Warm it, but do not let it come to a boil.

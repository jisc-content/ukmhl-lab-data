As to the Gauls, it is certain that, six centuries before Christianity was introduced, they knew the use of wine; for, when the Phocaeans came to found Marseilles, Petta, the daughter of a king of the country, presented, according to Athenaxus, to Euxenes their chief, a cup filled with wine and water. But who first planted the vine in Gaul, and who first cultivated it there? It would be difficult to answer these questions. According to Justin and Strabo, it appears that the Phocaeans were not only the first to introduce the vine among the Gauls, but the first to learn them to cut and cultivate it.

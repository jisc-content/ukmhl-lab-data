When it is set, take this division of the Plaster cast off, clean the edges carefully with your knife, and notch the edges as checks, or guides, for joining the other divisions. Oil it with a small brush, and put it back on the figure, then pour in more wet Plaster, and make another section or division of the Mould. Thus a Mould that could not be made in two pieces may be successfully formed in three or four. Dry the Moulds in the stove, and, when sufficiently set, give them a few coats of linseed oil to make them hard. Between each coat of oil, put them in the stove to dry.

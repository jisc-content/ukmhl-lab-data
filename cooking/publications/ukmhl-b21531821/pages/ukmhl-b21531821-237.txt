To preserve Apples during the winter. Spread a coarse cloth on the floor in an upper room, place a layer of apples, then a cloth, and so on alternately to any height you please; then throw a large coarse cloth over the whole, taking great care that the cloth be turned under the edge of the cloth first laid on the floor, all the way round so as to communicate with the floor on every side. The Americans fold them singly in papers, instead of laying cloths between the layers. 818. Another method of preserving Apples.

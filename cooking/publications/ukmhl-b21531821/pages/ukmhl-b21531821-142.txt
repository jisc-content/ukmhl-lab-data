116 PIES, TARTS, &C. 468. Mince Pies. Take six good-sized lemons, squeeze out the juice, and scrape out all the pulp and skins, then boil the rinds till they are quite tender, changing the water five or six times to take out the bitterness; chop them in a bowl with half a pound of apples and a pound of raisins stoned, add a pound of currants, a pound of sugar, the juice of the lemons, and three quarters of a pound of butter melted and stirred up well amongst them ; put it close down in a pot and tie a paper or bladder over it, and it will keep six or seven weeks in a cool, dry place. A little mace, and candied orange or lemon may be added, if approved. 459. Egg Mince Pies. Prune Tart. Give the prunes a scald, take out the stones and break them; put the stones into a little cranberry-juice, simmer with the prunes and sugar, and when cold make it into a tart. 462.

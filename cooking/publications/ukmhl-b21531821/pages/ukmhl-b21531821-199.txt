Take a pound of flour, rub into it half a pound of butter, the name of powdered sugar, and a little beaten mace; beat four eggs very well (leaving out two whites) with three spoons- ful of yeast, put to it quarter of a pint of warm cream, strain them into your flour, and make it up light, set it before the fire to rise; just before you put it in the oven put in three quarters of a pound of currants. Bake it in small tins in a quick oven. 674. Wedding Cake. Beat two pounds of butter to cream with the hand, then put in one pound of fine sugar sifted, half a pound of almonds blanched and beaten a little with orange-flower water, two pounds of flour well dried, half an ounce of beaten mace and cinnamon, mix these well together; then beat the yolks and whites of sixteen eggs separately, put to them a glass of rose-water; put the flour and eggs to the butter and sugar by degrees, and beat it with the hand an hour; then put in two pounds of currants cleaned and cried, half a pound of citron, and half a pentad of candied lemon. Butter a tin and bake it three hou*s.

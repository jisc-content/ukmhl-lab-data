Almond Cream. Blanch and beat four ounces of almonds, with about half an ounce of bitter ones, adding a little fose-water to prevent them from oiling ; boil a quart of cream, and let it stand while you beat the whites of four eggs to a froth, mix them with the almonds, then stir them in the cream till well mixed, adding sugar to the taste, set it on the fire to sim- mer, stirring it one way till it thickens, then pour it into a glass or china dish. When the cream is quite cold, stick some almonds in, blanched and cut lengthwise. 617. Barley Cream. Take quarter of a pound of French barley, boil it in three or four waters till tender; then set a pint of cream on the fire with some mace and nutmeg ; when it begins to boil, drain out the barley from the water, put in the cream, and let it boil till it be rather thick and soft, then season it with sugar and salt.

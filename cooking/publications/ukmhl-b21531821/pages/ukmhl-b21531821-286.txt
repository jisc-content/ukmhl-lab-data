Set half a pint of sherbet, and half a pint of water, or ginger beer, on the fire, beat three eggs very well, adding a little cold water ; when it boils, pour it by degrees upon the egg, stirring it, then return it into the pan, with sugar and nutmeg to the taste, stirring it a little on the tire, but do not let it boil; strain and serve it with toast. Ginger-beer atone may be midled the same way. MISCELLANEOUS. 983. To make Coffee a simple and easy ivay. Pour a quart of boiling water on a tea-cupful of fresh- ground coffee, set it on the fire, and keep it on the point of boiling for three or four minutes, holding it over the fire and taking it off at pleasure, so as to keep up the temperature, but to prevent any violent ebullition ; pour out a cupful two or three times, returning it into the coffee-kettle; set it on the hob a few minutes till the coffee clears. Coffee may be made at the table in a coffee-pot with a strainer in it, by infusion only, in the proportion of one cup of coffee-powder to six cups of boiling water, and it requires only a very few minutes to have good, clear coffee. 984.

To a quart of coarse oatmeal, add gradually two quarts of water, so that the whole may mix smoothly; stir it continually on the fire till it has boiled quarter of an hour ; then pour it out, and stir in a little salt and but- ter, with, or without pepper. This quantity will serve five or six persons for a moderate meal. 1245. Scotch Short Bread. Mix three ounces of good moist, sugar with a pound of fiour, take half a pound of butter, wash out the salt, and rub two ounces of it in the flour and sugar, melt the other six ounces, and mix up the paste with it; roll it out about half an inch thick, and mark it in squares ; stick caraway comfits on the top, and bake it in a moderate oven. When cold, break it in the squares. 1246. Pease and Mushroom Pie. Season some rather young pease and mushrooms with pepper and salt, add some butter, and a little water, cover with a good crust, and bake it. 1247. Beans and Cauliflower.

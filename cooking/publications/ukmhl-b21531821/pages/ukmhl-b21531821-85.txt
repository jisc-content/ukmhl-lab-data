SAUCES, &c. 214. To Melt Butler, which is rarely well done, though a very essential Article. Mix in the proportion of a tea-spoonful of flour to four ounces of the best butter, on a trencher; put it into a small saucepan, and twp or three table spoonsful of hot water, boil it quick a minute, shaking it all the time. Milk used instead of water, requires rather less butter, and looks whiter. A more economical and plain way of making it is to take about quarter of a pint of water, dredge into it as much flour as will make it a proper thickness, boil it, and then stir in about two ounces of butter till well mixed. Do not set it on the fire afterwards. 215. Melted Butter.

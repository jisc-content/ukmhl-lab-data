Queen Anne's Biscuits. A pound of flour well dried, half a pound of fine sugar powdered and sifted, a pound of currants well washed and picked, and half a pound of butter. Rub the butter in the flour, then mix in the sugar and currants, add ten spoons- ful of cream, the yolks of three eggs, three spoonsful of rose-water, and a little mace pounded fine. When the paste is well worked up, set it in a dish before the fire till it be thoroughly warm, then make it up into cakes, put them on a tin well buttered, prick them full of holes on the top, and bake them in a quick oven. 638.

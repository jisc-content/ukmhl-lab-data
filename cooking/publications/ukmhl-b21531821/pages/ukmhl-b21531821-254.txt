Repeatedly expose the fluid as it is obtained, and if the season be very cold, a pint of strong vinegar will be reduced, by the frequent exposure, to about a table-spoonful of fine-flavoured essence, and very pungent. 871, Vinegar in Balls. Take bramble-berries when about half ripe, dry them, and then beat them to powder, make it up into balls with strong vinegar, as large as nuts, dry them very well, and keep them in boxes. When wanted for use, dissolve a ball in some stale ginger beer, or vinegar that is not so sour and good as you wish, and it will become strong vinegar. Green bramble-berries, put into good sherbet or wine, will make good vinegar in a few hours. 872. Common Vinegar. Dissolve two pounds of molasses in nine quarts of water, pour it into a vessel with half a peck of cowslip pips, when cool add yeast, expose it to the rays of the sun and in three months bottle it for use. 873. Walnut Catsup. Wipe a hundred walnuts when fit to pickle, slice and pound them in a mortar with three quarters of a pound of bay- salt; boil two quarts of good gooseberry vinegar and pour it upon them, let it stand two days, then strain it off and bottle it, put a clove of garlic into each bottle; a quart more vinegar may be poured over the walnuts after the first is drawn eff; it will serve for present use if well stirred. 874.

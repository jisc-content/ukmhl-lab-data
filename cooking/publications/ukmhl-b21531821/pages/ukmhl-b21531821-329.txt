Let Electiicity (particularly in accidents from lightning) be early employed by a medical assistant. If apparently Dead from Apoplexy. The patient should be placed in a cool air, and the clothes loosened, particularly about the neck and breast. Bleed- ing must early be employed by a medical assistant; the quantity regulated by the state of the pulse. Cloths soaked in cold water, spirits, or vinegar and water, should be kept applied to the head, which should be instantly shaved. All stimulants should be avoided. In cases of Coup-de soleil, or strokes of the sun, the same means to be used as in apoplexy.

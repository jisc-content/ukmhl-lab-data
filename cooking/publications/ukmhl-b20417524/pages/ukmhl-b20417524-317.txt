318 VALUABLE RECIPES. GEAHAM MUFFINS. Mrs. L. J. Tilton. One egg, half a cup of sugar, piece of butter the size of an egg, one cup milk, three teaspoons baking powder, Graham meal to make a batter thick enough to drop in rings without spreading; thoroughly mix the baking pow- der with the meal; melt the butter and mix well with the sugar and egg; add the milk and r^radually stir in the meal. GEAHAIVI MUFFINS Mrs. J. H. Brown. One egg, one and a half cups of sour milk, one tea- spoon saleratus, a little salt, two tablespoons of melted lard or butter, two tablespoons molasses; make stiff enough to drop from spoon. Bake fifteen minutes. EYE MUFFINS. Mrs. Bartlett. Two cups of rye, one of flour, one of sugar, one egg, one teaspoon of soda, and a little salt; mix quite stiff with sour milk. INDIAN MEAL MUFFINS. Two cups of Indian meal scalded with as little water as possible, one coffeecup of flour, one teacup of sweet milk, one tablespoon of shortening, one-half cup of brown sugar, a small cup of yeast; mixed over night.

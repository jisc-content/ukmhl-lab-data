EOAST YEAL. Mrs. D. S. F. Prepare a leg of veal for the oven, by washing, drying, and larding it with strips of fat bacon or ham, and dredg- ing it well with flour, and seasoning with salt and pepper; baste frequently and serve with the gravy thickened. A roast fillet of veal should be prepared by stuffing it with bread crumbs, seasoned with chopped ham, summer savory, pepper and salt. Dredge lightly with flour and bake. NECK PIECE OR SHOULDEE OF YEAL. Mrs. C. C. Stratton, Evanston. Put a piece of butter the size of an egg into a kettle; put it on the stove; when it begins to fry, put in the veal, season it and let it fry until brown; then add water suffi- cient to cook it. When done take cream and flour well stirred, and thicken as for fricasseed chicken, and you have a nice dinner, very like chicken and much cheaper.

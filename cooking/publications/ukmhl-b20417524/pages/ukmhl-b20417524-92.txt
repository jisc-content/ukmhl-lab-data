(88) POULTRY AND GAMK "Whoso seeks an audit here, Propitious pays his tribute — game or fish, Wild fowl or venison, and his errand speed." — COWPER. BOILED FOWL. Take a young fowl and fill the inside with oysters; ' place in a jar and plunge into a kettle of water; boil for one and one-half hours; there will be a quantity of gravy in the jar from the juice of the fowl and the oysters; make this into a white sauce with the addition of egg, cream, or a little flour and butter; add oysters, or serve up plain with the fowl. This is very nice with the addi- tion of a little parsley to the sauce. EOAST TURKEY OE CHICKEN.

To be eaten as soon as taken from the oven. Very nice. OMELET SOUFFLE. Mrs, Lamkin. One pint boiled milk, three spoons flour, yolks of seven eggs, beaten with the flour; season with pepper and salt, and add a piece of butter size of egg; mix well, then add whites well beaten ; bake twenty minutes. FRIED OMELET. Mrs. F. B. Orr. Three eggs, two gills milk, two tablespoons flour, a lit- tle salt and pepper, fried on hot griddle. FEIAR'S OMELET. Mrs. DeForest, Freeport. Boil a dozen apples, as for sauce; stir in one-fourth pound of butter, ditto white sugar; when cold, add four well beaten eggs, and a few spoons of cream ; put it into a baking dish, well buttered, and thickly strewn with bread crumbs on the bottom and sides; strew currants over the top. Bake forty-five minutes; turn on a platter, and sift sugar over it. Serve with sugar and cream^ or a boiled custard ; the latter is much the nicer. OMELET. E. V. Case, Elmhurst.

It consists of both sides of the back, or a double sir- loin, and weighs from 40 to 100 lb. It is always roasted, but is now rarely prepared, except at some festive occasions of the English Court, or at some great public entertainment. It is generally accom- panied by a boar’s head. Basil, e. Basilic, /. An aromatic culinary herb, allied to thyme. It is included in the “sweet” as well as “ savoury ” herbs, on account of its pleasant aromatic smell and taste.

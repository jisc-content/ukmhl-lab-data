DUCKS A LA FRANCOISE. Put two dozen of roasted chesnuts, peeled, into a pint of stock, with a few leaves of thyme, two small onions, a little whole pepper, and a bit of ginger; take a fine tame duck, lard it, and half roast it, then put it into the gravy ; let it stew ten minutes, and add a quarter of a pint of red wine ; when the duck is done, take it out, boil up the gravy to a proper thickness, skim it very clean from the fat, lay the duck in the dish, and pour the sauce over it: garnish with lemon.

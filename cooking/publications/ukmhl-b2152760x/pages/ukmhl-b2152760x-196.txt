THE IMPERIAL AND 170 SWEETS. CHANTILLA CAKE. Cut a piece out of the top of a savoy cake, and scoop out the inside; put it on the dish that is to be sent to table; pour Lisbon wine into the cake, as the wine soaks out; pour it over the cake with the spoon; when the cake has absorbed as much wine as it can, pour the remainder in the dish, and pour custard down the sides, and also pour some in the middle; whip up some cream, the same as for a trifle, and put it in the middle of the cake; blanch a few sweet almonds, cut them in quarters, and stick them round the edges and on the sides of the cake.

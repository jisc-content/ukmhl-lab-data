PANCAKES. Beat up six eggs, leaving out half the whites, and stir them into a quart of milk; mix your flour first with a little of the milk, add the rest by degrees ; put in two spoonfuls of beaten ginger, a glass of brandy, and a little salt; put a piece of butter into your pan, then pour in a ladleful of batter, which will make a pancake ; move the pan round, that the batter may spread all over it: shake the pan; and when you think one side is done enough, turn it; and when the other is done, lay it on a dish before the fire, and serve it as quick as possible.

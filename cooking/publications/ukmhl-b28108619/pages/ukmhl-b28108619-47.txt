Note.—Phosphorus, an important element necessary to the nutrition of the tissues and to growth, is found in all proteid- containing foods, such as meat, milk, eggs, wheat and oatmeal, and the legumes. Iron abounds in meat and in the yolk of eggs —there is very little in milk; oat and wheat-meal and the legumes also furnish it. Lime, a necessary element, especially in the diet of children, exists largely in milk and eggs, also in rice and bread.

Then flavour it to taste with fruit syrup or fresh fruit juice. Make hot and use as required. Note this sauce is equally good when cold. Sauce au Sirop is also very cheaply made. Place 1 lb. of sifted sugar in a clean sugar boiler or sauce-pan. Add to it a gill of water, and stir over the fire until the sugar has become thoroughly dissolved. Then allow it to boil until it has become a very light syrup, but do not touch it while boiling. Remove from the fire ; add about a tea-spoon- ful of fresh butter and a tea-spoonful of either essence of lemon, ratafia or vanilla, whichever is preferred.

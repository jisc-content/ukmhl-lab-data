126 CHAPTER VI Entries And now we come to what is by many people considered the most important branch of all in the cook’s education, viz., the cook- ing of entrees. Entrees in The term entree may be said to embrace a very large field, for, from the mutton cutlets and tomato sauce of the English plain cook, to the Quails en Casserole or Ortolans a la Viennese of the chef with a world-wide reputation, almost every made dish is termed “an entree.” No matter how indifferently—I had almost said badly —it may be cooked.

Then add, one at a time, three well beaten eggs, and a tea- spoonful of essence of vanilla or lemon, whichever is preferred. When of the right substance the mixture should drop slowly from a spoon. Place on one side and leave for an hour. Then add the whites of two eggs whisked to a firm froth with a pinch of salt and a few drops of lemon juice; stir it lightly yet thoroughly. Have ready a saucepan half full of boiling lard or clarified beef dripping from which the blue smoke is rising. Drop in the mixture from a wooden spoon, a very few spoonsful at a time, and fry till of a light golden hue. Take out quickly, drain carefully on clean kitchen paper.

Pouiet Saute Poulet Saute A l’Espagnol.—This is Spanish method another nice way of treating a chicken. Take a spring chicken, joint it neatly, and reserve on a plate. Place a large spoonful of Lucca oil in a clean enamelled iron stew-pan, bring it to the boil, then add to it a large onion, peeled and sliced, and a tea-spoonful of mixed parsley. Add the chicken and fry for fifteen minutes, turning frequently in order that the meat may become equally browned all over. Next drain on clean kitchen paper, and reserve till cold. When cold dip each piece into well beaten yolk of egg, and then into fine bread-crumbs. Have ready a saucepan half full of boiling lard. Add the joints of chicken, and fry till of a very bright golden brown colour. Take out quickly, drain care- fully, and serve piled high on a doyley

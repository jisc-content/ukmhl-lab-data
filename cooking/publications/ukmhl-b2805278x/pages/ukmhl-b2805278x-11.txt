I THE ART OF PREPARING VEGETABLES FOR THE TABLE. Introduction In attempting to promote the full appreciation of garden products, and their economical employment as articles of food, we cannot entertain the hope of offering to the experienced cook or housekeeper anything deserving to be called ‘new,’ and it is no part of our purpose to indulge in speculations or disquisitions that would take us away from the busi- ness that is immediately in hand. It is intended, therefore, in what follows to present something like an elementary code of vegetable cookery that the cooks themselves may not disdain, and that many who are not cooks, but desire to learn something of the art of cookery, may give attention to as of direct practical value to them.

COMMON SENSE. 334 Bread Cake. On baking-day, take from your dough, after its second rising—2 cups risen dough. Have ready, also— 2 cups white sugar. 1 cup butter, creamed with the sugar. 3 eggs. 1 even teaspoonful soda, dissolved in hot water. 2 tablespoonfuls sweet milk—cream is better. £ lb. currants, well washed and dredged. 1 teaspoonful nutmeg. 1 “ cloves. Beat the yolks very light, add the creamed butter and sugar, the spice, milk, soda, and dough. Stir until all are well mixed; put in the beaten whites, lastly the fruit. Beat hard five minutes, let it rise twenty minutes in two well-buttered pans, and bake half an hour or until done.

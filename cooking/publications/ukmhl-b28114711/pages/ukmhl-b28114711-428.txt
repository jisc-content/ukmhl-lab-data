412 COMMON SENSE. Custard Sauce. 1 pint of milk. 2 eggs, beaten very light. i wineglass of brandy. 1 cup powdered sugar, stirred into the eggs. Nutmeg to taste. 1 teaspoonful vauilla. Heat the milk to boiling, and add by degrees to the beaten eggs and sugar; put in the nutmeg, and set within a saucepan of boiling water. Stir until it begins to thicken. Take it off and add the brandy gradually. Set, until it is wanted, within a pan of boiling water. Pour over the pudding when it comes from the mould. Jelly Sauce. i cup currant jelly. 1 tablespoonful butter, melted. i dessert spoonful arrowroot or corn-starch; wet with cold water. 1 glass pale Sherry. 3 tablespoonfuls boiling water. Stir the arrowroot into the boiling water and heat, stir- ring all the time, until it thickens; add the butter, and set aside until almost cool, when beat in, spoonful by spoonful, the jelly to a smooth pink paste. Pour in the wine, stir hard, and heat in a tin vessel, set within another of boiling water, until very hot.

If soaked in warm water, and the same be changed once or twice for warmer, it will boil soft in an hour. Boil in the last water. Fried Hominy. If large, put a good lump of butter or dripping in the frying-pan, and heat. Turn in some cold boiled hominy, and cook until the under-side is browned. Place a dish upside down on the frying-pan and upset the latter, that the brown crust may be upppermost. Eat with meat. Cut the small hominy in slices and fry in hot lard or drippings. Or, moisten to a soft paste with milk ; beat in some melted butter, bind with a beaten egg, form into round cakes with your hands, dredge with flour and fry a light brown. Hominy Croquettes. To a cupful of cold boiled hominy (small-grained) add a tablespoonful melted butter and stir hard, moistening, by degrees, with a little milk, beating to a soft light paste. Put in a teaspoonful of white sugar, and lastly, a well- beaten egg. Roll into oval balls with floured hands, dip in beaten egg, then cracker-crumbs, and fry in hot lard. Very good!

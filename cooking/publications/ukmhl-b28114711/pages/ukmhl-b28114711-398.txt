Cracker Pudding. i 1 quart milk. 1 cup powdered cracker. 5 eggs. 2 tablespoonfuls melted butter. £ teaspoonful soda—dissolved in boiling water. Heat the milk slightly, and pouring it over the cracker, let them stand together fifteen minutes. Stir into this first the beaten yolks, then the butter and soda; beat all smooth and add the whipped whites. Eat hot, with pudding sauce. Dorchester Cracker Plum Pudding. 2 quarts milk. 6 Boston crackers—split and buttered. 8 eggs—beaten very light. 2 cups sugar. Nutmeg, cloves, and cinnamon to taste. 1 teaspoonful of salt. 1 lb. raisins, seeded and cut in two. Make a custard of the milk, eggs, and sugar, seasoned with the spices, by heating the milk almost to boiling, then taking it from the fire and adding gradually the yolks, sugar, seasoning, and whites. Do not boil it again. Butter a pudding-dish ; put a layer of crackers in the bottom, moistening with a few spoonfuls of the hot custard.

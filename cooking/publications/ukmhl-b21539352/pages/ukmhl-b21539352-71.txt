Open Golden Tart. For Four Persons.—Enough short paste to line a mould; 2 ozs. of grated bread crumbs; i cupful of golden syrup. Line a plain fiat mould with short paste, rolled rather thin. Mix some golden syrup with some finely grated bread crumbs, cover the paste about half an inch in thickness. Add more golden syrup from time to time while baking if the contents of the tart seems too dry. Blay Custard Pudding, For Four or Five Persons.—4 eggs; 1 pint of milk; 2 tablespoonfuls of castor sugar; % lb. of apricot jam; 1 oz. of bread crumbs; % saltspoonful of vanilla. Beat the yolks of four eggs to a cream, add a pint of milk and two tablespoonfuls of castor sugar. Whip the whites of two eggs to a stiff froth, adding a pinch of salt.

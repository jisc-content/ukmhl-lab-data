BROWN STOCK. 2 lbs. shin or shoulder of beef. Small carrot. Small onion. 10 small peppercorns. Stick of celery, or | tea spoonful celery seeds tied in muslin. ^ teaspoonful salt. 2 quarts of cold water. Measure the water into a strong pan; add salt. Wipe and cut the meat into small pieces, removing fat and skin, and add to the water. Scrape the bone well, remove the marrow, wash to remove the fat, and put into the pan. Bring the stock slowly to boiling-point; about three-quarters of an hour. Remove any grey- looking scum ; add the prepared vegetables cut into large pieces. Simmer the stock from three to four hours. Strain through a hair sieve. When cold, skim thoroughly. The stock is then ready for soups, etc.

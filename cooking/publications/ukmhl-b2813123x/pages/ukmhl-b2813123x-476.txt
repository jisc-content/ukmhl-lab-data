To be kept six months before using. Should be served with curries. 851. ROSE SAUCE. (For puddings, rice moulds, &c.) Peel and slice a fine beetroot; put it into a bright saucepan with a pint and a half of water ; boil gently for twenty minutes, then add two pounds and a half of loaf sugar, the thin rind and strained juice of a lemon, and half a stick of vanilla. Boil quickly, and skim constantly till the liquor becomes a rich thick syrup of a deep rose colour; then strain. When nearly cold, stir in a gill of French brandy; and when quite cold, bottle and cork it closely. It will keep any length of time, if properly made. The vanilla, if washed in cold water and dried, will serve for a second or third use. The beetroot dredged with pounded loaf sugar, and dried on a wire sieve in a cool oven, may be served as a sweetmeat. 852. MUSHROOM SAUCE.

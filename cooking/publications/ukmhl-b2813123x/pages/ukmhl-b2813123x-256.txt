Trim off the fat and pith of six or seven cutlets from the best end of a neck of mutton ; put them into a pie-dish, with a tea- spoonful of salt, a saltspoonfnl of pepper, and three ounces of butter; cover the dish closely, and bake in a slow oven nearly an hour. Make a sauce as follows :—Clean and cut up one small carrot, a small head of celery, half a small turnip, an endive, a handful of spinach, and two Jerusalem artichokes; put them into a saucepan, with half a pint of stock or gravy, a salt- spoonful of salt, the sixth part of a nutmeg, grated, the grated peel of half a lemon ; boil for three-quarters of an hour; drain the cutlets before the fire on a napkin; stir the butter into the sauce ; boil up. Rub the sauce through a fine hair sieve ; add the strained juice of a lemon ; and serve the sauce in the centre of the dish, and the cutlets round it, the bones all meeting in the centre. 188.

This receipt is for two large sweetbreads. 287. SWEETBREADS, PLAIN-. Trim and blanch the sweetbreads as directed in the preceding receipt; dip them into a beaten egg, then into fine dried crumbs; place them on a tin dish; pour over each, three ounces of dis- solved butter, and bake for an hour in a moderate oven, basting constantly, and serve. Garnish with cut lemon. Note.â€”If the sweetbreads be small, thirty-five or forty minutes will be sufficient in all cases. 288. SWEETBREADS, WITH TRUFFLES. Blanch the sweetbreads in boiling water five minutes, and in cold for one hour ; remove the fat, skin, and pith ; dry them, and dredge them lightly with baked flour ; place them on a tin dish, baste well with dissolved butter (a quarter of a pound), and bake till nicely browned, about half an hour. Peel and slice six fresh truffles; put them into a stewpan, with a gill of Marsala, a salt- spoonful of salt, a saltspoonful of white pepper, the strained juice of a large lemon; simmer ten minutes; add a gill of stock (No. 2). Knead a dessertspoonful of baked flour with two ounces of butter, and stir in for five minutes; put in the sweetbreads, and continue to simmer half an hour longer ; then serve. 289.

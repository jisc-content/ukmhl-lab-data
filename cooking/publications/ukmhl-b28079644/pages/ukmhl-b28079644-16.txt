FISH, ETC. 4 SARDINES WITH PARMESAN. Ingredients.—One tin sardines, 2 ozs. Parmesan cheese (grated), salt, cayenne and pepper, lb. flour, one egg, 1 oz. butter, frying fat. Method.—Sift the flour on to a board, make a well in the centre of the flour, put in a pinch of salt, a little grated cheese, the yolk of the egg, and a few drops of water. Work this to a stiff but smooth paste, which must be well kneaded for at least ten minutes. Place in the cool for half an hour. Cut the tails off six or eight sardines, wipe them gently with a damp cloth. Roll out the paste as thinly as possible, and cut into oblong pieces. Oil the butter, dip each sardine in butter, and roll in grated cheese ; place on a piece of paste, and wrap up neatly ; seal the ends with white of egg. Drop into hot fat, fry gently a golden colour, take up, drain well. Dish up neatly, sprinkle over with grated cheese mixed with a pinch of cayenne pepper, and serve hot. LOBSTER CROQUETTES. Method.—Split the lobster, crack the claws, remove all the flesh of the lobster and mince it rather finely. The coral (if any) should be removed and pounded with the butter. Put the butter in a stewpan, when melted add the flour, stir over the fire for a few minutes, but do not let the flour take colour.

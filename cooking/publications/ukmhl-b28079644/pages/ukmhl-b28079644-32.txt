Ingredients.—Ten to twelve small croutes of bread, fried in clarified butter or lard, 3 ozs. fresh butter, a few sprigs of parsley, tarragon, and chervil, three large Gorgona anchovies (boned), two hard boiled eggs, a dust of Nepaul or Paprika pepper, two to three slices cooked ham. Method.—Pound the anchovies in a mortar, add to it the yolks of the hard boiled eggs and the butter. Mix well, season with a dust of pepper. Chop the parsley and other herbs finely (a teaspoonful in all), and add to the mixture. Rub this through a fine sieve, and put it in a forcing bag with a rose pipe. Stamp out the slices of ham to the size of the croutes, allowing one for each. Place these on the croutes. Force the prepared mixture tastefully on top of the ham, garnish each croute with hard boiled white of egg (rubbed through a coarse wire sieve). Dish up, garnish with sprigs of parsley, and serve.

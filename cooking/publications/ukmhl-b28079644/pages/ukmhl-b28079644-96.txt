Pour this upon hot buttered toast, cut into four, garnish with sprigs of fresh parsley, and send to table. FONDU SOUFFLEES. Ingredients.—i oz. potato flour, oz. fresh butter, one yolk and two whites of egg, f pint milk, 3 ozs. grated Parmesan cheese, seasoning. Method.—Mix the potato flour with a little cold milk, then stir on to it the remainder of the milk (boiling) ; put in the butter, and stir the mixture with a wooden spoon over the fire until it thickens. Cool a little, then add one or two yolks of eggs, the grated cheese, and seasoning (salt, pepper, nutmeg or cayenne). Beat the whites of eggs to a stiff froth, and mingle carefully with the above. Put the mixture into a well-buttered silver- plated vegetable dish, and bake from eight to ten minutes in a brisk oven. Serve immediately. The same mixture can be baked in little souffle cases instead of one large dish. COLD CHEESE SOUFFLEES.

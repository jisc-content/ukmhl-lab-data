The quantities given are sufficient for five pies. Pumpkin Pie.— 1 1/3 pints stewed pumpkin, 1 quart milk, y2 cup sugar, 3 eggs. To prepare the pumpkin, cut it into halves, re- move the seeds, divide into moderately small pieces, and bake in the oven until thoroughly done. Then scrape from the shell, rub through a colander, and proceed as follows: For one and one-third pints of the cooked pumpkin use one quart of hot, rich, sweet milk. Add one-half cup of sugar and the well-beaten yolks of three eggs; beat well together, add the whites of the eggs beaten to a stiff froth, and beat thoroughly. Line pie-tins with a stiff cream paste, fill, and bake in a moderate oven till the pies are barely firm in the center, or till the custard is well set.

Wash and cleanse thoroughly a large handful of dandelion, a good- sized bunch of chervil, three cabbage lettuces, a sprig each of balm and borage, and two handfuls of sorrel. Dry upon a clean vegetable cloth. Put two ounces of butter into an enamelled stew-pan, and as soon as it oils add the vegetables. Stir briskly till they begin to simmer, then add three pints of strong chicken broth (clear). Draw the pan to the side of the fire, and simmer gently for about half an hour. Next mix in very gently half a pint of boiling cream, a dessertspoonful of sugar, and a pinch of spice. Just before serving, stir in the well- beaten yolks of six eggs. Season to taste, make hot, and serve. Note : care must be taken, after adding the eggs, that the soup does not boil, or it will curdle and be spoilt.

To Carve a Turkey.—A turkey is usually large enough for a small company, so that the breast and wings, which are con- sidered the best parts, generally suffice. The slices should be cut from each side of the breast alternately, beginning close to the wing. A little stuffing or a small piece of liver ought to be served to each guest.

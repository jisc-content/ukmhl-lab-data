STOVES 15 methylated spirit is poured. This heats the paraffin to the point of giving off gas. This gas ignites at the point D, and unites with the atmospheric air, and a big rose jet bursts into flame at the top. The methylated spirit, being exhausted, goes out, and the heat generated by the flame now ignited continues to supply the necessary heat for the production of the gas. The pump at a has to be worked occasionally, and the more it is worked the hotter the flame. This stove has been supplied with an oven, and it is one of the quickest boiling stoves in the market. Some people consider the smell of the gas as rather a draw- back to indoor use. Another excellent make of oil stove with a blue flame

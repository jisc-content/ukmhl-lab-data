A WORD TO THE READER. WHILE foreign cook-books are accessible to all readers of foreign languages, and American ones have borrowed from them for what we know as “French cookery,” it is difficult often to judge the real value of a dish, or decide if experiment in new directions is worth while. The recipes in the following chapters, prepared originally for The Epicure, of Boston, were gathered slowly, as the author found them in use, and are most of them taken from family recipe- books, as valued abroad as at home.

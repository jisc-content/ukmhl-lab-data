Apple Charlotte. — Peel and core ten fine apples, and mince them fine. Melt in a saucepan a piece of butter the size of an egg and one cup of sugar, the grated rind of half a lemon, and half a teaspoonful of cinnamon. Stew in this syrup till they are soft — about twenty minutes. Mash fine; add two table- spoonfuls of apricot marmalade and let it cool. Butter a large charlotte mould; cut thin strips of rather stale bread, dip them in melted butter and line the mould, letting each lap a little. For the bottom, cut the bread in points, and lay them in the shape of a star or rosette. Fill the mould with the apple, cover with a large slice of but- tered bread, and bake in a moderate oven for forty minutes. Turn out on a plate and dust with powdered sugar.

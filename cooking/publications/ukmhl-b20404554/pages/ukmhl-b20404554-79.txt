BROILING AND FRYING. When a sick person recovers so far as to be past the stage for beef-tea, and able to eat a little solid food, the doctor generally orders for him a little broiled fowl or a mutton-chop. What curious, greasy, yet burned morsels come up to the sick-room, looking so unpleasant that even a person in good health, and perhaps at the time exceedingly hungry, would not like to swallow them !

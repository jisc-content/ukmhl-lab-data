If a motto were to be selected for all women who have to cook food, or to provide it for sick people, there is one, a very old-fashioned one, which I should like to recommend to them :— ' Whatsoever thy hand findeth to do, do it with thy might.' It might even be made a compound one by adding to it a second part, written more than one thousand years later than the first:— ' Not with eye service, as men tleasers ; but in singleness of heart, fearing god.'

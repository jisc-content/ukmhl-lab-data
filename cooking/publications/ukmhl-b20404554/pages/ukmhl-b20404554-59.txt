CONVALESCENT COOKEJIY. 39 ON COOKING IN GENERAL. If the cook be inexperienced, she ought to begin by carefully weighing all the proportions given in each recipe ; but by degrees, as she improves and gains confidence, she will be able in a great measure to dispense with scales. Indeed it is as well that she should accustom herself as much as possible to tell by sight the weights of many things, and to be so familiar with short ways of measuring that time may be saved without departing from exactness. It would be impossible to give an accurate table of measurements which would save the cook being obliged to use the scales, but a few general rules will be found useful.

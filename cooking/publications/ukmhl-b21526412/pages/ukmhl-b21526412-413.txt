403 GRAVIES AND SAUCES. Soup-Herb, and soon made Savoury Sauce. (No. 536.) Put an ounce and half of butter into a stew- pan ; as soon as it is warm, mix with it half an ounce of flour, and stir thoroup;hly together for a few minutes; then add a drachm of Zest, and pour in by degrees almost half a pint of boiling water, and a large spoonful of catsuj), (or if you have none, put in the like quantity of Port wine;) let it boil up for five minutes, stirring it all the time; then strain it through a fine sieve, and you have a charming extemporaneous relish for either roast or boiled poultry or game, chops, steaks, &c.

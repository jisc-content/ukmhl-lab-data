Some put it into the soup; but the best way is to send it up on a plate, as a side dish. Obs. —This is a very elegant soup, and neither expensive nor troublesome to prepare; in the Kitchens of some opulent Epicures, the roots are fried in butter, before they are put on to stew; if this is not done very carefully, and with very nicely clarified fat, all the sweet flavour of the vegetables will be 'overpowered by the rank empyreumatic savour of the fryingpan.

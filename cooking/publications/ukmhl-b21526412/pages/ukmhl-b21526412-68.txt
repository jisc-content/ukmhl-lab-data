RUDIMENTS OF COOKERY CHAPTER I. BOILING. Tins most simple of culinary processes is not often performed in perfection, though it requires less nicety and attendance than roasting, though to skim your pot well, and keep it really boiling (the slower the better) all the while, and to know how long is required for doing the joint, &c. comprehends almost the whole art and mystery. This, however, demands a patient and perpetual vigilance, of which few persons are capable. The Cook must take especial care that the water reallp boils all the while she is cooking, or she w'ill be deceived in the time; and make up a sufficient fire (a frugal Cook M'ill manage with much less fire for boiling than she uses for roasting) at first, to last all the time, without much mending or stirring.

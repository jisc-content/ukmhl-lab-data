Celery Sauce, White. — (No. 289-) Pick and wash four heads of nice white celery; cut it into pieces about an inch long; stew it in a pint of water, and a teaspoonful of salt, till the celery is tender*; roll an ounce of butter with a tablespoonful of flour; add this to half a pint of cream, and give it a boil up.

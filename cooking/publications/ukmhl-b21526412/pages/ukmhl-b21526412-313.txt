A couple of pound of the belly part of pickled pork will make very good pease soup, if the pork be not too salt; if it has been in salt more than two days, it must be laid in water the night before it is used, and put on in three quarts of water, with the ingredients mentioned in the first receipt; let it boil gently for two hours, then put in the pork, and boil very gently till it is enough to eat, this will take about an hour and a half longer; when done, wash the pork clean in hot water, send it up in a dish, and pour the soup into a tureen, with the accompaniments ordered in the first receipt for pease soup. Obs. — The meat is boiled no longer than to be done enough to be eaten; thus, you get excellent soup, without any expense of meat destroyed. Plain Pease Soap.—(No. 221.)

Send up with them, oyster, (No. 278,) egg, (No. 267,) bread, (No. 321,) and plenty of gravy sauce, (No. 329.) Capons or Fowls. — (No. 58.) These, must be killed a couple of days before they are dressed, or they will eat tough: they are managed exactly in the same manner, and sent up with the same sauces as a turkey, only they require proportionately less time at the fire; a full grown fowl, about an hour and a quarter, a moderate size one, an hour, and a chicken from thirty to forty minutes. Here also. Pork Sausages fried, are in general, a favourite accompaniment, or stuff them with turkey stuffing; see Forcemeats, (Nos. 374, 5, 6, and 7;) put in plenty of it, so as to plump out the fowl, which must be tied closely, (both at the neck and rump) to keep in the stuffing: some cooks, put the liver of the fowl into this forcemeat, and others, rub it up with flour and butter, to thicken and give flavour to the gravy, which again receives further improvement, by stewing the legs of the fowl in it, instead of sending them to table—and the fowl looks much better without them.

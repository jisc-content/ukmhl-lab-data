Cut some pickled gherkins into bits, about as big as capers; put them into half a pint of melted butter, with two teaspoonsful of lemon juice, or nice vinegar, and flavour it with a small teaspoonful of essence of shallots or gar- lick, see (Nos. 400, 401, and 402.) Dutch Sour Sauce, for Fish,—(No. 276.) Beat up the yolks of two eggs, and put them into a stewpan with two ounces of butter, a tablespoonful of flour, two blades of mace, and a tablespoonful of elder vinegar, or the same quantity of lemon juice; melt over a gentle fire, stirring the ingredients together all the while the same way, stirring in gradually a quarter pint of the water the fish was boiled in.

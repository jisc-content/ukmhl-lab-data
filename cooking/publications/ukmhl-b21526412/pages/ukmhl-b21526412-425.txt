Obs.—This is a piquante relish for broiled Uevlls, &c. Sauce for Rump Steaks, or Mutton Chops^ S^'c. — (No. 356.) See also (No. 331.) Take your chops out of the fryingpan, and for a pound of meat put in four tablespoonsful of boiling water, a large teaspoonful of flour, i. e. about three drachms, and a tablespoonful of mushroom or walnut catsup, or browning, (No. 322,) or (No. 449;) let it boil together a few minutes, and pour it through a sieve to the steaks. Obs.—To the above is sometimes added a sliced onion, a minced eshallot, or a little shallot wine, see (No. 402,) see also (No. 423,) and (No. 135,) with a tablespoonful of Port wine; to these some add a pickled cucumber or walnut, cut into little bits.

Wlien yon have picked them, powder them with a little beaten mace, or grated nutmeg, or white pepper and salt, add a little cold butter, and pound all well together in a marble mortar till It IS the consistence of paste. Put it into pots covered with clarified butter, and cover them over with wetted bladder. Lobster.—(No. 17(>.) Buy these alive, for those who sell them sometimes keep them too long before they boil them, and they then have not half their flavour. Choose those that are full of motion, which is the index of their freshness. The heaviest and those of a middle size are generally the best. Never take them when the shell is encrusted, which is a sign they are old. The male lobster is preferred to cat, and the female on account of her eggs to make sauce of.

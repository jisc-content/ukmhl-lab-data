Beef and mutton a little underdone * is not a great faultâ€”by some people it is preferred; but lamb, pork, and veal, are uneatable if not thoroughly boiled. A trivet set on the bottom of your boiling pots, raising the contents about two inches from the bottom, will be found a great improvement, and prevent that side of the meat which comes next the bottom from getting a burn, or being done too much. If you have not a trivet, use four skewers, or a soup-plate laid the wrong side upwards. Take care of the liquor you have boiled your meat in; for in these times, no good housewife has any pretensions to rational economy, who boils a joint without making some sort of soup, (see chapter 7.) If the liquor be too salt, only use half the quantity, and the rest water; and wash salted meat well with cold water before you put it into the boiler.

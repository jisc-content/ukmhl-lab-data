Tarragon Sauce. —(No. 314.) Pick and wash a handful of tarragon leaves, put them into a saucepan with half a pint of Gravy; let them stew till they are tender; rub them through a sieve; take three tablespoonsful of what they were boiled in, an ounce of butter, and a large tablespoonful of flour; warm these together, and add a tablespoonful of sherry, or Madeira, and same of tarragon vinegar ; or the juice of half a lemon, and a teaspoonful of sugar.

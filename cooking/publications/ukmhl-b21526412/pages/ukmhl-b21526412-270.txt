This process of feeding oysters, is only employed when a great many come up together in tlieir dirt, &c. The real Colchester, or Pyfleet barrelled oysters, that are packed at tlie beds, are better without being put in water; they are carefully and tightly packed, and must not be disturbed till wanted for table; these, in moderate weather, will keep good for a week, or ten days. If an oyster opens his mouth in the barrel, he dies immediately. To preserve the lives of Barrelled Oysters, put a heavy weight on the wooden top of the barrel, which is to be placed on the surface of the oysters. This is to be effected by removing the first hoop, the staves will then spread and stand erect, making a wide opening for the head of the barrel to fall down closely on the remaining fish, keeping them close together.

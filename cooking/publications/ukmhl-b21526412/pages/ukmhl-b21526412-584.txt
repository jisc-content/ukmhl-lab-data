Put into a stewpan a ladleful of melted bacon or lard; set it on a stove; put in about a dozen of the small pieces of the bacon, then stoop the stewpan and break in an egg. Manage this carefully, and the egg will presently be done ; it will be very round, and the little dice of bacon will stick to it all over, so that it will make a very pretty appearance. Take care the yolks do not harden; when the egg is thus done, lay it carefully on a warm dish, and do the others. *^* They reckon 685 ways of dressing eggs in the Fi'ench kitchen; we hope we have here given sufficient variety for English palates, in the half dozen preceding receipts.

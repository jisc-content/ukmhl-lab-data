See Receipt for Soup and Bouillie, (No. 238.) To salt Beef and Pork. — (No. 6.) Before you salt meat, remember to take out the kernels: there is always one in the udder of a round of beef, another in the fat in the middle of the round, and several about tlie thick end of the flank, and another in the mouse buttock; and if they are not taken out, all the salt in the world will not keep the meat. The Aut of Salting Meat, is to rub in the salt thoroughly and evenly into every part, and to fill all the holes full of salt where the kernels were taken out, and where the butcher’s skewers were.

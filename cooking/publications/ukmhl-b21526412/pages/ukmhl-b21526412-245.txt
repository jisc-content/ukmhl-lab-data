Lobster or shrimp sauce. A Brill—143.) Is dressed the same way as a turbot. Soles to Boil. — (No. 144.) Wash and clean your soles well; put them into a fish-kettle, with a handful of salt, and as much boiling water as will cover them; and set them on the side of the fire to boil gently about five minutes, according to their size, will be long enough, unless they be very large. Send them up on a fish-drainer garnished with sprigs of parsley. Obs. — Slices of lemon are a universally ac- ceptable garnish, with either fried or broiled fish; a few sprigs of crisp parsley may be added, if you wish to make it look very smart; and parsley, or fennel and butter, are excellent sauce, see (No. 261) and (No. 265,) or Chervil sauce (No. 264.)

Bread Sauce. — (No. 321.) Cut in slices sufficient stale bread, cover it with milk, and put it on to boil, with an onion, and half a dozen corns of white pepper or all- spice, tied up in a muslin bag; let it simmer gently by the side of the fire, till the bread has soaked up the milk, take out the spice and beat it well with a wooden spoon; if you wish it to look very smooth, rub it through a sieve. Rice Sauce.—(No. 321.*) Steep a quarter pound of Rice in a pint of milk, with onion, pepper, &c. as in the last receipt, when the rice is quite tender (take out the spice,) rub it through a sieve into a clean stewpan ; if too thick, put a little milk or cream to it.

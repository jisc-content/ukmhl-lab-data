Put ten or twelve Anchovies into a marble mortar, and pound them to a pulp; put this into an iron or silver saucepan with a tablespoonful of cold spring water, let them stand by the side of a slow fire, frequently stirring them together, till they are melted. Now stir in half a drachm of good Cayenne pepper, and let it remain by the side of the fire for ten minutes longer; then, while it is warm, rub it through a hair sieve*, with the back of a wooden spoon. A tablespoonful will be about equal to an Anchovy. A roll of thin cut lemon-peel infused with the Anchovy, imparts a fine fresh, delicate, aromatic flavour, which very much improves this Sauce.

Grate twelve ounces of vanilla chocolate into a stewpan, with twelve ounces of sugar, eight yelks of eggs, beaten, and a pint and a half of double cream; stir it, without ceasing, on a moderate hot plate till it begins to thicken; be careful not to let it boil, in order that the eggs may not set and curdle, strain it through a tammy ; pour half a pint of boiling new milk on three ounces of isinglass, strain and add it to the rest, stir it gently; have ready another pint of cream, whipped to a froth, mix it in gently and put it into the moulds.

PENTECOST BISCUITS. £ lb. flour. lb. butter, j lb. castor sugar. 1 egg. 1 teaspoonful of noyau. Reserve part of the white of the egg and 1 oz. of the sugar for decoration, for which some small pieces of citron peel will also be required. Place the butter in a basin and stand it on the top of the oven for 2 or 3 minutes so that it may be warmed through, but not reduced to the consistency of oil. Mix in the flour and sugar, the egg (well beaten), and the noyau. Make it into a stiff paste; turn it on to a floured board, roll it out thin, and cut it into shapes with small round cutters or with the top of a wine-glass. Place them on a floured baking-tin, brush them over with white of egg, sprinkle them with the castor sugar, and put a piece of citron peel on the middle of each biscuit. Bake them in a moderate oven for 15 minutes, and when done, place them on a sieve till cool.

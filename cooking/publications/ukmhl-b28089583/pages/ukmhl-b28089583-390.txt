Green butter with herring roes- Make a quarter of a pound of fresh butter from cream that you have set at home, and put it in ice : boil a hand- ful of spinach till tender, and then pass it through the sieve : save the pulp carefully and give it a dust of pepper. Take the roes (soft) of two herrings d la sardine from the tin, wipe them carefully to get rid of the oil, then pound them, in a mortar, and pass them through the hair sieve. Mince very finely a large bunch of curly parsley, so as to have at least a heaped up table-spOonful of it when minc- ed ; mince a dozen capers, and then mix the whole of the ingredients together with a wooden butter bat, and shape it as you like, setting it again in ice till wanted. There are numerous varieties of “ green butter” : this recipe has, however, been proved to be a nice one, and will be found useful when anchovies in oil may happen to be unobtain- able.

The great Careme advocated the use of the fat skimmed from the surface of the pot-au-feu after having been carefully strained through muslin. Clarified suet, for which I give directions in my chapter on pastry, is favorably regarded by Gouffe. Butter is hardly to be recommended for this kind of frying as it heats very quickly and is apt to burn. Oil is, of course, an excellent medium, but it is difficult to get good out in India, and too expensive. Lard (imported) may be used, but I do not like it even in England for it always adheres to a certain extent to the thing fried.

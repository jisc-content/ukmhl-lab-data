The “ FONDUE,” or “ cheese soujle,” is the dish par excel- lence of which, when successfully made, the good cook has just cause to be proud. It requires the most delicate management, and an atom will ruin the undertaking, for with all souffles, to fall short of perfection means failure. Pi’actice and experience go a long way towards turning out this piece de resistance satisfactorily, it is nevertheless one of those things in which the best hand may occasion- ally err; so, for a dinner party, beware of placing too great confidence in it, have another dish ready to go round in case the fondue fail to “ come off,” and do not enter it in your menu.

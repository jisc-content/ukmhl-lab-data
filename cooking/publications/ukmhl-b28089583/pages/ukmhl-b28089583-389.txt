Take one ounce of cocoa powder, and stir it into a pint of toiling milk, adding two ounces of sugar; when thoroughly mixed, strain the milk through a piece of muslin. Let it get quite cold, and then add to it the strained yolks of eight eggs, making therewith a rich custard. Set the custard upon ice, having stirred an ounce of dissolved gelatine into it, and when it begins to set, add a coffee-cupful of whipped cream, mix thoroughly, and prepare the Bavaroise as follows :—place a mould on ice and pour into it a layer of the vanilla jelly half an inch thick, when set, pour in about an inch of the cream, then a layer of jelly when the cream has set, and so on until the mould is filled.

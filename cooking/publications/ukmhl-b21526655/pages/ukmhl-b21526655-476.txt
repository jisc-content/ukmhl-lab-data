Take different kinds of fruit, stone the plums and slice the apples and pears, put them in alternate layers in ajar ; set them in the oven until they are quite soft; then pass the pulp through a coarse sieve, and to every pound of fruit put a pound of moist sugar, set it over a slow fire and stir it till very thick, then put it into a wide shallow pot and cut it in slices for use. Windfalls may be employed for this sort of sweetmeat. ANOTHER ALMACK PRESERVE. Put into a pan four dozen split plums, two dozen apples, and two dozen pears, pared thin and cored.

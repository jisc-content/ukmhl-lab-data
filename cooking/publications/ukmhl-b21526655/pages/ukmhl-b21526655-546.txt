One ounce of pearl barley, half an ounce of white sugar, and the rind of a lemon, put into a jug. Pour upon it a quart of boiling water and let it stand for eight or ten hours: then strain off the liquor, adding a I slice of lemon if desirable. This infusion makes a most delicious and nutritious beverage, and will be grateful to persons who cannot drink the horrid decoction usually, given. It is an admirable basis for lemonade, negus, or weak punch, a glass of rum being the proportion to a quart. BARLEY WATER WITH HONEY.—E. R.

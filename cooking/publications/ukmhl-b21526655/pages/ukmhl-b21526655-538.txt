CHAPTER XXII. PUNCH, LIQUEURS, WINE, See. AN EXCELLENT METHOD OF MAKING PUNCH. Take two large fresh lemons with rough skins, quite ripe, and some large lumps of double-refined sugar. Rub the sugar over the lemons till it has absorbed all the yellow parts of the skins. Then put into the bowl these lumps, and as much more as the juice of the lemons may be supposed to require; for no certain weight can be mentioned, as the acidity of a lemon cannot be known till tried, and therefore this must be determined by the taste. Then squeeze the lemon-juice upon the sugar, and with a Ijruiser press the sugar and the juice parti- cularly ■v\'ell together, for a great deal of the richness and fine flavour of the punch depends on this rubbing and mixing process being thoroughly performed.

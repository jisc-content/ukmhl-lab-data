Put a pint of water in a saucepan, add more than a quarter of a pound of butter, the rind of a lemon grated, two ouqces of sugar, and a little salt. As soon as the water is ready to boil, put in about a quarter of a pound of flour, then add six eggs well beaten; form little balls, or other shapes, and fry them. CURD FRITTERS. Rub down in a mortar a quart of dried curd, with the yolks of eight, and whites of four well-beaten eggs, two ounces of sifted sugar, half a nutmeg, and half a spoon- ful of flour. Drop the batter into a frying-pan, with a little butter or fine lard. APPLE FRITTERS.—E. R. Take the yolks of six eggs, and the whites of three; beat them well, and strain them ; then add a pint of milk, a little ginger and salt, half a nutmeg grated, and a glass of brandy. Make it into thick batter with fine flour ; slice the apples in rounds; dip each slice in batter, nd fry them in boiling lard over a quick fire. CHAPTER XII. FRENCH COOKERY. FISH. UNE GRANADE.

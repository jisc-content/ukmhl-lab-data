A pound of fine fresh suet, one ounce of ready-dressed veal or chicken, chopped fine, crumbs of bread, a little shalot or onion, salt, white ]iepper, nutmeg, mace, pennyroyal, parsley, and lemon-thyme, finely shred; beat as many fresh eggs, yolks and whites separately, as will make the above ingredients into a moist paste: roll into small balls, and boil them in fresh lard, putting them in just as it boils up. DIRECTIONS HOW,TO BLANCH, BRAISE, GLAZE, FORCE, AND LARD. TO BLANCH. Put the article in cold water over the fire, and when it boils up take it out and plunge it into cold water, and let it remain until cold. This gives plumpness and whiteness. Tongues, palates, &c., are said to be blanched when, after long boiling, the skin can be peeled off; and the latter will become thicker by being put into cold water as above.

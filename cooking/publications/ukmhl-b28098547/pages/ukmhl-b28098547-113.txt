Tongues, hams, and fish require soaking several hours, or overnight, according to the length of time they have been in pickle; then cover the meat with boiling water, and leave it by the side of the fire until done, scarcely allowing it to simmer. Let it remain in the water about half an hour, or until the water is almost cold, when the meat will be mellow and moist.

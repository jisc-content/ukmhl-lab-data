5 The Art of Ice Making. IT is said that ices were originally intro^ duced by Catherine de Medici in the sixteenth century ; at that time only so-called water ices and iced drinks were known. It was about this time that the French people learned how to freeze water artificially ; whilst water ices, shaped in moulds, were first served in France in 1660. Soon after this they were introduced into England. Ices de- rive their present great popularity from America, where they are consumed during the winter as well as summer months in enormous quantities. Cream ices, such as we now have, are comparatively of re- cent invention, for when these were first introduced they were very primitive com- pared with the deliciously - flavoured, coloured, and artistically-shaped ices of the present day. Broadly speaking, ices are divided into two cases, viz. : the cream or custard ice and the water ice. There are, of course, numerous ways of preparing and of freezing both water and cream ices.

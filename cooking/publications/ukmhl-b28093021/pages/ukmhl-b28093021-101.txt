Any desirable fruit may be iced by dip- ping, first, in the beaten white of an egg, then in coarse or granulated sugar. Do1 this until the sugar coating is sufficiently thick. Peaches should be pared and cut in halves, and sweet juicy pears are treated in the same way. Cherries, straw- berries, and other small fruits are iced with the stems on, only the largest being chosen. Pineapples should be cut into thin slices, and these again divided into quarters. The beverage known as “ Cups ” is generally a combination of liquors. Some of these preparations are exceed- ingly popular among all classes. The earliest known form of these is the “ lov- ing cup,” which still survives at society and municipal banquets. It was not until about a century ago that the word “ cup,” in the sense of a mixed drink, be- came known and introduced.

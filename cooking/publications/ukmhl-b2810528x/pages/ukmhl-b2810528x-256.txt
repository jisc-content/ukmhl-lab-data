Such liquids rapidly develop indigestible acid products in the stomachs of many persons; while for all, the sipping of sweet fluids, effervescing or otherwise, during a meal tends to diminish appetite, as well as the faculty of appreciating good cookery. If wine is refused, let the drink which accompanies dinner be of pure water—with a sparkle of gas in it, and a slight dash of vegetable acid in it if you will—but in obedience both to gastronomic and dietetic laws, let it be free from sugar.

All forms of vegetables are non-digestible by the infantile organs, and when given too early, as they not infrequently are, produce great disturbance, diarrhoea, colic, and, by no means rarely, fatal results. Not until some teeth have appeared is the child, as a rule, ready to make its first trial of vegetable food; and, for the first year, or even more, of life, it will generally thrive better on wholesome milk than on b any substitute.

Then heat the remaining pint to boiling point, and add to the pint which contains the gelatine, together with a thin paring of lemon peel, two teaspoonfuls of lemon juice, the same of mild vinegar, and one or two teaspoonfuls of tarragon vinegar. Then pour in two or three whites of eggs, lightly beaten, and stir well to fine the liquor. Bring the whole just up to the boiling point; then at once remove and keep on the hot plate close by, but not boiling, for three minutes only. Take it off and set it aside for three minutes longer, and then strain through flannel. It is now quite hot and clear; stir in at once a large teaspoonful of the meat extract, and set aside to cool until wanted.

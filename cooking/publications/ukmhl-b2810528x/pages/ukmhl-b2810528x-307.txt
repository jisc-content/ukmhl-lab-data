Very little attention is required, when the method of using the apparatus has been learned, and a far more nutritious and agreeable meal is provided than the workman usually gets. Note.—The modern stock-pot is a great improvement upon the old earthen pot, set aside by Gouffe for one of “copper tinned,” to hold a gallon. The cook should now be provided with one made of aluminium, with a tap which screws in about one inch from the bottom so as to be frequently removed to ensure perfect cleanliness.

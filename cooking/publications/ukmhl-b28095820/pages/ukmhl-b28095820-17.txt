IX SERVICE OF THE TABLE. i is not enough that it be well prepared, it must be served also, both as a whole, and in its details, with all the comfort requisite, and the attention indispensable. Whether the preparations of the kitchen are to be handed round to the guests, or deposited on the table, it is essential, that they reach the dining-room, while their acquired heat remains yet in its perfection ; for dishes garnished with sauce, especially, lose a great part of their good qualities, if they are not eaten quite hot.

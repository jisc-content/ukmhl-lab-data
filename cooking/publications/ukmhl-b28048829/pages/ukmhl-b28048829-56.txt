Spatchcock. Split a fowl in halves through the middle of the breast and back ; pepper and salt it, rub it over with butter; grease a gridiron, and broil it over a clear, \ bright Are. Put a lump of butter on a hot dish before the fire, let it dissolve; lay the fowl on a round of toasted bread, pepper and salt it, and serve very hot. Curried Mincemeat. Soak a slice of bread in milk, and grate eight sweet almonds; beat two eggs into half a cupful of milk, and mix the whole well together, with half a pound of minced meat, a small lump of butter, and a tablespoonful of curry-powder.

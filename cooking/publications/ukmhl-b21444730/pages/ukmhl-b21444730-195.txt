First may be named the wolf-fish, or cat-fish, which, although unlike in appearance to the usual occupants of a fishmonger's shop, is a white fish of excellent quality, ^ut excellent It lives almost entirely on Crustacea, a quality, circumstance, as Yarrell long ago pointed out, always associated with excellence in the fish so fed. The superiority of the cod caught at the Dogger Bank is said to be partly due to the same cause, as their food at this spot is largely crustacean. The halibut, from the North Sea, closely related to the turbot, is good and substantial food, and when large, as it often is, is also very cheap. The sea-bream, not unlike a large fresh-water perch, and the sufficiently basse, which has been sometimes styled a white salmon, are both useful varieties which have never received the attention they deserve, and which may be presented simply yet effectively at table. The gurnard is much better known, and an excellent dish may be made with it.

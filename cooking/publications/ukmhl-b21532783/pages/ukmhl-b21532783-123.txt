On each side there is a large spare-rib ; which is usually divided into two, one sweet bone and a blade-bone. The bacon is the whole outside, aud contains a fore-leg and ham ; whicli last is the hind-leg, but if left with the bacon it is called a gammon. There are also griskins. Hog’s lard is the inner fat of the bacon-hog, Pickled pork is made of the. flesh of the hog, as well as bacon. Porkers are not so old as hogs their flesh is whiter and less rich, but it is not so tender. It is divided into four quarters. The fore-quarter has the spring or fore-leg, the fore-loin or neck, the spare-riband griskin. The hind has the leg aud the loin. The feet of pork make several good dishes, and should be cut off before the legs are cured. Ob- serve the same of the ears. The bacon-hog is sometimes scalded to take off the hair, and sometimes singed. The porker is always scalded. To roast a leg of Pork. . When half-done, scorethe skin in slices, but do Dot cut deeper than the outer rind. Apple-sauce and potatoes should be sorted to eat with it.

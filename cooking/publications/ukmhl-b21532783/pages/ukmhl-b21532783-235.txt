Yeast or Suffolk Dumplings. Mahea'ery light dough with yeast, as for bread, but with milk instead of water, and put salt. Let it rise an hour before the fire. Twenty minutes before you are to serve, have ready a large stew-pan of boiling water; make the dough into baits, the size of a middling apple ; throw them in, and boil twenty minutes. If you doubt when done enough, stick a clean fork iuto one, and if it come out clear, it is done. The way to eat them is, tear them apart with on the top with two forks, for they become heavy by th -ir own steam. Eat immediately with meat, or sugar and butter, or salt. A Charlotte. Cut as many very thin slices of white bread as will cover the bottom and line the sides of a baking dish, bat first rub it thick with butter. Put apples in thin slices into the dish, in layers, till full, strew- ing sugar between, and bits of butter. In the mean time, soak as many thin '.dices of bread as will cover tlie whole, in warm milk, over which lay a plate, and a weight to keep the bread close on the apples. Cake slowly three hours. To a middling sized dish use half a pound of butter in the whole. Common Pancakes. Make a light batter of eggs, flour, and milk. Fry in a small pan, in hot dripping or lard.

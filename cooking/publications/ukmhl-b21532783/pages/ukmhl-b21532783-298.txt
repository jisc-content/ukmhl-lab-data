The bread should be in separate crums, and look light. Omlet. Make a batter of eggs and milk, and a very little flour : put to it chopped parsley, green onions, or chives (the latter is best,) or a very small quantity of shallot, a little pepper salt, and a scrape or two of nutmeg. Make some butter boil in a small fry- ing-pan, aud pour the above batter into it; when one side is of a fine yellow brown, turn it, and do the other. Double it when served. Some scraped lean ham, or grated tongue, put in at first, is a very pleasant addition.

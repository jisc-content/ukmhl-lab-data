Boil very tender a handful of whole rice in a small quantity of milk, with a large piece of lemon- peel. Let it drain ; then mix with it a dozen of good sized apples, boiled to pulp as dry as possible: add a glass of white wine, the yolks of live eggs, two ounces of orange and citron cut thin ; make it pretty sweet. Line a mould or basin with a very good paste; beat the five whites of the eggs to a very strong froth, and mix with the other ingredients; fill the mould, aud bake it of a fine brown colour. Serve it with the bottom upward, with the following sauce : two glasses of wine, a spoonful of sugar, the yolks of two eggs, and a bit of butter as large as a walnut ; simmer without boiling, and pour to and from the saucepan, till of a proper thickness ; and put in the dish.

Rice Flummery. Boil with a pint of new milk, a bit of lemon-peel, and cinnamon ; mix with a little cold milk as much rice-flour as will make the whole of a good consis- tence, sweeten, and add a spoonful of peach-water, or a hitter almond beaten; boil it, observing it do not burn ; pour it into a shape or pinl-basin, taking out the spice. Somersetshire Firmity. To a quart of ready-boiled wheat, put bydegr* es- two quarts of new milk, breaking the jelly, and then four ounces of currants picked clean, and washed; stir them and boil till they are done. Beat the yolks of three eggs, and a little nutmeg,, with two or three spoonfuls of milk ; add this to the wheat; stir them together while over the fire; then sweeten, and serve cold in a deep dish.

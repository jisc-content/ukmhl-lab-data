The sauce for wild fowl as will be described hereafter under the head of Sauces, may be used instead of common gravy. To roast Wild Fowl. The flavour is best preserved without stuffing. Put pepper, salt, and a piece of butter into each. It ild fowl require much less dressing than tame : they should be served of a tine colour, and well frothed up. A rich brown gravy should be sent in the dish: and when the breast is cut into slices, before taking oft' the bone, a squeeze of lemon, with pepper and salt, is a great improvement to the flavour. To take oft' the fishy taste which wild fowl some- times have, put an onion, salt, and hot water, into the dripping-pan, and baste them for the first ten minutes wi'h this; then take away the pan and baste constantly with butter.

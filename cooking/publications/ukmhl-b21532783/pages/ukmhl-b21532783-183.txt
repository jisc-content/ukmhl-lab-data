Tffke the meat from the claws, bodies, aud tails, of six small lobsters ; take away the brown fur, and the bag in the head ; beat the tins, chine, and small claws in a mortar. Boil it very gently in two quarts of water, with the crumb of a French roll, some white pepper, salt, two anchovies, a large onion, sweet herbs, and a bit of lemon-peel, till you have extracted the goodness of them all. Strain it off. Beat the spawn in a mortar, with a bit of butter, a quarter of a nutmeg, and a tea-spoonful of flour; mix it with a quart of cream. Cut the tails into pieces, and give them a boil up with the cream and and soup. Serve with forcemeat balls made of the remainder of the lobster, mace, pepper, salt, a few crums. and an egg or two. Let the balls be made up with a bit of flour, and heated in the soup. Craw-fish or Prawn Soup.

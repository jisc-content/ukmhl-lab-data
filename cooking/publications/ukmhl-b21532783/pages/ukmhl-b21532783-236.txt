Fine pancakes, fried without Butter or Lard.* Beat six fresh eggs extremely well; mix, when strained, with a pint of cream, four ounces of sugar a glass of wine, half a nutmeg grated, and as much flour as will make it almost as thick as ordinary pancake-batter, but not quite. Heat the frying-pan tolerably hot, wipe it with a clean cloth; then pour in the batter, to make thin pancakes. Pancakes of Rice. Boil half a pound of rice to a jelly, in a small quantity of water; when cold, mix it with a pint of cream, eight eggs, a bit of salt, aud nutmeg ; stir in eight ounces of butter just warmed, aud add as much flour as will make the batter thick enough. Fry in as little lard or dripping as possible. Irish pancakes.

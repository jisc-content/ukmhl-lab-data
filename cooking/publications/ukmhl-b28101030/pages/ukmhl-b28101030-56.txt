Cook in a hot oven for eight minutes, and serve im- mediately. (88) Scotch Eggs. (CEufs a VEcossaise.) Boil four eggs for ten minutes, cool and shell them. Skin one pound of pork sausages, and mix with one yolk of egg. Dip each hard-boiled egg' in flour, then coat each over with a thin layer of the sausage meat. Beat up a raw egg on a plate, brush over the covered hard-boiled eggs, then roll them in bread-crumbs and fry them in hot fat to a golden brown. Drain them on paper or a cloth. Cut each neatly in half crossways. Dish up on rounds of fried or toasted bread, and gar- nish with fried parsley. Serve hot with tomato sauce, or cold with mayonnaise sauce. (89) Stuffed Eggs with Prawns. (CEufs farcis aux Crevettes.) Take four hard-boiled eggs, twelve large or eighteen small prawns, three Gor- gona anchovies, one and a-half ounces of butter, one tablespoonful bechamel sauce. Remove the shell from the eggs, cut them in halves crosswise, scoop out the yolks and put them in a mortar, add the boned anchovies and picked prawns, and pound very fine.

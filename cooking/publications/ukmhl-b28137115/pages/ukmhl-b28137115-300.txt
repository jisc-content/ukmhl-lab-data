The great point in all salads, but especially those served with a roast, is that they be well blended, correctly seasoned, and nicely garnished. 'A salad must look cool, inviting, and dainty, if it is to be a success. There are salads and salads, but unless a cook or maitre d’hotel has what Hamlet calls a feeling for his business, the salad misses its character—that is, its dressing.

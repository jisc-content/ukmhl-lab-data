149 ROSEMARY AND LAVENDER times, too, have I helped him to rake it in from the rocks where it grows, as he would say, out of reach of the waves but within touch of the spray. Need I mention that in a woman’s garden you are certain to find rosemary growing ? It is there for the bees, but it is there for me as well, for the washes it makes for hair and skin, for the pot-pourri that scents the drawing-room, and, last but not least, for the reminder it is to friends that their memory is sweet. And side by side with rosemary grows lavender, not, perchance, “ for lovers true,” but for the linen press !

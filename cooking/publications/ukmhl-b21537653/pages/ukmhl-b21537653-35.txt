In a well-managed house there ought not to be enough left to keep even a dog ; whereas, if the truth were known, the contents of the dust-bins alone in England contain sufficient food to almost banish poverty from the land. This may seem a strong statement, but it is no more strong than true. Recollect waste is a crime; and were it in our power —which it is not—to be able to multiply food even to a miraculous extent, it would not the less be our duty to “ gather up the fragments that remain, that nothing be lost.”

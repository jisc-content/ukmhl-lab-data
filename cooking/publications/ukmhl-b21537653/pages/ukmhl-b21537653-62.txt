I have been asked Several times in strict confidence the question, “ But ought it to have looked like that ?” —a question often involving a necessary sacrifice of either truth or politeness. Francatelli observes : “ The palate is as capable and nearly as worthy of education as the eye and the ear.” Now, without entering into the question as to whether a patty to eat is equal to a Patti to hear or see in the way of enjoyment, there is no doubt that the palate is to a great extent influenced by the eye. For instance, a large cold sirloin of beef on the side- board at a good old-fashioned hotel, neatly decorated with bright green parsley and snow-white curly horse- radish ; the dish resting on an equally snow-white cloth; its companions consisting of as tempting- looking a York ham, and some bright silver flagons, the latter enabling the looker-on almost to realise the “ nut-brown ale ” talked about of old, though what it was like we have not the least idea.

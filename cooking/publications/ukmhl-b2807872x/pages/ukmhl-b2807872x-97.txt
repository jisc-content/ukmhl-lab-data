CHAPTER IV. EEMOVES OR RELEVfeS. BEEF. A ROAST sirloin of beef is called Aloyau d la hroche. For Aloyau de hoeuf d la Provengale the sirloin is larded and spread with a stuffing of marrow, anchovies, garlic, &c.; roasted, and served with Piquante sauce. The upper part of the sirloin, boned and rolled, is served as: Aloyau hraise d la royale—^larded and braised. d la Printaniere—braised and served with young vegetables. d la Portugaise—^larded and braised; served with stewed Portugal onions and brown sauce. d la Godard—braised, and served with slices of sweetbread, mushrooms, &c. The fillet, or undercut of the sirloin, is usually cut out and dressed as a separate dish.

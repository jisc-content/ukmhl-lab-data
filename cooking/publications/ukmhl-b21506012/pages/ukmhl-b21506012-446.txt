Some people make small forcemeat balls of hard-boiled eggs pounded with some parsley, pepper, salt, spice, flour, and a raw egg; or of chicken, with a little ham or tongue, savoury herbs, crumb of bread and eggs ; these they poach in gravy, and add to the soup when serving. I think, however, that the soup costs trouble enough without them, and so many people misunderstand what they are, that making forcemeat balls is often lost labour.

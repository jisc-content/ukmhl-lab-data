Celery, (celeri) is an exceedingly nice vegetable not only when sent round, raw, with cheese, but also when cooked in various ways as an entremets. In the latter case the heads should be very neatly trimmed and cut short, say five or six inches in length. They can then be split lengthwise in two or four pieces according to the thickness of the head. When prepared satisfactorily, the pieces should be plunged into fast boiling water, and boiled for ten minutes. They must now be taken out, drained, and wiped, and then put to stew gently for about two hours. As soon as tender, they must be strained, turned out upon a hot silver dish, and served like asparagus with a pat of butter melting over them, a piping hot “ Dutch sauce ” being sent round in a boat. Celery may be also stewed gently in weak stock, or blanc and then be presented a T Espagnole (covered with a thick rich brown sauce), au vcloute (with a white sauce), or cm jus in clear gravy.

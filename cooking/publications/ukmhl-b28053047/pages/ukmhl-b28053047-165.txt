159 HOT AIR BAKERS’ OVENS. This is an entirely new departure in ships’ ovens. It is built as a two decker, and is an exceedingly valuable apparatus for baking large quantities of bread, where a big number of passengers are carried. Only quite recently Messrs. Henry Wilson & Co., Ltd., have introduced on board ship a bakers’ oven heated by tubes containing water at a high temperature. It is more costly than the one illustrated here, but this has not prevented its adoption by nearly all the leading passenger lines. K

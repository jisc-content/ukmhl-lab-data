By its aid stewed fruits, tea, coffee, and other daily requisites are made palatable. FEUITS, JELLIES, AND CREAMS, SWEETENED WITH SACCHARIN. Lemon Cream. 335. Pare into a pint of water the peel of three large lemons ; let it stand four or five hours ; then take them out, and put to the water the juice of four lemons and four grains of saccharin, or eight tabloids dissolved in a little boiling water. Beat the whites of six eggs, and mix it altogether j strain it through a lawn sieve, set it over a slow fire, stir it one way until as thick as good cream, then take it off the fire, and stir it until cold, and put it into a glass dish. Orange cream may be made in the same way, adding the yolks of three eggs. Lemon Cream without Cream. 336. Put a quart of new milk into a stewpan with the peel of three small lemons cut thin, four grains of saccharin or eight tabloids, three-quarters of an ounce of bitter almonds, blanched and pounded to a paste, and about two ounces of gelatine or isinglass. Boil the whole over a moderate fire for eight or nine minutes, until the gelatine or isinglass is thoroughly dissolved; then strain it through a fine sieve into a jug with a lip to it, stir in the yolks of seven well- beaten eggs, and pour the mixture from one jug to another until barely cold ; then add the strained juice of three small lemons, stir it quickly together, and pour it into an oiled mould.

On the other side put a skewer in the small part of the leg, close on the outside of the sidesman, and push it through. Clean the liver and gizzard and tuck them between the pinions, and turn the point of the pinions on the back. Pass a string over the points of the skewers, and tie it securely at the back to keep the bird neat and firmly trussed. Cover the breast with a sheet of nicely buttered white paper. Place the bird on the spit or roasting-jack, and set it at some distance from the fire, which should be a very good and bright one. Keep the heat well to the breast. Put 4 ounces of butter in the dripping- pan, and baste it frequently. Just before it is done, remove the paper, dredge it lightly with flour, and baste with the butter, so as to brown and froth it. Serve with good brown gravy poured over, and garnish with small fried sausages or forcemeat balls. Sauce : bread-sauce.

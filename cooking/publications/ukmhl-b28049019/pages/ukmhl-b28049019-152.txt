136 MENUS MADE EASY. CHAPTER VI. ROASTS, OR ROTS. Black Diver—Macreuse. Dressed au chocolat, they are served covered with chocolate. Black game—Coqs noirs, or Coqs de hruyere. A la Stockholm, they are larded on one side, and harded with vine leaves on the other side. Bustard—Outarde canepetiere. A la Russe, they have a slice cut in the breast and a stuffing inserted, and are served with brown sauce. Capon—Chapon. Served with watercress, it is called au cresson. Chicken—Poulet. A la Forrester, it is covered with watercress leaves, mustard and cress, etc. Barde, it is harded—roasted with a slice of bacon over the breast, and served with the bacon on it. Roti a la Stael, it has a white crust from being sprinkled with flour during the roasting. Spring chickens—Petits poulets. Young chickens—Petits poussins. Duck—Canard.

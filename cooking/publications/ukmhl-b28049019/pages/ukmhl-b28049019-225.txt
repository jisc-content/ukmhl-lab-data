SAJOURY ENTREMETS. 209 Croiites a VAlbert are spread with a mixture of buttered eggs with cheese, and sprinkled with chopped tongue. a la Rosamonde are rolled fillets of anchovies on slices of tomato, garnished with hard-boiled yolk of egg. d la Burlington are spread with minced chicken in Tartare sauce, covered with a slice of tomato, and garnished with cream of chicken and a stuffed olive in the centre. d la Berne are spread with chopped olives, shallots and parsley, covered with chopped tongue, and have a hard-boiled yolk of egg seasoned with oil and vinegar on each. They are served hot. d la Mane are spread with foie gras, garnished with dried cherries stuffed with foie gras, and decorated with whipped cream. d la Clarisse are spread with shreds of ham and anchovy, covered with little slices of bacon, and are dipped in batter and fried. d la Normande are spread with a shrimp paste. d la Yarmouth are spread with bloater paste. In addition to these, croiites may be served aux harengs, with fillets of herring; de homard d la Tartare, with chopped lobster and Tartare sauce; d la mo'dle, with marrow; aux cham- pignons, or aux epinards, with a puree of mush- rooms or spinach; and may be garnished with yolk and white of egg, chopped celery, beetroot, parsley, etc. Cheese biscuits are often used for croiites instead of fried bread. , p

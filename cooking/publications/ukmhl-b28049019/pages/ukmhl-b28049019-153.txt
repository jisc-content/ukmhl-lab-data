ROASTS, OR ROTS. 137 Duckling—Caneton. Served with orange sauce, it is called au jus d’orange or a la higarade. Goose—Oie. Green goose—Oison or Oisillon. Grouse—Grouse. A la honnie lassie, they are served on brown bread toast with melted-hutter sauce. Guinea fowl—Pintade. Piquee, they are larded; hardee, barded. Hare—Li'evre. Hazel hen—Gelinotte. A la Victoria, they are stuffed and larded, and are served with a brown sauce. Lapwings—Vanneaux. Larks—Mauviettes. Leveret—Levraut. Ortolans—Ortolans. Partridges—Perdrix ov Perdrcaux. “Perdrix” is usually applied to older birds. A brace of par- tridges is Accolade de joerdreaux. Perdreaux de Biissie are Eussian partridges. Peahen—Paonne. Pheasant—Faisan. Pigeons—Pigeons. Young pigeons—Pigeonneaux. Bardes aufeuilles de vignes, they are roasted with vine leaves and slices of bacon over the breasts. Pintail—Pilet. Plovers—Pluviers. Prairie birds—Poides de Prairie. Fat Pullet—Poularde.

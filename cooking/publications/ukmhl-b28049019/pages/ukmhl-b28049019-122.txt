106 MENUS MADE EASY. CHAPTEE V. REMOVES OR RELEVES. BEEF. ■ A ROAST sirloin of beef is called Aloyau a la hroche. For Aloyau de hoeuf a la Provengale the sirloin is larded and spread with a stuffing of marrow, anchovies, garlic, etc.; roasted, and served with Piquante sauce. The upper part of the sirloin, boned and rolled, is served as— Aloyau en cannelon—^roasted; sauce raifort, with horseradish sauce. braise a la royale—larded and braised. d la Printaniere—braised, and served with young vegetables. a la Portugaise—larded and braised; served with stewed Portugal onions and brown sauce. d la Godard—braised, and served with slices of sweetbread, mushrooms, etc. The fillet, or undercut of the sirloin, is usually cut out and dressed as a separate dish.

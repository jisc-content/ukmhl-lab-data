R. 856. —Put a quart of water into a saucepan, and let it boil; then drop into it, lump by lump, 1 lb. of loaf-sugar. When all the sugar is dissolved, let it boil again and put it into a broad dish to cool; when cool it is fit for use. NEC TAR.-E. R. 857. —Take 2 lbs. of raisins chopped, and 4 lbs. of loaf- sugar, and put them into a spigot-pot; pour 2 gallons of boil- ing water upon them. The next day, when it is cold, slice 2 lemons into it. Let it stand 5 days, stirring it twice a-day. Then let it stand 5 days more to clear; bottle it, put it into a cold cellar for 10 days, and it will be fit to drink. MEAD.—E.

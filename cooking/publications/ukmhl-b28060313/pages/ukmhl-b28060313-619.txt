TOASTS. 814.— German Toast.—Take the remainder of any fricassee or ragout; any quantity will do: chop it finely, add a little i chopped parsley, and a little bit of shalot or chive: mix it up with 1 or 2 eggs beaten, according to the quantity. Put the whole with its gravy into a stewpan, and let it reduce and thicken on the fire. Let it remain until it is cold, then cut pieces of bread, toast them : lay the mixture thickly upon them. Boil an egg hard, cut it into small pieces, and stick them on the top ; brush the whole with egg beaten up, sift bread-crumbs over, and bake them in the oven ; squeeze a little lemon-juice on the top. This makes also a good corner i dish for dinner. Vegetable Toast.—Take any stewed vegetable, and make it into a puree. Add a little more seasoning, mix it up with | the yolk of an egg, and, if too thin, reduce and thicken it • over the fire, then spread it upon toast, brush it over with beaten egg, add bread-crumbs or vermicelli, and fry or bake them.

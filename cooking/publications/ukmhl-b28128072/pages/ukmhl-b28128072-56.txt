52 CHAPTER V RECHAUFFES, HASHES, ETC. To be able to prepare nice little dishes from the remains of a cold, joint, instead of sending it up cold is an art which the cook should achieve, for the using up, in a dainty form, of what are often contemptuously called “ scraps ” is a tre- mendous factor in keeping down the butcher’s bill. If only it could be impressed on people that a rechauffe by no means entails a twice- cooked dish, and one that consequently has had all the goodness drawn out of it, a great deal more variety in the daily bill of fare would be attained.

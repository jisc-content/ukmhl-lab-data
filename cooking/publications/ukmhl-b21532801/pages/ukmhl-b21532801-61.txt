Shoulder of Mutton.—This is a very good joint, and bv many preferred to the leg, it being very full of gravy, if properly roasted, and produces many nice bits. The figure represents it as laid in the dish with its back uppermost. When it is first cut, it should be in the hollow part of it, in the direction of a, h, and the knife should be pas ed deep to the bone. The prime part of the fat lies on the outer edge, and it is to be ait out in thin slices in the direction e. If many are at table, and the hollow part cut in the line a, 'b, is eaten, some very good and delicate slices may be cut out on each side the ridge of the blade-bone, in the direction c, d. The line between these two dotted lines, is that in the direction of which the edge or ridge of the blade-bone lies, and cannot be cut across.

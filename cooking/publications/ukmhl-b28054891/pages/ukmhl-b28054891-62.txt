In thickening soiq^s with a liaison of cream (or milk) and yolk of egg, the eggs must first be well beaten, then tlie cream should be added to them and thoroughly mixed. AVlien this is done take a tea-enp of hot stock and mix it slowly with the liaison. Strain it all through a fine sieve or muslin, and add gradually to the soup, which must on no account be allowed to boil after the liaison is added, although it should be stirred over a "entle fire until it thickens. In thickening soup with eggs only, beat the required number of eggs, add a little warm stock to them. Strain, and add gradually to the soup.

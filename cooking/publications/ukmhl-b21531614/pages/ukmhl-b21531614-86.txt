No. 56.—White Omelet. Prepare in the same way as macaroni omelet (No. 40); but boil for two hours instead of baking, and serve ■with onion sauce. No. 57.—Haricot Bean Omelet. Half a pint .of haricot beans, two table-spoonfuls of bread crumbs, four eggs, half an ounce of pai-sley, half a tea-cupful of milk, and one table-spoonful of olive oD. Steep the beans several hours in cold water ; boil them in fresh water till quite soft; mash with the milk, and rub them through a fine colander; add the bread crumbs, the parsley, chopped fine, the eggs, well beaten, the olive oil, salt, and pepper ; pour the omelet into a buttered dish ; bake it about an hour in a moderately hot oven, and serve with brown sauce. No. 58.—Forcemeat. Six eggs, two ounces of butter, half a pound of bread crumbs, half an ounce of parsley, half an ounce of beets, quarter of an ounce of sweet leeks, quarter of an ounce of sweet marjoram, wnter savory, and lemon-thyme (mixed), and three table- spoonfuls of cream. Boil four eggs hard ; take out the yolks, and mash them rvith the butter, adding the herbs and bread crumbs ; season with pepper, salt, and a little mace or nutmeg ; then add the cream, and two eggs, well beaten ; melt some butter in a tin ; put in the forcemeat, and roast it before the fire in a dutch oven, occasionally adding a little butter ; when brown on one side, turn it over, and when sufficiently done, serve with brown sauce, part of which should be poured on the dish, and garnish with the whites of the eggs chopped small. The forcemeat may be baked in a pie-dish, in a moderately hot oven.

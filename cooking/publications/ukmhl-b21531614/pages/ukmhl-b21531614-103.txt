95 VEGETARIAN COOKERY. No. 117.—French Beans. Cut off the ends and strip the strings from the sides ; then, if small, cut the beans down the middle and across, but if large, cut in thin slices in a slanting direction, putting them in cold water ; then put them into a pan of boiling water, adding salt and a small piece of soda ; boil them till tender, drain in a colander, and serve mth butter sauce. No. 118.—Stewed French Beans. Boil and drain the beans as in Receipt No. 117, then put them in a saucepan with a little cream, flour, and butter, mixed together; season with pepper and salt, and stew them gently about ten minutes. No. 119.—Boiled Haricot Beans. Wash the beans ; put them in fresh water, and let them steep for an hour or more ; drain and put them into a pan of boiling water with an ounce of butter and some salt; let them boil till tender; serve with butter, pepper and salt, or with butter sauce in a boat. No. 120.—Stewed Haricot Beans. One pint of beans, three ounces of butter, the juice of one lemon, and one ounce of parsley. Steep the beans two hours in cold soft water, drain and set them on the fire in two quarts of cold soft water, adding a salt- spoonful of salt, and one ounce of butter; when they boil, simmer them slowly two hours or more ; put them into a stew- pan with a little pepper, salt, chopped parsley, two omices of butter, and the lemon juice; set them on the fixe a few minutes, and stir them well.

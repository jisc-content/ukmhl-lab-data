Eight good-sized carrots, two roots of celery, one large turnip, one large onion, six ounces of bread, without crust, two ounces of butter, and half a pint of cream. Set on the fiire three quarts of water, with a piece of soda the size of a small nut; when it boils, put in the vegetables (previ- ously slieed), the bread, salt, a little Cayenne pepper, and mace ; boil the whole till the vegetables are perfectly soft; rub it through a sieve ; return it into the pan, and whilst boiling, stir in the cream, not allowing it to boil afterwards. The soup should be of the consistency of good cream. The green part of the celery should not be used. No. 8.—Celery Soup.

Take care to have the mushrooms fresh gathered and full grown; put a layer at the bottom of a rather deep earthen pan; sprinkle them with salt; then add another layer of mushrooms and salt alternately ; let them remain four or five hours ; then mash them well; let them remain covered, in a cool place, two days, stirring them well each day; then put them into a stone jar, with one ounce of black pepper-corns, half an ounce of allspice, half an ounce of bruised ginger, a little Cayenne, eschalots, and mace ; cover the jar closely, and set it in a pan of boiling water ; keep it boiling two hours ; take out the jar; pour the liquor through a hair sieve, and boil it gently half an hour; skim it well, and pour it into a clean dry jug; closely cover it, and let it remain in a cool place till the follo\ving day; then clear it off carefully through a flannel bag till clear; let it remain covered two days more; then pour it off, leaving the sediment, and bottle it in small bottles; cork them closely,and seal, or dip them in bottle cement. If kept in a cool dry place it may be preserved a long time. If from damp, or any other cause, any scum appears, beil it again with a few pepper-corns. The sediment of the mushrooms may be put into a bottle, and when settled a few days, a portion of it may be poured off and used for sauces, etc. No. 710.—Es.sencb of Mushrooms.

It should be about the same consistency as onion sauce (No. 196). No. 185.—Cream Sauce. Half a pint of cream, one tea-spoonful of arrowroot, and one table-spoonful of milk. To a small tea-spoonful of arrowroot, or potato-flour, add a table-spoonful of cold milk ; rub it smooth with a wooden spoon ; add gradually the boiling cream ; when- well mixed, season with pepper and salt; stir it over the fire till it just boils, and serve immediately. This sauce may be used with asparagus, or any other boiled vegetables, as a substitute for butter sauce. No. 186.—Cream Sauce for Puddings. Half a pint of cream, one tea-spoonful of arrowroot or potato- flour, and one table-spoonful of milk. To the arrowroot add the milk, cold; rub smooth, and add gradually the cream, previously boiled ; when well mixed, stir it on the fire till it boils, adding sugar and almond flavour, or lemon-peel, and serve immediately. No. 187.—Cucumber Sauce. Two ounces of butter, one dessert-spoonful of flour, two ounces of pickled cucumbers, and two table-spoonfuls of cream. Put the flour and butter in a saucepan, with two table- spoonfuls of water ; add pepper and salt; when it boils, put in the cucumber, cut very small; let it stew gently about five minutes, and then stir in the cream, or two table-spoonfuls of brown sauce.

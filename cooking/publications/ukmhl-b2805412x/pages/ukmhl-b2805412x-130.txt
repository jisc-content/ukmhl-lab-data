Many persons like to prepare this sauce at table, but it is a mistake to attempt it unless you have a proper silver dish and spirit burner. Lemon Pudding. Put through a tin strainer a quarter of a pound of bread-crumbs, chop very fine a quarter of a pound of suet, mix with two eggs, the grated rind of a lemon and the juice, a quarter of a pound of sifted loaf-sugar and a very small pinch of salt. Butter a mould or basin, put in the pudding, cover with a paper cap, and steam it for three hours.

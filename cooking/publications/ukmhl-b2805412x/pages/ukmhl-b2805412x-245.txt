221 PUDDINGS, CREAMS, ETC. Rich Tapioca Pudding. Put. two ounces of tapioca into a pint of boiling milk, pour into an earthen jar, and set it in a saucepan of boil- ing water to swell. Let the tapioca boil for an hour, or until perfectly soft, then beat it up with two ounces of butter and a custard made of two eggs, half a pint of new milk, two ounces of loaf-sugar, and a little grated lemon peel. Bake in a slow oven for about an hour and a half. Semolina Pudding. Boil two ounces of semolina in a pint of milk, sweeten and flavour with nutmeg, beat in an egg, put into a buttered tart-dish, and bake for an hour in a slow oven. Roderigo Pudding, Take a pint of water to a quarter of a pound of sago, twelve ounces of sugar, boil them three-quarters of an hour, then add twelve ounces of fresh fruit, raspberry or any other kind, boil for an hour, and put into a mould. When about to serve, turn out, and pour a little syrup round the base ; milk or cream should be eaten with it.

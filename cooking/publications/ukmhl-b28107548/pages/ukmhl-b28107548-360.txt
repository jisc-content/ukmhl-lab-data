The whole company united in singing “For He’s a Jolly Good Fellow,” so as to give Mr Mantell time to collect his thoughts before acknowledging his Christmas box in the shape of a cup. Some good stories from Mr Cooper Mitchell, a little more oratory, though speeches at the Gastronomes’ banquet are always kept within the shortest space, and with more songs, a very merry evening ended. If future banquets in the Piccadilly banqueting-hall are all nearly as successful as the first one held there it will become a hall of good will and good fellowship as well as a hall of good cheer.

Simpson s in the Strand 11 wrote: “A very fine tavern, with excellent vaults, good rooms for entertainments, and a curious kitchen for the dressing of meat.” It was at the Fountain that the opponents of Walpole held their meetings and that the Earl of Derwentwater and the other Jacobite lords on trial for rebellion, being taken daily backwards and forwards between the Tower and Westminster, made favour with their jailer to let them halt for an hour to eat what they expected to be their last good dinner on earth.

Paste for Raised Pies. — Take four pounds of flour, one pound of butter, and a little salt, mix these together, adding water, a little at a time, taking care not to put too much, as this paste must be made as stiff as possible; when tho» roughly mixed, give it two or three turns, roll it and cut it out to the shape you want for your pie. Sometimes the butter is melted in warm water, and so mixed with the flour; then it will not require so much water, and the paste will stand better; but as_ you work your paste, when you find it get too cold, warm it a little; the first method of doing it is the best, if intended to be eaten.

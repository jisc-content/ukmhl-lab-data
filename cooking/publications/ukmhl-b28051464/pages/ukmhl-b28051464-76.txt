Strain out the bones, bits of skin, etc., add the fish in pieces [these should have been kept covered in a cool place while the stock was cooking], and boil gently ten or fifteen minutes. Thicken with sufficient flour mixed smoothly with a small portion of stock and added before finishing. In order to make the dish complete and substantial, a few small suet dumplings should be well boiled and put into the tureen.” Note.—It is most important to thoroughly drain all fried fish before serving. If placed on a sheet of kitchen paper, or blotting paper, or a clean hot cloth, before a smart fire, the superfluous fat will quickly drain away, and the fish become dry and crisp. Nothing is more disgusting than a dish of greasy fish.

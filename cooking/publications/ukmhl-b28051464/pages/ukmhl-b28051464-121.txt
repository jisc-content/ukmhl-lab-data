RECIPES FOR BAKING-POWDER. 1. Equal parts of bicarbonate of soda, tartaric acid, fine flour. Sift all together two or three times, so that the ingredients may be thoroughly incorporated. 2. Equal parts of carbonate of soda, tartaric acid, and rice flour. Sift, and mix thoroughly. 3. Two ounces of bicarbonate of soda, one ounce of tartaric acid, one ounce of citric acid. Mix as above. Baking-powder made at home by any of these recipes is much cheaper than if bought in packets, and is more reliable, as common baking-powder made by unknown makers may contain injurious ingredients. SHORT CRUST FOR FRUIT PIES AND TARTS. Requisites.—Half a pound of flour, six ounces of butter, half a teaspoonful of baking-powder, quarter of a teaspoonful of salt, milk or water. (Some people add one ounce of castor sugar.) Method.—Mix the baking-powder and salt thoroughly with the flour, then put in the butter and rub it well into the flour till it disappears as butter and the whole is well mixed.

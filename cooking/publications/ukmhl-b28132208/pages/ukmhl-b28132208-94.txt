90 KOASTED POTATOES—BAKED POTATOES—SALADS. ROASTED POTATOES. 12 potatoes. 1 Roasted Joint. Pare the potatoes, and put them raw into a flat dripping- pan, below the meat while it is roasting before the fire. Turn them so that they may be browned equally all over. Tf the meat be very fat, take out a portion of the dripping, to keep the potatoes from being greasy. Serve hot with the meat, in a separate dish. BAKED POTATOES. 6 potatoes. I 3 oz. butter. Salt. 1 Pepper. Choose potatoes all as nearly of one size as possible, Avith good clear skins; wash and dry, but do not cut them; roast them slowly in an oven, or before the fire in a Dutch oven or despatch. Turn them frequently until they feel soft on pressure. They should be nicely broivned, but not burned. Serve hot on a clean folded napkin with colil butter, pepper, and salt separately. These make an excellent supper dish when cooked properly. This may be called the natural way of cooking potatoes; it brings out the flavour of the vegetable better than any other method of cooking them. SALADS. Salad is a general name for certain vegetables prepared and eaten raw. It is very Avholesome, and may be parUvken of freely. Salad is chiefly composed of lettuce, endive, radishes, green mustard, land and water cresses, celery, and young onions. All or any of these should be as fresh as possible; but if they have to bo kept, let them lie in a dark cool place on stone or brick. When the salads are to be pre- pared, wash them well but lightly, and let them lie for a quarter of an hour in cold water containing a little vinegar.

When tripe is purchased from the butcher in a raw state, it requires to be cleansed and boiled for a very long time, to be thoroughly soft and tender. The length of time will depend on the age of the animal from which it has been taken. Sometimes, for young tripe six or seven hoiire will be suffi- cient, while old tripe will perhaps take ten or twelve. In all cases, the tripe should be well washed and scraped, put into a saucepan with cold water, brought to the boil; throw away that water and wash the tripe again in pure cold water. Return to the saucepan with i>lenty of cold Avater, and boil or rather simmer it very slowly until tender; for quick boiling hardens it. A small piece of soda added to the water Avill help to keep the tripe a good colour.

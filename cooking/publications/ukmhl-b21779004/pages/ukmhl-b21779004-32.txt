HANDBOOK OF DINING. s XVII. To wait too long for a guest is a breach of politeness towards all who have arrived punctually. XVIII. A man who invites friends to dinner, and takes no personal interest in his dinner, is not worthy of friendship. XIX. The lady of the house should always take care that the coffee is excellent ; and the master of the house should be sure that the liqueurs are of the first quality. XX. When you invite a man to dinner, never forget that during the short time he is under your roof his happiness is in your hands.

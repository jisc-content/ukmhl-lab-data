6 HANDBOOK OF DINING. VI. Grood living is an act of our judgment, by which we give a preference to things agreeable to taste, to those which do not possess that quality. VII. The pleasures of the table are for all ages, all conditions, all countries, and of great variety; they are the concomitants of all other pleasures, and when all the rest are gone, they remain to console us for their loss. VIII. The dinner-table is the only place where men are not bored during the first hour. The discovery of a new dish does more for the happiness of mankind, than the discovery of a new planet.

PREFACE. (Condensed from Savarin.) Considering the pleasures of the table under all their aspects, it struck me forcibly that the sub- ject was worthy of something better than an ordi- nary cookery-book, and that there was a wide field to examine into essential and necessary func- tions, which have so direct an influence on the health and happiness of man, and even upon his affairs. This idea having assumed form and shape, the remainder came naturally. I looked around me, made notes; and often, in the midst of a sumptuous banquet, the pleasure of observation saved me from the ennuis of the dinner.

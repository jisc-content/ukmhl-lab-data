They may be basted with a very little of the water in which they were boiled; and if you have company who like melted butter to pour upon the chicken, the remainder of the liquor will be good use for that purpose. Three quarters of an hour is enough for com- mon sized chickens to roast. A smart tire is better than a slow one ; but they must be tended closely. Slices of bread, buttered, salted, and peppered, put into the stomach (not the crop) are excellent. Chickens should boil about three quarters of an hour. If old, they should boil longer. In as little water as will cook them. Chicken broth made like mutton broth. FRICASSEED CHICKEN, BROWN.

Rub half a pound of butter with a tea-spoonful of* flour, put to it a little water, melt it, and add near a quarter of a pint of thick cream, and half an ancho- vy chopped fine, not washed; set it over the fire; and as it boils up, add a large spoonful of red India soy. If that does not give it a fine colour, put a little more. Turn it into the sauce-tureen, and put some salt and hall a lemon: stir it well to hinder it from curdling. Sauee for Fowl of any sort. Rub down in a mortar the yolks of two eggs boiled hard, an anchovy, two desert-spoonfuls of oil, three of vinegar, a shalot, Cayenne if approved, and a tea- spoonful of mustard.

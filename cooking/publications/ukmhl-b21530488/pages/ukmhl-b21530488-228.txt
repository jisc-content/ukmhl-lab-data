Swell the rice with a very little milk over the fire; then mix fruit of any kind with it (currants, gooseberries scalded, pared and quartered apples, raisins, or black currants;) with one egg into the rice, to bind it; boil it well, and serve with sugar. Baked Rice Pudding. Swell rice as above : then add some more milk, an egg, sugar, allspice, and lemon-peel. Bake in a deep dish. Another, for the Family.—Put into a very deep pan half a pound of rice washed and picked; two ounces of butter, four ounces of sugar, a few allspice pounded, and tw’o quarts of milk. Less butter wdll do, or some suet. Bake in a slow oven. A George Pudding. Boil veiy tender a handful of whole rice in a small quantity of milk, with a large piece of lemon-peel. Let it drain; then mix with it a dozen of good sized apples, boiled to pulp as dry as possible ; add a glass of white wine, the yolks of five eggs, two ounces of orange and citron cut thin; make it pretty sweet. Line a mold or basin with a very good paste; beat the five whites of the eggs to a very strong froth, and mix with the other in- gredients; fill the mold, and bake it of a fine brown colour.

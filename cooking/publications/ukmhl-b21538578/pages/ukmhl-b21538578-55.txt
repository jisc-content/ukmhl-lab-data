Take all the bones and skin from any cold fish, and break it up in small pieces. 2. Put the butter in a stew-pan, over the fire, and when it is melted stir in the fiour; then mix it into a smooth paste with the milk or water, and add the cream, three or four grains of cayenne pepper, and a saltspoonful of salt and pepper mixed, and stir it over the fire until it boils and thickens. 3. Now stir in the anchovy sauce, and the fish; turn it all out on a plate and put it aside to cool. 4. When it is quite cold, form the mixture into cutlets, break an egg on to a plate, beat it up with a knife, and egg over each cutlet well ; then cover them with the bread- crumbs (not too thickly), put them in a frying-basket and fry them in boiling fat. 5. When they are fried, drain them on whitey-brown paper, and arrange tastily on a hot dish with fried parsley.

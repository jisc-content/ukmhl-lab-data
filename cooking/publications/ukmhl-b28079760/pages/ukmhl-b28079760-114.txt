110 THE PAPER-BAG COOKERY MANUAL. PASTRY AND CAKES. Pastry of all descriptions is eminently suitable for “Express’’ Paper-Bag Cookery. Pastry prepared in paper-bags is not only lighter, daintier, crisper, and more flaky than when baked in the ordinary way, but it likewise takes less time and is there- fore more economical. To Colour Pastry. In the case of pastry, it is not essential to butter the inside of the paper-bag, although refits should always be pricked in the upper portion of the bag at the beginning of the baking. The object of this is to colour the pastry a golden brown. Puff Paste. Sift half a pound of flour on to a pastry board, make a well in the centre, add a pinch of salt, one tea-spoonful of lemon juice, and about a gill of cold water. Knead the paste thoroughly, and roll out into an oblong strip. Next put 6 ozs. of fresh butter into a cloth, knead a little so as to squeeze out any moisture, and shape it into a flat cake.

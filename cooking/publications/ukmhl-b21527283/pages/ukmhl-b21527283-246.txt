Boil them in plenty of water, and drain them the moment they are done enough. If overboiled, they lose their beauty and crispness. Bad cooks sometimes dress them with meat j which is wrong, except carrots witli boiling beef. To hoil Vegetables green. Be sure the water boils when you put them in. Make them bod. very fast. Don’t cover, but watch them; and if the water has not slackened, you may be sure thOy are done when they begin to sink. Then talie them out immediately,or the colour wUl change. Hard water, especially if chalybeate, spoils the colour of such vegetables as should be green. To boil them green in hard water, put a tea-spoonful of salt of wormwood into the water when it bods, be- fore the vegetables are put in. Vegetable Marrow.—To boil or stew. This excellent vegetable may be boded as asparagus. When boded divide it lengthways into two, and serve it up on a toast accompanied by melted butter; or when nearly boiled, divide it as above, and stew gently in gravy like cucumbers. Care should be taken to choose young ones not exceeding six inches in length. To keep Green Peas.

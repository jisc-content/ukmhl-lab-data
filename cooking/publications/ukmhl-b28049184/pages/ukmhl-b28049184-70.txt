Ingredients— 1 dozen Oysters. Bread Crumbs. 1 oz. Butter. Pepper and Salt. Grated Nutmeg or Lemon Juice. Method— Put the oysters and their liquor into a small sauce- pan, and bring them to the boil. Remove the beards from the oysters, which is the part like a fringe which encircles them, and strain the liquor. Grease some china, or well-washed oyster shells. Sprinkle first into each a few bread crumbs, then put in two or three oysters. Season to taste with pepper and salt. Pour over a little of the oyster liquor or cream, cover with more bread crumbs, and pour some melted butter over the top. Brown quickly in the oven or in front of the fire, and serve very hot. Garnish them with small slices of cut lemon and sprigs of parsley. 68.—OYSTER SOUFFLES (BAKED). (Fr. Petits Souffles d’Huitres).

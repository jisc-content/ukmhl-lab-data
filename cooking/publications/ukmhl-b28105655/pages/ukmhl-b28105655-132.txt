HOUSEHOLD COOKERY. 128 MISCELLANEOUS. Porridge. 1 teacupful of Oatmeal. f teaspoonful of Salt. 3 breakfast-cupfuls of Water. Put the water in a clean pot, and when it boils stir the oatmeal very gradually in among it, and allow it to boil very gently for half-an-hour, stirring frequently; then sprinkle the salt in and boil for 10 minutes longer; dish in a plate or bowl. If porridge is preferred rather thick, a little more meal may be used. Put the flour, sugar, and salt into a basin, and mix them together ; then put the yolks of the eggs into a bowl, stir them till they are a little light, and add the milk to them. Add both gradually to the flour i'ii the basin, taking care to prevent it getting into lumps. Beat the whites to snow, and add them gently with the essence to the rest of the mixture. Melt a piece of butter the size of a walnut in a frying-pan ; then pour in all the mixture, and hold it over a very gentle fire for 10 minutes or a quarter of an hour, till set and well risen; then hold it in front of the bars to brown the top, or bake it in an oven for a quarter of an hour.

Pare G good-sized and firm apples, and take the cores out with an old penknife, so as not to break the apple. Put in a basin ^ lb. flour, lb. suet chopped very finely, a teaspoonful baking powder, and | teaspoonful of salt, which mix thoroughly, rubbing out any lumps in the suet with the fingers. Mix into a paste with cold water, and divide it into 6 equal-sized pieces; then take one of the pieces and make it round like an apple, flatten it in your hand, put the apple on the top of it, and fill the core with sugar till the hole is quite full; now work the paste up over and round the apple, closing it at the top, and leaving no crack or mark where the paste is fastened. Have a small piece of linen cloth dipped in boiling water, with some flour sprinkled on it, into which tie the dumpling pretty tightly, and drop it into boiling- water, to boil for 20 minutes.

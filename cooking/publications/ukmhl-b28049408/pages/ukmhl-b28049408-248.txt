DINNERS AND DINERS 220 We sat and watched St, Paul’s stand clear against the sunset, and Miss Dainty, her dog happy in her lap, suddenly said, cc If you give this place a good notice, I’ll never speak to you again.” “Why?” I replied. “The whitebait was delicious, the whiting pudding capital, the omelette good. I liked the fried slips and the rissoles.” “Yes, perhaps,” said Miss Dainty, with a pout. “ But they wouldn’t let me have my dog in the dining-room ! ”

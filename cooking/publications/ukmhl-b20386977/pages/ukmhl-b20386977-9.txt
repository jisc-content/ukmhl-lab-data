PREFACE. I HAVE compiled with great care and attention, the following Work on French Cookery, for the use of English families. In order to render it more useful, I have made every rule as plain and simple as it was possible. I have given the French names in most cases, because they are more generally known, and will, therefore, be better appre- ciated by the lovers of the gastronomic art. I fearlessly challenge any one to test my receipts by trying them: being quite sure that a first trial will produce a second, and a second, a third.

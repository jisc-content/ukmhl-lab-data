42 Profiteroles. These are made of choux paste omitting the sugar (p. 198). The paste must be made a little stiffer than for choux, and must be squeezed through a small paste forcer on to a greased tin, in tiny shapes. Bake until crisp. Use as a garnish for clear soup. Quenelle Garnish for Clear Soup. Make as for Quenelles of Veal (p. 62). Shape the quenelles in teaspoons ; or use a large plain pipe and force small portions on to a buttered saute-pan. Poach in the same manner as for Quenelles of Veal.

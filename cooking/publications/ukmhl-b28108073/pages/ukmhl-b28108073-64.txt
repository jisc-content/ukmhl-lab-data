Among animal foods the flesh of a certain rather restricted class of quadrupeds ranks foremost. And even with regard to these, our concern here is still further restricted to a very few that fall within the more immediate scope of our observation in Europe generally, and in our own country in particular. These comprise chiefly the ox, the sheep, the pig, and the goat, with their immature varieties—the calf, to wit, the lamb, the sucking pig, and the kid—and the pig’s.wild congener, the boar. Some would add the horse and the ass.

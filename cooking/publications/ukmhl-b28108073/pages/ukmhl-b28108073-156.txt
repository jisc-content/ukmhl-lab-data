So Peyrade—the name of his generous patron—directed the words “Speciality d\Escargots ” to be painted on the signboard over his door. The ingenuous youth proposed to supply his delicacies at thirty sous (Is. 3d) a dish; but his more experienced patron coolly put them at five francs a dish. I at first doubted the wisdom of this; but I speedily found I was mistaken. The snails took, and the “ highest quality” of the city of Lyons came flocking to the Rue St. Dominique to pay four shillings for a dish of snails. Would they have patronised the place at thirty sous ? I am inclined to doubt it. I can recommend the following way of preparing a savoury dish of tripe: Chop an ounce of parsley and of tarragon, half an ounce of savory and lemon thyme mixed, two ounces of mushrooms, a tablespoonful of capers, and two anchovies boned.

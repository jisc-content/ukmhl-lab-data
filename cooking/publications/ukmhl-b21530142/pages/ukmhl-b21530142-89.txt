The first drippings of the fluid are put back on to the serviette if they do not seem clear, and this operation is repeated until the required clearness is obtained. It almost invariably happens that, either during the cook- ing of the fish or during the clarification, the wine loses its colour through the precipitation of the colouring elements de- rived from the tannin. The only way of overcoming this difficulty is to add a few drops of liquid carmine or vegetable red; but, in any case, it is well to remember that the colour of red-wine aspic must never be deeper than a sombre pink.

Swell the rice with a very little milk over the fire ; then mix fruit of any kind with it (currants, goose- berries, scalded, pared and quartered apples, raisins, or black currants) ; with one egg into the rice to bind it; boil it well, and serve with sugar. Baked Bice pudding. Swell rice as above; then add some more milk, an egg, sugar, allspice, and lemon-peel. Bake in a deep dish. Less butter will do or some suet. Bake in a slow oven. A George pudding. Boil very tender a handful of whole rice in a small quantity of milk, with a large piece of lemon-peel. Let it drain ; then mix with it a dozen of good-sized apples, boiled to pulp as dry as possible; add a glass of white wine, the yolks of five eggs, two ounces of orange and citron cut thin ; make it pretty sweet. Line a mould or bason with a very good paste ; beat the five whites of eggs to a very strong froth, and mix with the other ingredients; fill the mould, and bake it of a fine brown colour. Serve it with the bottom upwards, and with the following sauce: two glasses of wine, a spoonful of sugar, the yolk of two eggs, and a bit of butter as large as a walnut; simmer without boiling, and pour to and from the sauce-pan, till of a proper thickness ; and put >n the dish.

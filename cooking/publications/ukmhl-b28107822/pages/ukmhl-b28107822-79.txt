Cooking, in the vocabulary of six wo- men out of eight, means waste, inefficiency and dis- comfort; many may think this exaggeration, but such is not the case, when w^e consider how much thereof is spent on food, how much is wasted by inefficient management, and how much that might be saved by proper management. Among the many important reasons and advantages of good cooking there are three essential ones ; first, it prevents waste; second, it is necessary for health ; last, but by no means of the least importance, it has much effect upon the temper and actions of weak mortals. Proper knowledge of the functions of food and their respective preparations of cooking, would save many pounds a year, it would provide better com- fort, better liouses and higher education. It is dis- tressing to note in the homes of the poor and even in some of the houses of middle classes, how exces- sive tlie ignorance and the wastefulness in cooking is.

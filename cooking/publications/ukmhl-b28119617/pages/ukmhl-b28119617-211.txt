Dessert. 1.—This soup which is simply the ordinary mock-turtle not thickened, is to my mind the nicer form of the two; tastes vary however, so I shall describe both methods. First, for the clear :—Procure the stock meat as usual, and either half an ox-head unskinned, or a sheep’s head (a calf’s head is the proper thing, but we must deal with fact, not fiction, knowing that we cannot get veal here), well,—clean the head thoroughly, keeping the skin on, and scalding it to get rid of the hair or wool: remove every atom of brain, and wash the meat in several waters, set it to soak awhile in cold water, and then place it in your soup kettle; cover it with water and let it boil slowly for half an hour, skimming all fat, and scum that rises : take it up (it will be about half done) and set it with a weight

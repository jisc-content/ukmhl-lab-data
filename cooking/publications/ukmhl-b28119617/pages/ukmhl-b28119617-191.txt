(Goufte.) “ Ramequins en caisses” :—Take two ounces of mild grated cheese, and two ounces of white bread-crumbs ; soak the crumbs in milk, and pound them in a mortar with the cheese, and a little butter, till the whole is well mixed ; now season the mixture with pepper and salt, adding a tea-spoonful of mustard powder, and the yolks of three eggs. Finally beat up the egg whites to a stiff froth, mingle it with the mix- ture, and fill your paper cases, which should be well buttered to prevent their burning outside, or “catching” the fondue within : bake them from ten minutes to a quarter of an hour, and serve them as soon as they have raised their heads, and have slightly taken colour. “ Beignets souffles au Parmesan” will be found at page 154?, and several dishes demanding cheese amongst the menus. N. B.—The best flour for fondues and souffles is potato flour. China souffle cases are nicer than paper ones.

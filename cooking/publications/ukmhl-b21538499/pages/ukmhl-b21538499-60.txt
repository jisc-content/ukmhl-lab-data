Economical Brown Soup .—Required: about 2 lbs. roast beef bones; i small carrot; i small turnip; i small onion; i dessert spoonful flour or com flour; i oz. dripping ; 2 quarts cold water ; 1 tablespoonful ketchup; # teaspoonful browning; % teaspoonful salt; 1 pinch pepper. Cleanse, prepare, and slice the vegetables (see page 37); break the bones into several pieces. Brown the vegetables with the dripping in a saucepan; draw it to the side of the fire, add the bones, water and seasoning; simmer gently for 3 or 4 hours, skimming carefully. Half an hour before it is ready, break the flour with a little cold water, and stir it carefully into the soup. When ready, remove the bones and vegetables, serve the soup. The bones may be used again in the stockpot; the vegetables may be used for vegetable soup, or for thickening any soup. Richer Brown Soup.—Prepare and cook as in last recipe; but, instead of roast beef bones, use 1 lb. leg of beef; cut it into two or three pieces, and brown it with the vegetables.

CHAPTER III. 18 On seasoning in the kitchen—Patent sauces not real cookery—Soups and vegetables not to be peppered in the kitchen—Pure wine only, no “ cooking ” wine—Grilling preferable to frying for fish and fowl, as well as for meat—Caution as to over- cooking. Do not allow your cook to send up meats un- seasoned. Salt and freshly ground black pepper should be judiciously used while cooking, if the flavour of the meat is to be fully developed ; but do not, on any account, admit nutmeg, mace, and hot sauces of the Peppershire kind into your kitchen.

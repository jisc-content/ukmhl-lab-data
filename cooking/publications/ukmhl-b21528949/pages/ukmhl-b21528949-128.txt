120 SICKROOM COOKERY. APPLE SNOW. 3 apples. 1 tablespoonful of castor sugar. 1 strip of lemon rind. 2 tablespoonfuls of cold water. 1 white of egg. Peel, core, aud quarter tbe apples. Pat them into a saucepan with a thinly cut strip of lemon rind and the water. Stew until soft, remove the lemon rind, and heat the apple to a pulp. Whip the white of egg very stiffly, and lightly stir in the apple pulp, add the sugar, and whisk until stiff. Serve in jelly glasses or on a glass dish. BOILED BATTER PUDDING. 4 oz. of flour. 2 eggs. \ pint of milk, teaspoonful of salt. Sift the flour and salt into a basin, add the eggs, and stir the milk in gradually. Beat well, and if possible let it stand for twenty minutes. Put in a well-greased pudding basin, scald and flour a cloth and tie it over the top. Boil for one hour.

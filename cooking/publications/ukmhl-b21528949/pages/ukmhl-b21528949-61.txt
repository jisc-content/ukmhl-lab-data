SICKROOM COOKERY. 53 RESTORATIVE SOUP. 1 lb. of knuckle of veal. 1 chicken. I lb. of skin of beef. 3 quarts of cold water. 1 teaspoonful of salt. Cut the meat into small pieces, removing all fat. Put the bones and meat into a saucepan with the water, and add the salt. When it boils skim well, and simmer very gently for five hours. Strain, and when cold remove the fat from the top. Heat as required. If vegetable flavour- ing is allowed, this soup will be improved by the addition of a small carrot, turnip, and onion, to be cooked in the soup. SHIN-OE-BEEF SOUP. 3 lb. of shin of beef. 2 oz. of dripping. 1 carrot. 1 turnip. 1 onion. 1 bunch of herbs. 1 piece of celery. 2 quarts of cold water. 1 teaspoonful of salt. 1 tablespoonful of fine sago. Cut the meat from the bones into small pieces. Break up the bone and remove the marrow. Make the dripping very hot in a saucepan, and fry the meat and bone a good brown in it. Pour off the dripping, add the water and salt, and bring it to the boil. Skim carefully, and put in the vegetables, peeled and cut into quarters.

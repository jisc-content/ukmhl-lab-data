87 SICKROOM COOKERY. POTTED CHICKEN. 1 lb. of cooked chicken. 2 oz. of cooked ham or tongue. 2 oz. of butter. Seasoning, nutmeg, or aromatic spice. Pass the chicken and ham through a mincing machine twice. Pound in the mortar with the butter and season well. Rub the mixture through a wire sieve. Put it into small china jars, and smooth over the top. Pour a tablespoonful of clarified butter over it. Omit the ham or tongue if objected to. CALF'S FEET. 2 calf’s feet. 1 onion. 1 blade of mace. 1 bunch of herbs. 1 quart of water. Pepper and salt. Put the feet in water and bring to the boil to thoroughly cleanse them, strain away the water, then put the feet into a saucepan, with the onion, mace, herbs, and a quart of cold water. Simmer very gently for four hours. Remove all the bones and cut into neat pieces. Have some parsley and butter sauce ready and pour over. CALF’S HEAD. \ a calf’s head.

Then into the sauce put some slices of truffles or mushrooms, and stew them ; add a little butter if necessary. Dish up the fish with the mushrooms and fried sippets. N.B.—Soles may be cooked in this way. 57.—Baked Whitings. Butter a dish; spread on this butter some finely chopped parsley, and a little onion, salt, and a little nutmeg; then place the whitings (boned whitings are preferable). Pour some hot butter over them, then equal quantities of white wine and stock. Put this dish in the oven, or on the top of your stove, with a cover over. When half cooked, turn the fish over carefully. When quite cooked, pour the sauce into a saucepan without disturbing the fish; add some butter and flour; simmer till thick, then add pepper and some lemon-juice. Pour the sauce back over the fish and serve in the dish the whitings are cooked in. 58.—Buttered Whitings. Sprinkle a little pepper and salt over the whitings, flour them thoroughly on both sides, place some butter on a gratin dish, lay the whitings upon it, and put them into a hot oven.

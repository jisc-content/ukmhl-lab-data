CHAPTER XIII. lEomatoea. FROM being one of the least known and most expensive vegetables^ tomatoes have become very common and plentifal_, bought and eaten on the streets as freely as cherries or apples^ and used in the kitchen almost as much as we can desire. Perhaps never better or more beneficial than when eaten raw^ still, to be really nice thus^ they ought to be freshly-gathered from the plant; and few persons are able to do this. Next to eating them rav/ we may rank eating them marinees/^ or en salade/^ viz._, slicing them in a dish, and dressing with salt and pepper, vinegar and oil.

534 COOKERY TERMS EXPLAINED. Mask—To coyer completely. Nouilles—An Italian paste, resembling macaroni; it is flat, instead of being in pipes. Panada—Bread soaked in milk, used principally for quenelles and fine farces. Passer—To fry lightly. Pate—A raised crust pie. Po'ile—A light braise for white meats. The difference between this and the braise is, that in the former the meat, or whatever it may be, need not be so much done as in the latter. Potage—Another term for soup. Purge—Any meat, fish, or other article, boiled to a pulp, and rubbed through a sieve. Quenelles—A fine farce ; it is generally poached when used. Poux—Butter and flour amalgamated together over the fire to a proper consistence, either white or brown. Salmi—A highly-seasoned hash. Sauter—To try very lightly. Sabotiere, or Sorbetiere—A pewter or tin vessel, in which are placed the moulds con- taining the substance to be frozen. Tammy—A silk sieve, or tammy cloth. Tourner, or Turn—To stir a sauce; also to pare and cut roots, vegetables, and fruits, neatly. Tourte—A puff-paste pie. Tanner—To take up sauce, or other liquid, in a spoon, and turn it over quickly.

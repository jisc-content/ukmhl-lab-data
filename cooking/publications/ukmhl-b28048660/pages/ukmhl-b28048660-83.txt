FISH. —*— 152. Whitings or Sole A. la Prevoyante. Cut your fish into fillets and marine it thus :—Lay them two hours before cooking in a flat dish, and pour over them one spoonful of vinegar, one of oil, six or eight onions sliced, a few sprigs of thyme, a little parsley, and four or five hay-leaves, pepper, and salt. Set the dish in a cool larder. To prepare the hatter in which they are to he cooked, put into a hason two tahlespoonfuls of flour, equal quantities of small heer and water, and one tahlespoonful of oil; heat it well up, keeping it quite thin. It should he about the consistency of thick cream. Whisk the white of a raw egg to a froth, and add it to the hatter just as you are ready to use it. Have your hot lard ready; take up the fillets of fish separately, and dab them about in the hatter till they are covered with it; as each is done, drop them into the hot lard and fry of a fine golden colour. It is a good guide to know if the lard is of the proper heat, to put in a few parsley leaves, and if it immediately crisps it is right. Tomato- sauce should he served with this, hut separately. 153.

Chicken-Tea. Skin and divide the chicken in pieces, leaving out the back ; put it in some clear water, with a blade of mace, a few white peppercorns, and an onion sliced; simmei till it is sufficiently strong, then strain, and when cold carefully remove all the fat. It may be drunk cold or heated again. 795. Yeey Steengthening Chicken-Beotii. Cut down and bruise two or three cock chickens ; put them in a saucepan with three quarts of water ; simmer till the juice is entirely drawn from them ; press it well out, strain, and return the soup to the saucepan ; add salt, whole pepper, rice, or vermicelli; a whole chicken may be boiled in the soup, and served so in it. In spring, young vegetables, parsley, and chervil may be added. 796. Stewed Paeteidges. Half-stew one or two partridges ; cut them up, take out the largest bones, put them in a pan with the liquor they were stewed in, with a little salt, a blade of mace, and a bit of lemon-peel; when done, serve with the gravy in the dish.

Black Currant Syrup. Pick any quantity of black currants you please clean from their stalks 3 put them into a large earthen jar 3 cover the jar with a cloth, and put it into a cellar or any damp place to stand for eight days 3 they will probably be mouldy on the top 3 remove this 3 stir them up well with a large silver spoon 3 put them on to a hair sieve, and the juice will run through quite clear 3 to each pint put one pound of powdered sugar 3 boil ten minutes 3 let it stand till cold 3 bottle, cork, and seal it up. This is very good for colds, coughs, etc. 763. To Preserve Cherries for Drying. Take the best Kentish cherries 3 remove the stones, and weigh the fruit 3 allow half a pound of double-refined sugar to each pound of fruit 3 clarify and boil the sugar to blowing height 3 put in the fruit 3 boil a minute or two 3 then take it off the fire, and let it stand a little 3 put the pan on the fire, and let it boil till the fruit looks transparent 3 leave the cherries to stand in the syrup four or five days, then lay them on sieves, and dry them on a slow stove 3 change them on to clean sieves every day till they are dry 3 put them away in card-board boxes. 764.

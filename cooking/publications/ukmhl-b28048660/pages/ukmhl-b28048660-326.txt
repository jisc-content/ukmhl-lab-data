S23. Barley Gruel. W ash four ounces of pearl barley; boil it into two quarts of water, with a bit ot cinnamon, till it is reduced to one quart; strain, and return it to the sauce-pan, with a little sugar and three-quarters of a, pint of port wine j make it quite hot, and it is ready to be used as wanted. It is very strengthening. 824. Ground-Rice Milk. Boil one spoonful of ground rice, rubbed down smooth/ with a pint and a half of milk, a bit of cinnamon and lemon-peel, a little nutmeg and sugar to taste ; add the sugar only when nearly done. It should be thick and smooth. 825. Thick Barley-Water. Wash a quarter of a pound of pearl barley, and boil it in a gallon of water till it is quite soft, smooth, and white ; then strain, and flavour with lemon-juice, and sweeten with a little syrup.

(& Chapter on (Bnfreee 73 BEEF MINCE. Have a pound of beef from the round minced very fine by your butcher, and free it from all sinews and stringy bits. Heat in the blazer two tablespoonfuls of butter, put in the meat and a teaspoonful of onion-juice. Stir for three or four minutes, or until the meat is hot through ; add salt, pepper, and the juice of half a lemon, and serve. FRICASSEE OF DRIED BEEF. One cupful dried beef, chopped fine. One tablespoonful butter. Half pint milk. Two eggs. Melt the butter in the milk, over hot water, put in the meat and cook about five minutes, add the beaten eggs slowly and stir until the sauce is thick. Serve on toast or fried bread.

Take a pint of beans, pour a quart of boiling water upon them and let them remain in soak until the next day; cut a lettuce in four pieces and put it with the beans in some fresh hot water, throw in a small bunch of parsley, and a slice of ham, boil them until the whole are tender. Chop up some onions, with a clove of garlic, fry them and then put them into a stewpan, put the beans to them, with a well beaten egg and some spice. Send them to table hot. Endive au Jus. Split some endive in half, blanch and drain them, season each with some pepper, nutmeg, and salt, and tie the endive together and put them into a stew-pan with some bacon sliced over them ; in the same way put in some veal and beef sliced, two onions, two carrots, two cloves and a bunch of sweet herbs, moisten the whole with some rich gravy, stew the en- dive for three hours, then drain and press them in a cloth, trim and dish them up for table. Green Peas. A delicious vegetable, a grateful accessory to many dishes of a more substantial nature. Green peas should be sent to the table green^ no dish looks less tempting than peas if they wear an autumnal aspect. Peas should also be young, and as short a time as possible should be suffered to elapse between rhe periods of shelling and boiling. If it is a matter of conse- quence to send them to table in perfection, these rules must be strictly observed.

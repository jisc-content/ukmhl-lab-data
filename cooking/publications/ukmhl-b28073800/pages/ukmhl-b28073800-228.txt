Sturgeon should be cut up in slices of about an inch, or a little more, in thickness, which, being half-fried, should be placed in a stewpan with some good veal broth, an onion, and a bundle of sweet herbs, and be allowed to stew until it becomes perfectly tender. Having then fried an onion or two in the butter in which the fish was previously fried, pour this, and also the gravy in which the fish was stewed, into a saucepan, adding to it a glass or two of Avine, some butter rolled in flour, or about the same proportion of cream, and a spoonful of ketchup, or half that quantity of Cornubian sauce.

These remarkable plats were succeeded by Lobster en Aspic, then ‘ Plumpudding’ & I’Anglaise, a roast turkey fol- lowing, and wound up with a large cake and ice. The next day’s dinner began with * Kachgemachte Schildkroten-suppe’ (mock turtle-soup); Petitspat (is of lobster; then—horribile dictu!—salmon, quite out of season, succeeded by ‘ Ente mit Truffeln’ (ducks with truffles). And now again come the startling dishes—‘ Kohl mit Austern ’ (cabbage with oysters!) —it makes one shudder to think of it, but was in fact so good that the writer asked for, and obtained the receipt from her hostess, and it will be found in the Appendix, No. 24 ; stewed lentils (‘Linsen’) and four different lands of sausages—Leber-Wurst, Frank- furter Brat-Wurst—which are very like Oxford sausages ; Blut-Wurst—almost identical with black puddings ; and Knack-Wurst—not unlike white puddings. These were followed by‘Kehbraten’ (roe- deer) with different kinds of compotes and salads. Biz la Malte and dessert brought the dinner to a conclusion,

Fried Potatoes. Cut raw potatoes into the shape and size of large corks, and then into thinnish slices ; throw them into hot clarified butter, and fry till they are crisp and of a good brown. Drain all the grease from them on a cloth, and serve very hot on a napkin, and sprinkle them over with a little fine salt. 348. Mashed Potatoes. The potatoes should be first thoroughly well boiled, carefully remove all defects or hard bits ; mash them very small with a wooden spoon, adding as much cream as will make them the right consistence, and season with peeper and salt; or, instead of cream, you may use butter and boiling milk, and they can be browned after they are dressed in the dish they are to be served on in the Dutch oven. 349. Potato Souffles. Roast eight potatoes in the oven ; when they are quite done scoop out the insides and mash them up with a little bit of butter and a small quantity of cream, some pepper and salt; mix thoroughly.

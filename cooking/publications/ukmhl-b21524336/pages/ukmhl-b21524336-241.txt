Marmalade or fruit tarts of any sort may be made in the same way. 584. Orange Tart. Pare off the rind, quarter and remove the core of a dozen and a half of China oranges; boil them in a little sugar and water about three minutes, and lay them on a sieve to drain. Take the juice that runs from them and the liquor they were boiled in and boil it to a syrup; put the oranges in for a minute or two, but without boiling them; then lay them in the shape of crust, which must be previously baked.

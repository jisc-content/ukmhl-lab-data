MEAT, POULTRY, GAME. • 211. Salt Beef. To salt beef for immediate use, common salt, previously •well dried at the fire, is better than any other pickle. The kernels should be carefully extracted, then sprinkle the meat well with salt, and six hours afterwards hang it up to drain. After it has drained, rub it thoroughly in every part with salt, and lay it in a salting-tub. Cover it over with a cloth saturated with the brine, and then with the cover, which should fit close to the tub. It must be turned and rubbed every day with the brine. A round of beef, of twenty-five pounds’ weight, will require one and a half pound of .salt, to be all rubbed in at first. Turned and rubbed every day, if not wished very salt, it may be eaten in four or five days.

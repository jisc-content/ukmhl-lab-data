Currants or cherries may he added if liked. 498. Marmalade Pudding. Take about half a teacupful of milk, one tablespoonful of fine flour, and about two ounces of white sugar ; put it on the fire, and stir till it boils; then add the yolks of four eggs, and one small pot of marmalade, reserving most of the chips to line the mould with. Mix all well up together, then beat up the whites of five eggs, and stir them lightly in. Butter a mould and line it completely with orange chips, put in the pudding, and steam it for half an hour with fire under and over. 499. Sir Watkin Wynneâ€™s Pudding.

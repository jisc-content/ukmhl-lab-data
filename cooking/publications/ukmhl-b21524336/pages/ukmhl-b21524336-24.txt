A near relation of the Compiler’s, and one who thoroughly understood and enjoyed good living, and from whom she acquired many receipts and much culmary lore, once said to her,—“ Original English, or what is called plain cooking, is the worst, and the most ignorant, and the most extravagant, in the known world !” There is no doubt considerable truth in this; but she is not sure that the word plain should not be omitted, and questions much if there is or ever was original English cookery. The obser- vation would then resolve itself into—“ English cookery is the worst,” etc. etc.; and this she thinks few will be inclined to dispute.

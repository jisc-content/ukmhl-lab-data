584 COSMOPOLITAN COOKERY. It may be as well to observe, though it should be scarcely requisite, that all the copper-vessels composing the ‘batterie de cuisine’, must be at all tines kept in a state of the utmost purity of Cleanliness, as well outside, as inside ; on the outside, they ought, as soon as may be after use, to be again, thoroughly scoured clean, polished, mirror-bright; seeing they are, after all, in fact, the kitchens principal and indispen- sable adornment; on the inside, they ought to be well-tinned, and frequently fresh tinned ; for the use of an ill-tinned saucepan would in cur the risk of permanent injury to health. In many countries the ‘batterie de cuisine’ is tinned on the outside also, as well as inside.

A short list of exceptional soups, each with a distinct individual character, may still be referred to as not belonging to the above-mentioned classes. Such as real turtle, made either in part or wholly from the live animal; a large proportion is prepared from the imported dried flesh, when the stock is almost invariably made from veal and beef ;* after this come mock turtle or calf’s head; ox-tail, mulligatawny; while giblet and “cock-a-leekie,” are almost stews ; the latter doubtless designed originally to present the patriarch of the poultry yard in an eatable form. Another Scotch soup, “hotch-potch,” like an Italian “minestrone,” may contain almost a meal of fragrant combinations.

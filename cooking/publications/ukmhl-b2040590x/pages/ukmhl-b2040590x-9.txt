PREFACE. In introducing this book to the notice of Con- fectioners and the public generally, the Author thinks it necessary to make a few remarks by way of preface. During the past twenty years, Confectionery, like other processes of manufacture, has consider- ably altered. The rapid development of the science of chemistry has been the means of ex- plaining or simplifying many of the difficulties which formerly beset the practitioner in the art of Confectionery. New fashions have come up, and tastes have changed, so that what was once popular and in the appreciation of the refined, is now considered obsolete and in bad taste.

To make Syrup of any Flower:—Clip your flowers, and take their weight in sugar; then take a high gallipot, and a row of flowers, and a strowing of sugar, till the pot is full; then put in two or three spoonfuls of the same syrup or still’d water; tye a cloth on the top of the pot, and put a tile on that, and set your gallipot in a kettle of water over a gentle fire, and let it infuse till the strength is out of the flowers, Avhich will be in four or five hours; then strain it thro’ a flannel, and when ’tis cold bottle it up.

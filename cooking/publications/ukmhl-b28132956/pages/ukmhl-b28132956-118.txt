Croquettes. Croquette is the name given to oval, round, ball, cutlet, or cork shapes of minced meats, fish, poultry or game. These shapes are egged, crumbed, and fried in clarified butter or dripping. Friandines. These are made of puff paste, rolled out thinly with a 2-inch fluted round cutter; a portion of prepared mince or salpicon of meat or game, etc., is placed in the centre of each round ; this is covered with a round of paste, egged, dipped in crushed vermicelli, and fried in clarified butter, lard, or dripping.

Mix 5 oz. of butter in 3 lbs. of dry flour, and 5 oz, of fine Lisbon sugar; add six ounces of currants, washed and dried, and some pimento finely powdered. If properly mixed it will be a stifi* paste. Bake in a brisk oven. A good Pound Cake. Beat a pound of butter to a cream, and mix with it the whites and yolks of eight eggs beaten apart. Have ready, warm by the fire, a pound of flour, and the same of sifted sugar, mix them, and a few cloves, a little nutmeg and cinnamon in fine powder together; then by degrees work the ingredients into the butter and eggs. When well beaten, add a glass of wine and some caraways. It must be beaten a full hour. Butter a pan, and bake it a full hour in a quick oven.

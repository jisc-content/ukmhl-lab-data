Boil six whitings, and a large eel, (or the eel and half a thornback, well cleaned), with as much water as will cover them; skim them clean, and put in whole pepper, mace, ginger, parsley, an onion, a little thyme, and three cloves. Boil to a mash. Pick fifty craw-fish, or a hundred prawns; pound the sheiks, and a little roll; but first boil them with a little water, vinegar, salt, and herbs: put this liquor over the shells in a sieve; then pour the other soup clear from the sediment. Chop a lobster, and add this to it, with a quart of good beef gravy: a'ld also the tails of the craw-fish or the prawns and some flour and butter; and season as may be liked if not high enough.

DOMESTIC COOKERY. ■222 A George Pudding. W Boil very tender a handful of whole rice in a small quantity of milk, with a large piece of lemon-peel. Let it drain; then mix with it a dozen of good-sized apples, boiled to a pulp as dry as possible ; add a glass of white wine, the yolks of five eggs, two ounces of orange and citron cut thin; make it pretty sweet. Line a mould or basin with a very good paste; beat the five whites of the eggs to a very strong froth, and mix with the other ingre- dients ; fill the mould, and bake it of a fine brown colour. Serve it with the bottom upwards, with the following sauce : two glasses of wine, a spoonful of sugar, the yolks of two eggs, and a bit of butter as large as a wal- nut; simmer without boiling, and pour to and from the saucepan, till of a proper thickness; and put in the dish. An excellent 'plain Potato Pudding. A crust or not, as you like. Bake it. If wanted richer, put three ounces more butter, sweet- meats and almonds, and another egg.

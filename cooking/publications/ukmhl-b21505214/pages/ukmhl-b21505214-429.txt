Wet a jelly-bag, and squeeze it dry, then strain the liquor, which is uncommonly fine. Lemonade that has the flavour and appearance of Jelly. Pare two Seville oranges and six lemons as thin as possible, and steep them four hours in a quart of hot water. Boil a pound and a quarter of loaf-sugar in three pints of water, and skim it. Add the two liquors to the juice of six China oranges, and twelve lemons ; stir the whole well, and run it through ajelly-bag till clear. Then add a little orange water, if you like the flavour, and, if wanted, more sugar. It will keep well if corked. Raspberry Vinegar. Put a pound of fine fruit into a China bowl, and pour upon it a quart of thej best white-wine vinegar; next day strain the liquor on a pound of fresh raspberries; and the following day do the same, but do not squeeze the fruit, only drain the liquor as dry as you can from it.

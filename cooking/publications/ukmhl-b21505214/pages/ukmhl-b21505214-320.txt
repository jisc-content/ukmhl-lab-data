Queues de Veau.-—Calves’ Tails. Having nicely cleaned and soaked four calves’ tails, cut off the small ends, and blanch them. Dry, flour, and fry them a fine brown in butter, drain the fat from them, and having ready a pint of weak broth, with a bunch of sweet herbs, ehibols, two bay-leaves, half a pint of mushrooms, pepper, and salt, boiled up and skimmed, wipe the sides of the casserole, put the tails in, and simmer very slowly until they are quite tender. Keep them hot while the gravy is strained, and boiled to a glaze, to cover them. If you have no mushrooms, mix a little of the powder into the gravy just before serving.

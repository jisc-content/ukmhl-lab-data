MUTTON. 73 The kernel in the fat on the thick part of the leg should be taken out by the butcher, for it taints first there. The bloody part of the neck should be cut off when first brought in. The brisket changes first in the breast; and if it is to be kept, it is best to rub it with a little salt, should the weather be hot. Every kernel should be taken out of all sorts of meat as soon as brought in : then wipe dry. For roasting, mutton should hang as long as it will keep, the hind-quarters especially, but not so long as to taint; for, whatever fashion may authorize, putrid juices ought not to be taken into the stomach ; but, by due care, and a good larder, meat may be hung long', to the great improvement of its flavour.

Sprinkle with salt, and keep twenty-four hours ; then wash and wipe it dry, wet over with egg, cover with crumbs of bread ; make some lard or fine dripping, and two large spoonsful of vinegar, boiling-hot; lay the fish in, and fry it a fine colour, drain it from the fat, and serve it with fried parsley round, and anchovy-sauce. You may dip the fish in vinegar, and not put it into the pan. HERRINGS AND SPRATS. To smoke Herrings. Clean, and lay them in salt and a little saltpetre one night; then hang them on a stick, through the eyes, in a row. Have ready an old cask, in which put some sawdust, and in the midst of it a heater red-hot; fix the stick over the smoke, and let them remain twenty-four hours. Fried Herrings. Serve them of a light brown, with onions sliced and fried round them ; or without onions. Broiled Herrings. Flour them first, and do of a good colour; plain butter for sauce. Potted Herrings, which much resemble Char. When in high season, choose a dozen of the finest herrings, clean and remove every scale ; wash them twice over, drying them with fresh cloths each time.

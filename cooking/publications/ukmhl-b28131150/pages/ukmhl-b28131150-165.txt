MARMELADE DE GOINGS.* Cut some ripe quinces into quarters, take out the cores, and put the fruit into a saucepan with a little water at the bottom. Do them very slowly, and, when they are quite soft, mash and pass them through a sieve; add an equal weight of fine loaf-sugar, put them into a preserving-pan, and do them slowly by the side of the fire until they are well thickened and of a good colour. Put it by in glasses or moulds well tied down.

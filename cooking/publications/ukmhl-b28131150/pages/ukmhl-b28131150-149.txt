MARMELADE DE POIRES.* Put some dressing pears into spring water, and simmer them slowly until they are tender; take them up, peel them, and scrape all the pulp away from the cores; to each pound of pulp put twelve ounces of loaf-sugar, place it in a preserving-pan over the fire, and stir it well until it is quite stifi*, then put it into pots or moulds. The pears may be slowly baked in- stead of being boiled, if more convenient.

Freeze till it becomes the consistency of a stiff batter, then put into a quart ice pudding mould. Grease the joints and hinges of the mould very thoroughly with lard or unsalted butter, so as to prevent any entrance of salt or water. Pack the mould in ice and salt for 2 or 3 hours. To turn out.—Remove the fat from the mould, loosen the lid and immerse for an instant in cold water. Dry the mould with a clean cloth and turn out on a folded d’oyley or paper.

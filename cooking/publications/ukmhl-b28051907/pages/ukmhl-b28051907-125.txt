NINTH LESSON. SAUCES. DRAWN BUTTER. Beat one cup of butter and two table-spoonfuls of flour to a cream, and pour over this one pint of boil- ing water. Set on the fire and let it come to a boil, but do not boil. Serve immediately. EGG SAUCE. Chop up two hard-boiled eggs, and stir into drawn butter. OYSTER SAUCE. Set a basin on the fire with half a pint of oysters and one pint of boiling water; let them boil three minutes, and then stir in half a cup of butter beaten to a cream, with two table-spoonfuls of flour ; let this come to a boil, and serve. CELERY SAUCE. Chop fine two heads of celery, and boil one hour ; at the end of that time have about a pint and a half of water with it, and stir in two table-spoonfuls of flour wet with cold water. Boil this ten minutes, and then stir in two table-spoonfuls of butter. Season with pepper and salt, and serve. /

Over and over again have I also seen in Continental kitchens large basius tilled with cold water in which to immerse the bare arms and lave the face, which simple expedient will give refreshing coolness, and to some extent counteract thirst. The skin may be said to drink in the water, as the weary and thirsty traveller oftens finds when immersing his bare legs and arms in seme dirty ditch or stagnant pool by the wayside. The skin here acts as a filter, excluding all impurities.

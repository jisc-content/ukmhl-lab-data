617 CAKES. CHAP. XXIII.] COCOA-NUT GINGERBREAD. Mix well together ten ounces of fine wheaten flour, and six of flour of rice (or rice ground to powder), the grated rind of a lemon, and three-quarters of an ounce of ginger ; pour nearly boiling upon these a pound of treacle, five ounces of fresh butter, and five of sugar,, melted together in a saucepan; beat the mixture, which will be almost a batter, with a wooden spoon, and when quite smooth leave it till it is perfectly cold, then add to it five ounces of grated cocoa-nut, and when it is thoroughly blended with the other ingre- dients, lay the paste in small heaps upon a buttered tin, and bake them in a very slack oven from half to three- quarters of an hour.

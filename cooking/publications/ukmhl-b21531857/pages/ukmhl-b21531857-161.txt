The gravy may be highly flavoured with mushrooms when this is done. BREAD SA.UCE WITH ONION. Put into a very clean saucepan nearly half a pint of fine bread-crumbs, and the white part of a large mild onion, cut into quarters; pour to these three quarters of a pint of new milk, and boil them very gently, keeping them often stirred, until the onion is perfectly tender, which will be in from forty minutes to an hour. Press the whole through a.hair sieve, which should be as clean as possible, reduce the sauce by quick boiling, should it be too thin, add a seasoning of salt and grated nutmeg, an ounce of butter, and four spoonsful of cream, and when it is of the proper thickness, pour out the sauce, and send it quickly to table.

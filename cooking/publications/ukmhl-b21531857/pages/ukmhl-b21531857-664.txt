Common galette: flour, 2 lbs.; butter, | to 1 lb.; no eggs. CORNISH HEAVY CAKE. Mix with a pound and a half of flour, ten ounces of well-cleaned currants, and a small teaspoonful of salt; make these into a smooth paste with clotted cream (any which is very thick will do), roll the cake till it is an inch and a quarter in depth, and bake it thoroughly in a quick oven, after having scored the top. Flour, 1-J lb.; currants, 10 ozs. ; salt, small teaspoon- ful; clotted, or very thick cream, J to full pint: 35 to 45 minutes, brisk oven. FLEED OR FLEAD CAKES. These are very much served as a tea-cake at the tables of the superior order of Kentish farmers. For the mode of making them, proceed as for flead-crust (see Chapter XYI.); cut the cakes small with a round cutter, and leave them more than half an inch thick; if well-made they will rise much in the oven.

BOUILLON. {The Common Soup of France; Cheap, and very Whole- some.) This soup, or broth, as we should perhaps designate it in England, is made once or twice in the week, in every family of respectabi- lity in France ; and by the poorer classes as often as their means will enable them to substitute it for the vegetable or maigre soups, on which they are more commonly obliged to subsist. It is served usually on the first day, with slices of untoasted bread soaked it; on the second, it is generally varied with tfr semoulina.

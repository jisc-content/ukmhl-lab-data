T^he Child'^s Cookery Book 36 Fish Balls Break the remains of any cold fish to pieces with a fork, remove all skin and bone, and shred very fine, add the same quantity of mashed potato, and make it into a stiff paste with butter, a little milk, and the yolk of one egg, pepper, and salt. Flour the hands well, and make into small round balls. Put about two ounces of dripping into a frying-pan, and make it very hot, then drop in the fish balls, being very careful not to let the fat splash out, and fry till they are a nice brown.

T'he Child'^s Cookery Book 76 Ginger Pudding Chop two and a half ounces of suet very finely, and add to it a quarter of a pound of flour. Mix it well together and then stir in a quarter of a pound of moist sugar and a tablespoonful of ground ginger. Mix all the ingredients thoroughly and put them dry into a well-buttered mould, tie a small pudding cloth over it, and drop the mould into boiling water. This must boil for three or four hours, and be kept boiling all the time.

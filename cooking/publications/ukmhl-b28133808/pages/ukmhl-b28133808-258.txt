Apple Pudding. 1 lb. of flour. 6 or 8 ozs. of suet. A pinch of salt. 1 teaspoonful of powder. Ingredients. Some apples. 3 tablespoonfuls or more of moist sugar. baking- The grated rind of a small lemon. 2 or three cloves. Method.—Prepare the paste, and line a basin as for beef-steak pudding. Put in the apples, which should be pared, cored and quartered, and sprinkle in the sugar and lemon-rind. Put on the cover of paste, and tie over it a well-scalded and floured cloth. Boil for one hour or longer; the length of time will depend on the fruit used. Plain damson, gooseberry, currant, or any fresh fruit puddings, aro made in tho same manner. Note.—All these puddings are nicer if they are served in the basin, which can then be lined half-way only with the paste. Yorkshire (or Batter) Pudding.

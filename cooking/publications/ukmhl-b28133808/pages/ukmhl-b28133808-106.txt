Pepper and salt to taste. Method.—Peel the onions, and cut them in slices from the top to tho bottom. Fry them in the butter, but do not let them discolour. Then boil them in tho water until quite soft. Pub them with their liquor through a hair sieve. Put this puree into a stowpan on the fire to boil. When boiling stir in a thickening of the flour. Stir and cook well. Then add tho milk or cream, pepper and salt to taste. Let the soup boil up, and it is ready. Serve with fried croutons of bread. Turnip Soup.

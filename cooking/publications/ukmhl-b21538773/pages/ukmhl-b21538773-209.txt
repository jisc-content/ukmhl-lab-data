o 193 CHAPTER IX The Management of the Oven OF the many trials that beset the culinary novice, either amateur or professional, few exceed in worry the oven. There are times when it really seems possessed with the spirit of “ cussedness,” and nothing avails to make it do its work properly. As a matter of fact, ovens require, and repay, study ; they vary greatly, even when apparently exactly the same in make, and unless this variation is noticed and attended to, trouble and failure are pretty certain to follow. The larger the oven, as a general rule, the more trustworthy it is, especially if a brick one. Iron ovens are always liable to variation, and the smaller they are the more they vary.

English and French mustard, working it up with thick cream instead of oil, adding this very slowly, as it is even more apt to curdle than oil, thin it with about a dessertspoonful of strained lemon juice, getting the sauce quite white and thick. Now rub the dish in which it is to be served once or twice with fresh cut garlic, stir into the sauce the hard- boiled egg white and two or three truffles, all cut into julienne strips, and serve in the garlic-rubbed dish, after standing for an hour or two on ice.

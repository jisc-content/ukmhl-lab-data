I ONION SOUP MAIGRE.—E. R. I Take six large onions, and a couple of turnips, boil I them in four quarts of water, with a little pepper, and two rolls of bread sliced, stew until the onions will go through a sieve; then take six other onions, two car- rots, two heads of celery, and one turnip, slice them very thin, flour and fry them in half a pound of butter. When getting tender, add some of the soup, and stew for some time longer; then strain the liquor, put it to the remainder of the soup, and boil it together for a short time: if not thick enough add some cream or other thickening.

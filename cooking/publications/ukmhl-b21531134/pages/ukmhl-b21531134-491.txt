Put a pint and a half of port or white wine into a howl, nutmeg grated, and a good deal of sugar, then milk into it near two quarts of milk, frothed up. If the wine be not rather sharp, it will requii-e more for this quantity of milk. In Devonshire, clouted cream is put on the top, and pounded cinnamon and sugar. STAFFORDSHIRE SILLABUB. Put a pint of cider, and a glass of brandy, sugar, and nutmeg, into a bowl, and milk into it; or pour warm milk from a large tea-pot some height from it. A VERY FINE SOMERSETSHIRE AND DEVONSHIRE SILLABUB. ’ In a large china bowl put a pint of port, and a pint of sherry, or other white wine ; sugar to taste. Milk the bowl full. In twenty minutes’ time cover it pretty high with clouted cream; grate over it nutmeg, put pounded cinnamon, and nonpareil comfits. DEVONSHIRE JUNKET. Put warm milk into a bowl; turn it with rennet; then put some scalded cream, sugar, and cinnamon on the top, without breaking the curd. EVERLASTING SILLABUBS.

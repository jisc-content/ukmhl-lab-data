The liquor in which a leg of mutton or half a calf s- head has been boiled will make an excellent stock for this soup. Take a peck of peas, and, in shelling them, separate the old from the young; boil the former por- tion until they are quite tender in the soup, then rub them through a sieve, return them to the stock, and let them stew gently together. Put in the young peas just time enough before the soup is served to allow them to be properly cooked.

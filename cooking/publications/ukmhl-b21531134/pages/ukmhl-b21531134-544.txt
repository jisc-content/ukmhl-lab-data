464 [Chaf. XXII. DOMESTIC COOKERY. SYRUP FOR I-IQUEURS.—E. R. Put a quart of water into a saucepan, and let it boil, then drop into it, lump by lump, one pound of loaf- sugar. When all the sugar is dissolved, let it boil again and put it into a broad dish to cool; when cool it is fit for use. CREME DE PORTUGAL.—E. R. Take eight good lemons, rough skinned, but without spots; pare the rind very thinly, and cut it into small slips, put it into a bottle with a pint of spirits of wine, and a dozen bitter almonds blanched and bruised. Let it stand six days, make a syrup with a pound of treble- refined sugar, add it when cool: shake the whole well together. Let it stand six days, and then filter it through hlotting-paper. This liqueur should he kept six months. CREME DE CACAO.—E. R. Take a pound of good Caracas cocoa, roast it as if to be used for chocolate, being careful to take out the bad grains, of which there are sometimes several, and they would spoil the liqueur : pound it well and infuse it in six pints of brandy, add half an ounce of cut vanilla; let it stand for eight days, then strain it. Melt three pounds and a half of sugar in three pints of water, mix it well together, and filter it. RAT.YFIE DES QUARTRE FRUITS.—E. R. Take thirty pounds of cherries, fifteen of gooseberries, eight of raspberries, and six of black currants, stone the cherries, and press the juice out of all the fruit to- gether : to each pint of juice put six ounces of pounded sugar.

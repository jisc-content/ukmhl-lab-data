[Chap. -X.VI. 328 DOMESTIC COOKERY. According to what it is wanted for should he the selection from the following list, observing, that of the most pungent articles least must be used. No one flavour should predominate greatly; yet if several dishes be served the same day, there should be a marked variety in the tastes of the forcemeats, as well as of the gravies. A general fault is, that the tastes of lemon- peel and thyme overcome all others; therefore they should only be used in small quantites. They should be consistent enough to cut with a knife, but not dry and heavy. Herbs are a very essential ingredient; and it is the copious but judicious use of them that chiefly gives the cookery of the French its superior flavour. FORCEMEAT INGREDIENTS.

A pound of fine fresh suet, one ounce of ready- dressed veal or chicken, chopped fine, crumbs of bread, a little shiilot or onion, salt, white pepper, nutmeg, mace, pennyroyal, parsley, and lemon-thyme, finely shred; beat as many fresh eggs, yolks and whites sepa- rately, as will make the above ingredients into a moist paste : roll into small balls, and boil them in fresh lard, putting them in just as it boils up. When of a light brown, take them out, and drain them before the fire. If the suet be moist or stale, a great many more eggs will be necessary. Put the article in cold water over the fire, and when it boils up take it out and plunge it into cold water, and let it remain until cold. This gives plumpness and whiteness. Tongues, palates, &c., are said to be blanched when, after long boiling, the skin can be peeled off; and the latter will become thicker by being put into cold water as above.

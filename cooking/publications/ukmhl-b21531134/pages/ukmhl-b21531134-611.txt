Cover the steel with sweet pil well rubbed on it, and in forty-eight hours use unslaked lime finely powdered, to rub until all the rust disappears. TO CLIvVN THE BACK OF THE GRATE, THE INNER HEARTH, AND THE FRONTS OF CAST-IRON STOVES. Boil about a quarter of a pound of the best black- lead with a pint of small beer and a bit of soap the size of a walnut. When that is melted, dip a painter’s brush, and wet the grate, having first brushed off all the soot and dust; then take a hard brush and rub it till of a beautiful brightness.

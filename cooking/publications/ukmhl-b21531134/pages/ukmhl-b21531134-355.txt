CHAPTER XIV. EGGS, CHEESE, S.4.L.\DS, &c. EGGS A LA TRIPE. Skin six or eight onions, mince them, put some butter into a stewpan and melt it, add about two spoonsful of flour, mix both well together, then add the onions, and wet them with milk, cream, or water; let them stew, taking care that the onions and butter do not be- come brown; season with a littk salt and white pepper; cut some hard eggs into slices, and when the sauce is ready put them in ; make the whole very hot, and serve it up. EGGS ON THE DISH.—E. R. Butter a dish well, sprinkle it with salt, then break the eggs very carefully so as not to disturb the yolk; add a little more salt and some white pepper; melt a small quantity of butter, pour it gently over, with one or two spoonsful of cream. Put the dish over a slow fire, and finish the eggs by covering them with a red- hot shovel. BURNT BUTTERED EGGS.—E. R.

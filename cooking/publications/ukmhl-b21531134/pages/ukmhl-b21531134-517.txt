Butter the pan, and put it into a quick oven. Three hours will bake it. VERY GOOD COMMON PLUM-CAKES. Mix five ounces of butter in three pounds of dry flour and five ounces of fine Lisbon sugar; add six ounces of currants, washed and dried, and some pimento, finely powdered. Put three spoonsful of yest into a Win- chester pint of new milk warmed, and mix into a light dough with the above. Make it into twelve cakes, and bake on a floured tin half an hour.

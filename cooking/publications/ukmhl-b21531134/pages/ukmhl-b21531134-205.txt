The conjurer will be found very useful to sportsmen of all kinds, whether going out with rod or gun; it will cook fish or game in great perfection, and may be heated with any fuel at hand. Anglers in want of material for the conjurer are recommended to smear their bait with asafcetida, which will exercise a fascinating influence over the fish, and induce them to seek it from a distance. N.B. A boiled shrimp is a good bait for perch. BEEF-STE-AK WITH VEGETABLES.—E. R. Cut the steak about three inches thick; dredge it with flour, and fry it in butter, of a fine brown. Lay it in a stewpan, and pour water into the frying-pan; let it boil, and add it to the steak, which is rendered richer by this process; slice in turnips, carrots, celery, and onions, adding pepper, salt, and a little mace. It should be highly seasoned, and sent to table with the surface orna- mented with carrots and turnips cut into shapes, and the vegetables round it. BEEF RAGOUT.—E. R. Fry two pounds of the leg-of-mutton piece until quite brown. Put it into a stewpm with six large onions, and pepper it well from a spicebox of mixed spice; boil water in a frying-pan, add it to the meat, and let it stew for four hours; serve it up with pickled walnuts, gherkins, and capers, just warmed in the gravy.

Peel and core six small or three large apples, put them in a stewpan with a quarter of a pint of either No. 1 or 2 Stocks, an ounce of sugar, half an ounce of butter, and a small piece of lemon-peel; let the whole simmer until the apples are stewed, then beat through a hair sieve and serve. BECHAMEL. No. 12. Put two ounces of butter into a stewpan, let it melt, add one and a half ounces of flour, and stir it in until smooth, add a sprig or two of parsley, six peppercorns, six mush- rooms, salt to taste, half a pint of White Stock (No. 3), and half a pint of milk, let it boil, stirring until it does, and then put it on one side to simmer for twenty minutes; strain and keep ready for use.

252 THE PYTCHLEY COOKERY BOOK. MARROW A LA MAD ERE. No. 534. Break and extract the marrow from two good bones ; divide lengthways, and cut into pieces an inch long, pare round the outside and make an oval shape, and put the pieces in a basin of tepid water for two or three hours to extract the blood ; a quarter of an hour before they are required for use put them in a stewpan of cold water over the fire, and when it just arrives at the boil the marrow should be soft, when strain and serve, either in croustades made of Pastry (No. 366), with a spoonful of Sauce Madere (No. 42) over each, or in a silver basin with croutons of toast and Sauce Madere handed round. NOUICLE PUDDING AU PARMESAN. No. 535. Warm two ounces of butter in a stewpan, mix in one and a half ounces of flour, when smooth add half a pint of new milk, stir over the fire until it becomes thickish, and it leaves the pan; remove from the fire and mix in two ounces of grated Parmesan cheese, then add two eggs, working them in one at a time and some Searcy Salt and salt; put a stewpan on the fire with a quart of milk, boil it up, put the above mixture in it in lumps the size of a cob- nut or in oval pieces the size of an almond, let them poach half an hour in it, strain off the milk, put the noukles in the bottom of a tart-dish and cover them with a pint and a half of Parmesan Sauce (No. 60), using the milk in which they were poached ; the sauce must not be as thick as that used for masking fish. Put in the oven and bake gradually for about half an hour. OMELET AUX FINE HERBS. No. 536.

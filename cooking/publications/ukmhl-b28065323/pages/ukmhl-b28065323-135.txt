PLUM PUDDING SAUCE. No. 65. Two ounces of butter, two ounces of white pounded sugar, two table-spoonfuls of brandy and one of sherry. Beat the butter with a fork until quite soft, in a basin placed in cold water or ice if to be had. Incorporate the sugar by degrees, and then the brandy and sherry mixed. This sauce should be thick and smooth like good iced cream. PIGEON SAUCE. No. 66. Boil the livers of as many pigeons as are to be dressed, chop them very fine, put them in a small saucepan with a tea-spoonful of chopped parsley, two or three mushrooms chopped in a gill of Brown Sauce (No. 22), half an ounce of butter, a salt-spoonful of pepper and salt mixed, stir it over the fire until it boils, and use it with roast pigeon, or if rabbit, using rabbit livers instead of pigeon livers.

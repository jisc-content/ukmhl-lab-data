PREFACE. The art of cooking should be taught in every public school. Every boy and girl should not only know how to cook, but should be able to quickly detect and understand the causes of bad cooking. Though there may come a day when foods can be tastily and wholesomely prepared without cooking, such a vast change is unques- tionably far removed from the present age. Therefore, knowledge of cooking is of almost as much importance as that of reading or writ- ing. Thousands become regular patrons of the doctors simply because of inferior cooking.

CHAPTER VI. VEGETABLES. Vegetables are very wholesome and nutri- tious and have many medicinal qualities. As a race, Americans eat far too much meat and too little vegetable food. The legumes (dried peas, beans, etc.) contain almost’ double the amount of muscle-making food to be found in beef, at a far less expense. Of course, vege- tables must be properly cooked and properly combined with other foods to be at their best. Dried or old vegetables require slow, long cooking; most of the young or green ones should be boiled only until tender. They should be put into boiling water, salted, and boiled, not too fast'. Use only enough water to cook them, as many of their useful salts are wasted when there is much water left to “drain off” when the vegetables are served. Soft

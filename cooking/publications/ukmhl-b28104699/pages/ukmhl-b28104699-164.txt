Poor Man’s Pudding.—One cup of sweet or sour milk, one cup of molasses, one-half cup of butter, one pound of raisins, two eggs, one teaspoonful of soda, a little cinnamon, whole- wheat flour sufficient to make as thick as cake. Boil four hours without stopping in a floured bag or mold, allowing room to swell. To be eaten with sauce. 32. Grandma’s Plum Pudding.—Mix one cup molasses and one cup chopped suet and one cup hot water, three cups flour, one tablespoon mixed spices, three teaspoonfuls baking powder, three cups flour, two cups chopped raisins, two cups currants. Sprinkle the fruit with flour to prevent it sinking to the bottom of the pudding. Put in bag or buttered mold and boil three hours. Enough for eight per- sons. 33.

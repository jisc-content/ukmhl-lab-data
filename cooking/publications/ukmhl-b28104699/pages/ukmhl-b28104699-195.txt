Three-quarters pound granulated sugar, one- half pound flour, one gill water, juice of one lemon. Boil sugar and water together until clear, pour into beaten eggs, beating well until cool, then add flour and lemon juice. Bake in square sheets, and cover with boiled icing. 13. White Cake.—C ream together one pound of white sugar and one pound of butter. Add by degrees one pound of warmed and sifted flour, and the beaten whites of sixteen eggs. Blanch one pound of almonds and beat them to a paste with a little rosewater, grate one cocoanut and cut into strips one pound of candied citron. Mix them well together and stir into the batter. Bake in a moderate oven till done. When cold cover sides and top thickly with cocoanut icing flavored with lemon juice, and sprinkle cocoanut over all.

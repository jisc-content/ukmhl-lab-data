CHAPTER V. BREAD, ROLLS, BISCUITS, ETC. Bread made of the whole grain flours forms a most important article of diet, since the grains contain more nourishment than any other foods. It would seem almost unneces- sary, in this age of hygienic reform, to speak at any length of the unwholesomeness of the ordinary bolted “white” flour as compared with the entire wheat or other coarser flours. Yet we see “white bread” served at so many tables, and so little use made of the whole wheat flour, that one must needs conclude that many people are unacquainted with the hy- gienic value of the latter.

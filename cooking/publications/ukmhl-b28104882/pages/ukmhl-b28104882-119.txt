XIII HOW TO SERVE A DINNER WITHOUT A MAID A DINNER of several courses, a party of guests, and no maid ! Surely no one would dream of attempting such a thing ? But if the party is arranged, the dinner thought out, and only the maid is lacking, why be defeated on that score ? The guest is surely not worth entertaining who would think less well of a hostess who waited upon him or her with her own hands, especially if knowing for a certainty that she has been her own cook in the first instance. When all is said and done, it is a question of management, of being superior to circumstances, of being equal to the occasion—in fact, of general- ship.

302 THE COMPLETE COOK Vanilla Sauce To half a pint of sweet melted butter sauce add essence of vanilla to taste. White Sauce {Plain White Foundation Sauce') Ingi'edients— One pint of milk, or half milk and half white, or fish stock. Two rounded tablespoonfuls of butter. One rounded tablespoonful of flour. One bay leaf: half a small onion. Half a teaspoonful of lemon juice. Salt: pepper. Method.—Melt the butter, mix in the flour and cook both for three or four minutes over a slow fire without browning them. Add the milk and stir until it boils. Put in the onion and bay leaf and simmer the sauce for ten minutes; season and strain it and it is ready for use. Note.—Use white stock, or fish stock, according to whether it is to be used for fish, meat, or poultry. Wine Sauce Ingredients— Two tablespoonfuls of any jam. One teaspoonful of lemon juice. One teacupful of boiling water. Castor sugar to taste. Four tablespoonfuls of sherry. Method.—Put the water, jam, and lemon juice in a pan. Boil it until it becomes like syrup. Add the wine and sugar also, if needed. Then reboil it and strain. Note.—Use a coloured jam which will contrast prettily with the pudding with which it is to be served.

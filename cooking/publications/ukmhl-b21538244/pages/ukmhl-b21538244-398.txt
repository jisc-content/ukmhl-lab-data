344 CHAPTER XVI SOUFFLES Cold Apricot Souffle Ingredmtts— Half a pint of thick apricot pur^e. Three level tablespoonfuls of castor sugar. Four eggs. One gill of cream. Four sheets of French gelatine. One teaspoonful of lemon juice. Six pistachio nuts. Cochineal. About two tablespoonfuls of boiling water. Method.—Rub about half a tin of apricots through a hair sieve to make the pur^e. Separate the yolks and the whites of the eggs. Put the yolks in a basin over a pan of boiling water with the sugar and pur^e, and beat them together until warm and thick. Dissolve the gelatine in the water, stir it into the fruit pur^e, etc. Whip the cream until it will just hang on to the whisk, and the whites to a stiff froth, add these very lightly to the fruit, etc. Strain in the lemon juice and, if necessary, add a few drops of cochineal. Have ready a china souffle case with a band of foolscap to come three or more inches above the mould, tied or pinned round outside it. Pour in the mixture, leave it until set. Then carefully draw off the paper band and serve the souffle in the case. Decorate the top of the souffle with two lines of finely chopped pistachio nuts. Note.—The purde may be made of apple, gooseberries, strawberries, etc., as wished. Beignets Ingredients— Half a pint of boiling water. Two w'hole eggs and one extra yolk.

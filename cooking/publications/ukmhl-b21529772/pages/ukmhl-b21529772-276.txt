Cut out the fillets of three fine soles; lay them on the table, with the outsides downwards; pass the knife between the flesh and the skin; trim them into pieces about five inches long; put them into a saute pan masked with butter; season with pepper, salt, and chopped pars- ley; cover them with a sheet of paper, and sautez them, or do them in the oven; turn them, drain the butter from them, and pour in a rather thick maitre dâ€™hotel (No. 54); let them simmer till done. If approved of, add a small particle of garlick. Dish them en miroton, and mask with the sauce. No. 644. Fillets of Soles the Dutch way. Cut out the fillets of three or four soles; take off all the skins, and trim the fillets; bring both ends together, laying one over the other, to form a loop of the fillet; put them into a saute pan, masked with clarified butter; season with pepper and salt; cover them with a sheet of buttered paper, and put them in the oven.

Vegetable Marrow Soup. Put two small vegetable marrows, quartered and cored, into a stewpan with an ounce of butter, an onion, a little pepper and salt, and half a pint of stock. Boil gently until done, then rub them through a sieve, and mix the puree with half a pint of new milk or cream, a quart of good white stock, and two lumps of sugar. Let all get hot together, without boiling. One or two yolks of egg give richness to the soup ; they should be beaten for a minute and stirred in with a little milk or cream, just before the soup is finished, but it must never boil after the addition of the eggs.

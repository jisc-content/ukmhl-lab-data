PUDDINGS, CREAMS, ETC «3 Apple Charlotte. Take the crust off a small tin loaf of baker’s bread, cut the crumb into slices, the third of an inch thick, and shape them so that they will fit compactly into a tart-dish; put them into the wire basket, and fry as directed for fried bread, taking care that they are very lightly done ; now place the bread in the tart dish, well buttered, so as to cover it perfectly, nearly fill the dish with well made dry apple sauce, flavoured with lemon, put on a cover of fried bread, bake in a moderate oven for an hour, turn out the pudding, and serve with sugar sifted over. Friars Omelet. Make six moderate sized apples into sauce, sweeten with powdered loaf-sugar, stir in two ounces of butter, and when cold, mix with two well beaten eggs. Butter a tart-dish, and strew the bottom and sides thickly with bread-crumbs, then put in the apple sauce, and cover with bread-crumbs, to the depth of a quarter of an inch, put a little dissolved butter on the top, and bake for an hour in a good oven.

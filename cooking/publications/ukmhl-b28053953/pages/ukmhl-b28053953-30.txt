Boiling meat is certainly more economical than roast- ing it, hut then there are only legs of mutton, pork and lamb, and salted beef, which can properly be so treated. When the broth in which the three first mentioned are cooked is used, and providing the meat has not to be eaten cold, there is much to he said in favour of boiling, hut in small families, or families with moderate means, these joints must now he regarded rather as a treat for high days than as daily fare. It must he understood that meat which has heen cooked at boiling point, instead of just helow it, has lost much of its savour, and is generally hard and indigestible. Boiled beef is so much relished by many persons that we hesitate to condemn it. Still the truth must be told; it is neither so wholesome, nourishing, nor economical, as fresh meat.

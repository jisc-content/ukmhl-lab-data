Make into a paste with the white of one or two eggs. Make into balls the size of marbles, roll in flour, and fry or brown before the fire. Mrs. Barnard. Egg Fondue. Make this by putting 6 eggs into a bowl and beat- ing them lightly; add 4 tablespoonfuls of finely chopped or grated cheese, a good pincn of salt, and a dash of cayenne pepper. Put a tablespoonful of butter into a saucepan, turn in the mixture and stir constantly until the eggs are smooth. Serve at once on squares of buttered toast, on a hot dish gar- nished with parsley. This makes a good luncheon dish or a good savoury for dinner. Eggs a la Nevern. 4 Eggs. 20zs. cooked Ham. 4 rounds of Toast. 1 teaspoonful of chopped Parsley. A little Butter. Cut the ham very fine, and melt the butter in a pan, and make the ham hot in it. Poach the eggs, put them on the toast, arranging the ham round the border. Sprinkle each egg with the parsley, and serve very hot. Eggs a la Regence. Half a tomato (large onej to each person, and also a small round of hot buttered toast.

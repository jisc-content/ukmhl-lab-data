Fair fa' your honest sonsie face, Great chieftain o' the puddin' race j Aboon them a' ye tak your place, Painch, tripe, or thairra, Weel are ye wordy of a grace As langs my arm. His knife see rustic labor dight, An' cut you up with ready slight, Trenching your gushing entrail bright Like onie ditch, And then, 0 ! what a glorious sight. Warm reekin' rich. Ye powers wha mak mankind your care. And dish them out their bill of fare, Auld Scotland wants nae skinking ware That jaups in higgles. But if ye wish her grateful pray'r, Gie her a Haggis, Burns.

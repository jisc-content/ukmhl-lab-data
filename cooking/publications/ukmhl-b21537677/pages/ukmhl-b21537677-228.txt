Lay the eggs, shells and all, in a pie-dish, alongside of each other; cover them with the juice of the lemons, leave them in this for four or five days, turning them over every day. At the end of the five days the shells will be quite dissolved. Beat all up well with the sugar candy, add the rum, and bottle for use. A teaspoonful should be given every morning, fasting. Milk and Suet—Very Nourishing. Half an oz. suet, 1 pint of new milk. Chop the suet finely, tie it loosely in a piece of muslin, and put it in the milk. Put this in a saucepan, and bring it slowly to the boil; let it simmer for ten minutes; strain it, and serve tepid, a wineglassful at a time. Poultice with Charcoal.

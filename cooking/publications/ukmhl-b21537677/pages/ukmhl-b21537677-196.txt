When cold cut into fingers. Brush them over with syrup and sprinkle with cocoanut, or ice them. Orange Curd. Two oz. butter, 2 oz. castor sugar, 2 yolks of eggs, juice of 1 orange, 2 oz. candied orange peel finely minced. Put the butter and sugar in a saucepan and stir them over the fire till thoroughly melted. Take off the fire and add the orange juice, candied peel, and the eggs, return to the fire and stir till thick. Do not allow it to boil. Croutes d’Orange. Make some economical Genoese pastry (see recipe), cut in rounds the size of a wine glass top, put some orange curd between each two, and ooat with transparent icing (see recipe). , Cornflour Cake. Half lb. Brown and Poison’s cornflour, 2 oz. flour, 6 oz. butter, 6 oz. sugar, rind of a lemon grated, 4 eggs, 2 table- spoonfuls milk or cream, 1 teaspoonful baking powder. Work the butter and sugar to a cream. Whip in the lemon rind, the eggs, and cornflour and flour mixed. Add the baking powder and cream, pour into a cake tin buttered and papered. Bake in a moderate oven 1 hour. If self-raising flour is used no baking powder is necessary. Economical Rice Cake.

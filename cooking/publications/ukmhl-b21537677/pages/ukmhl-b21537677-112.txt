Two oz. butter, 2 oz. flour, 1^ pint mills, or white stock. Melt the butter in a saucepan, add the flour, and stir till smooth like honey; mix in the milk or stock gradually, stir- ring all the time, till it comes to the boil, and allow it to boil for five minutes; add white pepper and salt and a squeeze of lemon juice. If the sauce looks too thick dilute with a little milk. Rich White Sauce. Make in the same way as above, using half milk and half cream. Lobster Sauce. Pound the coral of a lobster with half an ounce of butter, cut the flesh up into small pieces, and add to i pint of white sauce. Economical Dutch Sauce. One pint white sauce, the yolks of 2 eggsi, 1 table- spoonful of tarragon vinegar; stir the whole over the fire till thick and creamy; season to taste with salt and pepper. Tarragon Sauce.

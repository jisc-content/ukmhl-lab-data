Pile on a dish and serve plain otr with whipped cream. Chestnut Iced Pudding. Three dozen chestnuts, the yolks of 10 eggs, 1 lb. of sugar, 1 pint of rich cream, 1 oz. of candied peel, 2 oz. of currants, 2 oz. of sultanas, 1 glass of liqueur, vanilla flavouring. Blanch the chestnuts and pound them in a mortar with a little syrup. Pass them through a sieve, and mix them in a basin with 1 pint of rich syrup, 1 pint of cream, and the yolks of 10 eggs. Put mixture in a saucepan, and stir it over a slow fire till it just begins to boil. Take it off and pass it through a sieve. When it is cold put it in the freezer, and when nearly frozen add the sliced peel, the currants and sultanas; then add 4 a pint of whipped cream mixed with the whites of two eggs beaten to a stiff froth with a little syrup. When the pudding is perfectly frozen, put it in a mould, close the lid, and bury in ice till required. Turn out and serve. Vanilla Ice Cream—Ordinary.

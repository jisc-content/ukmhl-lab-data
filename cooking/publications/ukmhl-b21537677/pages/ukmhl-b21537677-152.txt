This must be repeated until the paste has had seven rolls, putting away to cool between each two rollings. For a vol-au-vent six rolls are sufficient. The paste can be made some hours before it is required. Common Short Paste. One lb. flour, 6 oz. butter or dripping or Hugon’s beef suet, pinch of salt, 1 teaspoonful baking powder, cold water to mix. Rub the dripping into the flour, add the salt and baking powder. Make into a stiff paste with as little water as jjossible and roll out to the required thickness. Great care should be taken not to make the paste too wet, as the stiffer it is, the lighter and shorter it will bake. Short Paste (good).—No. 1. Three-quarter lb. of flour, 6 oz. butter, 1 oz. castor sugar. 2 whole eggs. Rub the butter well into the flour without making it greasy, add the sugar, beat up the eggs, and with them mix to a smooth paste (using no water), roll out | inch thick and use. Short Paste.—No. 2. Three-quarter lb. flour, 6 oz. butter, 1 oz. castor sugar, 1 teaspoonful lemon juice, a little cold water. Rub the butter into the flour well, add the sugar and the lemon juice, mix into a stiff paste with as little water as possible, roll out not quite an inch thick and use. Rough Puff Pastry.

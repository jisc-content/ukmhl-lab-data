Half lb. butter, £ lb. castor sugar, | lb. fine flour, 1 egg. Cream the butter with the sugar, stir in the flour, then the egg beaten; mix it into a smooth paste, and turn it out on the board; take a small piece at a time, and roll it out very thin and cut it into fingers. Cook in hot fat; and serve with castor sugar sprinkled over. These are specially to eat with Manzanilla. Richmond Cakes. * Line some small moulds with puff paste, and half fill with the following mixture: 2 oz. of flour, 2 oz. castor sugar, 1 oz. of crushed ratafias, 1 gill of cream, 1 whole egg and 2 yolks, small pinch of salt, and 3 oz. of butter. Cream the butter, mix in the ingredients, beat all together thoroughly, and fill the moulds. Bake in a moderate oven, turn out, and serve hot with castor sugar sprinkled over. Imperial Cake. One lb. flour, 1 lb. butter, 8 oz. castor sugar, 1 teaspoonful of ground cinnamon, 1 teaspoonful of grated lemon peel, J teaspoonful of salt, 4 whole eggs, and 4 yolks, 1 gill of good cream, 3 oz. of sweet almonds, 1 oz. D.C.L. yeast. Put the butter in a basin, and cream it with a wooden spoon; add to it the sugar, the seasoning, the flour and 2 eggs; beat this thoroughly, then add the remainder of the flour, the other 2 eggs and 4 yolks; beat well, cream the yeast with a. tear spoonful of sugar, and mix with it the cream made tepid. Spread out the paste, and put the yeast in the middle. Mix the whole very thoroughly, and put it in a well-buttered mould; set it in a warm place to rise for one hour; bake in a moderate oven for two hours. o 2

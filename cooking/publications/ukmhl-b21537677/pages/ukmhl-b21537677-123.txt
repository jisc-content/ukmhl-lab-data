Lay a border of celery tufts round the edge of the bowl, put on ice till required, which should be as soon as possible. Potato Salad Dressing. Three large potatoes boiled, 1 tablespoonful of castor sugar, 1 tablespoonful of salad oil, 1 small spoonful of made mustard, salt, and pepper, 1 spoonful good brown sauce, 1 egg beaten, 3 spoonfuls vinegar. Boil the potatoes, let them dry, and beat them up lightly with a fork, add the salt, oil, and egg. Mix in another basin the mustard, sauce, sugar, pepper, and vinegar; add this gradually to the potatoes; stir till quite smooth. This is very good with salmon. Scotch Eggs. Four hard-boiled eggs, \ lb. of sausage meat. Boil the eggs seven minutes. Place them in cold water till cool enough to shell, then cover them with the sausage meat. Roll smooth, dip in egg and bread crumbs, and fry in hot fat till a golden brown. Cut in halves, stand each half on a fried round of bread, and garnish with watercress or any green salad. Beetroot a la Creme. Half pint of white sauce, 2 tablespoonfuls of cream, salt, pepper, cayenne, and lemon juice, a small beetroot boiled. Mix into the white sauce the cream and seasoning, colour with beetroot juice, and lay the beetroot cut in small slices in the sauce. Serve hot, with fried croutons round. Croustades a la Windsor.

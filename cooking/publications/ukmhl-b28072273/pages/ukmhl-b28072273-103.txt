Mousse au Cafe PralinSe is made in the same way, using four tablespoonfuls of strong black coffee, in- stead of the melted chocolate, and finish as before. Mousse au Cassis.—Stew one pound of ripe black currants with half a pint of water, 6oz. of caster sugar, and a few drops of carmine, tiU soft enough to pulp through a sieve; put this puree into a pan with the yolks of twelve and the whites of three eggs, 3oz. of caster sugar, and a liqueur-glassful of brandy, and whip over boiling water, as before, till stiff and warm, and then off the fire till cold, when you stir to it lightly a good tablespoonful of maraschino liqueur (or rather more liqueur syrup) and a pint of stiffly whipped cream; mould, and freeze in the cave for three to four hours.

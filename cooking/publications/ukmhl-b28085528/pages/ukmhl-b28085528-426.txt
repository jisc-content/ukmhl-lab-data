VEGETABLE SOUP. Put into the liquor in which a knuckle of veal, or any other fresh meat, has been boiled, one carrot, one turnip, two onions, one lettuce, all cut into small pieces; add a dessertspoonful of salt, the same of sugar, a saltspoonful of pepper, and a quarter of a grain of cayenne. Boil for one hour; then put in a pint, or more, of peas, and continue to boil till they are tender, which will be, according to the age, from twenty minutes to three- quarters of an hour. Note.—A nice soup may always be made from any fresh bones, using vegetables as directed above in the summer, and in the winter split-peas, as directed in the first receipt for soup in Plain Cookery. 793.

141 PIES Are those dishes which consist either of meat, or of fruit, covered with a farina- ceous crust, enriched with butter or other fat, and rendered fit for eating by baking. The crust of the pie is usually made of two parts by weight of wheaten flour, and one part of butter, lard, or other fat. The flour is made into a stiff paste with cold water, and rolled out on a board with a paste pin to the thickness of about one quarter of an inch, the board being pre- viously sprinkled over with flour to pre- vent the dough from sticking to the board.

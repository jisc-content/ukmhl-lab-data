Ill BOILING. Boiling is a much more common opera- tion than any of those we have considered, with the exception perhaps of roasting. It consists, as every body knows, in sub- jecting the materials of food to the influence of heat, through the medium of boiling water, or of steam. The water employed for boiling meat or pulse should be soft, and the joint should be put on the fire immersed in cold water, in order that the heat may gradually cause the whole mass to become boiled equally. If the piece of meat is of an unequal thickness, the thinner parts will be over- done before the more massy portion is sufficiently acted on by the boiling water.

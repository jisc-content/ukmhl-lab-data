Soups are decoctions of meat which dif- fer from broth, in being more concentrated, and usually also more complex in their com- position. They are in fact strong broths, containing either farinaceous roots and seeds, or other parts of vegetable substances. The erudite editor of the “ Almanack des Gourmands ” * tells us, that ten folio volumes would not contain the receipts of all the soups that have been invented in that grand school of good eating, the Pa- risian kitchen.

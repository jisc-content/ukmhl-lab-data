82 ROASTING ON A SPIT* changes also which roasting induces, are sufficiently slight, as a careful analysis will procure from meat, properly roasted, nearly all the elements which are to be found in it in the raw state. Slight as the change is however in a chemi- cal, it is considerable in a culinary, point of view. The texture of the meat is more relaxed and consequently it is more tender ; it is also more sapid and high flavoured. It is absolutely essential that the meat intended for roasting, has been kept long enough for the fibres to become flaccid, without which precaution the best meat does not become tender.

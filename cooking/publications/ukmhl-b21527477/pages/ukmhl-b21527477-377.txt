The glazing of the common cream-co- loured earthen ware, which is composed of an oxyd of lead, readily yields to the action of vinegar and saline compounds; and there- fore the jars and pots of this kind of stone- ware, should not be used for marmalades and other conserves. Pickles should in no case be deposited in cream-coloured glazed earthenware pots.

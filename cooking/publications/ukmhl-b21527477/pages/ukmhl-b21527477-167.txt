Vegetable substances are most commonly boiled or baked ; or, if occasionly fried or roasted, there is always much water pre- sent, which prevents the greater action of the fire from penetrating below the surface. The universal effect of cookery by boiling upon vegetable substances, is to dissolve in the water some of their constituents, such as the mucilage and starch, and to render those that are not properly soluble, as the gluten and fibre, softer and more pulpy.

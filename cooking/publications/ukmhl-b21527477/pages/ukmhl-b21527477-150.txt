No scorching or browning of the meat takes place if the process is properly con- ducted; for the temperature to which it is exposed does not exceed the boiling point of water. In the stewing of vegetables, saccha- rine matter is formed, the starch and mucilage are rendered soluble, and of course, set free the woody fibre, which either floats through the liquid or adheres together very slightly. It accordingly con- stitutes either a pasty fluid, or converts the vegetables to a soft pulp; sometimes their original shape being preserved entire, and at other times not.

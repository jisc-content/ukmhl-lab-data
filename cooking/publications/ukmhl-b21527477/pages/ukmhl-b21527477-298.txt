256 CONSERVATION OF RECENT FRUITS, BY MEANS OF SUGAR, IN A SOLID FORM. The name of candied fruits, or comfits, is given to such substances as are preserved by means of sugar in a solid state, so that the whole substance is impregnated and covered with sugar, in a crystalline, or solid state. . t CANDIED ORANGE, OR LEMON PEEL. Soak Seville orange peel, well cleaned from the pulp in several waters, till it loses its bitterness; cut it into thin slips, simmer them in a syrup composed of two parts, by weight, of lump sugar, and one of water, and continue the simmering till they are become tender, and nearly transparent.

20 COOKERY. IMPORTANCE OF THE ART OF COOKERY. As man differs from the inferior animals in the variety of articles he feeds upon, so he differs from them no less in the prepara- tion of these substances. Some animals, be- sides man, prepare their food in a particu- lar manner. The racoon (ursns lutor) is said to wash his roots before he eats them ; and the beaver stores his green boughs under water that their bark and young twigs may remain juicy and palatable.

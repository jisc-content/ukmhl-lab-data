252 CONSERVATION OF RECENT FRUITS, BY MEANS OF SUGAR, IN A LIQUID STATE. A great number of fruits in their natural state may be conserved in a fluid, transparent syrup, of such a consistence as will prevent them from spoiling. This method of con- serving fruits requires some care; for if they are too little impregnated with sugar, they do not keep, and if the syrup is too concentrated, the sugar crystallizes, and thus spoils the conserved fruit.

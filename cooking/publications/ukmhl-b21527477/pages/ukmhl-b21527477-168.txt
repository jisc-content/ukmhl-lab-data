128 COMPARATIVE DIMINUTION OF THE WEIGHT OF MEAT IN COOKING. It is evident, that whether the heat be applied directly or indirectly for cooking animal food, there must be a considerable diminution of weight. In the cooking of animal substances in public institutions, where the allowance of meat is generally weighed out in its raw state, and includes bones, and is served out cooked, and some- times without bone, it is a matter of im- portance to ascertain nearly their relative proportions.

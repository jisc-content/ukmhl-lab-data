182 PRESERVATION OF ANIMAL SUBSTANCES IN A RECENT STATE. As the supply of food is always subject to irregularities, the preservation of the excess, obtained at one time, to meet the deficiency of another, would soon engage the attention of mankind. At first this method would be simple and natural, and derived from a very limited observation, but in the progress of society, the wants and occupations of mankind would lead them to invent means, by which the more perishable alimentary substances of one sea- son, might be reserved for the consumption of another, or the superfluous productions of distant countries might be transported to others where they are more needed.

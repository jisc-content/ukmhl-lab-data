Of all the methods of preserving animal substances for domestic purposes, or sea store, the process found out by Mr. Appert, and pursued in this metropolis upon a large scale by Messrs. Donkin and Gamble, is unquestionably the best. It is as follows : Let the substance to be preserved be first par-boiled, or rather somewhat more, the bones of the meat being previously re- moved. Put the meat into a tin cylinder, fill up the vessel with the broth, and then solder on the lid, furnished with a small hole.

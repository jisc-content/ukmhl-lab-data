39 ROASTING IN A CLOSED OVEN. Roasting in a closed oven, or baking, con- sists in exposing substances to be roasted to the action of heat in a confined space, or closed oven, which does not permit the free access of air, to cause the vapour arising from the roasted substance to escape as fast as it is formed, and this circumstance materially alters the flavour of roasted animal substances. Roasters and ovens of the common con- struction are apt to give the meat a dis- agreeable flavour, arising from the empy- reumatic oil, which is formed by the decomposition of the fat, exposed to the bottom of the oven.

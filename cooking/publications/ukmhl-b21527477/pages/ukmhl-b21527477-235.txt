193 METHOD OF PREPARING BACON, HAMS, AND HUNG BEEF. Meat, when salted, is sometimes dried, when it gets the name of bacon, ham, or hung beef. The drying of salt meat is effected either by hanging it in a dry and well-aired place, or by exposing it at the same time to wood smoke, which gives it a peculiar flavour, much admired in Westphalia hams and Hamburgh beef, and also tends to preserve it, by the antiseptic action of the pyrolignic acid. When meat is to be hung, it need not be so highly salted. The method of preparing bacon is pe- culiar to certain districts.

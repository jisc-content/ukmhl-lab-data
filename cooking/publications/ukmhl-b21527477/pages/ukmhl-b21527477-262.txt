Cut a pound of the lean of boiled ham into pieces, pound it in a mortar with fresh butter, in the proportion of about two ounces to a pound of the ham, till it is a fine paste, season it by degrees with pounded mace, pepper, and allspice ; put it close down in pots, and cover it with clarified butter a quarter of an inch thick ; let it stand one night in a cool place, and tie it over with paper. Veal may be potted in a similar manner.

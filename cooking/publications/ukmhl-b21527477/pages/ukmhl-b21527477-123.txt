Fish is not usually dressed in this way, though the larger sorts are sometimes roasted. Those who relish eels and pike prefer them roasted to any other mode of dressing them. It is a general practice to move the spit back when the meat is half done, in order to clear the bottom part of the grate, and to give the tire a good stirring, that it may burn bright during the remainder of the process. The meat is deemed suffi- ciently roasted wrhen the steam puffs out of the joint in jets towards the fire.

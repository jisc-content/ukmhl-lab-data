53 COMPARATIVE ALIMENTARY EFFECTS OF ANIMAL AND VEGETABLE FOOD. Animal food alone is ill adapted to form the whole of our aliment. The inquiries of physiologists have determined, that animal food is highly stimulant, and like all other stimulants, after the excitement has been brought to its acme, debility must by ne- cessity succeed. This, however, is not so much the case where fresh meat is used as when the meat is salted ; but this may be, because our examples, with regard to fresh meat, are less marked than in the case of salted provision.

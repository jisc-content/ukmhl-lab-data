The flavouring ingredients should not be added till ten or fifteen minutes before the soup is finished. Clear soups should be perfectly transparent, and thickened soups, should be of the consistence of cream. The soup, says a writer, on Cookery, might be called the portal of the edifice of a French dinner, either plain or sump- tuous. It is a sine qua non article. It leads to the several courses constituting the essence of the repast, and lays the un- sophisticated foundation upon which the whole is to rest, as upon a solid basis, in the stomach.

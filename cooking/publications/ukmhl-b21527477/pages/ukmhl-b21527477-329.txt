In this country vinegar is prepared from a wort obtained by the infusion of malted grain ; the fermentation being excited by yeast. This vinegar is inferior in strength and purity to that from wine, and is more liable to become mouldy, or suffer the pu- trefactive fermentation. And this appears to be owing to the presence of a large por- tion of glutinous matter.

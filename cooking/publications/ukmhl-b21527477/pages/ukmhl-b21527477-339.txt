Tea is propagated by seeds, which are put into holes about five inches deep, at re- gular distances from each other; from six to twelve being sown together, as it is sup- posed that only a small number grow. When the tree is three years old, the leaves are fit to be gathered; and the men who collect them wear gloves that the flavour may not be injured. They do not pull them by handfuls, but pick them off one by one, taking great care not to break the leaves, and although this appears to be a very tedious process, each person gathers from ten to fifteen pounds a day.

The inexcusable negligence of persons who make use of copper vessels has been productive of mortality, so much more ter- rible, as they have exerted their action on a great number of persons at once. Though, after all, a single dose be not mortal, yet a quantity of poison, however small, when taken at every meal, must pro- duce more fatal effects than are generally apprehended; and different constitutions are differently affected by minute quantities of substances that act powerfully on the system.

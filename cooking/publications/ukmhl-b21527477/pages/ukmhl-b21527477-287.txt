Take out the jar, and pour off the juice clear from the sediment through a hair sieve into a stewpan (without squeezing the mushrooms) ; let it boil up, skim it, and pour it into a dry jar ; let it stand till next day, then pour it off as gently as possible, through a tammis, or flannel bag, (so as not to disturb the sedi- ment at the bottom of the jar.) Bottle it in pints or half pints ; for it is best to keep it in such quantities as are soon used : in each pint, put a dozen berries of black pepper, the same of allspice, and a table-spoonful of brandy.

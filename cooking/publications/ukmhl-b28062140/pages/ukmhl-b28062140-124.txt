Beauvilliers, who established his restaurant about 1782, was for fifteen years the most famous restau- rateur of Paris, and provided literally such delicate and sublimated dishes as those which had hitherto been found only on the tables of the king, of the nobles, and of the farmers- general. The great restaurateurs of modern Paris are nearly all direct successors of one or other of the famous cooks above men- tioned.

The reasons of this phenomenon are manifold. The hurry and unrest of contemporary life do not con- duce to the appreciation of fine cooking, nor is fine cooking possible where it is necessary to prepare food in very large quantities. But why are the restaurants of the very highest class declining in excellence? Can they not count upon the patronage not only of the elite of the gourmands of Paris, but also— thanks to club-trains and swift communica- tions of all kinds—upon the patronage of the gastronomers of the whole world ? This is true, but with important reserves. The de- cline of the art of cookery in the Parisian restaurants is due chiefly to the development of club-life !

Mcdtre d'Hotel.—Take butter of the size of an egg; chop parsley, chives, and even a sprig of tarragon, very finely; add freshly ground pepper and salt; knead the whole well together, and spread it over the broiled meat or fish the moment before serving on a hot dish. N.B.—Never put your dish into an oven “ to allow the butter to penetrate the meat,” as some recommend. As soon as the meat is off the gridiron it wants to get to table with the least possible delay. In hot weather a few drops of lemon-juice may be added to the maitre d'hotel, and even a tinge of nutmeg.

The preparation of many warm hors d'oeuvres requires the resources of a first-rate kitchen and a professional cook; for, although they are without sauce, nevertheless they require to be artistically presented. The warm hors d'ceuvres are served after the soup or after the fish ; they ought to be pretty to look at, and to furnish two or three delicate mouth- fuls. Several such hors d'oeuvres may be served on the same dish, which makes at once a handsome arrangement to the eye and offers greater choice to the guests, while at the same time it simplifies the service. For household cooking, however, the less said about warm hors d'oeuvres the better, for few private kitchens and few family cooks are equal to the task of preparing and serving these small dishes as they should be served. It is true you may get many of them from the neighboring pastry-cook’s, but the gour- met distrusts dishes provided by pastry-cooks and caterers.

Make a paste of six ounces of chopped suet and three-quarters of a pound of flour, butter a pudding- basin and line it with the suet. Flatten some rumpsteak and cut it into small pieces, also some ox-kidney. Put a good handful of flour on a board, well pepper and salt it, then roll each piece of steak and kidney in it, add some stock, cover with paste, tic it over with a buttered and floured cloth and boil for four hours. Stewed Beefsteak. Brown one pound of steak slightly in a fiyingpan, then place it in a stewpan with a carrot, a turnip, and a large onion all sliced ; cover the meat with sufficient stock. Let it stew very gently and sprinkle a little chopped parsley over it before serving. Thicken the gravy with a little butter and flour and add a few port-wine dregs, a little mushroom catsup and Worcester sauce, and send to table with it carrots and turnips cut into shapes and cooked in stock.

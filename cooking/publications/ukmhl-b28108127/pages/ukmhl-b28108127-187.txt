A refinement in table-service is to have many sets of porcelain, and to serve each course on dishes and plates of different design ; and, above all things, see that the plates are warmâ€”not burning hot, but suffi- ciently warm not to diminish the heat of the food that is served on them. The gourmet will prefer the exclusive use of ceramic dishes and plates in serving a dinner, because a metal dish when heated communicates a slight flavour of its own to the natural flavour of the viands. In the Parisian restaurants, even in the best, they have a vile habit of serving a duck, for in- stance, on a metal dish, and, while the maitre d'hotel cuts up the duck and deposits the pieces on the dish, he has a spirit-lamp burning beneath it. The dish thus becomes hot, the gravy bubbles, the pieces of duck get an extra cooking and absorb the taste of the heated metal, and the result of the whole operation is not roast duck but oxidized duck.

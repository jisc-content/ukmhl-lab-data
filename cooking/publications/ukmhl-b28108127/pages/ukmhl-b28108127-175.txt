The desirable thing, in the words of the poet, is a “ neat repast.” There is not only an art of preparing a delicate feast, but an art of eating one, and this latter art is not so ad- vanced as it might be. Method in eating is all-important, and the only method is the English, for the English eat with ease and without embarrassing their neighbours.

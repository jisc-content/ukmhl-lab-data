Lately the Belgians have taken to grape culture, and supply the Paris mar- ket with fruit from January to May ; from July to October the grape supply comes from Algeria ; from September to January the finest grapes are produced by the growers of Thomery, near Fontainebleau. Thus there are only two months out of the twelve when you cannot have fresh grapes at Paris. In all kinds of vegetables and salads the Paris market is unrivalled. The methods of cul- ture employed by the gardeners who supply this market are worthy of careful study. As a general rule, all dry vegetables are cooked by putting them into cold water, the temperature of which is gradually raised to boiling-point, while all fresh and green vege- tables are cooked by plunging them into salted water already boiling.

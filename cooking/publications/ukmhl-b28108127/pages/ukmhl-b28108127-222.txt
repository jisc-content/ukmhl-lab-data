In presence of such indifference, what is to be done ? How can we revive the spirit of criticism which alone can rescue the art of cookery from its actual state of decadence ? The case seems almost hopeless, for the men of the present generation do not appear to have the sentiment of the table ; they know neither its varied resources nor its infinite refinements; their palates are dull, and they are content to eat rather than to dine.

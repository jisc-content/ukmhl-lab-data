A Cingalese or Malay curry is especially noticeable for the preponderance of the cocoanut in its manufacture, and is especially suitable for fish or egg curries. It is, moreover, much milder than ordinary curries; indeed it is really more of a fricassee than a curry, as the powder shines chiefly by its absence.

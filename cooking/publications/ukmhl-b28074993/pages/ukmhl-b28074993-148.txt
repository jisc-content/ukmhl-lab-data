144 STATES3IEI^S DISBFJS; I CREAM PIES. BY MRS. C. A. BOUTELLB. (Wife of Representative from Maine.) ^AKE the crust the same as for spouge cake, and bake in four deep tin pans. When cool split in two with a sharp knife and fill with the cream filling. THE FILLING. One pint of new milk, one cup of sugar, one-half cup of flour, two eggs, Put the basin of milk into another of hot water. Beat the sugar, flour and eggs together until very light and smooth, and when the milk boils stir in with one teaspoonful of salt. Cook twenty minutes, stirring often. Flavor with lemon, unless some other is preferred. This will fill the four pies. The pint of milk must be generous, and the half-cup of flour scant.

STATESMEN’S DISHES; 76 CHICKEN CROQUETS. BY MBS. A. W. GRKELY. (Wife of the Chief of the Signal Sorvloe.) SNE large chicken, roasted, one pair small sweetbreads, and a little of the soft bread dressing with which the chicken has been stuffed, chopped fine together; Make a drawn butter of one pint of cream, in which an onion has stood until the cream is ready to boil; one-half cup of but- ter aud sufficient flour to make a thick sauce. Season the chicken mixture highly with chopped parsley, the juice of half a lemon, salt and red pepper— chili Colorado, not cayenne. Mix with the drawn butter and heat thoroughly, stirring constantly. When cool form the croquets with cracker dust, roll in beaten egg, then in cracker dust, and fry. £

Blend some flour with butter, add it gradually to the sauce, then add some lemon-juice to taste, and sei ve hot. 94.—Spanish Sauce. 2 slices of ham, 2 slices of veal, 2 carrots, and 2 onions sliced. Let this stew on the fire; then add equal quantities of stock or white wine, 1 clove of garlic, a bunch of parsley, 1 bay leaf, a couple of cloves. Simmer altogether for 3 or 4 hours on a slow fire; thicken with flour and butter; skim and pass through a sieve. 95.—Ravigote Sauce (Hot). Chop some herbs, such as chervil, tarragon, parsley, and chives, very fine; place in a saucepan with some- good stock, a little white wine, pepper, and salt; stew gently; add a little flour blended in butter, and a small quantity of lemon-juice; stir till thick, and. serve.

Mackerels, herrings, soles, filleted haddocks, etc., can be cooked in this manner. 58a.—Broiled Whitings. Sprinkle them with pepper and salt, and pour over a little oil. Leave them for an hour or two, then broil them before the fire; turn them frequently; serve over them any sauce liked. 59.—Cod “ a la Creme.” I Boil a nice piece of cod, either fresh cod or very slightly salted, in boiling water. Drain it and cut into fillets. Blend 3 or 4 ounces of butter with flour, pepper, and nutmeg (salt, if the cod used is not salted); let it thicken over the fire, stirring all the time. Now add half a pint of cream and some very finely chopped parsley. Stir carefully for five minutes; then put in the fillets of cod and simmer gently for 10 minutes; then serve. 60.—Bouille a Baisse of Cod.

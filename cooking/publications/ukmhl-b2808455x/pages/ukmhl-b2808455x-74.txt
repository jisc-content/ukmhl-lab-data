PLAIN COOKING 70 138. Ragout of Rabbit Have the rabbit nicely jointed, and fry in butter until light brown. Place in a stewpan with some strong stock and some onions, parsley, mixed herbs, a few allspice, and some lemon peel. Cover closely, and simmer for about an hour and a half, or until the meat is tender. Strain off most of the gravy, leaving enough in the pan to keep the rabbit hot. Melt a piece of butter in a saucepan, and stir in enough flour to make a thick paste, then gradually add the gravy, with salt and pepper, and let it boil for about ten minutes. Be sure and remove any scum as it arises. Then add some ketchup and lemon juice and a wineglass- ful of white wine, and let it boil up. Arrange the rabbit on a dish, and strain the gravy over it through a sieve. Garnish with some force- meat balls and fried or grilled bacon. 139.

Pick from the bones the meat of a large haddock, or any sort of white fish; mince it finely, and add the same propor- tions of minced suet and of grated bread, a few chopped oysters, and some boiled parsley chopped; season with a little pounded onion, Jamaica and cayenne pepper, salt, nut- meg, and lemon-peel; mix all well together, and bind it with the well-beaten yolks of eggs ; roll it into small balls, and fry them of a light brown, in fresh beef drippings.

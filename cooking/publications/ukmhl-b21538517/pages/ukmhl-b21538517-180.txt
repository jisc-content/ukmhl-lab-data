Mix with twelve ounces of flour a large teaspoonful of baking-powder and half a pound of stoned raisins chopped small, and two ounces of shred candied peel with a little spice. Add a little milk to the other mixture, then work in the dry ingredients till a fairly stiff dough is formed, mixing very thoroughly; then bake in a deep buttered tin for nearly two hours, in a quite moderate oven. Keep this cake several days before cutting. Indeed, all fruit cakes are better for being kept in a covered tin for some time before cutting, as they grow, more mellow in flavour by this means. Among fruit cakes a pleasant variety is made by using chopped figs or a mixture of chopped crystallised fruits in place of currants, raisins, or sultanas. CHERRY CAKE Beat four ounces of butter and the same of sugar together to a cream, and add three whole eggs well whisked, with half a pound of candied cherries and a very little chopped angelica, also a few drops of almond flavouring. Work in six ounces of self- raising flour, and when thoroughly mixed pour into a buttered tin and bake fully an hour.

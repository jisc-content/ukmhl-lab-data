They are not considered thoroughly wholesome. RECIPES. 95.—WATER ARROWKOOT. Ingredients— ^ oz. arrowroot. I i teaspoonful of sugar, pint of cold water. | Metliod— Put the arrowroot into a small basin, add to it a tablespoonful of the cold water, and break it with a wooden spoon until quite smooth. Then pour on the rest of the water, mix well, and pour into a small lined saucepan. Stir this over the fire until it boils and thickens, and then let it boil from 7 to lo minutes longer, to thoroughly cook the arrowroot. Sweeten to taste, and serve in a cup or small basin. A little nutmeg may be grated over the top of it, and wine or cream added as wished. Note.—It is not sufiScient to break the arrowroot with a little cold water, and then to pour boiling water on to it until it turns clear. This only half cooks the arrowroot, and might prove most injurious to the patient, besides being so sticky that it is most disagreeable to eat.

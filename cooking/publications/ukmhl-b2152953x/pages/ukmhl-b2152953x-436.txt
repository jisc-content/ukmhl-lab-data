In France (14th and loth centuries) the repasts were commonly divided into five parts, called courses, or dishes.47 The first course was composed of cherries, tender fruits, citrons, and salads.48 Milk-porridge, puddings, and pottages followed; it was the second course.48 The third consisted of roast, with various sauces.50 The second roast, or fourth course,51 presented the guests with venison and game. The fifth course took the name of fruit-course.

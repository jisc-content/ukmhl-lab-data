Stir the whole gently over the Are for a few minutes, and moisten gradually with half a pint of brown or white stock. Add a blade of mace and half a bay-leaf. Bring it to the boil whilst stirring and remove the scum. Then cover and cook gently on the side of the stove or in the oven for about half an hour. Remove mace and bay-leaf, skim off any fat from the sauce and send to table. Note.—If liked the fish can be filleted and rolled up. Soles, plaice or brill may be prepared in this way.

CHAPTER IX. THE BOAST, HE definition given of the word roast in the “ Dictionnaire des termes du vieux Fran9ois,”* is a very curious one. Here it is :—“ Rost et raust du rosty. Ce mot vient de rusticus parceque le feu noirci, et brule la viande comme le soleil qui hale le visage des pay- sans.” Anything more futile, trivial, and far-fetched than this it were impossible to conceive; yet a per- son dally accustomed to lexicographic studies will simply smile at meanings so forced and strained— meanings very common, however, with dictionary makers and lexicographers.

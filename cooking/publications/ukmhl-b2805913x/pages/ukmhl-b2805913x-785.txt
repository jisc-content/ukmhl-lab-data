The taste of the royal gourmand will be more fully disclosed by a bill of fare of one of the private dinners given at the Pavilion, Brighton, in 1817. • Carcme was at that period for eight months chef de cuisine to the Prince Regent of England (afterwards George IV.), and for seven months of that period the chef says he never quitted his post. During these seven months, if we are to believe this celebrated cusinier., bis Royal Highness never felt any attack of gout, whereas before the cookery was so highly spiced {aroma- tisee) that the royal gourmand was tormented with it both day and night.

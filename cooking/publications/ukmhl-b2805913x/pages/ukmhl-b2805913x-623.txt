Medical men in thetwelfth and thirteenth centuries, more especially in France, ordered some fruits at the commencement of dinner, Champler recommended that cherries, raspberries, peaches, and apricots should be so eaten; and that after dinner medlars, pistachio nuts, filberts, chestnuts, apples, quinces, and pears should be produced. Melon, in the time of Henry IV., and to this day, is eaten in France with the boulli, just after the soup.

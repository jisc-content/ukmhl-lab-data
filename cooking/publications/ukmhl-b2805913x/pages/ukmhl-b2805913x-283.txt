Add four well-beaten eggs and a teaspoon- ful of carbonate of soda dissolved in a little milk. Work the mass well. Put little balls, the size of a large walnut, on a buttered tin. Put on each ball a split almond or piece of candied peel. Set them at once in a moderate oven. 964. “ Schweizer Kropfli ”—Siviss Turnovers. Make a tart paste of half a pound of flour, six ounces of butter, an ounce and a half of sugar, and three tablespoonfuls of brandy. Poll it out thin ; cut out round cakes of three and a half or four inches across ; put on each a spoonful of fine preserve ; fold them over in half-circles ; brush with egg, and bake in a moderate oven. 965. “ Pfeffer Niisse ”— Spice Nuts. Beat four eggs, stir in a pound of sifted sugar, half an ounce of powdered cinnamon, a quarter of an ounce of powdered cloves, and the peel of a lemon cut as fine as possible. Stir these a quarter of an hour; then add a pound of flour by degrees and two ounces of candied peel. When this is well mixed, drop little lumps on buttered tins, and bake them in a moderate oven. 966. “ Braune Pfeffer Niisse ”— Gingerbread Nuts. Mix in a pan two pounds of flour with half a pound of sugar, a quarter of an ounce of cloves pounded fine, and a quarter of an ounce of cardamom-seeds. Make a pound and a quarter of treacle warm, and with it half a pound of butter; pour it into the flour; add a couple of beaten eggs and a teaspoonful of carbonate of soda dissolved in a little water. Knead the whole well, and put it by in a cool place for a few days, or only let it stand a night; then break off little pieces, roll them into balls, put them on buttered tins, and bake them about twenty minutes in moderate heat, dry, but not dark. They keep good for months in canisters.

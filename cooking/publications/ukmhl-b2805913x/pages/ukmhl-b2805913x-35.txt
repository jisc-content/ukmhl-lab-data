23 SOUPS. ■ WINE SOUPS. 84. TVein Suppe ”— Wine Soup. Melt two ounces of butter in a stew-pan ; brown in it two tablespoonfuls of flour; stir into this a pint of water ; add three or four ounces of sugar, the rind and juice of a lemon, a stick of cinnamon, and a bottle of wine. Let it simmer ten minutes. Have the yolks of six eggs in a tureen. Pour the wine soup slowly into them, and serve with sippets of toast. 85. “ Wein Suppe ”— TFtne Soup. {No. 2.) Beat the yolks of eight eggs and whites of two. Add a bottle of wine, an equal quantity of water, the peel of a lemon, a stick of cinnamon, and a quarter of a pound of loaf sugar. Stir constantly till it simmers, and serve over toasted slices of French roll. 86. “ Kloster Wein Suppe ”—Monastery Wine Soup. Boil a quarter of a pound of rice in a pint and a half of water until quite soft, and with it the thin yellow rind of a lemon. Add, by degrees, a bottle of any white wine. Sweeten with three or four ounces of sugar; and whisk in the yolks of four eggs when ready to serve. 87. “ Wem Suppe"— Wine Soup. (No. 4.) Simmer together two pints of wine, a pint of water, a stick of cinnamon, and sugar to taste. When it is on the point of boiling, stir well into it the yolks of four or six eggs ; and serve with sippets of sugared toast or sponge cakes. 88. “ Wem Suppe mit Citronen ”— Wine Soup ivith Lemon. Cut two lemons in thin slices ; take out the seeds. Cover the slices with powdered sugar in a tureen. Melt a piece of butter in a stew-pan, and throw in a cupful of grated bread to brown; keep the crumbs shaken or stirred until they are crisp, then strew them over the lemon slices. Make a quart of wine with a pint of water and sufficient sugar boiling hot. Pour it over the lemon shoes ; cover closely for a few minutes, and serve with sponge cakes.

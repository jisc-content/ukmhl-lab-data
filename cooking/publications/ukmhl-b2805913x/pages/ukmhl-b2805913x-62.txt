Another way.—Make a white butter sauce; when enough boiled and salted, throw in a tablespoonful of chopped parsley, and let it boil up. 180. “ Butter Sauce ”—Butter Sauce. Put a cup of milk or water in a saucepan, with two ounces of butter and a tablespoonful of flour, a little salt, and a grate of nutmeg. Stir it over a gentle fire till it boils ; then take it off; add an ounce more of fresh butter, and keep stirring till it is melted. Let the stew-pan be large enough for plenty of stin'ing, that the butter may be light and creamy. Any adjuncts may be used with this, such as lemon-juice, tarragon nnegar, fish essences, &c. 181. “ Bechamel Sauce ”— Bechamel Sauce Is a favourite everywhere, and worth makmg well. Peel about a dozen small white onions, or an equal proportion of larger ones. Slice them very thin ; put them in a saucepan with a slice of lean ham, a few white peppercorns, and two or three ounces of fresh butter. Cover close, and let them steam over a gentle fire till soft, taking care that they do not brown.

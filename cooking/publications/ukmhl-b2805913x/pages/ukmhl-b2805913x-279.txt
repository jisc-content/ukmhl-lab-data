SMALL PASTRY AND CONFECTIONERY. 267 944. “ Mandel Brod”—Almond Cakes, Half a pound of pounded almonds, half a pound of sifted sugar, half a pound of butter, half a pound of flour, and the yolks of six eggs, to be well mixed and kneaded. Form any little fancy devices, egg them over, and bake them slowly a pale colour in a cool oven. 945. “ Mandel Schnitten ”—Almond Strips. Ingredients:—Half a pound of butter stirred to a cream, half a pound of sifted sugar, half a pound of flour, half a pound of sweet almonds, and a quarter of an ounce of bitter ones pounded fine together, and two eggs well whisked. Leave out a thii'd of the almonds and sugar. Mix all the rest into a paste, roll it out a quarter of an inch thick; cut it into long narrow strips, and egg them over. Mix together the almonds and sugar left out; strew this over. Bake them a nice yellow in a moderate oven. 946. “ Macronen ”— Macaroons. Whisk the whites of two eggs to a stiff snow; mix in by degrees three-quarters of a pound of sifted sugar. Have half a pound of almonds finely pounded with white of egg. Stir all lightly together. Make a clean tin hot and rub it over with white wax; drop with a spoon little cakes on it; or drop them on wafer-paper : and bake them a pale yellow in a cool oven. Another way;—Pound half of the almonds and cut the other half in thin slices. Mix the sliced almonds in last of all. 947. “ Bitter Macronen ”— Bitter Macaroons. With the above mass, use one-fourth of bitter almonds. 948. “ Mandel Hippen”—Almond Tubes. The whites of four eggs whisked to a snow, half a pound of sifted sugar, half a pound of finely pounded almonds, and a quarter of an ounce of powdered cinnamon must be mixed well together. Spread very thin cakes on well-buttered tins, and bake them a pale yellow in a cool oven. While hot bend them round your finger, or mould them quickly on a rqund roller somewhat larger than a finger ; slip them off to cool, and then keep them crisp in a canister.

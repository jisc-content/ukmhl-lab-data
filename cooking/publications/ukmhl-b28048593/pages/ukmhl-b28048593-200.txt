BREAD AND BISCUITS. ‘ I took him in andgied him a scone.' ‘ Scottish Songs.’ Scones, Half a pound of finest flour, two ounces of butter, a pinch of salt and of sugar, a tea- spoonful of baking-powder, and three-quarters of a gill of milk. Rub the butter into the flour with the sugar, salt, and baking-powder, and mix in the milk with an iron spoon as lightly as possible. Roll out with a little flour to the thickness of an inch, and cut into three- cornered shapes. Bake in a quick oven twenty^ minutes, and serve up on a napkin. For these scones wheat-meal flour can be used if preferred to white. ‘ With bannocks of good barley-meal, O' thacjhere was r-ight plenty.' ‘ Scottish Songs.’

SAVOURIES. ‘ / had rather have a handful or two of dried peas. ’ ‘Midsummer Night’s Dream.’ mint, green or dried, one lump of sugar, one egg, a little salt. Boil the peas in three pints of water with the mint, sugar, and salt until they are quite soft, then strain through a wire sieve, putting aside the liquor, and passing the peas through the sieve with a wooden spoon. To this pea- puree add the butter melted, the breadcrumbs, the egg well beaten, and the mint; pour this into a buttered mould, and steam for half an hour. Sauce to be served round the creme can be made of the liquor in which the peas were boiled, thickened with half a teaspoonful of arrowroot, and flavoured with mint.

Ground black pepper and moist sugar, inti- mately mixed in equal quantities, and diluted with milk, placed in saucers, adding fresh milk and stirring the mixture as often as necessary, succeeds admirably in occasioning the death of the flies, without being, at the same time, dan- gerous to the persons or useful animals which might unguardedly taste of this mixture. FLY WATER. Dissolve two drachms of the extract of quassia in half a pint of boiling water; add a little sugar or sirup; then pour the mixture on plates. The flies are extremely partial to this enticing food, and it never fails to destroy them. TO DESTROY ANTS. Ants that frequent houses or gardens may be destroyed by taking flour of brimstone half a pound, and potash four ounces : set them in an iron or earthen pan over the fire till dissolved and united ; afterwards beat them to a powder, and infuse a little of this powder in water; and wherever you sprinkle it the ants will die, or fly the place.

THE FRUGAL 10G with hops is pleasanter than hops alone. Box- berry, fever-bush, sweet fern, and horse-radish make a good and healthy diet drink. The winter evergreen, or rheumatism weed, thrown in, is very beneficial to humours. Be careful and not mis- take kill-lamb for winter evergreen ; they resem- ble each other. Malt mixed with a few hops makes a weak kind of beer; but it is cool and pleasant; it needs less molasses than hops alone. The rule is about the same for all beer. Boil the ingredients two or three hours, pour in a half- pint of molasses to a pailful, while the beer is scalding hot. Strain the beer, and when about lukewarm, put a pint of lively yeast to a barrel. Leave the bung loose till the beer is done work- ing • you can ascertain this by observing when the froth subsides. If your family be large, and the beer will be drank rapidly, it may as well remain in the barrel; but if your family be small, fill what bottles you have with it; it keeps better bottled. A raw potatoe or two, cut up and thrown in, while the ingredients are boiling, is said to make beer spirited. Ginger beer is made in the following propor- tions:—One cup of ginger, one pint of molasses, one pail and a half of water, and a cup of lively yeast.

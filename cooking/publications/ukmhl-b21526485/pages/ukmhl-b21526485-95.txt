Do not pour them into your plates till the minute you put them into the oven; it makes the crust wet and heavy. To be baked with an under crust only. Some people bake the under crust a little before the custard is poured in; this is to keep it from being clammy. CRANBERRY PIE. Cranberry pies need very little spice. A little nutmeg or cinnamon, improves them. They need a great deal of sweetening. It is well to stew the sweetening with them; at least a part of it. It is easy to add, if you find them too sour for your taste. When cranberries are strained and added to about their own weight in suo-ar' they make very delicious tarts. No upper crust! RHUBARB STALKS, OR PERSIAN APPLE. Rhubarb stalks, or the Persian apple, is the earliest ingredient for pies, which the spring offers The skin should be carefully stripped, and the stalks cut into small bits, and stewed very tender .These are dear pies, for they take an enormous quantity of sugar.

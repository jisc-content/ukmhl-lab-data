The other day, I heard a mechanic say, “I have a wife and two little children ; we live in a very small house; but to save my life, I cannot spend less than one hundred and fifty a yeai. Another replied, “You are not economical; I spend but one hundred.” I thought to myself, “Neither of you pick up your twine and paper.” A third one, who was present, was silent; but after they were gone,- he said, “ I keep house, and comfortably too, with a wife and children, for eighty a year; but I suppose they would have thought me mean, if I hud told them so.” I did not think him mean; it merely occurred to me that his wife and children were in the habit of picking up paper and twine. Economy is generally despised as a low virtue, tending to make people ungenerous and selfish. This is true of avarice; but it is not so of eco- nomy.

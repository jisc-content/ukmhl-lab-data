No. 581.—Plum Cake. Half a pound of flour; ten ounces of butter; half a pound of sugar; five eggs; two pounds of currants; quarter of a pound of almonds ; three quarters of a pound of citron ; half a pound of can- died lemon; a little rose-water, or elder-flower water, and one tea- spoonful of baking-powder. ilix the baking-powder well with the flour, and rub in the butter; then add the currants, powdered sugar, the almonds, chopped fine, the citron and candied lemon, cut in small thin slices, the eggs, well beaten, and the rose-water; mix the whole together, and bake in one, or two tin moulds, well buttered. A few drops of almond-flavour may be added. No. 582.—Plum Cake.

No. 216.—To Mix Mustard. Rub out the lumps of the mustard with the back of a spoon ; add boiling water very gradually; also a little salt, and beat it till per- fectly smooth. If cream or new milk be used instead of water, it is much milder; a salt-spoouful of sugar may then be added. No. 217.—Crisped Parsley. Pick and wash young curled parsley; dry it in a cloth ; spread it on a sheet of clean paper in a Dutch oven before the fire, and turn it frequently till it is quite crisp. It may also be nicely crisped by spreading it on a dish before the fire, putting small pieces of butter upon it, and turning it frequently with a fork. No. 218.—To Dry Herbs.

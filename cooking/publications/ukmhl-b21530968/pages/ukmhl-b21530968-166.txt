No. 372.—Stewed Apples. Pare and core the apples ; put them into a pan with a little water; let them simmer over a slow fire, and when reduced to a pulp, add powdered loaf sugar, as required, and the grated rind of a lemon; put the whole into a basin, and when cold, turn it out on a glass dish. No. 373.—STEV^’ED Eibstone Pippins. Peel four apples ; cut them in quarters, and take out the cores; put them into a pan with four ounces of sugar, the juice of a lemon, the thin rind, cut into strips, and very little water; set them on a slow fire, and stew till tender; put them into a basin, and serve when cold.

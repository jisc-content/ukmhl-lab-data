No. 494.—Breakfast Bread. Four pounds of flour; one ounce of German yeast, and one pint of warm milk. Put the flour into an earthen bowl, adding a little salt. Dissolve the yeast in three quarters of a pint of warm water; make a hole in the middle of the flour, and clear off the yeast through a fine sieve into the flour, stinnng it to set the sponge; cover it, and set it in a warm place to rise; when risen, stir in the warm milk, and when well mixed, return it to a warm place for about three quarters of an hour; mould it into rolls, twists, or small loaves; place on warm tins, and bake in a quick oven.

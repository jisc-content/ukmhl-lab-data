No. 107.—To Preserve Eggs for 'Winter use. Put the eggs in a deep earthen jar or stew-pot in lime-water, with half a pound of salt in it. To prepare the lime-water, put four gallons of water to three pounds of slaked quick-lime in a covered vessel, and when clear it is ready for use. If covered with salt only, they keep very well. In preserving eggs, great care should be taken that the shells be perfectly free from cracks.

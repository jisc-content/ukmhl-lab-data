Some use only two pounds of sugar to a pound of almonds; others, two pounds and a quarter, or two pounds and a half. MACAHOONS. One pound of sweet almonds, one pound and a quarter to one pound and three quarters of sugar, two table-spoonfuls of orange-flower water, and the whites of eight or ten eggs. Mix and bake them the same as you would Almond Cakes. Use the orange-flower w'ater in pounding your almonds. Omit the pieces of almond on the tup, and drop them in an oval form on iron sheets covered with wafer paper. Wet the edges of your wafer paper, to join it together. EATAFIAS. Half a pound of sweet almonds, half a pound of bit- ter almonds, two pounds of sugar, the whites of ten or twelve eggs. Mix as for Almond Cakes. Drop them on whited- brown paper, half the size of a nutmeg; see they are all of a size. Bake on iron sheets, in a slow oven. When cold they can be taken from the paper. YOKK DROPS.

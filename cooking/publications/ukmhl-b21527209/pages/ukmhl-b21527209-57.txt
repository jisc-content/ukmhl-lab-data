It may be prepared from cold meat or marrow bone, or the liquor in which any piece of meat has been boiled; or it may be made only with water, without meat, bones, or stock. PEAS SOUP WITH MEAT OR BONES. 1 lb. split peas. 3 onions, 1 carrot, 1 turnip. 1 head of celery. 3 quarts water. 6d. worth of bones. 1 lb. ham. Parsley. 1 teaspoonful salt. Break the bones, and put them into a saucepan with the cold water. Wash the peas, and put them with the bones; add the salt, and let boil. Skim thoroughly before adding the vegetables, all cut into small pieces. Boil with the lid off the pan for half an hour, skimming as required. Then put the lid on the pan and let all boil for four hours, stirring frequently, as the soup is very apt to burn. When the peas are quite soft, put the soup, peas, and vegetables through a sieve. Return to the saucepan, and stir until the pulp is mixed with the soup. Add salt, a lump of sugar, and pepper, to taste, and boil again for a few minutes. Serve hot with toasted bread cut into small square pieces separately. PEAS SOUP 2 lb. split peas. 4 quarts water. 1 lb. dripping'. WITHOUT MEAT OR BONES. Pepper. 4 onions, 2 carrots, 1 turnip. Salt.

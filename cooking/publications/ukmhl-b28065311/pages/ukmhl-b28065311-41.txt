29 MEATS AND ENTREES When broiling or boiling meat, it should be cooked for a few minutes before adding the salt, or the juice will be drawn from it and the flavour spoilt. HOMELY BACON PUDDING Ingredients Six ounces of suet. Eschalot and sage. Twelve ounces of flour. Pepper and salt. Bacon. Make a good suet crust with the suet finely chopped, flour, and a pinch of salt. Mix to a stiff paste with cold water, roll it out flat, cover it with thin slices of bacon, season with finely chopped eschalot and sage and a little pepper and salt; roll it into the shape of a bolster, fasten the edges securely, and put the pudding into a floured cloth; tie it at both ends, put it into boiling water and let it boil well for two hours.

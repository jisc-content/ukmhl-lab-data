Fish Sauce (2). 1 oz. butter. 1 tablespoonful flour. \ gill cream. \ pint stock, made from fish bones. Salt, mace, and cayenne to taste. Rub the butter and flour together in a basin, add to them the hot fish stock by degrees. Mix very smoothly; pour into a small saucepan, stir until it boils and thickens, then add the cream, salt, cayenne, and mace to taste. Strain over the fillets. Sauce Piquante. 2 oz. butter. 1 tablespoonful French vinegar or Tarragon vinegar. 1 oz. flour. \ pint of the bouillon from the pot au feu or good gravy. Half a shallot. Put in the stewpan two ounces of butter and half a shallot finely chopped, and some flour, and stir gently till all melts and is nice and brown. Pour in by degrees the gravy or bouillon, and one tablespoonful of French vinegar. Let it boil a few minutes, and then serve in the sauce-tureen.

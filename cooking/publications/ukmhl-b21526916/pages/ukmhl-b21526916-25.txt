CAM? COOKERY. 21 Rub the gridiron with a piece of fat pork, and lay the fish upon it, being careful to have the inside downward. If the fish is very thick, cook thirty minutes ; but for an ordinary one, twenty minutes will be suf- ficient. Ilave the dish, in which you intend serving it, warm ; place it upon the fish, and turn the dish and gridiron over simul- taneously. If the fish sticks to the grid- iron, loosen it gently with a knife. Have some butter warm, but not melted, with which to season it. Shake on a little pep- per and salt, and send to the table. Baked Cod. Scrape and wash clean a cod weighing four or five pounds. Rub into it a heaping spoonful of salt. Make a dressing of three pounded crackers, a little chopped salt-pork about one tcaspoonful of parsley, a little salt and pepper, and two tablespoonfuls of cold water.

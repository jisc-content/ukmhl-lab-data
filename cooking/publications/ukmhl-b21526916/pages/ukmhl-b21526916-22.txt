CAMP COOKERY. 18 FISH. Chowder. Take either a cod or haddock ; skin it, loosen the skin about the head, and draw it down towards the tail, when it will peel off easily. Then run your knife down the back close to the bone, which you take out. Cut your fish in small pieces, and wash in cold water. Put the head on to boil in about two quarts of water, and boil twenty min- utes. For a fish weighing six pounds, pare and slice thin five good-sized potatoes, and one onion. Place a layer of potatoes and onion in the pot, then a layer of fish, diedge in a little salt, pepper, and flour. Keep putting in alternate layers of potatoes and fish until all is used. Use about one table- spoonful of salt, one teaspoonful of pepper, one teacup of flour in all. Have ready half a pound of salt pork

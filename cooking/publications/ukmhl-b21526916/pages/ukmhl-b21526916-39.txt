Wash one quart of oysters, if they are solid, in one quart of cold water; if not, one pint of water ; drain the water through a cullender into the soup-kettle; set the kettle on the fire, and when the liquor comes to a boil, skim it; then add one quart of rich new milk; just before it comes to a boil, turn in the oysters, and thicken with two spoonfuls of cornstarch wet with milk ; then stir in half a cup of butter, and season with pepper and salt.

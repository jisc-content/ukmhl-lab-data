Pluck, draw, and singe a fine turkey-hen ; lard it with thick fillets of highly-seasoned bacon, and place in a stew-pan with stock, half a wine-glassful of brandy, slices of bacon, half a calf’s foot, onions, carrots, a bouquet of mixed herbs, a head of garlic, thyme, laurel leaves, and two heads of cloves ; if a young turkey-hen, simmer for four hours, but if an old bird leave on the fire for six hours ; take the stew-pan off the fire and leave the turkey in it until nearly cold; drain, and place it on a dish ; pass the sauce through a silk tammy, clarify it, and when cold, pour over the turkey. If required for a hot dish, garnish the turkey with the vegetables in which it has been cooked ; reduce, the sauce and pour over it. Pennel sauce. Boil a bouquet of fennel and one of parsley, chop them up fine, and stir into melted butter. Fried quenelles. Make your quenelles, and dip into reduced béchamel sauce, roll them in bread-crumbs, egg and bread-crumb them a second time, fry, and serve, garnished with fried parsley.

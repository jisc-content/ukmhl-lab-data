Purée of leeks. Braised ribs of beef and stuffed onions. Eel pout à l’italienne. Roast pullet. French beans and haricots à la maître d’hôtel. Whipped raspberries and cream. Purée of leeks. Peel your leeks, eut them up into small pièces, boil in boil- ing sait and water ; when done, pass through a tammy ; warm the purée in a saucepan with sonie butter, a tumblerful of cream, a little sugar, and a pinch of flour ; reduce, thicken with yolks of egg, and serve. Eel pout à l’italienne. Fry the eel pout and dish them up, covered with the following sauce Take a small parsnip, a young carrot, two sliced onions, half a head of garlic, a laurel leaf, and three heads of cloves ; warm in either olive oil or butter ; moisten with two tumblersful of champagne and a little stock ; simmer for an hour, pass through a tammy, skim off ail grease, and reduce the sauce if too thin ; season with a pounded anchovy, some chopped capers, sait, and coarse black pepper. Bain-marie. Whenever a dish is ordered to be cooked in a bain-marie, it means putting the ingrédients in a china pot and placing it on the fire in a large saucepan filled with boiling water.

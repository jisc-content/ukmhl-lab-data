Singe, blanch, and drain the ears, scrape thera thoroughly, and place in a stew-pan, covered with slices of bacon ; add some carrots, onions, a bouquet of mixed herbs, and slices of leraon ; moisten with equal quantities of stock and white wine, and simmer over a slow fire ; when done, drain the ears, and stuff them with forcemeat, egg and bread-crumb, fry, and serve with clear gravy. French beans à la poulette. Choose some young and tender French beans, remove ail fibres by breaking off the ends ; wash, and boil in boiling water ; when done, toss them in melted butter seasoned with chopped chives and parsley; stir in a little flour, a pinch of sait, and some stock, reduce the sauce, thicken with yolks of egg, flavour with a few drops of lemon-juice, and serve.

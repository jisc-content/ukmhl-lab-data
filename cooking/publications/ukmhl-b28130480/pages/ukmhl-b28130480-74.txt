Cut up your fowls into joints ; warm four ounces of chopped carrots, and the same quantity of onions in some butter, when brown, add the pièces of fowl, season with sait, pepper, and a pinch of grated nutmeg, toss over the fire for ten minutes ; stir in two tablespoonfuls of flour and five minutes after add a tumblerful of stock, a wineglass of white wine, a bouquet of mixed herbs, and half a pound of peeled tomatoes with ail the pips carefully removed. Cook over a slow fire for twenty-five minutes, add two punnets of mushrooms, peeled and cut up to the size of a florin, leave on the fire for ten minutes, take out the bouquet of herbs, season with an ounce of finely-chopped parsley, dish up the pièces of chicken in a pyramid and pour the sauce and vegetables over them. Brown your onions over a slow fire in butter and sait, when a good colour stir in a little flour, moisten with good stock, reduce and pass through a tarnrny.

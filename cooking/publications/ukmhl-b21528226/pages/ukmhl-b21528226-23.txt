Braising Pan.—The food to be braised is put into the lower part of the pan. The lid is covered well with red-hot ashes or charcoal. Full directions for braising are given farther on. The stew-pan is a valu- able utensil; it will in case of need serve as a braising pan, if the lid be made to go a little into the edge of the pan as some are made. Stock Pot.—The stock pot receives in it bones, trimmings of meat, remains of cold game, &c. &c., in short everything available for ordinary or good soup.

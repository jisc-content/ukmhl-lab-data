169 GOOD CHEER FAVOURABLE TO HEALTH. This physiological truth is supported by history, which informs us that every time imperious cir- cumstances, as war, sieges, sudden chiinges in the seasons, have diminished the means of food, that state of distress has always been accompanied by contagious diseases and a great increase of mor- tality. M. du Belloy, Archbishop of Paris, who lived nearly a century, had a great appetite ; be loved good cheer ; I have often seen his patriarchal coun- tenance beam at the sight of some favomite dish. On every occasion Napoleon showed him the most marked deference and respect.

During my sojourn at New-York (says Savarin), I sometimes used to spend my evenings at a coffee- house and tavern, kept by one Little, who gave turtle-soup in the morning, and in the evening the refreshments customary in the United States. I was generally accompanied by Viscount de la Massue and Jean Rodolphe Fehr, formerly a broker at JMarseilles, both émigrés like myself. We used to have a Welsh-rarebit *, and a glass of ale or cider, and talk of the misfortunes of our country, of our pleasm'es, and our hopes.

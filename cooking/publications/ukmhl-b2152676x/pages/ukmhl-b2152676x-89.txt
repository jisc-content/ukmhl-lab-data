67 GASTRONOMT. —ORIGIN OF THE SCIENCES. CHAP. V. GASTRONOMY.—ORIGIN OF THE SCIENCES. The sciences do not resemble Minerva, who issued forth armed cap-a-pie from the brain of Jupiter; they are the daughters of time, are formed invisibly, first by the collection of methods indicated by experience, and later by the discovery of principles deducted from the combination of those methods. Thus the first old men whose prudence made the invalid send for them, whose compassion in- duced them to bandage wounds, were also the first pliysicians. The shepherds of Egypt, who observed that certain planets after a certain lapse of time re- appeared on the same line of the horizon, were the first astronomers.

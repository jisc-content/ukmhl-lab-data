PREFACE, XV And those who know me are aware that I am as accustomed to the tumult of society as to the privacy of the cabinet. Finally, when I speak in the singular, “ I,” let the reader remember I am conversing with him, but when I make use of the pronoun “We,” I profess and exact subjection : “ I am Sir Oracle, And when I ope my lips, let no dog bark.” Merchant of Venire, Act i. Scene 1.

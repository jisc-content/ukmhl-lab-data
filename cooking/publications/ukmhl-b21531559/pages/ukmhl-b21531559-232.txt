Peel the onions, and boil them tender : squeeze the water from them, then chop them and add them to butter that has been melted rich and smooth, as will be hereafter directed, but with a little good milk instead of water ; boil it up once, and serve it for boiled rabbits, partridges, scrag or knuckle of veal, or roast mutton. TO MAKE PARSLEY SAUCE WHEN NO PARSLEY-LEAVES ARE TO BE HAD. Tie up a little parsley-seed in a bit of clean muslin and boil it ten minutes in some water.

Boil the potatoes, peel them, and break them to paste j then to two pounds of them, add a quarter of a pint of milk, a little salt, and two ounces of butter and stir it all well over the fire. Either serve them in this manner, or place them on the dish in a form, and then brown the top with a salamander, or in scallops. CARROTS Require a good deal of boiling. When young, wipe off the skin after they are boiled; when old, boil them with the salt meat, and scrape them first. TO STEW CARROTS. Half boil, then nicely scrape, and slice them into a stew-pan. Put to them half a tea-cupful of any weak broth, some pepper and salt, and half a cupful of cream; simmer them till they are very tender,but not broken. Before serving rub a very little flour with a bit of butter, and warm up with them. If approved, chopped parsley may be added ten minutes before they are served. TO MASH PARSNIPS. Boil them tender, scrape, then mash them into a stew-pan with a little cream, a good piece of butter, and pepper and salt.

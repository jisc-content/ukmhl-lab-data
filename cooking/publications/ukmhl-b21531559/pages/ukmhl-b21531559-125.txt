Season with common salt and saltpetre, brown sugar, a little bay-salt, pepper, cloves, mace, and allspice, in tine powder, for a fortnight: then take away the pickle, put the tongue into a small pan, and lay some butter on it; cover it with brown crust, and bake slowly till so tender that a straw would go through it.

SWEET DISHES. 297 SNOW CREAM. Put to a quart of cream the whites of three eg^gs well beaten, four spoonsful of sweet wine, sugar to your taste, and a bit of lemon-peel j whip it to a froth remove the peel, and serve in a dish. COFFEE CREAM, MUCH ADMIRED. Boil a calf s foot in water till it wastes to a pint of jelly, clear of sediment and fat. Make a tea-cup of very strong coffee; clear it with a bit of isinglass to be perfectly bright; pour to it the jelly, and add a pint of very good cream, and as much fine Lisbon sugar as is pleasant ; give one boil up, and pour into the dish. It should jelly, but not be stiff. Observe that your coffee be fresh. CHOCOLATE CREAM. Scrape into one quart of thick cream, one ounce of the best chocolate, and a quarter of a pound of sugar j boil and mill it; when quite smooth, take it off, and leave it to be cold j then add the whites of nine eggs. Whisk; and take up the froth on sieves, as others are done ; and serve the froth in glasses, to rise above some of the cream. CODLIN CREAM. ic Pare and core twenty good codlins ; beat them in a mortar with a pint of cream j strain it into a dish j and put sugar, bread-crumbs, and a glass of wine, to it. Stir it well.

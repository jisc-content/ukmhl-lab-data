Lay over the bottom of a stew-pan as much lean veal as will cover it an inch thick ; then cover the veal with thin slices of undressed gammon, two or three onions, two or three bay-lccaves, some sweet herbs, two blades of mace, and three cloves. Cover the stew- pan, and set it over a slow fire ; but when the juices come out, let the fire be a little quicker. When the meat is of a fine brown, fill the pan with good beef- broth, boil and skim it, then simmer an hour: add a little water, mixed with as much Hour as will make it properly thick : boil it half an hour, and strain it.

ARTIFICIAL asses’ MILK. Boil together a quart of water, a quart of new milk, an ounce of white sugar-candy, half an ounce of eringo-root, and half an ounce of conserve of roses, till half be wasted. This is astringent; therefore proportion the doses to the effect, and the quantity to what will be used while sweet. Another.—Mix two spoonsful of boiling water, two of milk, and an egg well beaten ; sweeten with pounded white sugar - candy. This may be taken twice or thrice a day. Another.—Boil two ounces of hartshorn-shavings, two ounces of pearl-barley, two ounces of candied eringo-root. and one dozen of snails that have been bruised, in two quarts of water, to one. Mix with an equal quantity of new milk, when taken, twice a day. WATER CRUEL. Put a large spoonful of oatmeal by degrees into a pint of water, and, when smooth, boil it.

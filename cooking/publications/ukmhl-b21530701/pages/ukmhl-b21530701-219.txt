THE PRACTICE OT COOKERY. 175 CHAPTER Xf. GRAVIES, SAUCES, FORCEMEATS, AND OTHER THINGS. Browning for made dishes. Put into a sauce-pan one pound of good brown sugar, stir it constantly over a slow fire, boil it till it is as thick as treacle, and resem- bles it in colour; take the pan off the fire, stir it for a minute or two, and pour in very slowly a quart of boil- ing water, stirring constantly; put it again on the fire, and boil it for a little; pour it into a bowl, and when cold, bottle it. This browning will keep good for a year, and very little of it serves for colouring soups, gravies, or sauces. To clarify butter. Put the butter cut into slices into a nicely-cleaned brass pan, stir it gently till dissolved; when it boils, draw the pan to the side of the fire, skim it, and let it boil gently a second time, and if any scum again rises, take it off; let it settle for two or three mi- nutes, and strain it gently through a 6ieve which has a piece of muslin laid into it.

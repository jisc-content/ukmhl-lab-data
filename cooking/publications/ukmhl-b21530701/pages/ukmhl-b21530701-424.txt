It may be eaten with cream, or made into tarts or pud- dings. Gooseberry and Raspberry Cakes. Gather the gooseberries upon a dry day, and before they are quite ripe; top and tail them ; scald them, or put them into an earthen jar ; tie it over closely with bladder, and place it in a pan of water; let it boil till the fruit is sufficiently tender to pulp through a sieve. To a pound of the pulp add one pouftd of pounded and sifted loaf sugar, and the well-beaten white of one egg; mix all together, and whisk it for three hours ; drop the cakes upon wri- ting paper, and dry them in the sun, or upon a stove. For the raspberry cakes, allow a pound of sugar to a pint of the fruit, measured before it is scalded, or pulped through a sieve ; allow one white of an egg to each pound, and for each sort of cakes dry the sugar well, and use it . while it is warm.

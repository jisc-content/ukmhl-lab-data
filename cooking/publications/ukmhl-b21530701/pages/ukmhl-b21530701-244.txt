THE PRACTICE OF COOKERY. 200 CHAPTER XII. VEGETABLES. PREPARATORY REMARKS. Vegetables are always best when newly gathered, and should be brought in from the garden early in the morning; they will then have a fragrant freshness, which they lose by keeping. They must be cleaned with the greatest care, the out- side leaves of every description of greens removed, and they, and all other vegetables, more particularly when not recently gathered, should be laid for several hours in cold water, and well shaken to throw out the insects. A tea- spoonful of salt should always be put into the water in which they are to be boiled, and if it is hard, a tea-spoon- ful of salt of tartar, or potash, may be added to preserve the green colour of the vegetable. All vegetables should be boiled quickly, and, with the exception of spinach, in an open vessel, skimming them carefully. Kitchen greens should be kept in a cool and shady place. Potatoes, carrots, turnips, and beet-root should be stored up, without being cleaned from the earth adhering to them, in layers of sand, or laid in heaps, and covered with earth and straw.

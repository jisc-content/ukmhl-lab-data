A Cask which has not contained vinegar before, should have a quart of boiling hot vinegar poured into it, shaken till cold, and allowed to stand for some hours. Sugar Vinegar. To five gallons of water put eight pounds of coarse brown sugar ; let it boil as long as any scum rises, which must be carefully removed ; then pour it into a cask, and when it is about milk warm, or from 60 to 65 degrees Fahrenheit, cut off the crust, and toast a quarter of a slice of bread, and cover it over with fresh yeast; put it into the cask, leave it open for four or five days, and then paste over the bung-hole a piece of paper, and pierce it here and there %vith a pin. It should be kept in a warm, dry place, without being moved; and if made in March or April, it may be drawn off and bottled in September, when some persons boil it over again, to prevent the mother from forming in the bottles. The vinegar cask, as soon as emptied, should be again filled in the same manner. Gooseberry Vinegar.

Gather the fruit upon a dry day, mash, and drip it through a jelly-bag; to every pint of juice add a pound of pounded loaf sugar, and when it is completely dissol- ved, bottle it in pint and half-pint bottles, filling them only to the neck. When to be used, mix it with rich cream, add more sugar, and whisk it till thick. It may be made the day before it is required. Another way to make Raspberry Cream. Mix a little pounded loaf sugar with a pint and a half of good cream, about a tea-cupful of raspberry jelly, the grated rind of one, and the juice of half a lemon ; beat it well together, and, with a syllabub mill, mill it slowly for half an hour;, or till it be thick and solid. Put it into a glass dish, or serve it in custard glasses. Another way to make Raspberry Cream. Put six ounces of raspberry jam to a quart of cream, pulp it through a lawn sieve, mix it with the juice of a lemon and a little sugar, and whisk it till thick. Serve it in a dish or glasses. Strawberry cream may be made in the same way. For common use, substitute good milk for the cream. Whipt Cream.

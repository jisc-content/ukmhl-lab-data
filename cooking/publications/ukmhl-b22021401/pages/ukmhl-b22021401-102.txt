If a person wishes to have a slice underneath, the joint must be turned up, by taking hold of the end of the ribs with the left hand, and raising it, till it is in the position as here represented. One slice, or more, may now be cut in the direction of the line 1, 2, passing the knife down to the bone. The slices, whether on the upper or under side, should be cut thin, but not too much so. BRISKET OF BEEF. This is always boiled, and cut in the direc- tion 1, 2, quite down to the bone; the outside slice, which should be taken off pretty thick, ought never to be offered to any one.

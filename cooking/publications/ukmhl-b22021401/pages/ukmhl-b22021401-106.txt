The eye is considered by some as a very tit bit; and to take this from its socket, 1, the point of your carving knife should be forced down on one side of it, taking care, whilst cutting round, to keep the point of the blade slanting towards the inside, — by this means detaching the meat from the bone. The palate is found on the under side of the roof of the mouth ; by lifting the head up with your left hand, it may easily be separated from the bone. Some good meat may be cut from the under side, covering the under jaw, some fat pared from the ear. The brains and tongue should be served in a separate dish.

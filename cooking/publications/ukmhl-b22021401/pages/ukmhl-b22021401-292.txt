During the intense frosts of winter, if some Vinegar be put into shallow dishes, the watery parts will be converted into Ice — while the Spirituous, or Acetous basis, remains in a fluid state: by repeating this process a pint of strong vinegar may, in very cold seasons, be reduced to a few table-spoonsful of the Es- sence. Receipt for Quin’s Sauce. A pint and a half of strong Walnut-liquor, A pound and a half of Anchovies, A quarter of an ounce each of Mace, Cloves, and Fine Jamaica pepper. Half a tea-spoonful of Cayenne, Twenty-four Eschallots, and Two ounces of scraped Horseradish. To be boiled together, and the liquor strained therefrom.

Skate, if young, eats very fine, crimped and fried. Smelts — are allowed to be caught in the Thames on the first of November, and continue till May. The Thames Smelts are the best and sweetest, for two reasons,— they are fresher, and richer, than any other you can get; they catch them much more plentiful and larger in Lancashire and Norfolk, but not so good, as they are a fish that should always be eaten fresh ; indeed all river fish should be eaten fresh, except Sal- mon, which, unless crimpt, eats better the second or third day; but all Thames fish par- ticularly should be eaten very fresh; no fish ■eats so bad when kept.

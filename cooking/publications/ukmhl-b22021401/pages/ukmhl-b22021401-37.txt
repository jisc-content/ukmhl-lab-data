It shews, besides, that you are mindf ul of what you owe ; it makes you appear a careful as well as an honest man, and that still increases your credit. “ Beware of thinking all your own that you possess, and of living accordingly. It is a mistake that many people who have credit fall into. To prevent this, keep an exact Account for some time, both of your Expenses and your Income. “ If you take the pains at first to mention particulars, it will have this good effect, that you will discover how wonderfully small trifling expenses amount to large sums; and will discern what might have been, and may for the future, be saved, without occasioning any inconve- nience. “ In short, The Way to Wealth, if you desire it, is plain as the way to market. It depends chiefly on two words. Industry and Frugality ; that is, Waste neither Time nor Money, but make the best use of both.

The apparently whimsical English name of Punch, like the liquor itself, is of West India origin, the word in the aboriginal language signifying simply Five, being the number of ingredients there used : viz. 1. acid, or lime, or lemon-juice; 2. sweetness, or sugar; 3. spirit, or rum, &c.; 4. water; and, 5. spicy flavour, or nutmeg, &c. It is singular, too, that punch, the word for five, consists of just five letters. From the opposite natures of the several in- gredients, punch has also been called the liquor of contradictions.

Vanilla.— So named from the fruit resembling a Spanish knife, called vayna, or vaynilla. It has a balsamic odour and agreeable flavour ; it contains a peculiar volatile oil, with a quantity of benzoic acid. Vanilla is used to flavour sweetmeats (especially chocolate), cakes, liqueurs, lemonade and ice creams. Medicinally it acts as a slight stimulant, but is sel- dom used for that purpose. It grows principally in Brazil, Guiana, Mexico and the V'est Indies, but has been met with in Penang, Singai^ore and Java.

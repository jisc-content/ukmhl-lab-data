Pass cooked peas through a sieve to remove the skins; reheat with a few grains of sugar and salt, and a little cream; cook until dry, then with forcing bag and tube press a star of pulp upon each chop. Serve very hot.—Janet M. Hill. 30.—French Minced Veal, No. 2. Fry a chopped onion until a pale yellow, in a little butter; add 1 tablespoonful of flour and a little stock; add 1 pint of fine chopped veal and 1 tablespoonful of chopped parsley; season with salt and pepper. Mix well and heat hot, but not boil. Remove from the fire and stir in the yolk of 1 egg and the juice of a lemon beaten together. Pile the meat in the centre of a dish and make a fence of strips of toast around it. 31.—Hash Fritters. Stew gently pieces of cold veal or lamb in a little stock or gravy. Cut in small pieces. Add some bread crumbs and 1 beaten egg, salt and pepper. Make into small balls and cover the outside with mashed potato. Dip in crumbs, then in beaten egg and in crumbs again. Fry in hot fat.

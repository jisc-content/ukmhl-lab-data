When old and large they are best split, as well as cut aslant. Dish in a pyramid. 196. To Boil Cabbage, Greens, Savoys, and Brussels Sprouts. Strip off the coarse outer leaves, and pare off most of the coarse husk from the branch-stalks; cut off the stem close to the head; wash thoroughly, and put on with plenty of boiling water in an open pot, and a little salt of wormwood, or a bit of soda. Divide half-grown cabbages, and quarter large ones, but tie up to keep them in shape till served. See that they be well covered with boiling water. They will take from fifteen minutes to an hour, or more, according to the age. Small Brussels cabbage are boiled for ten minutes and arranged in the dish as asparagus, or on a buttered toast. The French serve a white sauce, and often send up a cruet of oil with these delicate cabbages. They are also served with Maitre d'Hotel butter. Nos. 590, 590^ 197. Spinage. This delicate vegetable requires very careful picking and washing. When perfectly clean, put it into plenty of boiling water and salt, and boil very quickly, pressing it down with a wooden spoon; ten minutes will boil it. Drain and squeeze it, and throw it into a great quantity of cold water, to pre- serve the colour green.

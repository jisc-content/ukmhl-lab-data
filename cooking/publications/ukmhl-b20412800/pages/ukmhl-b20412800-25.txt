The grumbling Doctor, wise enough to make a virtue of necessity, rallied his naturally good temper; for we hold that all gourmands are good-natured, except, perhaps, about meal-times; though it may be, as Lord Shaftesbury says of other good-natured persons, " because they care for nobody but themselves; and as nothing annoys them but what affects their own interest, they never irritate themselves about what does not concern them, and so seem to be made of the very milk of human kindness."—Such was Dr Red- gill.

A turnip boiled with the onions makes them milder. Clear Shalot Sauce. Put a few chopped shalots into a little gravy boiled clear, and near half as much vinegar, season with pepper and salt: boil half an hour. To make Parsley Sauce when no Parsley-leaves are to be had. Tie up a little parsley-seed in a bit of clean muslin and boil it ten minutes in some water. Use this water to melt the butter ; and throw into it a little boiled spinach minced, to look like parsley. Green Sauce, for green Geese, or Ducklings. Mix a quarter of a pint of sorrel-juice, a glass of white wine, and some scalded gooseberries. Add sugar, and a bit of butter. Boil them up. Dread Sauce. Boil a large onion, cut into four, with some black peppers and milk, till the onion is quite a pap. Pour the milk strained on grated white stale bread, and cover jt. In an hour put it into a sauce-pan, with a good piece of butter mixed with a little flour; boil the whole up together, and serve. Dutch Sauce for Meat or Fish. Put six spoonfuls of water, and four of vinegar, into a sauce-pan warm, and thicken it with the yolks of two eggs. Make it quite hot, but do not boil it: squeeze in the juice of half a lemon, and strain it through a sieve. Sauce Robart, for Rumps or Steaks.

It may be put on two or three inches thick, and will keep several months; when to be used, cut a piece out; stir it in warm water.—If to be used, for brewing, keep it by dipping large handfuls of birch tied together; and when dry, repeat the dipping once. You may thus do as many as you please ; but take care that no dust comes to them, or the vessel in which it has been prepared as before. When the wort is set to work, throw into it one of these bunches, and it will do as well as with fresh yeast; but if mixed with a small quantity first and then added to the whole, it will work, sooner.

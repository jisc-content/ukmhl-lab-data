Scrape into one quart of thick cream, one ounce of the best chocolate, and a quarter of a pound of sugar; boil and mill it; when quite smooth, take it off, and leave it to be cold ; then add the whites of nine eggs. Whisk ; and take up the froth on sieves, as others are done; and serve the froth in glasses, to rise above some of the cream. Codlin Cream. Pare and core twenty good codlins ; beat them in a mortar, with a pint of cream ; strain it into a dish ; and put sugar, bread-crumbs, and a glass of wine, to it. Stir it well. Excellent Orange Cream. Boil the rind of a Seville orange very tender; beat it fine in a mortar ; put to it a spoonful of the best brandy.

Place them in a dish, with or without milk, cream, or custard ; if the latter, there should be no ratafia. Dust fine sugar over the apples. Stewed Golden Pippins. Scoop out the core, pare them very fine, and as you do it, throw them in water. For every pound of fruit, make half a pound of single-refined sugar in syrup, with a pint of water; when skimmed, put the pippins in, and stew till clear; then grate lemon over, and serve in the syrup. Be careful not to let them break. They are an elegant and good dish for a corner or desert. Black Cups.

For direc- tions to do this, see the article Rice 'vci the Itidex. Anothery more easily made.—Cut up a chicken or young rabbit; if chicken, take oft’ the skin. Roll each piece in a mixture of a large spoonful of flour, and half an ounce of currie-powder. Slice two or three onions; and fry them in butter, of a light brown; then add the meat, and fry all together till the meat begins to brown. Put it all into a stew-pan, and pour boiling water enough just to cover it. Simmerverygently two or three hours. If too thick, put more water halfan hour before serving. If the meat has been dressed before, alittle broth will be better than water; but the currie is richer when made of fresh meat. To braise Chickens. Bone them, and fill them with forcemeat.

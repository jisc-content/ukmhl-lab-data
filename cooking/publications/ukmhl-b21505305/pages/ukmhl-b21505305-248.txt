Another way.—Blanch and pound four ounces of al- monds, with a little orange-flower, or rose-water; then stir in the yolks of six and whites of three eggs, well beaten, five ounces of butter warmed, the peel of a lemon grated, and a little of the juice ; sweeten with fine Lisbon sugar. When well mixed, bake in a deli- cate paste, in small pans. Another way.—Press the whey from as much curd as will make two dozen small ones; then put it on the back of a sieve, and with half an ounce of butter rub it through with the hack of a spoon ; put to it six yolks, and three whites of eggs, and a few bitter almonds pounded with as much sugar as will make the curd properly sweet: mix with it the rind of a lemon'grated, and a glass of brandy. Put a pufi'-paste into the pans, and ten minutes will bake them. Dough Nuts. Rub a quarter of a pound of butter into a pound of flour, then add five ounces of sugar, two eggs, about a desert spoonful of yeast, and sufficient milk to make it into a stiff paste. Let it stand to rise, then roll it out, and ciit it into shapes, with a paste cutter, and boil them in lard, till they are of a nice brown color. A Tipsy Cake, Put a sponge cake into a deep china or glass dish, pourround it some white wine (raisin is very suitable,) and a wine glass of brondy. Let the cakes soak up the wine, and then strew sifted sugar over it, and pour in the dish a rich thick custard.

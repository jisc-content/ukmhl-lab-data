Note.-—In this, as in all puddings, it is best to beat the yolks and the whites of eggs separately. 50. Snow Pancakes. Take half a pound of flour, and add to it gradually one pint of milk, theii mix in quickly three large spoonsful of clean snow ; melt the lard or dripping in the pan, and put in two tablespoonsful of the mixture ; turn when done on the under side, and brown the upper ; roll up, and place on a hot plate in the oven until all are done. Serve very hot, with sugar sifted over, and a cut lemon or orange on another plate. 51. Curds. Half a pint of sour cream, two pints of milk, two eggs well beaten : put the milk on to boil, then add the eggs and cream ; put into a strainer, and when nearly cold into a mould for the next day. To be eaten with sugar. 52. Alexandra Pudding. Two eggs, their weight in flour, sugar, and butter, two table- spoonsful of raspberry jam, and an eggspoonful of soda : beat up all well together, steam for two hours, and serve. 53. Baked Ground-Rice Pudding. Boil two ounces of rice in half a pint of milk ; then add two ounces of butter, three ounces of moist sugar, the peel of half a lemon, the yolks of two eggs, and when cold the juice of half a lemon and the whites of the two eggs beaten to a froth. Stir lightly, and bake in a moderate oven about an hour. 54.

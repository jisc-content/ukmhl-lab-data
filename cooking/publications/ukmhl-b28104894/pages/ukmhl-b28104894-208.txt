Cover this all with cold water, bring it gently to the boil, skim well, and leave it to simmer by the side of the fire for two or three hours till all the goodness is extracted from the ingredients ; then strain, and when cold remove the fat. Mutton Pie.—This is made very much in the same way as beefsteak pie, only using stock made from mutton trimmings and bones, and alternating the meat layers with sliced potatoes. Season with Gouffe’s mignonette. Giblet Pie.—Have either two sets of turkey giblets or four of fowl; put the livers aside and blanch the giblets for two minutes in boiling water, at once rinsing them well in cold water and draining them on a cloth; now cut them up into neat, not too small pieces, and put them in a stewpan with a carrot, an onion stuck with four cloves, a good bouquet, and a rather high seasoning of salt and pepper or mignon- ette. Moisten it with a quart of bone stock, and let it all simmer together gently for an hour; now remove the bouquet, the carrot and the onion, strain off the gravy and drain the giblets on a cloth.

BANQUETS OF THE NATIONS 186 Soupa apo Hortarica. (Green Soup.) Chop finely two tablespoonfuls of onions, which put in a saucepan with a piece of butter and fry, but not colour ; sprinkle in two tablespoonfuls of flour ; cook for a few minutes; remove the saucepan from the fire and pour in slowly half a gallon of rich broth. Stir well until the liquor boils, then simmer it till the liquor is moderately thick. Put to the above two cleansed and trussed young fowls, and cook for about three- quarters of an hour. When cooked, take them out, skim the fat from the liquor, and pass through a fine sieve into another pan. Stir in a few tablespoonfuls of boiled sour crout, and cook for about fifteen minutes longer. Cut off all the flesh from the fowls, put it into the soup, and boil for another ten minutes ; add two or three tablespoonfuls of nettle or sorrel leaves boiled and passed through a sieve.

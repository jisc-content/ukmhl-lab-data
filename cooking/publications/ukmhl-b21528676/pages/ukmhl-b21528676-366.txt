Cut two pounds of beef, veal, mutton, lamb, kid, pork, or venison into thin slices of about three inches long. Smear them with a mixture composed of a tea- spoonful each of turmeric, coriander, ground onions, red chillies, a point of garlic, and half a teaspoonful of salt. Let the meat soak for an hour, slightly shake the pieces, and fry on both sides in plenty of boiling ghee. Chickens, pheasants, partridges, guinea-fowls, and pigeons may be cut in pieces and cooked the same way. Tihun. (Stewed Meats.) Cut a cleaned duck into eight pieces, and fry them in boiling ghee or butter in which you have first browned a finely chopped onion. Put the whole in a pot with three cupfuls of water, and of turmeric, coriander, and poppy seeds a teaspoonful, of caraway and cumin seeds half a teaspoonful, a pinch of assafoetida, half a tea- spoonful of salt, and a quarter of a salt-spoonful of red pepper. Bring to the boil and stew slowly for an hour. Now put in eight potatoes cleaned and cut in quarters, one large carrot cut in two-inch pieces, and some broad beans or haricot beans which have been softened by soaking.

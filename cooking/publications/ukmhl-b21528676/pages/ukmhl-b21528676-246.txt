Potato Quenelles. Roast in the cinders six large potatoes, washed and surrounded with paper ; peel carefully, and pound them with four ounces of fresh butter, a little salt, pepper, nutmeg, six yolks of eggs, and a little double cream ; rub this through a sieve ; form into small round balls, lay on buttered saute-pan, and simmer slowly in stock for some minutes, without letting them boil. These may be eaten as a separate dish. Gekookte Zalm. (Broiled Salmon.) Cut some thin slices from the tail end of a small salmon, and rub them over with salt. Cover a gridiron with bay leaves; put the slices of fish on them and broil over a moderately clear fire, turning them when done on one side. Pour almond sauce over the fish, and serve with potatoes.

IRELAND peel and slice the onions; peel the button onions and blanch them. Put a layer of potatoes at the bottom of a stewpan; put a layer of meat over that; season with pepper and salt; strew over a few slices of onion ; proceed thus until the meat is used up. Add the small onions when blanched (whole) among the layers ; cover the top with a layer of potatoes ; add the stock ; and let it simmer for about two hours. This stew should not be skimmed, as the fat will be ab- sorbed by the potatoes ; any scum which rises to the top before it commences to simmer should, of course, be removed. When done, dish up neatly on a dish, and sprinkle over a little chopped parsley, and serve. Packet. Save the blood of a large goose into a china dish, and stir in a spoonful of vinegar, which will keep it from getting firm. Mix a little lard and goose’s fat in a pan, in which fry finely chopped onions ; switch a couple of cold eggs with some warm milk, and mix with the blood. Season with pepper, salt, chopped parsley, a little maijoiam, and a little sage. Stir in a heaped soup- plateful of breadcrumbs, pour into a large dish, and bake in the oven as you would a custard.

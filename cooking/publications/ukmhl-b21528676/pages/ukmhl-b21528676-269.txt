BENGAL (Hindu) 233 Begoon Bhahja. (Egg Plant.) Wash and slice two pounds of the plant into pieces rather less than a quarter of an inch thick. Wipe them with a dry cloth and fry in two ounces of boiling mustard oil, first having steeped them for an hour in a teaspoonful each of ground cinnamon, ground coriander, red chillies, a pinch of saffron, and salt to taste. Eat with dal, rice, and chutnies. Dal Pithas. (Lentil Croquettes.) Fry six sliced onions in boiling mustard oil, lay on the one side, and fry two teaspoonfuls of ground onions, half a teaspoonful ground red chillies, half a teaspoonful of ground ginger, half that quantity of ground garlic, a teaspoonful of coriander seeds, a quarter of a tea- spoonful of aniseed. Put to the one side the fried condiments, &c. Having washed a quarter-pound of lentils, steep them for four hours, dry them thoroughly, and fry in the mustard oil, and put m a pot with the fried condiments, with just sufficient water to cover the ingredients, adding a very small teaspoonful of salt. Simmer very slowly for about half-an-hour, or until 0 soft mass. (If to be eaten with another dish the sliced fried onions should be strewn over the top.) Roll out rather thinly a piece of dough much the same as puff paste, and cut out rounds with a cutter about five inches in diameter.

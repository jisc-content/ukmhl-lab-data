Set the mould on broken ice mixed with rock salt at the bottom of a pail; fill up the pail with more ice and salt, and freeze for two hours. Plunge the mould in warm water to wash off the ice and salt, and turn the cream on to a piece of paper laid on the table ; dip a long knife in warm water, cut the brick lengthwise thiough the centre, and divide each piece into thiee, so that the Neapolitan will then be in six equal-sized square pieces, each having the three kinds of cream. Dress on a cold dish, with fancy paper over, and serve.

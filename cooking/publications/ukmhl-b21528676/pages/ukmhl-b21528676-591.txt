Whisk the yolk of an egg in some thick cream and add to the above, being careful not to boil. Add more wine if it is thicker than double cream. Sladak Omlet. (Sweet Omelet.) Put the whites and yolks of six eggs into separate basins. Beat the yolks with one teacupful of cream and two tablespoonfuls of castor sugar ; then beat the whites to a stiff froth, mix them with the yolks, and fry the omelet in boiling butter. When done, spread a layer of any kind of jam over the top, fold one half of the omelet over the other, sprinkle with castor sugar, and ser\ e on a folded napkin on a dish, or with cold rum sauce poured over. Cold Rum Sauce. Put into a saucepan a half pint of rum, three ounces granulated sugar, half a split vanilla bean, the grated nnd of a medium-sized orange, and one gill of Marsala wine. Place the pan on the stove, and as soon as the liquid catches fire put on the cover and boil for one minute only. Set the pan on one side to allow the con- tents to infuse for five minutes, strain into a jar, cover tightly, and let it cool thoroughly. May be poured over any suitable sweet dishes, such as puddings, creams, pancakes, omelets, blanc-mange, etc.

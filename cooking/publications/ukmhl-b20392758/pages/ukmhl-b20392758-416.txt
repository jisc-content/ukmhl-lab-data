This homely, but capital English joint, is almost invariably served at table as shown in the engraving. The carving of it is not very diflicult: the knife should be carried sharply down in the direction of the line from 1 to 2, and slices taken from either side, as the guests may desire, some liking the knuckle- end, as well done, and others preferring the more underdone part.

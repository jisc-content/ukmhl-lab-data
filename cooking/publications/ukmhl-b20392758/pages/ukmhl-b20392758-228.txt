NoU.—^The thick parts of the middle of the back are the best slices in a turbot; and the rich gelatinous skin covering the fish^ as well as a little of the thick part of the fins, are dainty morsels, and should be placed on each plate. WHITING, &c. Whiting, pike, haddock, and other fish, when of a sufficiently large size, may be carved in the same manner as salmon. When small, they may be cut through, bone and all, and helped in nice pieces, a middling-sized whiting serving for two slices. Note.—The thick part of the Eel is reckoned the best; and this holds good ^ of all flat fish. The TAIL of the Lobster is the prime part, and next to that the claws. IISH-CAST£BS«

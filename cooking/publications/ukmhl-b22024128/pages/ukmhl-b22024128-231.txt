Warm three quarts of new milk; turn it with a quart of buttermilk, drain the curd through a sieve ; when dry, pound it in a marble mortar, with near half a pound of sugar, a lemon boiled tender, the crumb of a roll grated, a nutmeg grated, six bitter almonds, four ounces of warm butter, a tea-cupful of good cream, the yolks of five and whites of three eggs, a glass of sweet wine, and ditto of brandy. When well incorporated, bake in small cups or bowls well buttered ; if the bottom be not brown, use a sala- mander; but serve as quick as possible, and with pud- ding sauce. Curd Puddings or Puff's. Turn two quarts of milk to curd, press the whey from it, rub through a sieve, and mix four ounces of butter, the crumb of a penny loaf, two spoonfuls of cream, and half a nutmeg, a small quantity of sugar, and two spoon- fuls of white wine.

Stew two or three flounders, some parsley-leaves and roots, thirty peppercorns, and a quart of water, till the fish are boiled to pieces; pulp them through a sieve. Set over the fire the pulped fish, the liquor that boiled them, some perch, tench, or flounders, and some fresh leaves and roots of parsley ; simmer all till done enough, then serve in a deep dish. Slices of bread and butter are to be sent to table, to eat with the souchy. HERRINGS and SPRATS. To smoke Herrings. Clean, and lay them in salt and a little saltpetre one night; then hang them on a stick, through the eyes, in a row.

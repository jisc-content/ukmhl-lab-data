Put four ounces of butter into a saucepan with water, and when it boils, pour it into as much flour as you choose : kneaxl and beat it till smooth; cover it, as at the top of page 136. Raise it; and if for custard, put a paper within to keep out the sides till half-done, then fill with a cold mixture of milk, egg, sugar, and a little peach-water, lemon-peel, or nutmeg. By cold is meant that the egg is not to be warmed, but the milk should be warmed by itself—not to spoil the crust. The above batter will aiake a great deal of raised crust; which must not be rich, or it wall be difficult to prevent the sides from falling. Excellent short Cnists.

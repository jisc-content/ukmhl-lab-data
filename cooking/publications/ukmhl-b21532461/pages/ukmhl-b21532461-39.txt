Almond Knots.—Take two pounds of almonds, and blanch them in hot water; beat them in a mortar, to a very fine pasted with rose water, be careful to' keep them from oiling. Take a pound of double-re- fined sugar, sifted through a lawn sieve, leave out some to make up the knots, put the rest in a pan upon the fire, till it is scalding hot, at the same time have the almonds scalding hot in another pan ; then mix them together with the whites of three eggs beaten to froth, and let it stand until it is cold ; then roll it with some of the sugar left out for that purpose, and lay them in platters of paper. They will not roll into shape, therefore lay them ns well as may be, and bake them in a slow oven.

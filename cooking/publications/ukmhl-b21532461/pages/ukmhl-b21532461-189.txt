Chickens en Caissons.*—Cut the white parts of two chickens into fillets, and ma- rinade them in oil, with salt, pepper, sweet herbs chopped &c. Make some little cases of white paper, and put a thin slice of bacon at the bottom of each, lay in tlie fillets, strew in some truffles sliced, and moisten them with consomme, or stock; put the cases in a baking-pan, and bake them. When done, put them on a dish with the other parts of the chickens (which must be trussed with the head under the skin of the breast) and pour over the whole some veal gravy and some lemon juice. Chicken- en Capilotade.*—Put into a stewpan a little butter and flour; add mushrooms, parsley, and shalots cut small; dilute these with equal quantities of stock, and red or white wine. When the sauce is well boiled, skim it; cut a roasted fowl in pieces, and put it into this sauce; stew it gently for a quarter of an hour. Add some gherkins cut in thin slices.

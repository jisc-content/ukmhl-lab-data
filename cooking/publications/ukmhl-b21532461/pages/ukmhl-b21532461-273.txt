Egg Pie.—Boil a dozen eggs hard, mince the yolks very small with their weight in beef suet; put to them some salt, beaten spice, lemon peel, rose water, sugar, a quarter of a pound of dates, stoned and sliced, add a pound of currants; you may also add an apple shred small; mix all these together, fill your dish, and bake it. Serve it to table with a little wine. Serve them with fried bread, grate Parmesan cheese over, and brown it with a salamander.

Cockles {Pickled,).—Boil two quarts of cockles in their own liquor for half an hour, skimming them well ; then take out the cockles, strain the liquor through a cloth ; take a pint of it, and add to it three quar- ters of an ounce of mace and half an ounce of cloves; boil these together once, and then put it to the cockles and remaining liquor, and stir it well; add to this a spoonful of salt, three quarters of a pint of white wine vinegar, and a quarter of an ounce of whole pepper, and let it stand. When cold, put the cockles in small barrels or jars, as close as they will lay ; pour in on them as much liquor as you possibly can, and as it soaks in put in more. Cover them veiy close. They may he soon eaten. .

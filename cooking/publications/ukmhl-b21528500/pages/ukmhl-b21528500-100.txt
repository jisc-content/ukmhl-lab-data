ANCIENT CUSTOMS. In an old history relating to the year 950, there is an account of the houses and ways of the Saxons. Even in those early days there was considerable ceremony and even luxury about all matters connected with food and eating at a Saxon Thane’s table. Everyone was seated at a large, square table, each strictly in order with his respective rank, as they had a law by which those who took higher seats than they were entitled to, were pelted with bones or any other refuse. The Thane’s wife would be seated in a chair of state on a platform raised above the other guests, and placed at the head of the table, and she always cut and distributed the bread. Hence the title of loaf-dien or server of bread, 8S

Mode.—This is made precisely in the same manner as stock No. 278, and, for ordinary purposes, will be found quite good enough. When, however, a stronger stock is desired, either put in half the quantity of water, or double that of the meat. This is a very good foundation for all white sauces. 802.—ZWETSCHEN SAUCE. (Fr.—Sauce aux Pruneaux.) (Piquant Sauce for Plain Puddings.) Ingredients.—I lb. of best French prunes, 1 glass of wine, juice of 1 lemon and part of rind, £ teaspoonful powdered cinnamon, sugar to taste. Mode.—Simmer the prunes in a saucepan with just enough water to cover them, until soft. Then remove them from the pan, take out the stones, crack them, and save the kernels; then return the fruit to the pan, add the other ingredients, simmer for 7 or 8 minutes, then strain through a coarse sieve, adding more wine or water if too thick.

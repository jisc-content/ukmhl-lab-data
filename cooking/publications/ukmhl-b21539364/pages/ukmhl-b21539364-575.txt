RECIPES FOR COOKING MUTTON & LAMB. CHAPTER XVII. 1069.-BAKED MINCED MUTTON. (Fr.—Emince de Mouton.) (Cold Meat Cookery.) Ingredients.—The remains of any joint of cold roast mutton, 1 or 2 onions, 1 bunch of savoury herbs, pepper and salt to taste, 2 blades of pounded mace or nutmeg, 2 tablespoonfuls of gravy, mashed potatoes. Mode.—Mince an onion rather fine, and fry it a light brown colour ; add the herbs and mutton, both of which should also be finely minced and well mixed; season with pepper and salt, and a little pounded mace or nutmeg, and moisten with the above proportion of gravy Put a layer of mashed potatoes at the bottom of a dish, then the mutton, and then another layer of potatoes, and bake for about half an hour. Time.—? hour. Average Cost, exclusive of the meat, 3<f. Seasonable at any time. Note.—If there should be a large quantity of meat, use two onions instead of one. 1070.-BOILED BREAST OF MUTTON AND CAPER SAUCE. (Fr— Poitrine de Mouton aux Capres.)

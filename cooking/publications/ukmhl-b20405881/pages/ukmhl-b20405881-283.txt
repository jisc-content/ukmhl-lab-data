Butter the interior of a plain round mould; cut some pieces of bread the size and thickness of a shilling; dip them in clarified butter, and lay them round the bottom of the mould. Cut also eight pieces of bread in the shape of diamonds; dip them in butter, and with them form a star in the centre of the circle. Cover the whole with a round piece of bread the size of the bottom of the mould, and of the thickness of a penny piece. Cut about thirty other pieces one inch wide and four inches in length, dip one after the other in clarified butter, standing thetn upright, half way over the other, round the mould. Then have ready prepared two dozen apples, which peel and cut into slices; put in a preserving- pan with three ounces of butter, half a pound of loaf sugar, a little lemon peel, adding a glass of sherry; place them over a sharp fire, tossing them over oc- casionally, and keep them together in a cake. When quite tender, fill in the mould ; place another layer of bread over the apples, and put the mould into a hot oven until the bread becomes well browned, when take out and turn the whole over on your dish; have a small quantity of red currant jelly in a stewpan wiih a glass of sherry; melt it over the fire, and when quite hot pour round the Charlotte. Sugar, and salamander, the top crisp, and serve.

Line the inside of a plain round mould with Savoy biscuits, cutting and placing them at the bottom, standing them upright and close together; fill with any kind of cream, place the mould in ice, and let it remain till ready to serve; turn over on dish, and remove the mould. 386. Chartreuse Cake of Variegated Fruits. Line a Charlotte mould very tastefully with various kinds of fruits, such as stoned cherries, strawberries, pieces of peaches, apricots, &c., dipping them into jelly, and forming some design at the bottom of the mould, building them in reverse rows up the sides, having the mould previously placed in ice; when well set finish as with the last. 387.

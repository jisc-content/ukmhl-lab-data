CEE AM PIE. Mrs. M. A. Green. Boil nearly one pint of new milk; take two small tablespoons of corn starch beaten with a little milk; to this add two eggs ; when the milk has boiled, stir this in slowly with one scant teacup of sugar and one-half cup of butter, two teaspoons of lemon. Cakes : Three eggs, one cup of white sugar, one and a half of flour, one teaspoon of baking powder, mix it in flour; three tablespoons of cold water; bake in two pie-pans in a quick oven ; split the cake while hot, and spread in the cream. ORANGE TART, Mrs. P. Squeeze two oranges and boil the rind tender, add half a teacupful of sugar and the juice and pulp of the fruit, an ounce of butter ; beat to a paste ; line a shal- low dish with light puff paste, and lay the paste of orange in it. MINCE PIE. Mrs. Samuel Platt. Two pounds of boiled tongue, four pounds of suet, six pounds of currants, three pounds of chopped apples, the peel and juice of two lemons, a pint of sweet wine.

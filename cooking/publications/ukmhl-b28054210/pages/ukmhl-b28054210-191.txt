One pint bread crumbs, one pint suet, four eggs, four apples minced fine, one cup currants, half-cup raisins, one cup milk, spice to taste; thicken with flour, one and a half cups sugar; put in a mould and steam or boil three and a half hours. SWEET MANCHESTER PUDDING. One pint bread crumbs, three eggs, one cup sugar, one pint milk, piece of butter size of an egg, flavour with lemon; put a layer of bread crumbs in the dish, then one of jam, and so on till full; finish with crumbs; pour the custard on the top. To be eaten with cream. SMALL AND CHEAP PLUM PUDDING. One cup suet, one cup raisins, one cup currants, one cup molasses, one egg, four cups flour, one cup milk, one teaspoon soda, one teaspoon cloves, one teaspoon cinnamon; boil or steam three hours. QUEEN OP PUDDINGS. One pint of bread crumbs, one quart milk, four eggs, (theyolks), piece of butter size of an egg, sugar to sweeten, flavour with lemon. When baked, spread with jelly, then the whites of the eggs beaten to a stiff froth, with a little sugar and lemon spread on the top ; brown slight' ly in a hot oven. To be eaten with sugar and cream.

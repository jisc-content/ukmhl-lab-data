The candy should not be boiled quite to the brittle stage. The proper degree can be ascertained if, when a small skimmer is put in and taken out, when blowing through the holes of the skim- mer, the melted sugar is forced through in feathery filaments; remove from the lire at this point and rub the syrup against the sides of the dish with an iron spoon. If you wish to make cocoanut candy, add this while soft and stir until cold. EVEETON TOFFEE. Miss Wyllie. Take one pound of treacle, the same quantity of moist sugar, half a pound of butter; put them in a saucepan large enough to allow of fast boiling over a clear fire; put in the butter first, and rub it well over the bottom of the saucepan, and add the treacle and sugar, stirring together gently with a knife; after it has boiled for about ten minutes ascertain if it is done by having ready a basin of cold water, and drop a little of the mixture into it from the point of a knife ; if it is sufficiently done, when you take it from the water it will be quite crisp.

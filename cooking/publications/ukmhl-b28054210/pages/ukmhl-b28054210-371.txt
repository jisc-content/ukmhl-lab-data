Should be well rinsed and washed in clear water to prevent the dye from mak- ing it tender after colouring. Yellow.—For five pounds of cloth dissolve one-half pound of sugar of lead in a tub of warm water and twelve ounces of bichromate of potash in another tub of cold water; soak, rinse, and wring the cloth in the lead water first, then in the other, and return from one to the other, till the right shade of colour is obtained. Orange.—Dip the yellow coloured cloth into strong lime w’ater; if it should not turn, boil it; rinse all well. Green.—Put your blue cloth in the yellow dye in the same manner as for colouring yellow.

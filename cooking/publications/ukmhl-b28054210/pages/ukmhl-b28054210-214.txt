208 THE HOME COOK BOOK. CENTENNIAL APPLE PIE. Mias L. NYinstanley. Peel one dozen and a half of good apples, take out the cores, cut them small, put into a stewpan that will just hold them with a little water, a little cinnamon, two cloves, the rind of a lemon; stew over a slow fire until quite soft, then sweeten them, pass it through a hair sieve, add the yolks of four eggs and one white, quarter of a pound of good butter, half a nutmeg, the peel of a lemon grated, and the juice of one lemon ; beat all well together; line the inside of the pie dish with good puff paste; put in the pie and bake half an hour. PUMPKIN PIE. Mrs. Carson. One quart of strained pumpkins, two quarts rich milk, one teaspoon of salt, and two of ginger cooked with the pumpkins ; six well beaten eggs, and one and a half tea- cups of sugar. MINCE PIE. Mrs. Carson. Three cups chopped cooked meat, six cups of apples chopped fine; make moist with boiled cider and sweeten with molasses or dark sugar ; spice to your taste, using cloves, cinnamon, allspice, and a very little black pepper; put currants and raisins into the pies when ready to bake.

WHITE COEN BEEAD. Mrs. E. S. Clieeseborough. One pint of meal thoroughly scalded with hard-boil- ing water; butter the size of an egg, and one well- beaten egg; add milk to make it just thin enough to flow over the pan. Have the batter an inch thick, and then bake. YPSILANTI EGG EOLLS. Miss Norris. Allow one egg for each person, two cups of milk for three eggs, four teaspoons of flour; beat whites and yolks separately, and add the eggs last. Put a very little of the mixture into a hot frying pan well greased with butter; roll as you would omelet, and put on a platter. - Send in hot For breakfast or tea. Can be eaten with sugar.

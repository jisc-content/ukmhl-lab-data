SPONGE CAKE. Mrs. J. H. Mead. One dozen eggs, ten ounces of flour, one pound fine white sugar ; break the eggs into the sugar, place on the stove and heat till milk warm, then take them off and beat till quite cold; flavour with essence of lemon, stir in the flour very lightly; butter your baking tins and sift sugar all over the bottom and sides; put in the mixture, sift sugar on the top and bake in a very moderate oven. LEMON CAKE. Mrs. J. H. Mead Half a cup of milk, half a cup of butter, two small cups of sugar, three small cups of flour, whites of four eggs, two teaspoons of cream of tartar, one teaspoon of soda; flavour with lemon; beat the butter to a cream, add eggs well whipped, then sugar; mix cream of tartar in the flour, and soda in milk.

Mrs. C. Bolford. One-half pound grated cocoanut, three-quarters of a pound of white sugar, six ounces of butter, five eggs (the whites only), two tablespoonsful rose water, one tea- spoonful nutmeg. Cream the butter and sugar, beat till very light, and add the rose water; then add the cocoanut wuth as little and light beating as possible ; finally whip in the stiffened whites of the eggs writli a lew skilful strokes, and hake at once in open shells. Eat cold, with powdered sugar sifted over them. These are very pretty and delightful pies.

Colleges of cookery have of late been established in France and Switzerland, and such institutions are sure to follow in England, America, and other countries; it is therefore no idle dream to believe that the time is not far distant when the cook shall be personally as highly appreciated and valued as the dishes he prepares. The Eany Use of the Word “ Kitchen.” The etymology of “kitchen” is rather mixed: in French it is cuisine; Italian, cucina; in Latin, coquina ; and in German Kiiche. We find that in Saxon it is cycane; in Welsh, ccquin; in Danish, Kicekhen; and in Scotch, kyshen. Hence the names in German, Saxon, Danish, and Scotch resemble more the homely English word of “ kitchen.” The word itself seems to be in English an adaptation of the Latin coquina : it denotes the place where food is cooked.

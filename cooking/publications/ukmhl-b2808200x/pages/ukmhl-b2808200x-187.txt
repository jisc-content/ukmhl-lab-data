Garnished with angelica and a piping of whipped cream, and with stewed fruit in the centre, it is called Puit de fruits aux blanches couronnes. Gateau de Nantes—a pile of rings of puff pastry, with an almond paste with chocolate in the centre, decorated with icing. Gateaux a la Conde—thin oblong or fancy shapes of puff paste, the surface covered with chopped almonds, sugar, etc. This is sometimes called Patisserie d'amandes d la Conde. d la creme—pastry of cream, flour and butter, in fancy shapes. Petits gateaux d'abricots are square pieces of puff paste folded over apricot marmalade. d la Polonaise are little folded envelopes of puff paste, garnished with red currant jelly. Eventail aux cerises—strips of puff pastry on a border of apple marmalade with stewed cherries in the centre.

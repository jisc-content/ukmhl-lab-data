Galling Curds and Whey as in Italy. Take a number of the rough coats that line the gizzards of turkeys and fowls; clean them from what they contain, rub them well with salt, and hang them to dry; when used break off some hits and put over them some boiling water ; in eight or nine hours use this liquor as you do other rennet. It makes a much more delicate curd. Put three or four pints of milk into a pan, make it a little warm, and add some of the Gallino hquor; when the curd is come, put it with a saucer into a china basin, filling it up as the whey runs from it without breaking or pressing the curd; if done only two hours before it is wanted it is very light and good. 858.

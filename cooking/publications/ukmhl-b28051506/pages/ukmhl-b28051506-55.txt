Pound all this together till quite smooth, then add half a pound of very fresh butter, a spoonful of olive-oil, and one of elder or tarragon vinegar. Taste if it requires more seasoning, and rub it all through a sieve. None of the herbs should predomi- nate ; and if not green enough, add some spinach or parsley juice. Put it on the ice, and use it for anything cold, such as salads of fish, game, etc. To make the greening of spinach or parsley, pick and wash two large handfuls of spinach or parsley; pound them in a mortar; squeeze them through a tammy, and pour all the juice into a small stew-pan ; set it on the fire, but take care it does not boU; and when it just begins to curdle, strain it through a silk sieve, and use as re- quired. 60, Salad Sauce.

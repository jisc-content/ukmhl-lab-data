Tliis, wliich is a most popular drink all over Germany, should be made of a bottle of light Ehine or Moselle wine, into which you infuse for an hour a large handful of “ woodroffe,” a sweet-smelling plant, which is common enough in England, growing in almost every wood—in German it is called “ Waldmeisteradd a couple of lumps of sugar, and ice it well. The woodroffe, after it has infused for about an hour, should be removed, and only a few fresh sprigs added to float about on the top —some should be in each glass served. It is excellent, and supposed to be peculiarly wholesome. Care must be taken to use only the leaves of the plant, which has a small white flower. On the Ehine it is usual to add some strawberry and a few black-currant leaves, and almost everywhere a slice of orange is served floating in each glass; but it is far better with nothing but the wine, sugar, and woodroffe, well iced.

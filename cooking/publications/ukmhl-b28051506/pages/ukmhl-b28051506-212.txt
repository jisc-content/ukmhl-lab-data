Beat six yolks and three whites of eggs, mix them with one spoonful of flour and four of bread-crumbs; add a little salt and nutmeg, with sugar to taste, four ounces of shred suet, and the same of picked and washed currants; mix well together. Make the batter into egg-shaped pieces with a spoon, and fry in very hot butter, or they may be baked in pattypans. 495. Puddings in Haste. Mix shred suet with grated bread-crumbs, a handful of currants cleaned or a few stoned raisins, the beat yolks of three eggs and the white of one, with a little grated lemon-peel, to a stiffish paste. Eoll this in flour, and with two spoons make it into small balls ; have ready a pan of fast-boiling water, drop them in; when done they will rise to the top. 496. Sponge-Cake Pudding. Steep sponge-cake in brandy, butter a mould and stick it over with dried cherries; put the cake in and make a custard with three whole eggs and four yolks, about haK a pint of milk, and a little sugar and nutmeg; fill up the mould and boil it an hour.

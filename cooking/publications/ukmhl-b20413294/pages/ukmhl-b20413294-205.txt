199 MANUAL FOR ARMY COOKS. ICING. To every pound of white sugar allow the whites of four eggs and one ounce of fine starch. Beat the whites to a strong froth and gradually sift in the sugar, which should be reduced to the finest possible powder, and gradually add the starch. Beat the mixture until smooth, then apply with a broad knife or spoon equally over the cakes. ICE CREAM. 1 pint milk. 1 cupful sugar. 1 scant cupful flour. 2 eggs. 1 quart cream. 1 tablespoonful flavoring extract. Boil the milk; mix together the sugar, flour, and eggs and stir into the boiling milk. Cook twenty minutes, stirring often. When cool add another cupful of sugar, the flavoring, and cream. Freeze in ice and salt. Fruit of all kinds, chocolate, coffee, etc., can be used as flavoring. When cream is expensive and can not be procured, a good substitute is obtained by using cornstarch or condensed milk and following the recipes upon the packages.

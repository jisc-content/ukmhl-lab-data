I large plump Surrey fowl, 2 oz. butter, J gill stock, \ gill cream, J gill bechamel sauce, seasoning, 2 whites of eggs, ij gills white chaud-froid sauce, meat glaze, 8 oblong flat or kite-shaped souffle cases. Remove the fillets from the fowl, free them from skin, etc., flatten them, season, and fry lightly in butter in a saute-pan. Add the stock, cover with buttered paper, and poach in the oven for 15 minutes. Take up, drain and press the fillets. With the remainder of the fowl, prepare a light souffle farce, using the cream, bechamel sauce, whites of eggs (previously whisked), and seasoning. Put this in a buttered plain mould (flat shape), and cook in a bain- marie in the oven. When done, unmould and let cool. Cut the chicken fillets into the shape of the paper caises, in a similar manner cut the chicken souffle, and sandwich them together with a little dissolved meat glaze.

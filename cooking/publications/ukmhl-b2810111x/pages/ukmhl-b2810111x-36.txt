’Recherche ’Entrees. 24 Ramequins de Poulet. (Chicken Ramakins.) 8 oz. of cooked finely minced chicken free from skin and bone, 2 table-spoonfuls of cooked spinach, a table- spoonful of grated Parmesan cheese, a hard-boiled egg, and 2 raw yolks of egg, a teaspoonful of finely chopped parsley, a few drops of lemon juice, pepper and salt, and a little stock. Chop finety the hard-boiled egg. Rub the spinach through a sieve, mix both with the chicken, parsley and cheese, put all in a stew-pan ; add very little pepper and salt ; moisten with stock, and stir till hot. Then add the raw egg yolks. Butter some small paper or china rama- kin cases, three-parts fill them with the mixture, and bake about ten minutes in a moderately heated oven. Dish up the cases, garnish with fried crisp parsley and serve. A Petites Cotelettes a TEcarlate. (Chicken and Tongue Cutlets.) Prepare a forcemeat by pounding the chicken or rabbit meat in a mortar, add the cream, panade and yolk of egg, and season to taste. Butter 6 cutlet moulds. Shape each slice of tongue precisely the same size as cutlets, place each slice on a fried crouton of bread. Chop the trimmings of tongue rather finety, put it with the forcemeat, pound again, and rub the whole through a sieve. Chop the mushrooms as finely as possible, and mix with the farce.

Care must be taken that the water does not reduce too much; in that case it must be added to. The stock is best to simmer very slowly by the fire all day, and then be strained through a sieve. The next day the fat may be removed, and the stock is ready for use. GREEN PEA SOUP. 1 Peck of Pease. Stock Parsley. Mint. Onion. Sugar. Pepper and Salt. Shell the pease, which should be young; put the shells into a stewpan with a quart of water, a teaspoonful of salt, and 1 of sugar; boil for one hour, then pulp through a cullender or sieve; return to the pot, and add 1 quart of good stock, made with shin of beef; add 1 onion and a few leaves of lettuce, chopped very finely, a very little parsley, mint, and the pease; boil till the vegetables are tender (about fifteen minutes); then season to taste, and serve. POTATO SOUP. 1 lb. Neck of Mutton. 1 Carrol; 2 lbs. Potatoes. Pepper and Salt 3 Onions. This soup is excellent made with bones, either raw or cooked; with any inferior piece of beef or mutton; with liquor in which tripe has been boiled; or with part of a pluck.

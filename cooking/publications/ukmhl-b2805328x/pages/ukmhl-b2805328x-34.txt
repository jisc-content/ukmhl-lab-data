Eggs, Buttered (or Scrambled).—Take l|oz. to 2oz. of fresh butter, three eggs, half a gill of cream, milk, or white sauce, half a small saltspoonful each of salt and white pepper. Melt about loz. of the butter in a stewpan, break the eggs, and mix them into the butter, season them, and stir the whole gently over a slack fire till they begin to set, then work in the rest of the butter; cut up into small pieces, stirring it all well together till quite amalga- mated, when you stir in the cream, milk, or sauce, and it will be ready ; pour it at once on to hot buttered toast, and serve as hot as possible.

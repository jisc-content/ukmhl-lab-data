Spring or Forehand of Pork, Cut out the bone; sprinkle salt, pepper, and sage dried, over the inside; but first warm a little butter to baste it, and then flour it: roll the pork tight, and tie it; then roast by a hanging jack. Pork Griskin Is usually very hard; the best way to prevent this is, to put it into as much cold water as will cover it. and let it boil up ; then instantly take it off', and put it into a Dutch oven; a very few minutes will do it. Remem- ber to rub butter over it, and then flour it, before you put it to the fire. Blade-hone of Pork Us taken from the bacon-hog; the less meat left on it in moderation, the better. It is to be broiled ; and when just done, pepper and salt it. Put to it a piece of but- ter, and a tea-spoonful of mustard; and serve it covered, quickly. This is a Somersetshire dish.

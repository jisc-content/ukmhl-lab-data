Gravy for a Fowl when there is no Meat to mahe it of. Wash the feet nicely, and cut them and the neck small; simmer them with a little bread browned, a slice of onion, a bit of parsley and thyme, some pepper and salt, and the liver and gizzard, in a quarter of a pint of water till half-wasted. Take out the liver, bruise it, and strain the liquor to it. Then thicken it with flour aud butter, and add a tea-spoonful of mushroom-ket- chup, and it will be very good. Veal Gravy.

Lay over the bottom of a stew-pan as much lean veal as will cover it an inch thick; then cover the veal with thin slices of undressed gammon, two or three two or three bay-leaves, some sweet herbs, two blades of mace, and three cloves. When the meat is of a tine brown, fill the pan with good beef-broth, boil and skim it, then simmer an hour; add a little water mixed with as much flour as will make it properly thick : boil it half an hour, and strain it.

298 DOMESTIC COOKERY. EXCELLENT ORANGE CREAM. Boil the rind of a Seville orange very tender; beat it fine in a mortar; put to it a spoonful of the best brandy, the juice of a Seville orange, four ounces of loaf sugar, and the yolks of four eggs; beat all together for ten minutes; then, by gentle degrees, pour in a pint of boiling cream ; beat till cold ; put into custard cups set into a deep dish of boiling water, and let them stand till cold again. Put at the top small strips of orange-paring cut thin, or preserved chips. RASPBERRY CREAM. Mash the fruit gently, and let them drain ; then sprinkle a little sugar over, and that will produce more juice ; then put the juice to some cream, and sweeten it; after which, if you choose to lower it with some milk, it will not curdle; which it would, if put to the milk before the cream ; but it is best made of raspberry jelly, instead of jam, when the fresh fruit cannot be obtained.

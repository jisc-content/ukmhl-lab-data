Take half a spoonful of coriander seeds, and four cloves, and bruise them in a mortar. Put three quar- ters of a pint of good gravy, and a quarter of a pint of essence of ham, into a stewpan. Peel half a lemon, and cut it into very thin slices, and put it in with the coriander seeds and cloves. Let them boil up, then put in three cloves of garlic whole, a head of celery sliced, two bay-leaves, and a little basil. Let these boil till there is but half the quantity left. Then put in a glass of white wine, strain it off, and if not thick enough, put in a piece of butter rolled in flour. It is very good with roasted fowls, and some like it with butcher’s meat. LIVER SAUCE. Chop boiled liver of rabbits or fowls, and do it as directed for lemon-sauce, page 188, with a very little pepper and salt, and some parsley. EGG SAUCE.

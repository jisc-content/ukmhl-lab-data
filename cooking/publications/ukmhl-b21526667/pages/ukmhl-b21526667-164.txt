Or for steaks, pies, or broth. TO ROLL LOIN OF MUTTON. Hang the mutton till tender; bone it3 and lay a seasoning of pepper, allspice, mace, nutmeg, and a few cloves, all in fine powder, over it. Next day prepare a stuffing as for hare ; beat the meat, and cover it with the stuffing ; roll it up tight, and tie it. Half bake it in a slow oven ; let it grow cold; take off the fat, and put the gravy into a stew-pan ; flour the meat, and put it in likewise; stew it till almost ready 3 and add a glass of port wine, some ketchup, an anchovy, and a little lemon-pickle, half an hour before serving j serve it in the gravy, and with jelly-sauce. A few fresh mushrooms are a great improvement 3 but if to eat like hare do not use these, nor the lemon-pickle. MUTTON UAM. Choose a fine-grained leg of wether-mutton, of twelve or fourteen pounds weight j let it be cut ham- shape, and hang two days. Then put into a stew-pan half a pound of bay-salt, the same of common salt, two ounces of saltpetre, and half a pound of coarse sugar, all in powder ; mix and make it quite hot; then rub it well into the ham. Let it be turned in the liquor every day; at the end of four days put two ounces more of common salt 3 in twelve days take it out, dry it, and hang it up in wood-smoke a week.

Green peas, sliced cucumber, or lettuce, may be added and served altogether, when done, and the fat skimmed off. PORK, &c. Bacon-hogs, and porkers, are differently cut up. Hogs are kept to a large size; the chine (or back- bone) is cut down on each side, the whole length, and is a prime part either boiled or roasted. The sides of the hog are made into bacon, and the inside is cut out with very little meat to the bone. On each side there is a large spare-rib 3 which is usually divided into two, one sweet-bone and a blade-bone. The bacon is the whole outside : and contains a fore- leg, and a ham ; which last is the hind-leg, but if left with the bacon is called a gammon. There are also griskins. Hogs’ lard is the inner fat of the bacon-hog. Pickled pork is made of the flesh of the hog, as well as bacon. Porkers are not so old as hogs 3 their flesh is whiter and less rich, but it is not so tender. It is divided into four quarters. The fore-quarter has the spring or fore-leg, the fore-loin or neck, the spare-rib and gris- kin. The hind has the leg and the loin.

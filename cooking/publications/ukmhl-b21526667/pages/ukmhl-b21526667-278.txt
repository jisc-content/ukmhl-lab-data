DOMESTIC COOKERY. <230 Another, for the Family.—Put into a very deep pan half a pound of rice, washed and picked ; two ounces of butter, four ounces of sugar, a few allspice pounded, and two quarts of milk. Less butter will do, or some suet. Bake in a slow oven. A GEORGE PUDDING. Boil very tender a handful of whole rice in a small quantity of milk, with a large piece of lemon-peel. Let it drain; then mix with it a dozen of good-sized apples, boiled to pulp as dry as possible ; add a glass of white wine, the yolks of five eggs, two ounces of orange and citron cut thin; make it pretty sweet. Line a mould or basin with a very good paste ; beat the five whites of the eggs to a very strong froth, and mix with the other ingredients ; fill the mould, and bake it of a fine brown colour. Serve it with the bottom upward with the following sauce: two glasses of wine, a spoonful of sugar, the yolk of two eggs, and a bit of butter as large as a walnut; simmer without boiling, and pour to and from the saucepan, till of a proper thickness; and put in the dish. lady Sunderland’s puddings.

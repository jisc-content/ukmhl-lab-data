Take a pound and a half of very fresh crimped skate, wash it well in cold water and salt, divide it in convenient pieces. Put in a stewpan enough water to cover it, say three pints; add a wine-glassful of French vinegar, a sliced onion, a sprig or two of parsley, a dessert-spoonful of salt, and a salt-spoonful of pepper. Bring up to boiling-point over the gas turned three-quarter full on. Put in the fish, and when the water boils again, skim, reduce the gas to half full on, and simmer for six or eight minutes, according to thickness. When done it looks quite white and opaque. Drain it, put it on a hot dish, and pour black butter over it. Serve hot.

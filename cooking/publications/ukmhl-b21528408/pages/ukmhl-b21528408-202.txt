Melt three ounces of butter in a stewpan over the gas turned three-quarter full on. Break six eggs separately, making sure that each egg is quite sweet. Put them in the stewpan, add a quarter of a pint of milk or, if available, the same quantity of cream, a salt-spoonful of salt, and a pinch of pepper. Whisk very briskly over the gas until the eggs begin to set. Turn the gas down to half full on, and continue whisk- ing. Add three ounces of cooked ham or tongue cut in thin slices and divided in half-inch squares. Stir well, and turn out on a hot dish (a hot water dish, if possible). Ornament with fried croutons prettily shaped, and serve at once. These eggs can be prepared also with cooked peas, asparagus tops, purees of lettuce, celery, etc., or simply with a dessert- spoonful of chopped herbs (parsley, chervil, tarragon, and a very little chive or shallot).

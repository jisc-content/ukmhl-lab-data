Hunter’s Gingerbread is a handy ginger biscuit for luncheon. Put into a bowl a pound and a half of flour, three ounces of sugar, one ounce of minced candied peel, a table-spoonful of powdered ginger, with a few drops of essence of lemon, and mix together with a pound of treacle slightly warmed with three ounces of butter. Make it into a smooth paste. Roll it out on your pastry slab, cut it into strips about three inches long and one broad, and bake on a buttered tin. Store these gingerbreads in a tin box. If the same paste be rolled a quarter of an inch thick, stamped out in small ovals, and baked till crisp, you will have “ Gingerbread nuts.”

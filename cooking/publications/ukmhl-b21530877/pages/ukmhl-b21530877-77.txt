EGGS, &C. 63 124. Minced Eggs and Parsley. Boil some eggs rather hard, peel and chop them small with some boiled parsley, mix them up with a little good melted butter and a small portion of salt* Serve with sippets round the dish. 125. To preserve Eggs for eating in the Shell. Boil any number of fresh eggs for the space of one minute and a half, and when wanted for use, after any length of time, let them be reboiled for the same space of time as at the first. 126. To preserve Eggs for the Winter. Put them in a deep earthen pot in lime water, with a large handful of salt in it; or they may be covered with salt only ; they keep very well. 127. To preserve Eggs for Winter use. Fill a box with eggs, with a layer of bran between each layer of eggs ; when quite full, lock it, or fasten it so that it will not give way; then tie a string round it, and hang it up in a cool, dry place, turning it twice a week. 128. To preserve Eggs for Winter use. Dissolve eight ounces of chlorate of lime in a gallon of water, then put in the eggs. They may also oe preserved many months by merely altering their posi- tion daily in order to prevent the yolk settling and sticking to the shell.

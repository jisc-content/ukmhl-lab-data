Mix some good oatmeal with a little salt and warm water, and a spoonful or two of yeast, beat till it is quite smooth and rather a thick batter, cover and let it stand to rise, then bake on a hot bake-stone, in the game manner as crumpets, but not quite so quick. Mix some oatmeal and water well-together, about the same consistence as common dough, then roll it out into cakes as thin as possible; bake them on a stone or iron-plate of a moderate heat, over the fire ; when baked on both sides, set them on an edge before the fire till perfectly dry. This bread will continue good many weeks, if kept in a dry place. It is very good with cheese or but- ter, and may be toasted, if preferred. 655. Potato Cakes. Boil and mash some good mealy potatoes with a little butter and salt, add a little good yeast and a few spoonsful of milk, with as much flour as will make it the consistence of dough ; roll it into cakes, let them rise two hours, then bake in a moderate oven. The potatoes should be well-dried after being boiled. 656. Ferment for Bread, used by the Inhabitants of Long Island, in the State of New York.

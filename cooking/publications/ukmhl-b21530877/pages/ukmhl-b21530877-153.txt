Common Rice Pudding. Wash and pick half a pound of rice very clean, put it in a dish with two quarts of milk, and sugar to the taste ; bake it in a moderate oven. It may be made richer by adding butter, eggs, and powdered cinna- mon, or grated nutmeg, but it is very good without. 414. Butch Rice Pudding. Soak four ounces of rice in warm water half an hour, drain and put it into a stewpan with half a pint of milk and half a stick of cinnamon ; simmer till the rice is tender. When cold, add four eggs well beaten, two ounces of butter melted in a tea-cupful of cream, three ounces of sugar, a quarter of a nutmeg grated, and some grated lemon-peel; put a light puff paste into a mould or dish, and bake it in a quick oven. 415.

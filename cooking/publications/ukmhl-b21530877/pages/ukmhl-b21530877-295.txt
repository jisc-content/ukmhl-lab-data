Onions. In the month of September choose the smallest white round onions, scald them to take off the brown skin, have ready a very clean stewpan of boiling milk and water, throw in as many onions as will cover the top ; as soon as they look clear on the outside, take them up as quick as possible with a slice, and lay them on a cloth; cover them close with another, and scald some more, and so on. Let them lie to be cold, then put them in a jar, or wide-mouthed glass bottles, and pour over them the best vinegar, just hot, but not boiling. When cold, cover them. 869. Onions. Peel some small onions, and throw them as you peel them into salt and water, changing the brine once a day for three days together, then set them over the fire in milk and water till near boiling; drain and dry them, then pour on them the following pickle, boiled and stood till cold again ; distilled vinegar, salt, mace, white pepper, and a bay-leaf or two. They will not look white with any other vinegar. 870. Lemons.

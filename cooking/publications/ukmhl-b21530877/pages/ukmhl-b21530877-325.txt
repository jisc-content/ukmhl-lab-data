In two days close it up, and in three weeks bottle it. 956. Lemonade. Pare five lemons very thin, and put to the rinds half a pound of loaf-sugar, and a quart of boiling water ; cover it close, and let it stand till cold; then add to the juice of the lemon nearly half a pint of cowslip or other pale sherbet; pour in a pint of boiling skimmed milk to break it; then run it through a jelly-bag till fine. If, while running, you find it is not acid enough, put a wine-glass of clear lemon- juice or vinegar into the jelly-bag. 957. Lemonade. Squeeze the juice of as many lemons as will flavour two quarts of spring water pleasantly, put in a little of the rind pared very thin, loaf sugar to the taste, and the whites of three eggs beaten to a froth; set it over a clear fire, let it boil a few minutes, strain it I through a jelly-bag, and when cold it will be fit for use.

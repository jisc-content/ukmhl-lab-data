Pare them the size of thick corks, then cut them in slices half an inch thick, put them in a stewpan with skinned green onions and parsley chopped, a little pepper, salt, and butter; moisten with vegetable broth, or mushroom liquor, shake or stir them on the fire till the herbs are done. 189. To boil new Potatoes. When first gathered, the skin is best rubbed off with salt and a coarse cloth, then wash them very clean, and put them into salt and water some time before they are boiled ; when you put them in the pan, put as much water as will barely cover them, adding a little salt; boil them very slow till nearly done, then pour the water from them very dry; cover them close with a clean cloth, and then the pan-cover, set them where they will keep hot, but be careful they do not burn in the pan, as it quite spoils the flavour of the potatoes. .Dish them up with some bits of butter on the top. 190. To mash Potatoes. Pare, and boil them slowly and carefully, dry them well, and mash them very fine, adding a little salt and butter to them ; then add a little hot new milk, beat them well with the masher, and they will be Teady for the table. 191.

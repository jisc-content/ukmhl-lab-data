To three quarts of water take half a pint of peas, two good-sized carrots, three turnips cut in small pieces, and an onion sliced, a crust of bread, an ounce of Scotch barley, some leaves and stalks of celery; let it boil till reduced to two quarts, then strain it through a sieve, and add a little chopped celery, parsley, and sweet leek; pepper and salt to the taste. A little butter may be added, if preferred, but it is very good without. 61. Broth. Take two quarts of water, four ounces of butter, a stale crust of bread, a little barley or rice, boil them one hour, adding a little more water, two or three carrots, rasp and boil them well, with thyme, leeks, and celery, toast your bread, and pour it on. A little catsup may be added. 62. Vegetable Broth, for making Soups, fyc. Half fill a pan with whatever vegetables are most ap- proved, and some seasoning herbs, add water nearly to fill the pan, and let it boil till the vegetables are tender, then strain it off, and keep it for use as wanted. It will not keep more than two days, and the fresher it is used the better. It is much used by the French in making soups. 63. Brewis.

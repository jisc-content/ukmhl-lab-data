Take a pound of potatoes, after they are boiled and peeled, and beat them in a marble mortar, with a quarter of a pound of butter. Boil an ounce of lemon-peel, and beat it in the mortar by itself; put to it the lemon-juice, and mix it with the potatoes, add to them six yolks of eggs and four whites, with sugar to the taste. Put it into a dish with a crust round the edge, and bake it in a slow oven. 405. Potato Pudding. To half a pound of boiled potatoes, beaten in a marble mortar with two ounces of butter, add a quarter of a pint of cream, the rind of a lemon grated, and the juice strained in, sugar to the taste, two ounces of almonds beaten with orange-flower water, some candied orange- peel cut thin, and the yolks of six eggs well beaten, with a little salt. Bake this in a dish with a puff crust round the edge of it for an hour in a moderate oven. Sift powdered sugar over before it is sent to table. 406.

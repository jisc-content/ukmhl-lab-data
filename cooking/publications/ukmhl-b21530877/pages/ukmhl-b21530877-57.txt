Put a quart of water on the fire with as much dry crust of bread cut to pieces, as the top of a roll, (the drier the better,) and a bit of butter. Boil and beat it with a spoon, keep it boiling till the bread and water are mixed; season it with salt. It is very good for a weak stomach. Take two quarts of new milk, some cinnamon, half a dozen bitter almonds, a little salt, and a very little sugar, boil them together on a moderate fire; blanch half a pound of sweet almonds, beat them to a paste in a marble mortar, mix a little milk with them by degrees, then put them in a saucepan on the fire with a little grated lemon-peel and a little lemon-juice, then pass it through a coarse sieve and mix it with the milk in the soup-pan and let it boil up. Cut some slices of French roll and dry them before the fire, soak them a little in the milk, lay them at the bottom of the tureen, and pour in the soup. Boil a pint of milk, with a little salt, and (if ap- proved) sugar; lay some sliced bread in a dish ; pour over it a part of your milk to soak it, and keep it hot, taking care that it do not burn. Beat up the yolks of five eggs, and add them to the remainder of the milk just when you are going to serve it up.

Turnip Soup. Pare six or seven turnips, put them into a gallon of water with some white pepper, an onion with a few cloves stuck in it, a bunch of sweet herbs, some mace, and a large crust of bread; let them boil an hour and a half; then strain it through a sieve; take three heads of celery cut into small pieces, put them in with two raw turnips, and two young carrots cut in pieces, cover them close, and let them stew; cut some tur- nips and carrots in dice, flour and fry them brown in butter, with two large onions cut thin, put them into the soup with an ounce of vermicelli, let all stew till the celery is tender and the soup good: add salt to the taste. 38.

Boil or broil with stuffing as under, having salted them a dag. To dig Haddock. Choose them of two or three pounds weight: take out the gills, eyes, and entrails, and remove the blood from the backbone. Wipe them dry, and put some salt into the bodies and eyes. Lay them on a board fora night; then hang them up in a dry place, and after three or four days they will be tit to eat; skin and rub them with egg; and strew crumbs over them. Lay them be- fore the fire, and baste with butter until brown enough. Serve with egg-sauce. Whitings, if large are excellent this way; and it will prove an accommodation in the country where there is no regular supply of fish.

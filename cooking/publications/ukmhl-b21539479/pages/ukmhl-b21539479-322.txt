294 KING EDWARD'S COOKERY BOOK Strawberry Syrup. 1| lb. strawberries. I 2 gills water. 1 lb. 2 oz. sugar. | Oil. Boil sugar and water to 240° F, Crush the strawberries in a mortar, put them into the syrup and let it heat again; it must not boil. Put into a basin and leave all night; next day pass through a hair sieve or tammy. Put into bottles, pour a little oil over, cork, and seal with sealing-wax. N.B.—The fruit boiled up with mere syrup makes a second quality.

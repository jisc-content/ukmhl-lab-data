Raspings and parsley. Panada. 1 oz. butter. 1 oz. flour. 1 gill milk or chicken stock. Remove the meat from the chicken and pass through a mincing machine; make the panada (seep. 159); pound the chicken with the panada, butter, three yolks and one white of egg; rub through a hair sieve. Add the cream, seasoning, and lemon-juice, and the remaining whites beaten to a stiff froth. Mix lightly, turn into a prepared tin (1^ pints), steam for forty minutes, turn out and coat with white sauce, using half stock (made from the chicken bones) and half milk. Decorate with chopped parsley and raspings. Make the panada (see p. 159), add the yolks of eggs, the meat and seasoning; stir in lightly the whites beaten to a stiff froth, put into well-buttered ramakin cases, bake in a quick oven for fifteen minutes.

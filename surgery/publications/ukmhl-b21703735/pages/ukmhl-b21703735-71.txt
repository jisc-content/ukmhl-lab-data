Artificial hyperemia is not a panacea; but the successes already obtained with the method in these cases are such as should make it incumbent upon every thinking otologist to investigate and test for himself the truth of the claims made for it. Certainly, it must be welcome news to learn that a means has been discovered, that will aid him in his efforts at combating a disease that is so frequently followed by some of the most dreaded sequelae, i.e., sinus phlebitis, brain abscess and meningitis. We must not lose sight of the well-known axiom that prophy- laxis—properly directed work, in order to prevent the appear- ance or development of a threatening disease—is the highest aim of medical science.

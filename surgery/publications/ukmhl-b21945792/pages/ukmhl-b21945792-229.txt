217 REMEDY OF STRICTURES. of three practitioners in his neighbourhood, " One has an okl bougie made a.d. 1700 ; another, two or three old-fashioned catheters; and the third, some gum elastic catheters totally unfitted for the purpose." Such being the case, I think prudence requires that every patient who has had a stric- ture divided should learn to introduce bougies ; since by doing so at intervals of a week or fort- night he will be perfectly secure from future trouble.

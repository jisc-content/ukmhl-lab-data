Special Forms of Bandage: The Spica. This is simply a figure-of-eight, and the term " spica" refers to the cross-like arrangement seen at the intersections of the figure-of- eight, which bear a certain resemblance to the arrangement of glumes in a spikelet of wheat. (From the Lat. spica, an ear or spike of corn). The terms ascending and descending spica are employed according to the manner in which the successive turns mount or descend upon the limb. In order to cover in large prominences, such as the heel, the bent knee, and elbow, a modification of the figure-of-eight, known as the " Divergent Spica," is made use of.

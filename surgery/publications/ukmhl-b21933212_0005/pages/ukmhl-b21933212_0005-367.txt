335 EPISPADIAS The method may be advantageously modified by sliding a flap in from one side, with its raw surface looking downwards, instead of using the superimposed flaps mentioned above. The deeper surface of this flap, which should be of large size, naturally gramdates and contracts, but if it is made large enough in the first instance, no constriction of any consequence is produced ; indeed, any slight narrowing that may occur is probably beneficial to the patient in allowing him to obtain some sort of control over the urine and in preventing the constant dribbling which is such a distressing feature of ectopia vesicae.

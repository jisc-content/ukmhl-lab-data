No special rules need be laid down for treatment, but it should be remembered that in many cases the condition is best met by the careful excision of the whole coccyx, which will remove all diseased tissue, and leave the patient with less pain and general suffering, either on sitting down or defecation, than even if the disease were brought to a standstill by less radical measures. 'For in those cases in which the active disease has been arrested by the ordinary modes of treatment, a very painful and harassing state of things remains, which has often ultimately to be met by excision.

Can the medical man not be allowed to dress as he pleases, so long as he does not put on either ridiculous or offensive habili- ments ? Why should he not be able to pre- scribe as well in a blue coat as in a black one? If I were ill, I should probably hardly observe what dress my physician was in, but if I were to notice at all, I rather think I would prefer a light dress. There is sombreness enough about a sick-room without the physician's adding to it by his sombre clothing. And unless my physician actually made himself a dandy, (for then I might have doubts as to the capability of his mind,) I would not find fault with him.

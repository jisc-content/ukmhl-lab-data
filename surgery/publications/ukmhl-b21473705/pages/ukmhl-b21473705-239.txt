AN ESSAY, ETC. Few subjects have furnished literary men with a greater variety of fanciful theories^ and specu- lative systems^ than the origin and personal characteristics of nations: in discussing which many able writers have employed far more ingenuity than sound argument; wasting erudi- tion in support of mere hypothesis. Yet from PiNKERTON, the founder of the present system^ down to Dr. Macculloch_, the last writer of distinction, who has given it his support, they all appear, however differing upon collateral points, to have adopted one fixed and common principle; the fallaciousness of which it is the object of the present Essay to expose.

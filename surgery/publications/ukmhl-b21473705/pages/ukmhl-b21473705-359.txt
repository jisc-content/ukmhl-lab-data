Whilst, at the same time, there may be generally observed a greater shrewdness of look, and intelligence of eye, than amongst a rustic population, where the mental faculties have not received an equal degree of cultivation. Among the peasantry, I have noticed that those of the wine countries are spare, sallow, and ill favoured: which must be attributed to the scantiness of provisions, especially of animal food. The people of rich agricultural districts are robust and ruddy, yet have a clownish and awkward appearance. But those of pastoral countries, where the supply of food is sufficient, are more symmetrically formed, of better car- riage, and more intelligent expression, and in every respect a superior race to the mere tillers of the soil.

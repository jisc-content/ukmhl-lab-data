Appearances are occasion- ally met with corresponding to those of a superficial burn pro- duced by heat. The chief difference from ordinary burns is the extreme slowness with which healing takes place Localised paralysis of groups of muscles, or even of a whole limb, may follow any degree of lightning-stroke. Treatment is mainly directed towards combating the shock, the surface lesions being treated on the same lines as ordinary burns.

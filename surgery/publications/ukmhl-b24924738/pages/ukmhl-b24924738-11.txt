IN the present Work, an attempt is made to lay before the^reader a sketch of the principal subjects of Surgery. However much the execution of the present under- taking may stand in need of indulgence, I do not conceive, that the mere attempt re- quires any apology. Every student must long have felt the want of a publication, in which the symptoms, and most approved treatment, of surgical diseases, are accurately explained in as few words as possible. Every practitioner must acknowledge, that a work of this kind, if executed with only moderate success, is one, from which great utility will be derived.

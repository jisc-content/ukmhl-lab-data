His mind was not at all affected; neither were the bodily powers in the least disturbed; no bad symptoms of any kind followed the injury; the wound healed most favourably by adhe- sion^ and he was soon discharged from the hospital. About a year afterwards^ while I was at the house of a lady in the city, whom I was attending, a man walked into the room, and said to me, How do you do?” Not re- collecting him, I looked at him with some surprise, when he informed me that he was the man whom I bad seen before in St. Tho- mas’s Hospital, with a wound in the head, and through which he had lost some of his brain. He stated, that he had been quite well ever since; he had a depression at the pait where he received the wound; was not subject to fits; and it was certain his mind had not sustained any decided injury from the acci- dent, for he was at the time I saw him con- ductor of an extensive business.

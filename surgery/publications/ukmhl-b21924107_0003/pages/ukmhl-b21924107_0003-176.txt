But chronic abscesses are slower in their march: take, for instance, the psoas abscess to which we have alluded: it is often six months before matter makes its appearance in that complaint. If a person applies with a psoas abscess, and you ask how long he has had pain in his loins, he will tell you for four, five, or six months. There are varieties in the irritability of different constitutions; but when you see a man with swelling in the groin, which, if he coughs, rebounds under your hand, and has a fluctuating feel, and who, for four or five months, has had pain in his loins, you will say, that he has psoas abscess; so these are the criteria by which you are to know it. Chronic abscesses sometimes occur in the female breast.

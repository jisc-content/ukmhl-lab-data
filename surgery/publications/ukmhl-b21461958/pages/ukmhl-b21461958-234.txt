Precepts continued. 224 If the lower end of the artery cannot be found at the time, the upper only having bled, a gentle compression maintained upon the track of the lower may prevent mischief; but if dark-coloured blood should flow from the wound, which may be expected to come from the lower end of the artery, and compression does not suffice to suppress the haemorrhage, the bleeding end of the vessel must be exposed, and secured near to its extremity.

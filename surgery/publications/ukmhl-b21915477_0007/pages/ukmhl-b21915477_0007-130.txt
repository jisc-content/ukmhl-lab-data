WOUNDS AND WOUND-TREATMENT. 74 these cases. But even here, antiseptic irrigation may be dispensed with. The cleansing of such wounds, including ample exposure with the knife, the removal of tissues which are much contused and lacer- ated, and of gross impurities, by irrigation, scraping, or exc'sion, is a mechanical procedure. What we cannot remove in this manner we will not be likely to destroy with antiseptic irrigation, especially when the impurities, as is often the case, are ground into the tissues. These wounds will also, in all probability, heal as well under thorough aseptic cleansing and irrigation, followed by packing with iodoform-gauze, as when treated with strong germicidal solutions.

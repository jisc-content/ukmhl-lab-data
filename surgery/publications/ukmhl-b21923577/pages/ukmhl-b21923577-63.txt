Disease may be considered in one or other of these views only, and treatises have been written upon parts of the subject, under especial names. The consideration of nomenclature constitutes nosology; that of symptoms, symptomatology ; of causes, (Etiology, &c. j and there have been treatises on the art of diagnosis. There is no doubt that useful information may be given in this way; but the several divisions of the science of medicine elucidate each other so materially, that the subject will be most advantageously treated as a whole.

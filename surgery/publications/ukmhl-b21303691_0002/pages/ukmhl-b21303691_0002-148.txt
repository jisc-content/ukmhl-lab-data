In cases where we have to do with congenital fistulae, nothing short of complete removal of the whole track by dissection will effectually cure the trouble. Attempts at destroying the mucous lining of the track by scraping, or by means of caustics, almost invariably fail, probably from the fact that islets of epithelium escape the action of the remedies.

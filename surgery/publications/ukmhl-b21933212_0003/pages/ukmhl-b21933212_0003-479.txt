The amount of urea also often shows a great increase. These facts may be useful in the case of a person found unconscious. In doubtful cases of poisoning it may be useful to test the cerebro- spinal fluid for the suspected poison, which may at times be detected in this situation. Thus, in a person suffering from carbolic acid poisoning, in whom there was no other clue to the nature of the poison (the patient being comatose), carbolic acid was present in the fluid in considerable amount.

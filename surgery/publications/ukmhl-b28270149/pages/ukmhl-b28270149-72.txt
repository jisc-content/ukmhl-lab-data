CHAP. IV. OF PILES. Piles, or hemorrhoids, are soft tumours, which form either within the rectum or about the anus. In the first case they are covered with the mucous membrane of the intestine, and are termed inward piles. In the second case they are covered entirely or in part with the common integument, and are termed outward piles. Inward piles, and with some exceptions outward piles, arise from congestion of the hemorrhoidal veins. The same causes therefore lead to the production of both kinds.

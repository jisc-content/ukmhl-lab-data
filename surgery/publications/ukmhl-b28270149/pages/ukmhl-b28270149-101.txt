It was evident that no operation would be of service; and that as the tenderness and pain in the part, though still considerable, were progressively lessening, no treatment would be necessary beyond the use of a poultice and occasional doses of opening medicine, with abstinence from wine and heating food. The tumour I concluded to be an outward pile, no part of which would on its diminution be drawn or forced within the sphincter. The result proved that this opinion was right: the tumour only shrunk.

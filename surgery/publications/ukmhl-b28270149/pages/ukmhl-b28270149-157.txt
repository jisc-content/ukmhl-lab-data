She died six months afterwards; and, on examining the spinal chord, it was found for the length of two inches in a state of softening at its lumbar portion. This is one of a numerous class of cases in which constipation of the bowels is found to depend upon spinal irritation, and in which the abdominal symptoms are the first which show themselves after the inva- sion of the disease. The reaction of the one organ upon the other is very remark- able. An amendment of the state of the bowels is an evidence of a temporary alle- viation of the spinal disease. The neglect of relieving the bowels by medicine is fol- lowed by an aggravation of the nervous symptoms.

In such cases, there is still room for skilful palliative treatment, through which the patient’s periods of suffering may be rendered less frequent, and the suffering less severe. By the judicious use of ape- rient medicines, of astringent and narcotic applications, of the rectum bougie, ac-* cording to the circumstances of the case, even by directions as to the shape of the seat of a water-closet, very considerable additions may be made to the patient’s comfort.

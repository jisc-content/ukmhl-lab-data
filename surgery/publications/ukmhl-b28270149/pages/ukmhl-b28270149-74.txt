To convey an adequate idea of the nature of these varieties, and of the methods to be followed in their treatment, it is necessary to consider separately the two classes into which they are divided. SECTION 1. Of Inward Piles. Inward piles vary from the size of a pea to that of a large walnut. They are some- times single, at other times there are seve- ral. Sometimes they grow immediately within the sphincter; at other times at some distance above it. They are sometimes attached by a narrow pedicle, at other times they have a broad or elongated base.

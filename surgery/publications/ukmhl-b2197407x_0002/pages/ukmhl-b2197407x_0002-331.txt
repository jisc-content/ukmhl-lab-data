The diagnosis from a localized empyema may thus sometimes be a matter of difficulty. lu some cases the rib cartilage is necrosed, as the result of a tuberculous deposit beneath the perichondrium. The treatment consists in freely opening the abscesses and sinuses, and scraping or gouging the carious bone. In some cases it may be advisable to excise completely the affected portion. In these operations great care must be taken not to damage the plem-a.

WOUNDS Fig. 34.—Anterior View of the Body Illustrating the Directions in WHICH Incisions should be made.—The correct incisions are indicated by the single dark lines. In them the lips of the wound tend to fall together, while those of the faulty incisions—denoted by ellipses—tend to gape. (This and the following figure are reproduced from Kocher's Operative Surgery, and English edition 1903.)

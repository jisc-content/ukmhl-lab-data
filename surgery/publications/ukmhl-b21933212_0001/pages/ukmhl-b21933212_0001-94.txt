In the case of diabetes, the patient must be put upon an anti-diabetic diet and codeine (see p. 80). Operations are not satisfactory in a diabetic patient, and this is the one form of ulcer in which skin-grafting cannot be recommended. If the ulcer be extensive and tend to become gangrenous, the case should be treated as one of diabetic gangrene. In less severe cases the ulcer should be allowed to heal if it will, and Unna's bandage should be employed afterwards, with the object of preventing a recurrence.

The opening in the skin has a constant tendency to close and leave a narrow sinus leading into a comparatively large cavity. When the cavity is of such a size or in such a situation that it is evident that a long time must elapse before it can fill up, it is a good plan to remove freely the skin around the margin of the opening at the time of the operation ; any overhanging portion must always be cut away, as it would only become inverted and delay healing. Inversion of the edges of the skin sometimes occurs during healing, and the inverted portion may have to be excised.

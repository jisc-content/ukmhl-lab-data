(+ Amenorrhoeae, dyspepsiae, plicae, sypbilidis, scro- fulae, scorbuti, dystociae, diarrhoeae symptoraa.) XXI. HECTICA. Hectic fever. A frequent weak pulse, with night sweats or liquid evacu- ations, flushings of the face or extremities, and a sediment in the urine. The fever generally increases in the afternoon, but some- times is most observable in the morning. A hectic fever, though perhaps always occasioned by some other disease, may yet require the greatest share of our attention, and may be most conveniently considered as idiopathic. 1. H. dehiVium. Without emaciation. 2. H. tabes. With great emaciation, but no cough. 3. H. jjethisis. With cough or pain in the chest. Hectica. Stahl de hectica febre. Hall. 1705. Schulze de febre lenta.

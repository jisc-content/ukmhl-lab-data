The malignant polypus, on the contrary, is always at- tached to the mucous membrane, and also to the subjacent parts, by means of a broad base; and its form and structure vary according to the particular action which it has assumed. Most frequently it is encephaloid, of an irregular form, and often present- ing a cauliflower appearance, its surface being studded with numerous excrescences, of medullary consistence and colour.

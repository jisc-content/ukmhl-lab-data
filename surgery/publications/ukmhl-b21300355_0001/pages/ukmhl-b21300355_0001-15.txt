It does not follow, that the older the Surgeon is, the more experienced and trustworthy he must be. The greatest number of well-assorted facts on a particular subject constitutes expe- rience, whether these facts have been culled in five years or in fifty. It is only from experience, directed and aided by previous study, that accuracy of diag- nosis and celerity of decision can be acquired. Besides knowing in what manner to proceed, the Surgeon must know well wherefore he acts, and also the precise time at which he should interfere.

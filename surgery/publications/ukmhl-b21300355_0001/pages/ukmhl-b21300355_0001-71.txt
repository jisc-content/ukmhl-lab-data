The glands not unfrequently become enlarged during the exhibition of this medicine ; and such an occurrence must be watched attentively. In illustration of the good effects of stimuli in certain cases, it may be mentioned that the cavities of abscesses are often speedily effaced by granulations, and that obstinate sores frequently contract and cicatrize, after the occurrence of a febrile attack, though they had previously shown no dispo- sition to heal. In glandular swellings, Deobstruents, as they have been called, are used; and with this view, mercury is often had recourse to ; this medicine, however, instead of producing a salutary effect, tends still farther to impair the constitution. Iodine, exhibited both externally and internally, is of great use when the swellings have become stationary, or are inclined to subside. When the tumours are irritable, foment- ations may be employed, and advantage taken of sea- bathing, warm or cold.

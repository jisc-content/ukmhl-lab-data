Reading will be found extremely useful, when it is select; but I Reading, would not recommend works to young students which lay down systems for their guidance ; such a plan of reading is bad. It is better to read the detached works on particular diseases. To the pupils generally, but more particularly to those who are allowed to pass in this metropolis but one or two seasons in attending the different hospitals, I strongly recommend punctuality in their attend- ance at lectures, and at the dissections and hospital practice. In St. Thomas’s Hospital alone, you have an opportunity of seeing above 800 in-door, and a vast number of out-door patients, whose cases are equally useful as interesting to students. I caution pupils from speaking unguardedly before the patients ; it can do no good to let them know what is intended for their cure, which it veiy often prevents.

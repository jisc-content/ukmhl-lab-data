90 By the last paragraph in the memorandum of the Army Medical Board, the provisions of which are in all respects worthy of the enlightened head of that depart- ment, we find that no importance is attached to the place at which the requisite knowledge is gained. This board is of opinion, that the sound information necessary for those employed in the medical service of the army, may be got in other quarters as well as in London and the Scotch Universities.

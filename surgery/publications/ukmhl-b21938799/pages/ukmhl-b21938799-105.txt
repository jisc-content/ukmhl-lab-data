FRACTURE OF THE LOWER JAW. 71 Mujudior, October 27th, 1858, which entered over right malar bone, and made exit through the mouth, lacerating its left angle and carrying away the greater portion of the right superior maxillary bone, fractured the right zygoma, displaced the right malar bone, and fractured also left superior maxillary and left ramus of lower jaw. The left superior maxilla has been allowed to get into the middle of the mouth, so that its teeth come against the centre of the tongue. The fracture of the lower jaw is pretty wrell united, but of course the jaw cannot be brought into apposition. Invalided.

In a case of this kind under the care of Rose Bradford, the patient, a soldier aged 30, was admitted to hospital on account of epileptic attacks. Multiple small tumours, one of which was excised and proved to be a cysti- cercus, were present in the subcutaneous tissues of the scalp, trunk, and limbs, and beneath the tongue.

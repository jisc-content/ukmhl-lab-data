Introduce a catheter, of the form shown, by the urethra, to favour cicatrisation and consolidation of the parts. Great care is required in the after-treatment. The bladder must be kept tolerably empty, by the use of the catheter, Fig. 49. Fig. 50. and the bowels at comparative rest by the administration of opium. After eight or ten days the sutures may be removed, and the bladder gradually allowed to distend, and at length to empty itself by its proper expulsive effort.

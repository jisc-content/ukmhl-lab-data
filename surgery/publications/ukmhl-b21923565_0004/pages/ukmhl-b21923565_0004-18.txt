998 REGIONAL SURGERY. to a delicate handle composed of silver wire. In xasing it the surgeon should first cut the membrane to adapt it to the orifice in each case, then dip it in warm water, and pass it down through the tubular ear-speculum till it reaches the orifice of the tympanum. In many cases the improvement is immediate, but of course we cannot expect benefit in those cases where the cavity of the tympanum and internal ear have suffered in their organisation from previous disease. The artificial membrane requires to be removed almost daily to be kept clean, but the patients soon learn to do this for themselves.

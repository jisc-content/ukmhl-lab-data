If dissected out, it will probably be replaced by a similar growth ; if left uninterfered with, its tissue is almost sure to undergo contraction in due course. I have never known anything but disappointment follow operative treatment. Sometimes keloid growths apparently occur independently of previous injury, but the probability is that even so they started from a scar of a trivial or of an unnoticed lesion. In any case, the presence of keloid is strongly suggestive of tuberculosis.

In certain cases of febrile disease we occasionally meet with acute ulceration of all the coats of an artery at some one point, without any appearance of the usual atheromatous or other deposit, and in such cases the blood is directly effused into the cellular tissue of the neighbourhood, where it forms a diffuse aneurismal swelling.

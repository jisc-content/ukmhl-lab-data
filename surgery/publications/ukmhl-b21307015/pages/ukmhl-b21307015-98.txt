The metacarpal hones and phalanges are long bones, of a length proportionate to the arm and to the moving powers which are intended for their action ; they give breadth and extent to the hand, and facility in all the movements which that important organ is destined to perform. The Muscles are naturally divided into groups, which concur mutually in certain actions that are obviously necessary to the effective movements of the limb.

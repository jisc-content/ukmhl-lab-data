111 Sir, The Students and Pupils of the School of Medicine attached to the Rich- mond Surgical Hospital beg to take the earliest opportunity of presenting to you their sincere congratulations on your re- storation to health, and the resumption of your public duties. Instructed, as we always have been, by your writings, and peculiarly gratified by your Introductory Lecture on the 8th inst., it is our unanimous request, that an address valuable for the information it contains, and admirable for its bold and manly expo- sition of principles, true in themselves, but hitherto too much neglected, should be communicated to the profession and public at large.

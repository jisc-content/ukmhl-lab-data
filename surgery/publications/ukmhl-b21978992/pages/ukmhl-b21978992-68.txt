In the case of compound fractures, again, it frequently happens that the mortification is not of limited extent, but preceded by a quickly spreading gangrenous inflammation, engages the whole limb, and proves fatal before the end of many hours. The discrimination be- tween those cases of external injury in which the mortification is to remain local, and those in wliich it is to extend, requires no less ex- perience than judgment; and it appears not improbable that some at least of the cases, in which amputation has been reported as suc- cessful on accoimt of spreading gangrene, were not really of the for- midable nature supposed.

These preparations are attended with so much trouble in the making, that it is of some consequence to be able to preserve them. If we endeavour to do this, by merely var- nishing and drying them, we shall soon see our labour de- feated ; for the change from the horizontal position, or a change of temperature, will, in all probability, burst the vessels.—By preserving them in spirits of turpentine, we shall not only avoid the changes of temperature, and the destruction by insects, but add much to the beauty of ihe- preparations. •/ r,5 0115

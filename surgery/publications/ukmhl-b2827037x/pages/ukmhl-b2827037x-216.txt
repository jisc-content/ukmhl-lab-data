The stomach- pump was used, and brought up an opaque fluid, with a few drops of blood. The stomach was well washed out till the water brought up was clear. On the following day there was consider- able vomiting, pain in the stomach, and feeble pulse; these symptoms were relieved by leeches to the epigastrium, and saline medicines, with opium. She was convalescent Dec. 6th. Another case of poisoning with mercurial precipitate was ad- mitted soon afterwards; the patient, a female, recovered, but was severely salivated.

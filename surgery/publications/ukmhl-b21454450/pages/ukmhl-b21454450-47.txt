CHAPTER II. OF IRRITABLE URETHRA. It has been stated in the preceding Chapter, that, for the most part, strictures are preceded by a peculiar state of the canal denominated Irritable Urethra; wherefore it is obvious that the causes giving rise to both maladies will be similar. The urethra, however, may labour for a considerable period under this affection before contraction takes place in any part of it, of which I have seen repeated examples. The morbid sensibility which is its principal feature may affect the whole canal, or be confined to one portion of it; ia which latter case it is almost invariably situated in that part enve- loped by the prostate gland.

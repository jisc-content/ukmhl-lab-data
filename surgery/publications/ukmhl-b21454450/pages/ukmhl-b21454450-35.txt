A TREATISE ON STRICTURE OF THE URETHRA, IN WHICH THE DIFFERENT MODES OF TREATMENT ARE ADAPTED TO THEIR RESPECTIVE CASES. CHAPTER I. OF STRICTURES OF THE URETHRA IN GENERAL. Whether strictures be considered with refer- ence to the frequency of their occurrence, the occasional difficulty experienced in effecting their removal, or the sufferings they produce, it must be admitted that there is no complaint which more strongly demands the study of the surgeon. It is the object of this Chapter to consider the different situations of strictures, the causes giving rise to them, and the kind and degree of alteration of structure by which they are ac- companied.

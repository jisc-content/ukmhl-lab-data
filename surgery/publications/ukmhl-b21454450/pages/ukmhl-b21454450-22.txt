The concluding Remarks on Tracheotomy are intended to excite the attention of the profession to this operation as a more constant remedy than it has hitherto been for one of the most insidious and afflicting maladies to which human nature is liable. Lastly, I would observe, that in whatever points throughout the volume I may have agreed with or differed from preceding writers, it contains nothing (unless expressedly so stated), which has not fallen under my own observation.

CHAPTER IV. OF THE SYMPTOMS OF STRICTURE. There is a considerable variety in the symptoms of stricture in different individuals. In the first place, many of them may be absolutely want- ing; secondly, where they all exist they may vary in severity; and thirdly, there are cases in which, although there is much difference in the degree of rapidity which marks their pro- gress, the symptoms do eventually acquire the same general character. These varieties may no doubt be influenced by a correspondent variety of causes, but in the majority of cases they will be better explained by the usual habits of the patients.

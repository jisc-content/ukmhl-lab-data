TEE GRAFT OF SURGERY. 105 clearly proved by its having attained a ninth edition in 1641 (ninety-three years after its original publication). The surgeons of St. Bartholomew's Hospital, William Bedon, Richard Story, Edward Baily, and William Clowes, thought it "so learned a worke of Anatomie," as worthy to be by them " students in Chyrurgerie, not without our great study, paines, and charges, newly revived, collected and published abroad to the commodity of others " long after the death of the author.

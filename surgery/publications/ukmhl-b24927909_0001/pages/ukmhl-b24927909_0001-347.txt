At the expiration of the month, she got up daily, and walked about the ward ; although, on her admission into the hospital, she was incapable of walking at all. There was not the least oedema of the limb. Its circumference, at the calf, was but one- third of an inch less than the opposite side. Having walked many times the length of the ward, she became tired, and thought that the limb which had been operated on felt more fatigued than the other. The aneurismal tumour remains at this time of a considerable size. It is certainly more than one-third less than at the time of the operation. I have related the case thus particularly, in order that the reader may judge of it for himself.

SECTION XII.—AMPUTATION AT THE ANKLE-JOINT. I. Syme's amputation.—The essence of this operation consists in the removal of the entire foot, and in preserving the integument of the heel as the most natural pad for the stump to rest upon. The incisions employed are different in the hands of various sui-geons. But that generally adopted is represented in the cut {Vig. 1). The knife is carried round down to the bone.

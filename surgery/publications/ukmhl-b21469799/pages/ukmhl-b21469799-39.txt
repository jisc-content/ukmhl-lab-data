To expect that the absorbent system was able to take up so large a body, was a bold idea. Nothing is so flattering to man, as to find his inductions formed from reasoning, attended by the expected results. I will not say that Mr. Hunter felt what Columbus did, when the discovery of land justified the view which he had taken of the form of the earth; or what Dr. Franklin did, when the sparks from his electrical kite, proved that the idea he had formed of light- ning being electricity, was just.

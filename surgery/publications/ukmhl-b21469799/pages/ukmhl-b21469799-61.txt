51 His Disseitation on the operation of Litho- tomy affords useful and important hints to the Surgeon; his own success in that operation, evinced his dexterity and his skill. His proposal for a new method of extract- ing the opaque crystalline lens, displays much ingenuity. But while the world lasts, it will have reason to remember with gratitude the name ofEarle. That frequent disease, the hydrocele, is now no longer dreaded. Previous to his time, the common way of curing hydrocele was by a severe operation, which requires a long confinement. But the present manner of treating it, now generally adopted, is attended with very little pain, and scarcely any confinement.

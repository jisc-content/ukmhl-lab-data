In the beginning of my practice of this, I always had my caustics and hot irons in readiness; that if any thing happened otherwise than I expected in this my new work, I might have assistance from the old practice; until at length, con- firmed by happy experience of almost an infinite number of particulars, I bid eternal adieu to all hot irons and caustics, and I think it fit that all surgeons should do the like." After the death of Pare, Surgery seems to have made a retrograde movement; even the use of his ligatures was opposed by the Fabricii.

He says that in the year 1536, being a sur- geon in the French army, after a battle with the Imperialists, there were a great many men with gun-shot wounds. He owns he was not very expert at dressing such wounds, but that all the surgeons at that time dressed them with boiling elder oil, with which the wounds were filled as full as possible. It happened there were so many wounded men, that the scalding oil was all expended; and that he might not seem to have left the rest undressed, he was forced to apply a dressing made of the yolk of an egg and oil of roses.

His Present Majesty, whose calamity we all deplore, whose many virtues and amiable qualities will long live in the recollection of the British nation, was ever conspicuous for the zeal with which he promoted the interests of the arts and sciences. This I may be allowed to advance without the imputation of flattery, which at all times would have been unseasonable, and would now, alas! be vain.

Hippocrates, born about 46o years before our aera, probably borrowed his autho- rities from the great collection of the votive tablets in the temples of Esculapius; and re- garding medicine and philosophy as insepa- rable, he justly merited the title of the Father of Physic. But owing to the prejudice which still prevailed respecting the treatment of dead bodies, little progress was made in Anatomy; it is even disputed, whether Hippocrates himself had ever dissected a human body; and Galen is said to have undertaken a journey to Alexandria, for the express puipose of gratifying his curiosity by the sight of a human skeleton.

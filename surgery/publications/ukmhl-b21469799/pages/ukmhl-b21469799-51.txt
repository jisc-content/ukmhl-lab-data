About sixty years ago, the Abbe Needham thought he had discovered spontaneous gene- ration, having detected some animalcules or vermes in a paste made of rye flower, which had been baked in an oven, in a bottle ex- hausted of air, and closely corked. But these vermes were discovered to be true worms, which are found in spoiled corn, possessing the singular property of retaining life for an indefinite time when perfectly dried, and of becoming reanimated when moistened with a little water.

At the end of two or three months, assistance may be derived from the use of a simple lateral poroplastic or aluminium splint with a food-piece, or some other form of apparatus which aims at correcting the deformity. If this regime is systematically persevered with from within a few days after birth, by the time the child is able to put its foot to the ground the deformity will be so far corrected that some form of apparatus can be worn. Fig. 291.—Congenital Talipes Equino-varus, in man mt. 24. If the equinus element resists correction, the tendo Achillis should be lengthened. If the sole can be brought into contact with the ground, the weight of the body also acts in the direction of correcting the deformity. The turning in of the toes is best overcome by strapping the feet at night to a wooden board with the whole lower limb rotated outwards so that the toes of each foot point directly outwards. On account of the tendency towards relapse, the manipulations and massage must be persevered with for at least a year.

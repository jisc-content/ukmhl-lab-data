Genu Valgum and Genu Vaeum. In the normal limb, a line drawn from the centre of the head of the femur to a point midway between the malleoli passes through the centre of the knee-joint. If the line passes outside Fig. 285. the centre of the knee-joint, the condition is one of genu valgum; if inside, it is one of genu varum (Fig. 285). Genu Valgum—Knock-knee.—In this deformity the leg joins the thigh at an angle which is open outwards. When the affection is bilateral the projecting knees tend to knock against each other in walking, and the term X-legs may be applied to it.

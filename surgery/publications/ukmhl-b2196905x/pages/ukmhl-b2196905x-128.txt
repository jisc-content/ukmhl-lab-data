In rare instances, as the result of accidental inoculation, the tonsil may become the seat of a primary syphilitic sore. The chancre, which is always single, usually appears as a superficial ulceration with an indurated base, dis- charging a scanty fluid, and it is soon followed by enlargement of the glands about the angle of the jaw. The diagnosis of this condition is often somewhat difficult, especially in the early stage before any secon- dary symptoms have showed themselves. The history of the case and the subacute nature of the affection may, however, perhaps throw some light upon its nature.

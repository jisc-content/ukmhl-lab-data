Sometimes the injury to the bladder is not recognised at the time of operation, and is only suggested by the patient passing blood-stained urine by the urethra, and by the onset of septic complications associated with urinary infiltration, the escape of urine from the wound, and the formation of a urinary fistula. In multiparous women a hernial protrusion of the bladder into the vagina—vaginal cgstocele—is by no means uncommon.

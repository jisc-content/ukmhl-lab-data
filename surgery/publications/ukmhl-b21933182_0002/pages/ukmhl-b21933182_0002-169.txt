Carcinoma.—The variety of carcinoma most frequently met with in the upper jaw is the squamous epithelioma. In a majority of cases the tumour originates in the mucous membrane of the alveolus or palate, and it is often associated with irritation produced by a carious tooth or by an ill-fitting tooth plate.

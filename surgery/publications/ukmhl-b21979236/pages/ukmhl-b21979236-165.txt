If the artery is detached too far from its sheath, it will he liable to slough, and secondary haemorrhage ensue at the seat of ligature. In the largest arteries, if one half only of the circumference is liberated, the proceeding must be repeated on the other side. -/. As soon as the artery is loosened all round, a bent probe (or strabismus-hook) is passed carefully round the vessel, and always from the side where the vein lies, whilst a pair of forceps keeps the sheath upon the stretch (fig. 282).

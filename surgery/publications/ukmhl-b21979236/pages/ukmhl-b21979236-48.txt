English wooden splints (after Bell, Pott and Cline) are neatly cut out of wood to the shape of the limb; to the outer side are fastened strips of leather, beneath which straps provided with buckles can be drawn through (fig. 45 and 46). Fig. 45. Two of BELL'S splints for the leg. Four of BELL'S splints for the thigh. They are padded with cotton wool, and can be very easily applied, loosened, and taken off, especially if the straps are provided with Emmert's buckles (fig. 46).

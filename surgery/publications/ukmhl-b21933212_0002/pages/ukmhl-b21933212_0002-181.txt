EXPOSURE OF NERVES IN THE LOWER LIMB 143 EXPOSURE OF THE ANTERIOR CRURAL NERVE Should this nerve require to be exposed, it is best reached where it passes into the thigh between the psoas and ihacus muscles. The nerve trunk has only a short course, as it soon breaks up into its terminal branches. The psoas muscle intervenes between it and the femoral artery. It should be exposed by a vertical incision two inches or more in length just external to the line of the artery ; after the fascia lata has been divided, the hip joint is slightly flexed, and the nerve will be found on the outer side of the femoral artery.

158 SYPHILIS. slit up. The bowels should be kept open, and a nutrient, but non-stimulant diet given, and febrifuge mixtures to allay the febrile excitement, as in cases of irritable gangrene. In some cases we require to give stimulants to support the strength, but generally in the acute stage a non-stimulating diet should be given. Perfect rest must of course be enjoined. From the rapid spread of the ulceration, a good deal of bleeding may occur, but this natural depletion does good rather than otherwise. In the phfigedenic chancre we must avoid the use of mercury, which is not likely to do good, and very apt to do harm; the diseased condition must be treated on the general principles of treatment in ordinary phageda^na, which have been already explained.

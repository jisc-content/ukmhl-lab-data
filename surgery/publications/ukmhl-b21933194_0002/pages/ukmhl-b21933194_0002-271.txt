There is transmitted pulsation in the tumour, but no expansion. The diagnosis has to be made from lymphoma, adenoma, tuberculous glands, sarcoma, and carcinoma. In a large proportion of the cases operated upon it has been necessary to ligature the carotids and to excise portions of the internal jugular vein, and the mortality has hitherto been very high. Operation is therefore only to be recommended when the growth is rapid, or the symptoms have become urgent.

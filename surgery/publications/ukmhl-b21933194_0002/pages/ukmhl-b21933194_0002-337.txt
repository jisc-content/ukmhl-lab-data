Tumours in the mediastinum, anti masses of tuberculous or malignant bronchial glands, are liable to press upon the air- passages and recurrent laryngeal nerve, particularly in children, and to give rise to difficulty in breathing, hoarseness, and a crowing cough. Such glands may also press upon the oeso- phagus and interfere with swallowing, or they may, after forming adhesions with its walls, drag upon it and give rise to one form of “traction diverticulum.”

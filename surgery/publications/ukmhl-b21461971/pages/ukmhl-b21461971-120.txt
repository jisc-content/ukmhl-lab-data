It would be incompatible with the design of this undertaking to describe all the minor modes of lithotomy, which have at different aeras been recom- mended by surgical writers. The many disastrous events which have resulted from the use of the gorget, have led some modern lithotomists to dis- card it from practice, and substitute the knife, or some other cutting instrument in its place. Hence I have been induced to describe the method of using it.

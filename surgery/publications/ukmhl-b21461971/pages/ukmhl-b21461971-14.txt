He trusts that what he has published will be received with that liberality and candour which are the best ornaments of professional excellence. He has no wish to disarm the severity of criticism by acknow- ledgments or professions, because he makes no pretensions beyond such as are founded on a sincere desire to advance the interests of surgical science, and to facilitate the operations of surgical skill. JAMES BARLOW. Blackburn, January, 1822.

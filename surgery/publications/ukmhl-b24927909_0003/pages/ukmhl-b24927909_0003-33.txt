It may further be stated as an argument against the hasty performance of this oper- ation, that it is likely to aggravate the in- flammation of the brain, which in the ma- jority of cases comes on in consequence of the injury. If it can be shewn, that injury done to the scalp and bone, where there is no frac- ture or concussion, may sometimes be pro- ductive of inflammation of the brain, it would then follow, that the injury inflicted on these parts in the operation of trephin- ing would probably aggravate the inflam- matory symptoms, which are to be expected to succeed to all violent blows on the head.

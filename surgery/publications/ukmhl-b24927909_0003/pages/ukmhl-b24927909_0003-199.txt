But should this be attempted in other cases, it becomes very essential to keep the thorax filled with air, lest the lungs should become prematurely inflated, the newly- healed part lacerated, the haemorrhage re- newed, or inflammation induced ; and the surgeon would be able, I believe, without much contrivance, to regulate the inflation of the lungs as circumstances seemed to in- dicate. Surgeons used formerly to keep canulae in the thorax in these cases, with a design to give an outlet to fluids ; but such means might have been beneficial, by pre- serving the lungs collapsed; and they might have been continued from being found ser- viceable, though the manner in which they became so was unknown.

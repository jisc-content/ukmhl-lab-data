Slow degenerations of the spinal chord are not easily combated with success. Considerable changes of structure have taken place, as shown by the symp- toms, before the patient becomes alarmed and applies for relief. He has had a feeling of distension about the lower part of the bowels, and, voiding his urine with some difficulty, perhaps suspects stricture of the urethra as the cause. He lifts his feet awkward- ly, sets them down clumsily, and all of a piece ; his knees totter, there is no feeling about his but- tocks, and a numbness round the anus. At length he is for the first time alarmed by incontinence of urine having supervened, or by the limbs having sunk un- der the weight of the trunk, and come to the ground with violence. The remedial means are local abstrac- tion of blood from over the seat of the disease, fol- lowed by friction and counter-irritation. Strychnine may be tried in some cases.

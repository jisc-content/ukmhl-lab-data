Malignant tumours of the chest wall may be either carcinomata, when they are secondary to cancer in other situations, more especially the breast; or sarcomata, when they are primary growths affecting the sternum and ribs. They are usually of a very malignant type, grow rapidly, and speedily disseminate in other organs. It is only in the very early stages that these growths can be removed with any prospect of non-recurrence.

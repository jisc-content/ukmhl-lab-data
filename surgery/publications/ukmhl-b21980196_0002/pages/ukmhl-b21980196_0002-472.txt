Every sudden division of organic parts produced by mechanical violence, and at first accompanied with more or less bleeding, is called a Wound ( Vulnus, Lat. ; Wunde, Germ. ; Plaie, Fr.). 267. Wounds are differently divided, according to the instruments by which they have been produced, according to the condition of the wounded part, according to their form and direction, and according to their seat. 268.

As a rule, neither heat nor cold should be applied to the eyes continuously for any long time; an hour or a half hour a day will usually be enough. Leeches are frequently called for to reduce congestion and inflammation. They may be applied to the temples or behind the ears. Setons in the ears or the nape of the neck are too much neglected by many ophthalmologists. In chronic cases, they often bring about cures when all other means fail.

393 SALINE SOLUTION FOR TRANSFUSION. of the following:—Chloride of sodium, 50 grains ; chloride of potassium, 3 grains; sulphate of soda and carbonate of soda, of each 25 grains ; phosphate of soda, 2 grains ; water, 1 pint. The solution should be of a temperature of from 98° to 100° Falir. Whatever fluid be injected, whether blood, milk, or the saline solution, the quantity must be regulated by the effect. A few ounces will usually be found sufficient.

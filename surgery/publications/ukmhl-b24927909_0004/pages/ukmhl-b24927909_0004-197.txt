189 ON LUMBAR ABSCESSES. September 22d, a fortnight after the former evacuation, I discharged four ounces of similar serous fluid mixed with pus. During its evacuation, which was very speedy, I had applied my fingers beneath Poupart’s ligament, as if to obstruct the descent of any matter from the loins. I then desired the man to cough; but no matter descended, and the collection appeared to me entirely confined to the thigh. I found some difficulty in introducing a trochar, when the abscess contained so little fluid. This was remedied by first intro- ducing a lancet through the fascia, and then conveying the trochar through the aperture made by the lancet.

AFFECTIONS OF THE TONGUE 4 Fig. 26.—Excision of large areas of Leucoplakia from the Tongue. 1. The curved incision (A) across the dorsum, well behind the leucoplakic area. 2. (A) Anterior margin of the tongue; C, the incision, turning down the flap c. 3. Transverse section of the tongue showing (c) the mucous membrane of the under surface of the tongue turned down as a flap, and (b) the muscular fibres of the tongue cut across transversely on the level of the incision A in i. 4. The completed operation. The flap of mucous membrane (a) from the under surface is brought up and sutured to the incision on the dorsum. The tongue is thus considerably shortened, but is freely movable.—Bullin.

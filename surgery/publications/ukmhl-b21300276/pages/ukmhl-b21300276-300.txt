Portal alludes to the change of situation which the kidnies undergo, when the body is laid on the belly; in this case, they are at a greater distance from the suiface, a fact very essential to be known in the treatment of wounds and ulcers of these organs. To convince himself of this point, he has several times made punctures into the lumbar region, and although he thrust the instrument to the depth of near three inches, the kidnies were not injured; this explains the account given by Douglas, of his having in consequence of the excessive depth of the kidney, attempted in vain to iierform the operation of nephro- tomy. Stones have been extracted through fistulous sores in the loins, but there is not on record a well authenticated instance where a cut was made through the healthy skin into the kidney, for the purpose of removing calculi from this organ. Position of body, however, is not the only circum- stance which influences the relation of the abdominal viscera, to each other, and to the walls which enclose them. At an early age we observe a remarkable deviation in this respect. In infancy the abdomen is longer and larger in all its dimensions, than at the adult period of life.

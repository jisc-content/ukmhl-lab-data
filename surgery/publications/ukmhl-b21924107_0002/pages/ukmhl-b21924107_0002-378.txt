I have seen many instances where the dura mater and pia mater have been wounded, and the patients recover; but few where the dura mater has been wounded alone. After trephining, the elevator should be introduced to raise the depressed bone, and thus return it to its natural situation. You see there is no necessity to operate where there is any additional risk, because an ope- ration in a part where there is no such risk, can be as well performed, and the elevator be introduced under the depressed bone. The scalp is to be returned over the opening, and a poultice should be applied, which, I believe, is the application mbst congenial with the feelings, and most conducive to the safety of the patient. If there be a necessity for taking away more than one portion of bone, the same plan is to be pursued in each operation. OF WOUNDS ON THE SCALP.

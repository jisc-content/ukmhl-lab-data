Patients will sometimes recover from abscesses of this part, in which . immense quantities of matter have formed. I remember one of enormous magnitude, from which the patient recovered. Case. Dr. Saunders, the lecturer on medicine at Guyâ€™s Hospital, asked me to see a woman who had a large abscess in the side. I made an opening into it with a lancet, and it dis- charged a surprising quantity of matter, as much as would fill two thirds of a wash-hand basin; but I have heard, indeed, of cases of much larger quantities of matter having been discharged. After pressing out the matter, I passed a roller tightly round the abdomen, with a view of producing the adhesive pro- cess, which now and then occurs. I did not see the patient again; but some days after I met Dr. Saunders, who asked me how I thought the patient was proceeding: I told him I supposed he asked me, because he thought me very sanguine; and he replied, that I should be gratified to learn, that the woman was doing extremely well.

You must hear the untoward cases of your profession, as well as those of which the issue is favourable, in order to form a correct judgment in your minds of what surgery really is. It is for these rea- sons, that I shall never hesitate, coute qui coute,"' to detail to you, and perhaps to the public, those cases which have ter- minated unfavourably. I have a duty to perform, and I shall never shrink from the discharge of it. It is by detailing to you the unfavourable as well as the favourable cases, that I can alone perform that duty; for it is by such a course alone that I can point out to you the rocks which you are to avoid, as well as the haven in which you are to endea- case. vour to anchor.

In young subjects the periosteum is thicker, more active, more substantial, and more easily stripped off. It may also be said that it is more precious, and is in more need of being preserved. In cases attended by chronic inflammation the perios- teum is generally very easily detached, but in .such a condition it is often of doubtful value. It may be said, in conclusion, that the subperiosteal operation is excellent in theory, but is only excellent in practice in a few selected cases.

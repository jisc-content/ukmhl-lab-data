If the globe be collapsed, as is frequently the case when excision is carried out, the operation becomes a very meagre affair, scarcely removed from the humble procedure of de- taching a slough with scissors and forceps. It is well in these cases, however, to take care to remove the globe alone, and to leave the muscles with as little of their substance displaced as possible.

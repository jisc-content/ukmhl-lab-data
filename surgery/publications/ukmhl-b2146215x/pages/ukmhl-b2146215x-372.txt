Having dissected the muscles, and applied a knowledge of their action on the hones, and joints, to practical purposes; the student will trace the main arteries, and their branches, the principal veins, the anterior crural, and sacro sciatic nerves, and obserVe their relative position with respect to the muscles, to the bones, and to each other. The abdominal aorta terminates at the fourth lumbar vertebra, by dividing into the common iliac arteries. Each common iliac passes obliquely out- wards, aiid downwards, and just at the sacro iliac symphysis, divides into the internal and external iliac branches. The internal iliac artery is principally distiibuted to the pelvic viscera; but there are branches, viz.=^ the obturator, glutgeal, and ischiatic, which supply the muscles of the thigh, and are important anasto- mosing vessels when the external iliac is obliterated.

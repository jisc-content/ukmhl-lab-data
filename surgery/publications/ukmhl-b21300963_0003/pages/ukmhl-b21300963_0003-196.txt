If, however the Sceptics had even the power to injure me, still I should not fear them; because I place between us the undisguised truth, which they can neither conquer nor con- front. For truth possesses a power which poets have represented by symbols ; like the jEgis of Minerva, or the spear of Ithuriel, it has the power, not only of protecting and maintaining what is right, but of revealing, abashing, and appalling, what is wrong.

LECTURE VII. 332 The opinion that Intelligence must have ordained the order of Nature, is not only impressed by her decrees upon the bulk of mankind, but is confirmed by the obser- vations and reflections of the most ob- servant and intellectual individuals of the human race. Those who think that intel- ligence may exist distinct from organiza- tion, are disposed to admit that the intelli- gence with which they are endowed, may have a separate existence. Those who think that perception is not essential to life, but is an attribute of something different, are also disposed to admit the separate existence of perception and intelligence, and thus do these two opinions produce and support each other.

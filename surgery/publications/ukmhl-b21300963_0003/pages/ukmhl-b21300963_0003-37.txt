Fontana, it must be granted, possessed considerable talent in microscopical obser- vations, for he says, that he could readily distinguish the nature of any animal sub- stance, which might be placed on the field of his microscope, by regarding its ultimate fibres, and according to him the muscular fibres are much smaller than those of the nerves. Proscaska and others assert, that the ultimate muscular fibres are continued throughout the whole length of a muscle. How marvellous, (could we but see it,) would such a slender thread appear, con- tinued throughout the whole length of the human sartorius. Haller, however, affirms that the fibres are not continued, but that one set terminating another begins. Sus- pecting that Haller employed the solar mi- croscope on this occasion, as he says he had done on others, I examined muscular fibres with this instrument.

How extraordinary a man, thought I, is this, who could bestow such surprising labour on so hope- less a subject. I also candidly acknowledge that I now think, as probably the com- prehensive and discerning mind of Mr. Hunter then perceived, that much good may eventually be derived from patient and accurate observation with respect to this subject. We find many disorders and consequent diseases arise from sympathy, and that the organ sympathetically dis- turbed, often suffers more than that origi- nally affected. Yet its disorder may not be susceptible of cure by medical treatment, whilst the cause remains.
